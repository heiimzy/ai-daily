⚡ WAIC 2026 Terminal Explosion: AI Agent Phone Wars, Qwen Smart Glasses Upgrade, Bose AI Earbuds Debut

July 19, 2026 — Day 3 of WAIC 2026 has been dominated by an explosion of AI-powered terminal devices. From AI Agent smartphones to smart glasses and AI earbuds, Chinese tech companies are collectively unveiling a new vision of "AI stepping out of screens and into everyday life." The Nubia NaviX Ultra sold out its first 30,000 units instantly; StepX Neo debuted with a custom agent operating system; Qwen and Bose jointly launched AI agent earbuds. Here is the full panorama of WAIC Day 3's terminal race.

AI Agent Smartphone Three-Way War: Nubia, StepFun, Honor

The most striking trend at this year's WAIC is the concentrated emergence of AI Agent smartphones. Three manufacturers have revealed their competing visions of the "next-generation phone," each taking a distinctly different technological path.

Nubia NaviX Ultra: Sold Out Instantly, Doubled on Resale Market

ZTE's Nubia brand unveiled the NaviX Ultra on WAIC's opening day, and it became the hottest consumer electronics product at the conference. Calling it the "world's first AI Agent smartphone," the device runs ByteDance's Doubao Phone Assistant and can be activated by voice or a dedicated button to execute multi-step tasks autonomously.

According to Nubia CEO Ni Fei, the first batch of 30,000 units sold out rapidly after launch, with prices doubling on the second-hand market. The NaviX Ultra also received the 2026 SAIL (Super AI Leader) Award — the only consumer electronics product to earn this honor.

Unlike approaches that "stack AI features onto existing operating systems," the NaviX Ultra uses a native agent architecture integrating environmental perception, intent recognition, and task decomposition. Internal testing shows it completes complex multi-step office tasks 67% faster than conventional AI smartphones with 42% fewer errors. Priced at 3,499 yuan (~$516), it is available in Blue Horizon, Black, Dreamscape, and White.

StepX Neo: Self-Developed Step AOS Agent Operating System

StepFun's device brand StepX showcased another highly anticipated AI Agent phone at WAIC: the StepX Neo. Unlike Nubia, StepX chose a more radical path — building its own operating system from scratch.

The StepX Neo runs Step AOS (Agent Operating System), the industry's first mobile OS purpose-built for AI agents. Step AOS fundamentally rearchitects the phone's resource allocation logic: while traditional phones optimize energy around human-driven app usage, Step AOS designs the computing architecture around one or more concurrently running AI agents.

The StepX Neo's AI assistant "Amoo" can handle translation, scheduling, and information retrieval tasks even offline. According to StepFun, Amoo learns user preferences over time — for example, if a user frequently brings their dog, the assistant will automatically filter for pet-friendly restaurants when asked to make a reservation. Step AOS also features dedicated memory management, decision engine, and security subsystems. The decision engine automatically switches between lightweight and high-performance models based on task complexity.

Founded in 2023 by former Microsoft employees, StepFun has not yet announced pricing or a release date for the StepX Neo.

Honor: Partnering with Alibaba for AI Agent, Launching This Year

Huawei-spin-off Honor showcased an AI agent co-developed with Alibaba at WAIC, slated to ship on new devices later this year. Honor has not yet disclosed specific product names or technical details but confirmed deep integration with Alibaba's AI capabilities.

IDC analyst Arthur Guo noted that China's smartphone market has declined for five consecutive quarters, with 2026 potentially recording its steepest annual drop ever. AI phones are becoming manufacturers' primary escape route — IDC predicts AI phone shipments in China will approach 600 million units this year.

Wearable Terminal Explosion: AI Glasses and AI Earbuds Take Center Stage

Beyond smartphones, AI wearables have become another major highlight of WAIC 2026.

Qwen AI Glasses Upgraded to "Agent Glasses"

Alibaba-affiliated Qwen announced on WAIC's opening day that its AI glasses have been fully upgraded to "Agent Glasses." The upgrade enables the glasses to invoke third-party Skills and Agents through the agent platform — achieving true ecosystem openness. Three technical upgrades stand out: full-duplex voice — supporting simultaneous listening and speaking with real-time interruption; eye-tracking — where gaze becomes a command; and the industry's first real-time user vital sign monitoring.

Earlier this year, AI glasses sales surged at Shenzhen's Huaqiangbei electronics market, partly driven by government subsidies. In June, the Chinese government included AI glasses in its national subsidy program for the first time, offering up to 500 yuan (~$74) per product.

Qwen × Bose AI Agent Earbuds Make First Public Appearance

The Qwen-Bose co-branded AI agent earbuds made their public debut at WAIC. These clip-on earbuds support simultaneous interpretation, meeting minutes, health recording, and other functions. With the earbuds capturing audio and the glasses tracking visual gaze, the AI assistant can deliver a "anytime, anywhere, always responsive" experience.

The Qwen AI clip-on earbuds expand Qwen's wearable AI product portfolio, marking AI's transition from phone screens to multi-form-factor随身 devices.

AGIBOT Embodied AI Forum: Chinese Robot Exports Accelerate

On the afternoon of WAIC 2026 Day 3, AGIBOT hosted the WAIC 2026 Embodied AI Forum at the Golden Hall of the Shanghai World Expo Center, bringing together researchers, technology leaders, and robotics entrepreneurs from China and overseas to discuss the path forward for Physical AI.

AGIBOT revealed its latest overseas expansion progress at the forum: international business now accounts for over 30% of the company's revenue, with products reaching Europe (Volkswagen auto parts production lines in Germany), Asia-Pacific (Singtel Enterprise, Genting Malaysia), and North America. AGIBOT Co-founder and CTO Peng Zhihui stated: "In terms of manufacturing, engineering talent density, and application scenarios, China has significant advantages. China should take on the responsibility of a major country and export its technologies and products globally."

According to China's Ministry of Industry and Information Technology, domestic humanoid robot production exceeded 40,000 units in the first half of this year, with full-year figures projected to surpass 100,000. AGIBOT deployed over 30 robots across WAIC venues for guidance services and has reached a cumulative production volume of 15,000 robots, commanding approximately 39% of the global humanoid robot market share.

At the forum, AGIBOT demonstrated industrial deployment cases of its Expedition A3 Ultra humanoid robot — integrated into Longcheer Technology's Nanchang factory within 36 hours, processing approximately 3,000 tablet quality-inspection units per shift with over 64 hours of cumulative continuous operation and downtime losses below 4%.

Industry Outlook

WAIC 2026 Day 3 reveals a clear trajectory: AI is moving from "models" to "terminals," from "screens" to "wearables." The explosion of AI Agent smartphones, smart glasses, and AI earbuds signals that artificial intelligence has officially entered the era of "one agent per person." Simultaneously, embodied intelligence industrialization is accelerating — from factories to warehouses, from exhibition halls to homes, robots are transitioning from "can demonstrate" to "can work."

As Nubia CEO Ni Fei put it: "Many so-called AI phones on the market simply stack AI functions on top of an existing system — that actually makes it more cumbersome for users." The next-generation terminal needs to redesign the operating system and interaction paradigm for the agent era — and that is the core insight WAIC 2026 has delivered to the entire industry.
