⚡ July 17 AI Industry Midday: WAIC 2026 Opens in Shanghai, Anthropic Files for IPO, Tencent Cloud Slashes DeepSeek-V4 Prices Up to 97.5%

1. WAIC 2026 Opens in Shanghai: 1,100+ Exhibitors, 300+ Global Debuts

The 2026 World Artificial Intelligence Conference (WAIC) kicked off on July 17 across three Shanghai venues — the World Expo Center, Zhangjiang Science Hall, and West Bund Art Center. Chinese President Xi Jinping attended the opening ceremony and delivered a speech.

This year's WAIC sets multiple records: a total exhibition area exceeding 100,000 square meters for the first time, 1,100+ enterprises showcasing over 4,400 cutting-edge exhibits, with more than 300 products making their global debuts. The conference features 140+ forums with 1,500+ distinguished global guests.

Domestic Computing Power Takes Center Stage

Computing infrastructure stands out as the highlight of this year's conference. Huawei unveiled the Atlas 950 SuperCluster, with a single cabinet integrating 64 Ascend computing cards, scalable to 8,192 Ascend 950DT chips — 56.8 times the scale of NVIDIA's NVL144 solution. Sugon showcased the 100,000-card AI supercluster "Dengfeng" (Summit), recognized as one of the conference's "Treasures of the Hall."

Orientation Computing (Dongfang Suanxin) globally debuted the DF1000, the world's first software-defined near-memory computing 3D chip. The company stated that at 14nm process, the chip delivers actual performance comparable to 4nm advanced process products, and has entered mass production with delivery expected by late 2026.

AI Phone "Three-Way Battle": STEPX, Honor, and Nubia

The consumer electronics track reached a milestone breakthrough. StepFun (Jieyue Xingchen) unveiled the world's first native AI agent phone STEPX Neo, powered by the industry-first Agent-native OS Step AOS. Honor CEO Li Jian revealed the Robot Phone at WAIC, featuring a 4-DOF titanium alloy mechanical gimbal. Nubia's second-generation Doubao AI Phone NaviX Ultra also made its global debut, demonstrating cross-app autonomous execution and long-term memory capabilities.

"We're evolving from putting AI into phones to designing phones for AI." — StepFun launch event

Embodied AI Evolves From Solo Acts to Teamwork

Over 200 enterprises are participating in this year's embodied intelligence exhibition area. Ant Lingbo's "One Brain, Multiple Robots" smart pharmacy was recognized as a "Treasure of the Hall," where three different-brand robots autonomously collaborate to complete medication dispensing within 90 seconds. The solution is already operational in Guoda Pharmacy's Shanghai stores.

Siemens made its China debut of Eigen, the world's first AI agent for industrial automation engineering, capable of independently completing engineering tasks and improving execution efficiency by 2 to 5 times.

2. Anthropic Files Confidential IPO Paperwork, Valuation Nears $1 Trillion

On July 17, Anthropic confidentially submitted a draft S-1 registration statement to the U.S. Securities and Exchange Commission (SEC) for its initial public offering, taking the lead over OpenAI in the AI company IPO race.

Following its $65 billion Series H round last week, Anthropic's post-money valuation reached $965 billion — surpassing OpenAI's recent $730 billion valuation. The company's quarterly revenue is approaching $11 billion, with an estimated net profit of $560 million. Anthropic has committed at least $330 billion in computing spending across America's three major cloud providers.

OpenAI CEO Sam Altman responded: "The most intense competition in the AI industry is not about who gets to Wall Street first." However, industry observers expect OpenAI to accelerate its own IPO timeline, targeting a filing within the next few weeks.

3. Tencent Cloud Slashes DeepSeek-V4 Prices Up to 97.5%

Tencent Cloud announced on July 17 massive price cuts for its DeepSeek-V4 series models on its agent development platform, with the highest reduction reaching 97.5%.

  • DeepSeek-V4-Pro: Inference input and output prices both reduced by 75%, from 0.012/0.024 yuan per thousand tokens to 0.003/0.006 yuan
  • Cache hit price: From 0.001 yuan to 0.000025 yuan per thousand tokens, a 97.5% reduction
  • DeepSeek-V4-Flash: Cache hit price reduced 90%, from 0.0002 to 0.00002 yuan

The price cuts come amid an escalating AI model price war. As one of China's most-called model families, the reduction is expected to significantly lower the barrier for developers and drive AI application ecosystem growth.

4. Google Gemini 3.5 Pro Faces Third Delay

Bloomberg reported on July 17 that Google's flagship AI model, Gemini 3.5 Pro, has been delayed for months — its third postponement. According to 10 current and former employees, Google has invested substantial effort in improving the model's coding capabilities, but results have been disappointing.

Google's stock fell 4.43% on Thursday following the news. AI researchers noted Gemini's greatest advantage lies in accessing Google Search data, but Anthropic and OpenAI have taken the lead in building the strongest AI models.
