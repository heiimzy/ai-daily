⚡ July 16 Financial Roundup: US PPI Falls 0.3% Unexpectedly, CXMT Launches Record STAR Market IPO, Bitcoin Tops $65,000

1. US June PPI Unexpectedly Falls 0.3%, Wholesale Inflation Cools Broadly

The U.S. Bureau of Labor Statistics reported Wednesday (July 15) that the Producer Price Index (PPI) for final demand declined 0.3% in June, well below expectations for a flat reading and marking the largest monthly drop since April 2025. May's data was revised down from an initially reported 1.1% gain to 0.6%.

On a year-over-year basis, PPI rose 5.5%, below the 6.2% consensus estimate and down from 6.0% in May. Excluding volatile food and energy prices, core PPI rose just 0.1% month-over-month, missing expectations of 0.3%. Core PPI year-over-year came in at 4.7% versus 5.2% expected.

Gasoline prices plunged 12% in June, the primary driver of the decline. Energy product prices fell 6.4% overall, while goods prices dropped 1.4% — the largest decrease since July 2022. Services prices edged up 0.2%.

"The June PPI aligns with Tuesday's CPI report, both indicating inflation pressures are easing. However, the escalating US-Iran conflict could push energy costs higher in coming months, leaving the inflation outlook uncertain." — Reuters

Following Tuesday's CPI report showing consumer prices falling 0.4% month-over-month (the biggest drop in four years), this second consecutive soft inflation print has significantly reduced expectations for Fed rate hikes. Fed Chair Kevin Warsh, delivering his first congressional testimony, faces a market now pricing July rate hike odds below 10%.

2. CXMT Launches Record-Breaking STAR Market IPO — Up to 66.6 Billion Yuan

ChangXin Memory Technology (CXMT, ticker: 688825), China's leading DRAM manufacturer, opens its IPO subscription today (July 16). The online subscription code is 787825. The offering price is set at 8.66 yuan per share, with each lot of 500 shares requiring 4,330 yuan from successful retail bidders.

CXMT is initially offering 6.688 billion new shares, representing approximately 10% of post-IPO total shares. If the full 15% greenshoe (over-allotment) option is exercised, total shares offered would increase to 7.691 billion. Based on the offering price, expected proceeds are approximately 57.919 billion yuan (up to 66.607 billion yuan with greenshoe), making it the largest IPO in STAR Market history and the fifth-largest IPO overall in A-share history.

Unprecedented Institutional Demand

The bookbuilding phase attracted 333 institutional investors managing 11,537 accounts, with bids ranging from 7.26 yuan to 65.19 yuan per share. Ultimately 285 institutions submitted valid orders totaling 1.238 trillion shares — equivalent to 462.85x the initial institutional allocation.

Strategic investors include 65 institutions: the National Social Security Fund, major insurance companies, and lead underwriters CICC and CSC (each committing 1 billion yuan). CXMT founder Zhu Yiming will distribute 50% of his 1.536 billion incentive shares to employees over 10 years post-listing.

Explosive Growth Trajectory

CXMT posted Q1 2026 revenue of 50.8 billion yuan, up 719% year-over-year, with net profit turning positive at 24.762 billion yuan. The company expects H1 2026 revenue of 110-120 billion yuan (+612% to +677% YoY) and net profit of 50-57 billion yuan (+2,244% to +2,544% YoY).

At the offering price, CXMT's market cap reaches approximately 579.2 billion yuan. Some analysts predict the stock could surge 5x or more on debut, making it potentially the year's most lucrative new listing.

3. Wall Street: Cooling Inflation Fuels Tech Rally, Bank Earnings Smash Expectations

U.S. stocks closed mixed on Tuesday (July 14), with the Nasdaq and S&P 500 extending gains on the back of cooler-than-expected CPI data:

  • S&P 500: +28.25 pts (+0.38%) to 7,543.59
  • Nasdaq Composite: +233.83 pts (+0.90%) to 26,107.01
  • Dow Jones Industrial Average: +9.63 pts (+0.02%) to 52,508.27

Record Bank Profits

All five major U.S. banks beat Q2 earnings estimates: Goldman Sachs reported a record EPS of $20.98 (highest single-quarter profit ever by a U.S. bank); JPMorgan net income hit $21.2 billion with equities trading revenue surging 86% YoY; Wells Fargo net income rose 17% to $6.4 billion; Citi revenue reached $24.8 billion with EPS of $3.15; Bank of America net income grew 27% to $9.1 billion. The results were fueled by the SpaceX IPO, surging M&A advisory fees, and a boom in equities trading revenue.

Tech mega-caps Microsoft, Apple, Google, Meta, and Amazon rose 2-4% each, driving the Nasdaq's outperformance. However, IBM plunged 25% after warning of weaker Q2 revenue, dragged down by flat consulting revenue and a 7% decline in infrastructure sales.

4. Bitcoin Breaks $65,000 as Crypto ETFs See Capital Revival

Bitcoin surged past $65,000 following Wednesday's PPI release, reaching a one-month high. New York Fed President John Williams added to the positive sentiment, stating in a morning speech that "there are encouraging reasons to expect that inflation has peaked."

U.S. spot Bitcoin ETFs saw net inflows of approximately $181 million on Tuesday, reversing the prior day's $425 million outflow. BlackRock's IBIT led with $139 million, followed by Fidelity's FBTC at $21 million. Ethereum ETFs added $58 million, entirely through BlackRock's ETHA. Total Bitcoin ETF assets recovered to ~$78 billion, while Ethereum ETF assets crossed $10 billion.

Bitcoin currently trades at ~$65,100 (+0.63% 24h), with Ethereum at $1,918 (+2.47%). The Crypto Fear & Greed Index remains at 22 ("Extreme Fear"), though this marks an improvement from recent lows.
