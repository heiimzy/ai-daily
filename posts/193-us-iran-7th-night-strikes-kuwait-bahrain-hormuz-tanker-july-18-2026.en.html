⚡ July 18 International Politics: US Launches 7th Night of Iran Strikes, Iran Retaliates Across Gulf, Hormuz Tanker Blasts Reported

🔴 US Seventh Night of Strikes: Targets Expand to Infrastructure

The U.S. Central Command (CENTCOM) launched its seventh consecutive night of strikes against Iran at 3:00 PM ET Friday (03:00 UTC July 18), marking the second week of hostilities since the U.S.-Iran ceasefire memorandum of understanding collapsed. CENTCOM stated that forces struck "surveillance sites, military logistics infrastructure, underground weapons storage, and maritime capabilities."

For the first time in over a week, CENTCOM explicitly mentioned striking "military logistics infrastructure" — a significant escalation signal. President Trump had previously threatened to bomb Iranian bridges and power plants. According to Iranian state media, at least five bridges were struck in southern Iran. Attacks on Bandar Khamir bridge in Hormozgan province killed seven people and also hit the local train station. A maritime surveillance tower at Chabahar port was completely destroyed — Defense Secretary Pete Hegseth shared images of the collapsed tower on social media.

Iran's Energy Ministry acknowledged for the first time that power infrastructure was hit, urging residents in southern provinces to reduce air conditioning use. Iranian media reported that electricity facilities and desalination pumps were struck in Jask County, cutting drinking water to several villages.

🟠 Iran's Broad Retaliation: Strikes on US Bases Across Four Gulf States

In response to the seventh night of U.S. strikes, Iran launched major retaliatory operations — "Operation Thunder Phase 14" and "Operation Victory Phase 17" — targeting U.S. military facilities across multiple countries:

  • Kuwait: Iran's IRGC struck Ali Al Salem Air Base and Camp Arifjan logistics hub using drones and missiles, claiming to have disabled radar systems and destroyed a weapons maintenance hangar and drone facility. Kuwait's army confirmed several soldiers were wounded. A power generation and water desalination plant was hit, causing fires and shutting down multiple generation units. Kuwait International Airport suspended operations and rescheduled most flights.
  • Bahrain: Iranian forces targeted Sheikh Isa Air Base, claiming destruction of aircraft shelters, fuel storage tanks, and communications bridges. Iran also claimed to have destroyed Bahrain's main artificial intelligence center. Bahrain's Interior Ministry sounded air raid sirens four times in a matter of hours.
  • Jordan: Iran claimed to have hit fuel storage at Al-Azraq Air Base. Jordan's military said it intercepted and shot down 10 Iranian missiles that entered its airspace, with no casualties or damage reported.
  • Saudi Arabia: According to Axios, Iran fired a ballistic missile at a U.S. military base in Saudi Arabia — the first direct Iranian attack on the kingdom in four months. Saudi civil defense issued warnings in Al-Kharj and Yanbu.

Qatar's Interior Ministry confirmed intercepted attacks with shrapnel injuring a child. Explosions were also reported in Iraq's Kurdistan region near Erbil and Sulaymaniyah. Iran's IRGC additionally claimed to have struck a U.S. special operations command center in Syria's al-Tanf region.

🟡 Strait of Hormuz: Tanker Explosion Dispute and Maritime Escalation

Maritime conflict escalated sharply. Iran's Revolutionary Guards claimed two oil tankers "exploded and caught fire after hitting mines" while attempting to cross the Strait of Hormuz, blaming "deceptive American intelligence agencies" for guiding them into the minefield. CENTCOM swiftly denied the claim, posting on X: "Like most IRGC claims, this is false."

Meanwhile, the IRGC said it had "stopped" four ships attempting to transit the critical waterway in a combined missile and drone operation. Iran also attacked a Thai-flagged vessel in the strait. The U.S. escalated its naval blockade — Marines boarded a tanker near the strait for inspection, the U.S. military "redirected" three vessels attempting to breach the blockade, and disabled one oil tanker that disobeyed orders.

Armed men seized another vessel off Yemen, raising new security concerns at the Red Sea chokepoint. Iran has reportedly told Houthi allies to block the Bab al-Mandeb strait if the U.S. attacks Iranian power infrastructure.

According to MarineTraffic.com data, commercial vessel crossings through the strait fell to just eight ships on Thursday — a three-week low. Brent crude oil climbed more than 10% this week, breaking above $88 per barrel.

🟢 Iran Warns of Full-Scale "Offensive" Phase

Major General Mohsen Rezaei, a senior military adviser to Iran's Supreme Leader, issued a stark warning on state television. He stated that if U.S. strikes continue for another two to three days, Iran's armed forces would "move beyond the stage of deterrence and retaliation and enter the phase of offense and complete destruction."

"If U.S. attacks continue for another two to three days, the Armed Forces of the Islamic Republic of Iran will move beyond the stage of deterrence and retaliation and enter the phase of offense and complete destruction. The United States should know that the attritional strategy of 'war and negotiation' has reached a dead end."

Rezaei warned that Iran would deploy additional military capabilities including ground forces, and the war's scope would expand. IRGC aerospace force commander Majid Mousavi said "targeted strikes from across Iran will continue" until the U.S. halts operations against Iranian coastal facilities.

🔵 Iraq Signs Pipeline Deal to Bypass Strait of Hormuz

At the U.S.-Iraq business summit in Washington D.C., Iraq's government signed dozens of agreements with Western oil companies. The most significant was a deal with Chevron to rebuild the long-defunct Iraq-Syria crude oil pipeline, connecting Kirkuk in northern Iraq to Syria's Mediterranean port of Baniyas — providing Iraq a bypass route for oil exports that avoids the Strait of Hormuz.

Analysts noted that with the strait effectively closed due to the U.S.-Iran conflict, this alternative pipeline has taken on dramatically increased strategic importance. The agreement was one of the key outcomes of Iraqi Prime Minister Mohammed Shia al-Sudani's visit to Washington.

⚪ UN Deeply Concerned; Anti-War Sentiment Grows in U.S.

UN Secretary-General Antonio Guterres expressed "deep concern" over the escalation, particularly attacks on civilian infrastructure, calling them "unacceptable." He stressed that dialogue is the only path forward.

In the U.S., a new poll showed Trump's disapproval rating on Iran reached 69%, with 68% of respondents saying the war is "not worth fighting." Rising gasoline prices are becoming a political liability for Trump ahead of November's congressional midterm elections. Axios reported that the White House has informed Israel it is sending "dozens more" refueling aircraft in preparation for potentially expanded military operations. Trump stated that at the request of Qatar, Saudi Arabia, and UAE leaders, he has postponed a planned "very significant" military strike scheduled for Monday (July 19), but said he "may have no choice."

Separately, Israel launched multiple airstrikes on Gaza, killing at least 14 Palestinians — eight of them struck by a drone while attending a funeral. Israeli warplanes also conducted two dawn raids on southern Lebanon.
