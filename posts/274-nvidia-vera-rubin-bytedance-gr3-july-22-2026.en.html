⚡ July 22 Dual Breakthrough: Nvidia Vera Rubin Delivers 10x Tokens Per Watt, ByteDance GR-3 Masters Tasks With Just 10 Demonstrations

Nvidia Vera Rubin: 10x Tokens Per Watt Marks a Generational Leap

On July 21 at 11:00 PM ET, Nvidia unveiled a fresh set of performance benchmarks for its next-generation Vera Rubin platform, demonstrating a massive leap in AI factory efficiency. Cloud partner CoreWeave's early production runs showed that running DeepSeek's R1 model on Vera Rubin delivers a staggering 10x improvement in tokens per watt compared to the previous-generation GB200 NVL72 system based on Blackwell architecture.

Nvidia stated that these gains were validated across more than 250,000 distinct configurations and over 1.4 million GPU hours of testing. Remarkably, the optimizations developed for Vera Rubin can also be retroactively applied to the GB200 NVL72 system, improving throughput per megawatt by more than 4x over just three months — underscoring the power of Nvidia's hardware-software co-design approach.

Vera CPU: Custom Silicon Built for AI Agents

Nvidia also released new benchmarks for its Vera central processing unit, built on a custom microarchitecture codenamed Olympus Core and specifically optimized for the irregular control flows of autonomous AI agents:

  • 1.9x faster agentic performance compared to x86-based alternatives
  • 6x latency improvement for AI agent workloads with bursty traffic patterns
  • Nearly 100% performance lead over AMD's flagship EPYC Turin CPU on select industry benchmarks
"These gains could only be achieved through hardware-software co-design," Nvidia emphasized, noting that the company develops the software stack and networking systems needed to optimize chip performance alongside the hardware itself.

Network Revolution: NVLink 6 and Spectrum-6 Outperform Ethernet

Beyond chip-level breakthroughs, Nvidia revealed milestone data for its latest generation networking interconnects:

  • Sixth-generation NVLink 6: Delivers 2.3x higher simulated decode throughput for massive LLMs compared to Ethernet-based networks
  • Spectrum-X platform: Enables 1.6x faster remote direct memory access bandwidth with 1.7x fewer switches, resulting in 5x optical power efficiency and 10x reliability
  • Spectrum-6 deployment: Now arriving at AI factories operated by CoreWeave, Microsoft, Nebius, SpaceXAI, and Tesla

On the infrastructure optimization front, Nvidia demonstrated that dynamic full-stack optimization allows deployment of 40% more GPUs within the same power envelope. A new 45°C closed-loop liquid cooling system saves approximately 4 million gallons of water per megawatt annually compared to standard cooling methods.

ByteDance Seed Unveils GR-3 VLA Model: 77% Task Success With Minimal Training Data

ByteDance's Seed team released GR-3, a new Vision-Language-Action (VLA) model capable of generalizing to unseen objects and environments, understanding abstract language instructions, and performing fine-grained manipulation of deformable objects.

GR-3 features 4 billion parameters and introduces an innovative "triple training" paradigm combining web vision-language data (2.8B tokens), real robot trajectory data (101 hours), and VR-based human demonstration data (68 hours). This diverse data fusion enables rapid task transfer with minimal human demonstrations.

10 VR Demonstrations Boost Success Rate From 60% to 80%+

The team collected human object manipulation data using VR devices and found that just 10 trajectory demonstrations per object improved GR-3's success rate from under 60% to over 80%. For example, after only 10 human demonstrations, GR-3 can decompose a command like "clean up the desktop" into 17 individual actions.

Tested on the ByteMini dual-arm mobile robot platform, GR-3 achieved an overall task success rate of 77%, far surpassing the previous SOTA VLA model π0's ~40%. GR-3 completes visual-language-policy forward inference in just 6 seconds with end-to-end power consumption as low as 28W, demonstrating feasibility for near-commercial deployment scenarios.

Abstract Concept Understanding and "Smart Refusal"

GR-3 demonstrated impressive semantic comprehension capabilities. When given the instruction "put the blue bowl in the basket" — but no blue bowl was on the table — GR-3 accurately assessed the situation and remained still, while baseline models randomly grabbed items. When handling multiple identical objects like cups, GR-3 correctly followed the instruction to "put them all in the trash."

GR-3 also generalized to clothing types not included in its training data. When all training clothes were long-sleeved, GR-3 effectively handled short-sleeved garments. Public image-text data provided 42.8% and 33.4% success rate improvements on unseen complex instructions and new object tasks respectively.

Industry Impact: Infrastructure and Embodied AI Converge

These two breakthroughs from Nvidia and ByteDance represent the dual frontiers of AI development:

  • AI infrastructure keeps getting cheaper: Vera Rubin's per-watt efficiency leap means the same power budget can support 10x more AI inference, directly reducing cloud service costs and end-user API prices
  • Embodied AI moves from demo to near-production: GR-3 achieves with 4B parameters what previously required hundreds of billions, and sub-30W power consumption opens the door to edge deployment at scale

Nvidia shares edged up in after-hours trading as markets absorbed the Vera Rubin roadmap. ByteDance's Volcano Engine has published GR-3 technical documentation, and hardware manufacturers including DJI and Ecovacs are already evaluating migration value internally.
