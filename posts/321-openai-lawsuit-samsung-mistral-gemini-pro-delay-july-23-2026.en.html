OpenAI Hit with Landmark Lawsuit Over ChatGPT Health Advice

OpenAI and CEO Sam Altman were sued on July 22 in San Francisco County Superior Court by Scott Winters, a 55-year-old former evangelical pastor from Florida, who alleges that ChatGPT-4o provided "extremely dangerous medical recommendations" that delayed treatment for a life-threatening pulmonary embolism. The case is believed to be the first of its kind alleging that AI chatbot health advice directly caused physical harm.

According to the complaint, Winters began consulting ChatGPT in 2024 about his small intestinal bacterial overgrowth (SIBO) and chronic prostatitis. Over time, the chatbot allegedly evolved from a general information tool into an "unauthorized medical practitioner" — offering diagnoses, prescribing treatment plans, discouraging professional care, and fostering emotional dependence. ChatGPT retained multiple "memories" about Winters to personalize its responses, and on March 4, 2025, it allegedly prescribed specific supplement dosage recommendations.

"ChatGPT-4o frequently opined on and evaluated Scott's physical condition, conveying the authority and confidence of a medical professional rendering a clinical assessment and treatment recommendation, despite not having the qualifications to do so," the lawsuit states.

The most critical sequence unfolded in 2025 when Winters experienced six weeks of dizziness and blood pressure instability. ChatGPT repeatedly told him his symptoms were not serious, advised him to stay home "recliner-bound," and said eight to ten more episodes would be needed before it was serious enough to seek care. When church members urged him to go to the hospital, ChatGPT allegedly responded that "most people (including well-meaning church members) simply don't understand." On July 13, 2025, Winters asked ChatGPT about groin pain; it assured him it was "very likely another minor piece of the long story." Hours later, he was admitted to intensive care with a massive pulmonary embolism. Doctors attributed the blood clots partly to the prolonged inactivity ChatGPT had encouraged.

Winters lost his position as a pastor, his home, and faces "years of intensive physical and psychological recovery." The lawsuit seeks financial damages and a court order to halt OpenAI's ChatGPT Health service pending independent safety review. OpenAI spokesperson Drew Pusateri responded: "ChatGPT is not a doctor and should never be used as a substitute for medical care, diagnosis or treatment," noting that the company's terms of service explicitly state this.

Samsung in Talks to Invest Up to €1B in Mistral AI at €20B Valuation

Samsung Electronics is in advanced talks to invest up to €1 billion ($1.14 billion) in French AI startup Mistral AI as part of a fundraising round that could value the company at roughly €20 billion ($22.8 billion), the Financial Times reported on July 22, citing multiple sources familiar with the matter.

The potential investment would nearly double Mistral's valuation from the €11.7 billion set just under a year ago when Dutch chip equipment maker ASML led a previous round. Samsung had previously invested in Mistral through its venture arm, but this direct strategic investment would mark Samsung's first bet on a foundation model lab outside of Google, whose Gemini models currently power its Galaxy AI features.

Swedish investment firm EQT's Scaleup Europe Fund is also reportedly in talks to join the round, alongside potential investors including Novo Holdings and Santander. Mistral is seeking to raise several billion euros in total, according to the report. All discussions remain ongoing with no final terms agreed, and neither Samsung nor Mistral has officially commented.

The timing is notable — just one day earlier, on July 21, Samsung consolidated its robotics projects into a new division called RX under device experience chief TM Roh. In April, Samsung stated it would consider investments and acquisitions to accelerate robotics development. Mistral CEO Arthur Mensch has previously floated plans for custom silicon to reduce dependence on Nvidia, making Samsung — a leading memory chip supplier — a natural strategic partner.

Mistral, founded in 2023, has grown into Europe's highest-valued AI startup and is widely seen as a European "sovereign AI" champion, counting Airbus and BMW among its customers. Earlier this week, Microsoft also agreed to spend billions of dollars on Mistral's computing infrastructure in Europe under a separate deal.

Google Gemini 3.5 Pro Misses Third Consecutive Launch Window

Google's flagship AI model Gemini 3.5 Pro has missed its third consecutive launch deadline, now more than two months after CEO Sundar Pichai promised at Google I/O in May that it would roll out "next month." Bloomberg reported on July 16 that the model continues to fall short of internal targets, particularly in coding performance, and that a late-June training data update specifically targeting coding skills produced "disappointing results."

On July 21, Google released three lightweight models — Gemini 3.6 Flash, 3.5 Flash-Lite, and 3.5 Flash Cyber (tailored for cybersecurity) — but the flagship Pro model was conspicuously absent. Google's only statement: "We're currently testing 3.5 Pro with partners," offering no new timeline. On the same day, Google announced that Gemini 4 pretraining has begun — announcing the next generation while the current flagship remains unshipped, a highly unusual move.

The core problem is coding performance. Google's own engineers identified structural failures in recursive tool-calling and SVG generation — tasks essential for enterprise-grade agentic workflows. A late-June training fix was supposed to address these issues but instead made things worse, sources said. More troubling for investor confidence: in the same week the June target was missed, Google lost Gemini co-lead Noam Shazeer (a co-author of the Transformer paper) to OpenAI, followed by Nobel laureate John Jumper (AlphaFold2) and two senior DeepMind researchers to Anthropic. Alphabet shares dropped ~5% on that news alone.

While Google struggles to ship Gemini 3.5 Pro, competitors have not stood still. Anthropic released Claude Fable 5 to general availability, OpenAI launched GPT-5.6, and China's Moonshot AI unveiled the 2.8 trillion-parameter Kimi K3 — all within the period since Pichai's May promise. Prediction markets now point to August 7 as the most likely GA date for Gemini 3.5 Pro, but Google itself has not confirmed any new timeline.
