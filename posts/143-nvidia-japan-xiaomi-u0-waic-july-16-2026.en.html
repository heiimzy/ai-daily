⚡ July 16 Tech Roundup: Nvidia Launches Japan AI & Robotics Push, Xiaomi Open-Sources Robotics-U0, WAIC 2026 Opens Tomorrow

1. Jensen Huang in Tokyo: Vera Rubin in Production, Japan Physical AI Ecosystem Kicks Off

Nvidia CEO Jensen Huang appeared in Tokyo's Akihabara district Wednesday for the 30th anniversary of the Nvidia-SEGA partnership, followed by a major announcement on Thursday (July 16) at Japan's Ministry of Economy, Trade and Industry (METI)-hosted "Physical AI" event. The visit completes Huang's strategic tour of Asia's AI supply chain after stops in South Korea and Taiwan.

Vera Rubin in Production — Delay Rumors Dismissed

Addressing recent reports from research firm SemiAnalysis suggesting Vera Rubin could be delayed by over 12 months due to circuit board manufacturing bottlenecks, Huang directly refuted the claims in Tokyo: "That's not true. Vera Rubin is in production, and giant amounts of production are coming." He emphasized that the AI infrastructure buildout is "the largest infrastructure expansion in human history" and that Nvidia has "more orders today than at the last time I spoke about orders at GTC."

Nvidia's Q2 revenue guidance stands at $91 billion, with supply-related commitments reaching $119 billion. Morgan Stanley subsequently raised its Vera Rubin rack system price assumption to approximately $49 billion per gigawatt, implying significantly higher customer spending per deployment than the Blackwell platform.

Cosmos Physical AI Ecosystem: Toyota, FANUC, SoftBank Sign On

Nvidia unveiled its "Cosmos" world foundation model-driven physical AI strategy in Japan. Cosmos is a physical AI foundation model that enables AI to perceive and understand its surroundings so robots and autonomous vehicles can operate independently. Nvidia also launched "Cosmos 3 Edge," an optimized version requiring roughly one-fourth the computing resources of previous models, designed to run directly on Jetson edge AI computers.

Japanese companies joining the Cosmos ecosystem include:

  • FANUC, Fujitsu, Kawasaki Heavy Industries, Kubota, Mitsubishi Electric, NEC, SoftBank, and Yoshikawa — using Cosmos to develop manufacturing and robotics AI models;
  • Omron, Sony, and Woven by Toyota — leveraging Nvidia's Isaac robotics platform and Jetson computers for next-gen robot development;
  • Toyota — expanding cooperation from autonomous driving to factory digital twins and smart city projects using Nvidia's DRIVE AGX platform and Omniverse.

Jetson T3000/T2000: Next-Gen Edge AI Computers

Nvidia also unveiled the next-generation edge AI computers "Jetson T3000" and "Jetson T2000," designed for industrial environments such as factories and robotics. The new products support Agentic AI for robot software development and memory optimization, with hardware shipping planned for Q1 2027.

"Tomorrow can be called the starting point of Japan AI." — Jensen Huang, speaking at the SEGA event in Tokyo, highlighting Japan's historic strengths in precision manufacturing and the potential for AI and robotics to address the country's labor shortage.

2. Xiaomi Open-Sources Robotics-U0: 38B-Parameter Embodied AI Model with 83x Efficiency Gain

Xiaomi officially released and fully open-sourced Xiaomi-Robotics-U0 on July 15 — a 38-billion-parameter multimodal autoregressive embodied generation foundation model, the industry's first unified solution covering four core embodied generation tasks. All source code and weights are available on GitHub, Hugging Face, and ModelScope.

Breaking the Data Bottleneck in Robot Training

The biggest challenge in robot training is data collection — capturing extreme, dangerous, and long-tail scenarios in the real world is expensive and difficult. Xiaomi-Robotics-U0's core value is its ability to autonomously generate and augment the images and video data needed for robot training, effectively building a "data factory" for embodied intelligence.

The model unifies four capabilities:

  • Embodied scene generation — automatically generating robotic work environments
  • Embodied trajectory transfer — transferring motion trajectories across different scenarios
  • Robot interaction video generation — creating video data of robot operations
  • General text-to-image and image editing — supporting natural language control over scene elements

The model employs a proprietary five-dimensional decoupled structured control paradigm, allowing independent natural language adjustment of workspace layout, objects, clutter, lighting, and backgrounds.

FlashAR+: 83x Speedup in Inference

Xiaomi's self-developed FlashAR+ inference acceleration scheme, combined with vLLM paged KV cache batch scheduling, compresses 1024×1024 image generation from over 400 seconds to just 5.44 seconds — an 83x improvement over traditional autoregressive architectures. This makes large-scale embodied data production industrially viable.

In real-world robot tests, models trained with U0-augmented data showed an average 26% improvement in task completion under unfamiliar lighting and background conditions. On the WorldArena embodied video benchmark, competing against 126 models globally, Xiaomi-Robotics-U0 ranked first overall, leading in instruction following, interaction fluency, and multi-view consistency. In embodied transfer tests, it comprehensively surpassed closed-source GPT-Image-2.0.

3. WAIC 2026 Opens Tomorrow: Huawei, Nubia Lead Chinese AI Showcase

The 2026 World Artificial Intelligence Conference (WAIC 2026) officially opens in Shanghai on July 17. Multiple Chinese companies are set to unveil major AI products during the event.

Huawei's Shengteng AI and Atlas 950 Super Node

Huawei will showcase its latest Shengteng AI processors and the Atlas 950 Super Node at WAIC. The company previously noted at WAIC preview events that China's AI industry continues to expand, with humanoid robot annual production expected to exceed 100,000 units. Huawei's full-stack AI capabilities, from chips to platforms and training to inference, will be on display.

Nubia's World-First AI Agent Smartphone

Nubia announced it has completed AI agent model filing with Chinese regulators and will unveil the world's first AI agent smartphone at WAIC 2026. The device is described as an AI-native terminal that "understands, works, remembers, and stays secure," marking the transition of on-device AI models from concept to commercial production.

China Normalizes On-Device AI Model Filing

China's Cyberspace Administration recently approved seven mobile-side generative AI services, including Apple Intelligence, Huawei's Xiaoyi AI, Samsung Galaxy AI, Xiaomi, OPPO, Vivo, and Nubia — the first time China has conducted special approval for smartphone on-device AI models. Huawei's Xiaoyi AI, powered by HarmonyOS 7, introduces an Agentic self-evolving architecture, opening 2,100 system capabilities as a tool library and building a memory system covering over 200 types of user data.

Industry analysts note that on-device AI deployment is a key trend, offering advantages in reduced data transmission risk, faster response times, and better user privacy protection.
