⚡ WAIC 2026 Day 2: Tencent Unveils Full-Stack Embodied AI, 300 Robots Deployed, Industrialization Era Begins

I. Tencent Launches Hy-Embodied Three-Model Suite, Completing Full-Stack Robot AI Pipeline

On July 18, the second day of the 2026 World Artificial Intelligence Conference (WAIC) in Shanghai, Tencent delivered the day's biggest announcement — a comprehensive full-stack embodied AI solution spanning cloud infrastructure, model layers, platform layers, and application layers. The company unveiled three dedicated embodied AI models under the Hy-Embodied series, forming a tiered and collaborative robot capability matrix.

Hy-Embodied-RxBrain-1.0 serves as the robot's cognitive decision-making brain, pioneering collaborative computation between text reasoning and visual imagination to build continuous closed-loop cognition. In the RxBrain-Bench joint embodied planning test, it achieved a composite score of 0.68, significantly outperforming comparable models. Hy-Embodied-VLM-1.0 is a lightweight visual perception model requiring only one-tenth the computational resources of its predecessor while matching performance — achieving near-A32B flagship levels at A3B scale across 37 evaluation tasks. Hy-Embodied-VLA-0.5 unifies vision, language, and action modeling in a single architecture, trained on over 10,000 hours of high-precision data, capable of adapting to robotic arms, wheeled platforms, and humanoid robots alike.

Tencent also introduced the TairosAgent embodied AI agent framework and Apexio native agent, integrating perception, decision-making, and motion control into a unified system. The Tairos platform has been upgraded with full open-source capabilities for models, agents, and development tools. On the infrastructure side, Tencent Cloud deployed a multi-thousand-GPU cluster and launched the industry's first EaaS (Embodied AI as a Service) offering.

"True intelligence requires connecting language, vision, spatial cognition, physical control, and environmental feedback in a perception-body-action loop," said Zhang Zhengyou, Tencent's Chief Scientist and head of Robotics X Lab.

II. WorkBuddy Goes Multi-Platform, Enterprise AI Suite Expands

Tencent WorkBuddy launched as a standalone client across iOS, Android, and HarmonyOS — the first general-purpose efficiency agent app to cover Huawei's homegrown operating system. After integrating the Hy3 model, WorkBuddy achieved a 93.7% task completion rate in document editing, file management, and task collaboration.

Two other notable product debuts: Marvis, an OS-level personal AI assistant with 6 built-in agents (including PM, system Ops, app operator, web agent, asset manager, and search expert), demonstrated the ability to remotely clean systems, pre-update games, and adjust mouse settings from a single voice command. Toast (吐司), a natural-language app generation platform, has already created nearly 50,000 applications since its May launch.

For enterprise clients, Tencent Cloud launched ADP 4.0 International, supporting LINE, Telegram, and other overseas social channels with automated multi-language adaptation. The "AI Navigator+" intelligent agent, built on WorkBuddy, integrates business and legal corpora from seven Southeast Asian and Middle Eastern countries, featuring 22 "country expert" models for cross-border trade decision support.

III. 300 Robots Clock In: From Dancing Novelties to Factory Workers

The defining theme of WAIC 2026 is unmistakable: embodied AI has crossed the chasm from proof-of-concept to industrial deployment. Exhibitors in the embodied AI sector surged from 80 last year to over 200, with 208 embodied intelligence terminals and more than 300 physical robots on display. The key difference? Nobody is showing off backflips or parkour anymore. The benchmark is simple: can a robot work an 8-hour shift on a real production line?

60 AGIBOT Robots Deployed Across Four Venues

AGIBOT (智元机器人), in partnership with Qingtianzu, deployed 60 robots across WAIC's four venues, providing traffic management, exhibition navigation, and concierge services. Humanoid traffic control robots stationed at major intersections near the Expo Center can identify traffic lights, manage vehicle flow, detect e-bike violations, and issue voice warnings — marking the first large-scale "deployment-state" public service by embodied AI in China.

Ant Group's Robot Pharmacy Named Top WAIC Exhibit

Ant Group's LingBot (蚂蚁灵波) partnered with Guoda Pharmacy to create a "robot smart pharmacy" that was named one of WAIC's top 10 exhibits. Three different robot models from different manufacturers — all driven by the same LingBot-VLA 2.0 foundation model — autonomously coordinate to receive orders, pick medications, and package them, completing the entire process in just 90 seconds. The model, pre-trained on 60,000 hours of high-quality real-world data, has already been adapted to 20+ robot configurations from 17 manufacturers and is operating in Guoda's Shanghai locations without any store remodeling.

"Train Today, Work Tomorrow" — Robots Enter Factories at Scale

YOUIBot (优艾智合) built a miniature production line on the exhibition floor, where five "Xifeng" humanoid robots and one mobile搬运 robot performed material picking and delivery tasks at a line-side warehouse. Its tiered training framework supports 24-hour continuous data collection, achieving "train today, skilled worker tomorrow." Geek+ (极智嘉) launched the Gravity unified embodied AI framework with a dual-brain architecture — a "cognitive brain" for complex instruction parsing and an "action brain" for physics-based sandbox simulation before execution. NetEase Lingdong demonstrated its "dark factory" solution, where robots operate entirely without external lighting, achieving 120% of skilled human worker efficiency while reducing single-station labor requirements by 70%.

Zhishang Technology (智身科技) presented its full product matrix for the first time, with cumulative production surpassing 15,000 units, including the explosion-proof quadruped "Qiuqiu SP1" and new humanoid robot "Yinyi NE01." Pudu Robotics showed the PUDU D7 industrial semi-humanoid robot, supporting 14kg payload and 2-meter operating height.

IV. Economic Data Confirms: AI Is Becoming Productivity

Data released by China's National Development and Reform Commission at WAIC shows China's AI-related industry exceeded 1 trillion yuan (~$147 billion) in 2025, with 2026 growth projected above 30%. AI penetration across key industrial sectors has crossed 80%. Tax bureau data shows that industrial enterprises' spending on embodied AI robots in January–May 2026 was 2.3 times the full-year 2025 total.

Shanghai's AI industry is entering a concentrated value-realization phase. The number of billion-dollar market cap companies in the city grew from 16 to 26, with tech companies' market cap share surging from 21.8% to 51.7%. The Ministry of Industry and Information Technology has launched a national humanoid robot and embodied AI real-scenario training initiative, while Shanghai has set a target of deploying 100,000 humanoid robots in factories by the end of the 14th Five-Year Plan period.

"The value of embodied AI ultimately gets realized at the customer site," said Chen Xi, CEO of GeekPlus, in an interview. GeekPlus defines warehouse picking as the "training bullseye for physical AI" — combining high frequency, real-world feedback, clear metrics, transferability, and near-commercial closure. "In warehouses with millions of SKUs, we can train the brain and the grasping capability, and that capability seamlessly transfers to commercial and everyday scenarios."

WAIC 2026 runs through July 20. With the unmistakable shift from digital intelligence to physical intelligence, industry observers have already dubbed 2026 "Year Zero of Embodied AI Industrialization."
