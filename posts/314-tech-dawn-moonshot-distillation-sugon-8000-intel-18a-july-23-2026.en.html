1. White House OSTP Director Formally Accuses Moonshot AI of Stealing Anthropic's Fable Model

On July 22, White House Office of Science and Technology Policy (OSTP) Director Michael Kratsios took to X to formally accuse Chinese AI startup Moonshot AI of using large-scale distillation to copy Anthropic's most advanced Fable model to build its Kimi K3 system — marking the first time a senior US official has directly named and accused a specific Chinese company of stealing a specific American AI model.

"We have information that Moonshot AI distilled Anthropic's Fable for the development of its K3 model," Kratsios wrote. "Large-scale, covert industrial distillation intended to steal US proprietary technology and undermine American research is unacceptable."

The Smoking Gun: K3 Calls Itself Claude

Kimi K3 launched on July 16 as a 2.8-trillion-parameter model that quickly became the largest free AI model ever released. It ranks third on the Artificial Analysis Intelligence Index with a score of 57, trailing only Claude Fable 5 (60) and GPT-5.6 Sol (59). K3 scored a clean win over Fable 5 in script generation (Elo 2,840 vs 2,760) and tops the frontend code generation leaderboard.

But researchers discovered a bizarre anomaly: K3 was caught identifying itself as "Claude" — Anthropic's AI assistant name — in at least one conversation. Ryan Greenblatt, Chief Scientist at Redwood Research, ran a statistical analysis showing K3 claims to be Claude far more often than random chance would explain. The finding is widely viewed as the strongest circumstantial evidence of distillation — residual traces of the source model's self-identity embedded in the distilled model's behavioral patterns.

Kratsios further stated that Moonshot built "a sophisticated internal platform to conduct large-scale distillation against US models," allowing the company to "quickly switch between multiple methods of access to avoid detection."

Chip Export Violations: GB300 Access Through Thailand

Beyond model theft, Kratsios alleged that Moonshot AI acquired servers equipped with Nvidia's advanced GB300 chips and accessed them in Thailand — a workaround of US export controls. This allegation is potentially more consequential than the distillation claim: export control violations are specific offenses with real penalties that can hit suppliers and intermediaries, whereas model copying sits in legally murkier territory.

The allegation also answers a question that puzzled the industry since K3's launch: how did Moonshot assemble the enormous computing power needed to train a 2.8-trillion-parameter model under US chip restrictions? K3 requires 288GB of HBM memory per GPU and node configurations of 64+ accelerators — computing power far beyond what's readily available in China under current export controls.

Treasury Secretary Scott Bessent told Fox Business on July 21 that US authorities were "finding watermarks of our US large language models on many of the Chinese models," and warned of sanctions: "If we see overseas models stealing from our great companies, we have the ability to sanction them." Bessent indicated decisions would come "in the coming days or weeks."

Kimi K3 Open-Weight Release in Doubt

Moonshot had committed to releasing Kimi K3's open weights on July 27. The White House accusation now casts legal and regulatory uncertainty over the plan. Meanwhile, DeepSeek's stable V4 arrives July 24, making the final week of July the most consequential stretch for open-weight AI model releases ever. The industry is watching closely for Moonshot's formal response.

2. Sugon 8000: China's First All-Domestic 100,000-Card AI Supercluster Officially Completed

At the Guanghe Organization 2026 Intelligent Computing Application Conference in Zhengzhou, Henan, Sugon Information Industry officially announced the completion of Sugon 8000 (Dengfeng) — China's first all-domestic 100,000-card AI supercluster. This milestone marks the official transition of China's AI infrastructure from the 10,000-card era to the 100,000-card era of large-scale deployment.

Full-Stack Self-Reliance: From Silicon to Systems

Sugon 8000 adopts a "supercomputing-AI fusion" technical路线, abandoning the traditional separation of scientific and AI computing to achieve native integrated computing — efficiently supporting everything from FP64 high-precision scientific computation to INT8 low-precision AI training.

Key technical highlights include:

  • scaleFabric high-speed network: Self-developed InfiniBand-native RDMA network enabling highly reliable connectivity for 100,000-card clusters, solving the communication bottleneck in大规模 parallel computing
  • ParaStor distributed storage: Won first place in both production full-node and 10-node categories at the 2026 Global IO500榜单, reaching world-class performance levels
  • Immersion phase-change liquid cooling: World-leading thermal management supporting MW-level power density per cabinet — critical for 100,000-card cluster heat dissipation
  • Application optimization: Over 300 supercomputing-AI融合 applications optimized on the 100,000-card core nodes, spanning large model training, autonomous driving, drug discovery, materials science, weather modeling, and 20+ other critical domains, with 70+ achieving 10,000-card scalability

Sugon 8000 has been connected to the National Supercomputing Internet, providing powerful domestic computing support for China's AI research and industry. Deputy Director of the National High-Performance Computer Engineering Technology Research Center, Cao Zhennan, noted that the 100,000-card cluster will create a transformative leap for the supercomputing internet — connecting not just machines and centers, but people, applications, and ecosystems.

Industry observers view Sugon 8000 as China's most powerful systemic response to chip export controls — proving that the country can not only design high-performance AI chips but also efficiently organize them into world-class大规模 computing systems. The system will directly serve cutting-edge domains including weather modeling, quantum computing, new materials, and autonomous driving.

3. Intel Stock Surges 8.4% on Triple Catalyst: Xeon Memory Boost, Google Cloud AI Deal, 18A Breakthrough

Intel shares surged 8.4% to $105.45 on Tuesday (July 21), marking the chipmaker's biggest one-day gain in months. The rally was driven by three concrete catalysts, with Intel's Q2 2026 earnings report due after market close on Thursday July 23.

Catalyst 1: Xeon 6700P Memory Speed Upgrade

Intel announced that select Xeon 6700P processors will support 1DPC 8000 MT/s RDIMM memory — up from the current 6400 MT/s standard — delivering 20% more memory bandwidth, 25% faster speeds, and ~6% latency reduction. Production begins August-September 2026. For AI data center customers relying on Xeon CPUs to orchestrate data flow to and from GPU accelerators, this directly improves total throughput.

Catalyst 2: Expanded Google Cloud AI Partnership

Intel and Google Cloud deepened their AI collaboration, with plans to deploy Gemini Enterprise across Intel's engineering, supply chain, and corporate operations. The relationship is bidirectional — Intel is both a Google Cloud AI tools customer and a foundry services provider to Google Cloud. This strategic alignment gives Intel's AI narrative concrete operational validation.

Catalyst 3: 18A Yields Hit 85%, ASML Confirms High-NA EUV First

The most significant long-term catalyst: Intel disclosed its 18A process node yields have reached approximately 85%, up sharply from 65% last quarter and approaching TSMC's N2 benchmark of ~90%. Even more critically, ASML confirmed on its July 15 earnings call that Intel is the first customer to ship volume logic chips using High-NA EUV (0.55 NA) lithography — select Panther Lake layers are already dual-qualified on the next-generation scanners.

Additionally, Intel's 18A secured a manufacturing commitment from a major cloud service provider — the first confirmed external commercial order for Intel Foundry Services at the 18A node. Combined, these catalysts sent Intel stock soaring 8.4% as the market positions for Thursday's Q2 earnings (consensus: $14.4B revenue, $0.22 non-GAAP EPS).
