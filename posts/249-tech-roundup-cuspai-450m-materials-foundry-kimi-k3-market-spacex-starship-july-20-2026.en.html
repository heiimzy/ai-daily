⚡ July 20 Tech Roundup: CuspAI Raises $450M Launches AI Materials Foundry with NVIDIA/Meta/Samsung, Kimi K3 Shockwave Wipes $314B from US AI Valuations, SpaceX Starship Delay Drags SPCX Below $125

1. CuspAI Raises $450M, Launches AI Materials Foundry with NVIDIA, Meta, Samsung and 45+ Partners

British AI-driven materials startup CuspAI announced on July 20 that it has closed a ~$450 million Series B round led by Kleiner Perkins and NEA, with participation from Jeff Bezos's investment firm Bezos Expeditions. The raise brings CuspAI's total funding to over $650 million, positioning it among the best-funded startups in the AI-for-science space.

On the same day, CuspAI launched the AI Materials Foundry — a global coalition of more than 45 technology companies, industrial firms, and research institutions. The founding members include:

  • NVIDIA — providing compute infrastructure
  • Meta — contributing its frontier atomistic chemistry model, the Universal Model for Atoms (UMA)
  • Samsung and Hyundai Motor Group — both existing CuspAI customers
  • Henkel, Applied Materials, Tokyo Electron, Lam Research, and other global industrial leaders

Technology core: CuspAI's MIRA platform integrates generative materials design, simulation, synthesis route planning, and coordinated experimental validation into a complete discovery cycle. The company's inverse-design "search engine for materials" lets customers specify required properties; the AI then proposes candidate structures optimized for real-world manufacturability — not just simulation plausibility. The company claims its approach achieves synthesizable candidates up to ten times faster than traditional lab-based methods.

Nobel laureate Geoffrey Hinton and Turing Award winner Yann LeCun serve as advisors. NVIDIA CEO Jensen Huang has publicly named CuspAI as a company he is watching closely.

"CuspAI converts its customer list into shared infrastructure — pooled compute, pooled datasets, and a common software layer. For a startup whose edge rests on proprietary data and synthesis-aware models, turning customers into co-investors in that platform is a competitive move as much as a technical one." — Bloomberg

The competitive landscape is intensifying: Periodic Labs (founded by ex-OpenAI and DeepMind staff) raised a $200M seed at a $1B valuation, Flagship Pioneering's Lila Sciences secured $200M, and rivals from Orbital Materials to XtalPi are chasing the same market. Microsoft and Meta have released their own materials models. CuspAI's focus on semiconductors — covering logic, memory, interconnects, packaging, power delivery, and thermal management — positions it at the intersection of AI and the physical world's most critical industry.

2. Kimi K3 Shockwave Reshapes Global AI Valuations: Bloomberg Analyzes Winners and Losers

Moonshot AI's Kimi K3, released on July 16, continues to send shockwaves through global capital markets. Bloomberg reports that the 2.8-trillion-parameter open-weight model, which matches or surpasses leading US models on key coding benchmarks, has forced investors to reassess core assumptions underpinning the global tech rally. According to IG market analyst Tony Sycamore, two unlisted US AI companies have seen their combined expected valuations slashed by $314 billion.

Bloomberg's detailed winner/loser analysis:

Winners:

  • China's domestic semiconductor ecosystem: SMIC rose 5.4% in Shanghai as markets priced in increased infrastructure spending from heightened AI model competition. Gary Tan of Springbouy Investment Management noted: "With strong government support, China's push for open-source AI may accelerate domestic deployment of cutting-edge models, requiring more computing resources and driving demand for underlying hardware, especially networking equipment and memory chips."
  • AI agents and software developers: Saxo Markets' Charu Chanana said a powerful, cheaper open-source model lowers application development costs and could accelerate AI adoption across coding, customer service, and industrial workflows.
  • Memory chip makers: K3's 2.8 trillion parameters and 1M-token context window demand far more memory capacity than prior generations. Sumitomo Mitsui DS Asset Management noted Samsung and SK Hynix remain in the strongest position among semiconductor stocks due to limited supplier count.

Losers:

  • AI model developers: Z.AI (Zhipu AI), previously regarded as China's best open-source model developer, saw its Hong Kong-listed shares plunge nearly 40% in two trading sessions. Alibaba managed to hold ground after releasing the Qwen3.8 Max preview, but competitive pressure continues to mount.
  • High-end chip makers: NVIDIA and AMD face challenges to their scarcity premium. Mark Malek of Siebert Financial warned: "Every time any laboratory in the world releases a frontier-level model for free, regardless of which country it's from, the investment thesis that AI capex will ultimately pay off takes another hit."

Moonshot AI is simultaneously accelerating plans for a Hong Kong IPO that could value the company at over $30 billion. Annualized recurring revenue hit $300 million in June — tripling from $100 million in March — with API business now comprising over 70% of total revenue. Goldman Sachs and China International Capital Corp. are in discussions to play roles in the offering.

3. SpaceX Starship Flight 13 Delayed Again as Engine Ignition Fails, SPCX Drops Below $125

SpaceX's 13th Starship test flight was scrubbed after multiple Raptor 3 engines failed to ignite during the countdown, triggering an automatic launch abort. The company has rescheduled for Thursday, July 23, though Elon Musk later suggested the attempt could slip to Friday.

Market impact: SpaceX stock (SPCX) traded near $124 on Monday, falling more than 5% in a single day. The stock now sits roughly 23% below its $135 IPO price and approximately 38% below its all-time high. Nearly $100 billion in market value was erased in the aftermath. Evercore ISI still rates the stock Outperform with a $230 target, arguing the decline reflects profit-taking rather than deteriorating fundamentals.

Flight 13 was originally intended to carry 20 V3 Starlink satellites — the first deployment of the upgraded satellite version. The company said it has modified Starship's propulsion system to address the ignition issue. Each delay adds to the estimated $15 billion Starship development program's cost while intensifying competition from Blue Origin and pressure to meet NASA's 2028 lunar landing deadline.

Despite the setback, SpaceX's operational performance remains robust: 165 successful Falcon 9 launches in 2025, averaging one orbital mission every 2.2 days with a perfect mission success record. The company has also begun monetizing idle AI computing capacity through GPU leasing agreements, creating a new revenue stream alongside Starlink and launch services. Analysts expect the upcoming first quarterly earnings report to be a key catalyst for SPCX.

4. WAIC 2026 Closing Day: ACE Robotics Debuts Kairos 3.1 Embodied World Model, KEENON Showcases Humanoid Robots

The 2026 World Artificial Intelligence Conference (WAIC) concluded in Shanghai with multiple significant announcements marking the industry's transition from demonstration to commercial deployment.

ACE Robotics launches Kairos 3.1: The company unveiled its action-oriented world model integrating generative, physical, and cognitive intelligence through a unified architecture. The system creates a full-cycle "understand-reason-execute-reflect" self-evolving intelligence loop. The accompanying Ambient Capture Engine 2.0 achieves sub-millisecond sensor synchronization across 20+ heterogeneous sensors. ACE also debuted three commercial solutions — "XiaoMan" for instant retail, "XiaoXin" for hospitality, and "XiaoTu" for outdoor autonomous operations — with plans to deploy across 1,000 retail locations within the next year and 10,000 within two years.

KEENON Robotics showcases humanoid robots: The global leader in commercial service robot shipments demonstrated its "general-purpose humanoid + specialized service robot" complementary strategy at WAIC. The XMAN-R1 humanoid handles operational and interactive tasks while the DINERBOT T9 manages delivery workflows. In a hotel laundry scenario, humanoids load washing machines, retrieve clean laundry, and fold garments, while the T9 handles transportation. KEENON has deployed over 100,000 service robots across 70+ countries and regions.

The closing of WAIC 2026 — featuring over 1,100 exhibitors — solidifies 2026 as the "mass production元年" (year one of mass production) for embodied intelligence, with humanoid robots emerging as the most prominent theme of the conference.
