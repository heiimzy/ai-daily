⚡ July 17 Financial Roundup: Wall Street Slides on Iran Escalation, Retail Sales Surge Beats Estimates, SK Hynix ADR Plunges 8%, SpaceX Dips Below IPO as Short Sellers Net $8.7B

Wall Street Closes Lower as US-Iran Conflict Escalates

U.S. stocks ended Thursday in the red as the White House confirmed a significant expansion of airstrikes against Iranian military installations beyond the Persian Gulf, marking the first time strikes reached Semnan province near Tehran. The escalation rattled global equity markets, driving capital into defensive pockets and punishing growth-oriented sectors.

The tech-heavy NASDAQ Composite bore the brunt, cratering 387.28 points (-1.47%) to close at 25,881.95 — its worst single-day drop in nearly a month. The S&P 500 fell 38.63 points (-0.51%) to 7,533.77, while the Dow Jones Industrial Average held up relatively better, shedding 105.67 points (-0.20%) to 52,552.97.

Semiconductor stocks continued their brutal selloff. The PHLX Semiconductor Index (SOX) plunged over 4%, with memory chip makers taking the heaviest hits. Micron Technology fell for a second straight day, now down nearly 12% in two sessions. SanDisk dropped 10.8%, while SK Hynix's ADR crashed 7.94%. Energy and defense stocks rallied on safe-haven flows and rising oil prices as Brent crude recovered above $73/barrel on renewed concerns over Strait of Hormuz supply disruptions.

US Economic Data Broadly Beats Expectations

Multiple U.S. economic data releases on Thursday delivered strong upside surprises, underscoring the resilience of the world's largest economy:

  • June retail sales surged 0.6% month-over-month, far exceeding the 0.1% consensus estimate and rebounding sharply from May's -0.9% decline. Excluding autos, sales climbed 0.5%, while the control group — a key GDP input — also rose 0.5% versus 0.3% expected. Year-over-year, retail sales increased 3.5%. Gains were broad-based, led by e-commerce, motor vehicles, building materials, clothing, and home furnishings.
  • Initial jobless claims fell to 221,000, beating the 235,000 estimate and down from the prior week's revised 228,000. Continuing claims came in at 1.956 million, slightly below expectations. The data underscores a stable labor market with low layoff rates and no immediate cause for concern among policymakers.
  • The Philadelphia Fed Manufacturing Index surged to 15.9 in July, far exceeding the -1.0 forecast and rebounding from -4.0 in June — its strongest reading since February. New orders jumped to 18.4 from 2.3, shipments rose to 23.7 from 8.3, and the employment index turned positive at 10.3 from -9.8.

The strong data further reduced expectations for near-term Fed rate cuts. The CME FedWatch tool now prices just a 2.6% chance of easing at the July 29-30 meeting, with September cut probability falling to ~55% from 70% last week.

SK Hynix ADR Posts Record Volatility Since Nasdaq Debut

SK Hynix's American depositary receipts tumbled 7.94% to $162.44 on Thursday, marking the steepest single-day decline since its Nasdaq debut on July 10. Since listing, the ADR has experienced extraordinary swings — gaining 13.1% on opening day, falling 9.32% the following Monday, surging 27% Tuesday, then declining for two consecutive sessions. The stock now sits about 16% below its post-IPO high of $193.92 but remains above the $149 IPO price.

Multiple factors are amplifying the volatility:

  • South Korea's financial regulators imposed sweeping new rules late Thursday, banning new single-stock leveraged ETF filings, raising the minimum margin threshold from 10 million won to 30 million won, and accepting only cash as collateral. The move triggered forced liquidations of leveraged positions, particularly in SK Hynix and Samsung-linked products.
  • Newly launched leveraged ETFs from Direxion, GraniteShares, and ProShares — offering 2x daily returns on SK Hynix ADR — have mechanically amplified intraday swings, creating a vicious "sell-to-rebalance" cycle.
  • Seoul-listed SK Hynix shares plunged another 11%+ to 1,847,000 won on Friday morning, adding to pressure on the ADR.

Despite the turmoil, analysts note SK Hynix's fundamental outlook remains strong. As a dominant HBM (high-bandwidth memory) supplier for AI accelerators, the company is expected to report DRAM ASP growth of ~30% QoQ and NAND price growth of ~50% in its upcoming July 23 earnings, though the short-term trading structure has entered "expectations overdrawn" territory.

SpaceX Briefly Dips Below IPO Price, Short Sellers Net $8.7B

SpaceX shares briefly fell below their $135 initial public offering price on Thursday, touching $134 before recovering to close at $136.28. The stock has now fallen nearly 40% from its post-IPO high of $225.64, marking a dramatic reversal for the most hyped IPO in American capital markets history.

According to Ortex Technologies, short sellers are sitting on an estimated $8.7 billion in paper profits since the IPO. Nearly 49% of SpaceX's free float is now out on loan, predominantly for short selling. "SpaceX has been a rollercoaster for the short sellers, and it has ended up firmly in their favor," said Ortex co-founder Peter Hillerberg. "Rather than take profits, the bears kept adding the whole way down."

The selloff reflects growing investor concern over SpaceX's debt-funded AI and space infrastructure spending. With massive capital commitments to Starlink, Starship, and AI data centers, the company's ~$1.8 trillion valuation is facing increasing scrutiny. The 13th Starship test flight conducted Thursday failed to generate meaningful positive catalyst, underscoring the shift in market sentiment.

Fed Officials Strike Cautious Tone in Concert

Multiple Federal Reserve officials delivered coordinated hawkish messages on Thursday:

  • Governor Adriana Kugler signaled support for holding rates steady, citing low unemployment and rising tariff-driven price pressures. She noted June PCE at 2.5% and core PCE at 2.8%, both above May levels, and emphasized the need for a restrictive stance to anchor inflation expectations.
  • San Francisco Fed President Mary Daly said the Fed still has "some work to do" on inflation and supports two rate cuts this year but stressed they should not be preemptive, with the eventual policy rate likely settling above 3%.
  • Atlanta Fed President Raphael Bostic described the economic outlook as "highly uncertain" due to tariff impacts that could take months to materialize, explicitly pushing back against July rate cut expectations.

President Trump took to Truth Social after the strong economic data release to pressure the Fed: "Too Late: Great numbers just out. LOWER THE RATE!!! DJT." However, markets broadly expect the Fed to maintain its independence and hold rates steady at the July 29-30 FOMC meeting.

Citi Warns: Market Risk Indicators at Highest Since 2008

Citigroup's latest bear market预警 (warning) indicators show global markets triggering 10 of 18 warning signals, with the U.S. market triggering 11.5 — the highest reading since the 2008 Global Financial Crisis. The composite indicator evaluates stock valuations, market sentiment, credit spreads, yield curves, capital flows, corporate fundamentals, and financing activity.

The Citi strategist team led by Beata Manthey noted that while elevated, the current warning count remains below the 17.5 signals triggered before the 2000 dot-com bubble and the 13 before 2008. The team believes markets have not yet entered "euphoria" territory and retain upside potential in the near term, but caution that upcoming mega-cap tech earnings, the July FOMC meeting, and US-Iran developments could introduce significant volatility.

In parallel, Goldman Sachs reported that retail investors have become the "new market makers" of U.S. equities, with self-directed accounts holding approximately $12 trillion in stock assets and accounting for ~30% of daily trading volume. July has recorded not a single day of net selling from retail, with buy-the-dip conviction unshaken by either geopolitical shocks or tech stock pullbacks — though whether this resilience holds during a sustained bear market remains to be tested.

Asian Markets Open Lower Friday

Asian markets opened under pressure on Friday: Japan's Nikkei 225 extended Thursday's 2.79% plunge; South Korea's KOSPI continued its slide after Thursday's devastating 6.37% crash, with SK Hynix dropping another 11%+ and Samsung Electronics falling over 8% as leveraged ETF liquidations persist; China's A-shares saw the Shanghai Composite open lower after losing the 3,900 level on Thursday (-1.85%), while the Sci-Tech 50 (Kechuang 50) had crashed 4.02%. Investors are closely watching the follow-through effects of the strong U.S. economic data on global capital flows and central bank policy expectations.
