⚡ July 15 Tech Roundup: China's First Automated Stem Cell Platform, Perovskite Solar Hits 28.04% Record, World Bank Invests in Quadric AI Chip, xAI's Unpermitted Turbines Pollute Black Communities

🧬 China Debuts World's First Fully Automated Chemical Reprogramming Stem Cell Platform

On the morning of July 15, at the "2026 Autonomous Laboratory Conference" as part of the Zhongguancun Forum, a team led by Academician Deng Hongkui of Peking University unveiled a groundbreaking achievement — the world's first intelligent cell chemical reprogramming and clone screening platform.

The system achieves fully automated closed-loop operation from blood collection through material replenishment, chemical reprogramming, AI-powered screening, and quality-controlled stem cell output. The platform monitors experimental data in real time and feeds results back to researchers, representing a product of "basic research + AI + intelligent manufacturing" cross-innovation.

Between 2024 and 2026, Deng's team published a series of studies in Cell and other top journals on using chemically reprogrammed induced pluripotent stem cells to produce pancreatic islet cells for treating Type 1 diabetes. Results show transplanted patients have regained blood glucose self-regulation, with stable efficacy lasting nearly 3 years. Beyond diabetes, the technology is widely expected to be applicable to tumors, degenerative diseases, and other major conditions.

"Standardized, closed-loop operations improve experimental precision and efficiency. The autonomous lab not only reduces the cost of clinical applications but also facilitates broader adoption in medical institutions, research units, and pharmaceutical manufacturers." — Academician Deng Hongkui

☀️ Perovskite-Organic Tandem Solar Cell Breaks World Record at 28.04%

On the same day, Guangming Daily reported a major breakthrough from the Institute of Chemistry, Chinese Academy of Sciences. A team led by Academician Li Yongfang and Professor Meng Lei achieved a certified steady-state photoelectric conversion efficiency of 28.04% for perovskite-organic tandem solar cells, setting a new world record. The results were published in Nature on July 13.

The new solar cells are as thin as蝉翼 (cicada wings), with two layers that each "capture" different colors of sunlight. A newly introduced "photoconvertible additive molecule" is key to the transition from "light-fearing" to "light-harnessing." The certified device maintains 90% of initial efficiency after 625 hours of continuous illumination.

Unlike traditional silicon cells, the key advantage of perovskite-organic tandem cells lies in being lightweight and flexible:

  • Roll-to-roll printing: Thickness is only a fraction of traditional silicon cells; can be bent, folded, or cut
  • Wearable devices: Integrated into clothing, backpacks, tents — eliminating "charging anxiety"
  • Building-integrated photovoltaics: Applied to glass curtain walls, curved roofs, and curtains
  • Aerospace: Extremely high specific power ideal for space stations, satellites, and deep-space exploration

💻 World Bank's IFC Makes First-Ever AI Chip Investment: Leads Quadric's $46M Series C

On July 15, AI chip licensor Quadric announced the second close of its Series C funding round, bringing the total to $46 million and total capital raised to $90 million. The round was led by the International Finance Corporation (IFC), the private sector arm of the World Bank Group — marking the IFC's first-ever investment in an AI chip company.

Quadric's core technology is the Chimera General-Purpose Programmable Neural Processor Unit (GPNPU). Unlike conventional fixed-function NPUs, Chimera is fully programmable, allowing a single chip to adapt to newer, more complex AI models long after manufacturing. CEO Veerbhan Kheterpal calls it "a living platform" — "because the stack is software, the same chip runs new models and gets faster long after it ships."

Quadric has secured design wins with automotive giant Denso and is expanding into humanoid robotics, wearables, and advanced networking markets.

🏭 Reuters: xAI Installed 59 Unpermitted Gas Turbines Polluting Black Communities

A Reuters investigation published today reveals that Elon Musk's xAI has installed 59 natural gas turbines for its Colossus 2 data center project without securing federal Clean Air Act permits — more than double the 27 turbines xAI publicly acknowledged.

Just 30 of those turbines, operating at 80% capacity, could emit nearly 2,500 short tons of nitrogen oxide, 4,000 short tons of carbon monoxide, and 22 short tons of formaldehyde annually — far exceeding the 100-short-ton threshold that triggers federal permitting requirements.

Key findings:

  • Within 5 miles of the site, ~46% of Mississippi-side residents are Black (vs. 33% countywide)
  • On the Tennessee side, ~94% of residents are Black (vs. 52% countywide)
  • NAACP and Southern Environmental Law Center sued xAI in April to halt operations
  • DOJ argued on June 15 that restricting turbines could threaten national security
  • Mississippi regulators approved 41 permanent turbines after just one public hearing
"Once they got their foot in the door in Memphis, I feel like it's going to be a continuous movement of xAI into these other communities. It's all about the money, and it's not about the health or wellness of the people that live in or near these communities." — Sarah Gladney, 72-year-old resident

The outcome of this lawsuit will help define how the Clean Air Act applies to the rapidly growing AI compute sector, where companies are racing to build off-grid power plants across the country.
