⚡ July 18 Tech Roundup: WAIC 2026 Opens with 300+ Product Debuts, Huawei Atlas 950 SuperPoD Revealed, Honor Robot Phone Launches, TSMC Record Earnings Trigger Historic Chip Stock Crash

1. WAIC 2026 Opens in Shanghai at Record Scale

The 2026 World Artificial Intelligence Conference (WAIC) opened in Shanghai on July 17 at its largest scale ever, spanning over 100,000 square meters across four exhibition venues with more than 1,100 exhibitors and 3,000 exhibits, including over 300 global product debuts.

Chinese President Xi Jinping attended WAIC's opening ceremony for the first time in the conference's nine-year history. In his keynote, Xi emphasized that "AI development should not be a solo performance by a single country, but a symphony of international cooperation." He announced China's leadership in establishing the World Artificial Intelligence Cooperation Organization (WAICO), which 29 countries including Russia, Indonesia, Brazil, Pakistan, and Kazakhstan signed on July 16. The organization is headquartered in Shanghai, and UN Secretary-General António Guterres attended the signing ceremony.

Xi also pledged 5,000 AI training opportunities for developing countries over five years and the establishment of international AI application cooperation centers across six regional blocs. The moves are seen as China's bid to build a competing global AI governance framework to counter the US-led "Pax Silica" initiative.

Kazakh President Kassym-Jomart Tokayev, Cambodian Prime Minister Hun Manet, Thai Prime Minister Anutin Charnvirakul, and UN Secretary-General Guterres also addressed the conference.

2. Huawei Atlas 950 SuperPoD: China's Largest AI Supernode

The most technically significant product at WAIC 2026 is Huawei's Atlas 950 SuperPoD, publicly displayed for the first time. The system represents China's most mature demonstration that AI infrastructure can be built entirely without US-origin components.

The Atlas 950 SuperPoD connects 8,192 Huawei Ascend 950DT AI processors using Huawei's proprietary UnifiedBus 2.0 all-optical interconnect protocol. Unlike conventional NVLink+InfiniBand architectures, UnifiedBus 2.0 functions as a memory fabric — each Ascend chip contains a Unified Bus Memory Management Unit (UBMMU) that maps every other chip's memory into its local address space, enabling all 8,192 chips to share a single memory pool and operate as one logical machine.

Huawei self-reports 8 exaflops of FP8 compute and 16 PB/s of interconnect bandwidth, claiming 6.7x the compute of Nvidia's NVL144 — though neither figure has been independently verified. The Atlas 950 SuperCluster, expandable to over 500,000 Ascend NPUs, is slated for Q4 2026 delivery.

At the Zhangjiang chip exhibition area, more than ten competing domestic GPU architectures were showcased, including MetaX, Moore Threads, and Oriental Suanxin, which debuted the world's first software-defined near-memory computing 3D AI chip DF1000. Built on a domestic 14nm process with claimed 4nm-class performance, the DF1000 won the WAIC 2026 SAIL Award.

3. Consumer AI Products Flood WAIC: Honor Robot Phone, Nubia NaviX Ultra, iFlytek AI Glasses

The consumer AI exhibition at WAIC's West Bund venue featured over 100 companies and 200 products. The highlight was Honor's Robot Phone, officially launched in Shanghai on July 18 (today). The device features an ultra-compact 4-degree-of-freedom (4-DoF) titanium-alloy gimbal system with an embodied AI camera, capable of autonomously performing tasks like dimming the screen, canceling motion sickness mode, setting alarms, and booking taxis. At WAIC, Honor also hosted a forum titled "From Digital Screens to Embodied Intelligence" featuring Wired founder Kevin Kelly as a special guest.

Nubia unveiled the NaviX Ultra, billed as the "world's first mass-produced AI agent smartphone." It integrates a government-filed on-device large model with system-level GUI Agent architecture, using MCP and A2A protocols to interface with mainstream apps without simulated touch operations. All core AI computation runs locally on the device.

iFlytek demonstrated AI glasses weighing just 40 grams, featuring integrated multi-scenario translation with an industry-first lip-movement recognition noise cancellation system. Priced at 4,299 yuan ($595), the glasses can lock onto speakers in complex crowded environments and filter ambient noise.

Unitree Robotics unveiled its unmanned robot factory for the first time. Over 200 embodied intelligence exhibitors (up from 80 last year) showcased more than 300 physical robots on site. Industry consensus at WAIC is that 2026 marks the "first year of mass production" for humanoid robots, with Chinese companies shipping approximately 90% of global humanoid robot units in 2025.

4. Historic AI Chip Stock Selloff: TSMC's Record Quarter Triggers Market Panic

In stark contrast to WAIC's celebratory atmosphere, global AI chip stocks are experiencing a historic selloff. TSMC reported blockbuster Q2 earnings on Thursday: revenue of $40.2 billion (up 36% YoY), net profit of $22 billion (up 77.4% YoY), its fifth consecutive record profit quarter. Yet the stock plunged 7.29% in Taiwan on Friday.

The culprit: TSMC raised its 2026 capital expenditure guidance to $60-64 billion (from $52-56 billion) and announced an additional $100 billion investment in its Arizona fabs, bringing total US commitment to $265 billion — the largest single foreign direct investment in history. Wall Street interpreted the capex surge as evidence of AI overspending without proportionate returns.

"Investors are no longer willing to take rising AI infrastructure spending on faith. Good news is not enough to sustain confidence when the industry has spent nearly $1.6 trillion on AI development over the past decade." — The Next Web

The fallout was severe across Asia. Taiwan's TAIEX plunged 2,953.71 points (6.47%), the largest single-day point decline in history. Foreign investors dumped a record NT$189.04 billion ($5.86 billion) in shares. The Philadelphia Semiconductor Index has fallen approximately 19% from its June 22 all-time high, nearing bear market territory. The Bloomberg Asian Chip Stock Index tumbled more than 5%, now down about 19% from its June peak.

Major US chip stocks followed: Nvidia fell 2.2%, AMD dropped over 1%, Arm declined 5.4%, Micron fell 5.6%, and Marvell plunged 8.7%. The semiconductor sector now accounts for nearly 20% of S&P 500 weight, up from just 5% in 2022 — concentration levels exceeding the dot-com era.

Goldman Sachs prime brokerage data shows hedge fund net exposure to AI and chip stocks at its lowest level this year. Analysts attribute the selloff primarily to profit-taking and valuation correction rather than a fundamental reversal in AI demand. Concerns over Moonshot AI's Kimi K3 launch — which demonstrated China's narrowing AI gap — compounded fears about US AI dominance and chip pricing power.
