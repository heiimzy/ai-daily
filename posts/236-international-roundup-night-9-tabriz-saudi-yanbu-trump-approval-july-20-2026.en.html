⚡ July 20 International Roundup: US Bombs Iran for 9th Night Expanding to Tabriz; Iran Strikes Saudi Arabia for First Time in 4 Months; Trump's Iran War Approval Plummets to 29%

🔴 US Bombs Iran for 9th Consecutive Night — Strikes Expand to Northwestern Tabriz

The United States launched its ninth consecutive night of airstrikes on Iran early Monday, with the scope of attacks expanding significantly, Al Jazeera reported. Iran's IRIB state broadcaster reported explosions in the southwestern Khuzestan province port cities of Mahshahr and Imam Khomeini, and in areas near Sirik and Jask in Hormozgan province, as well as near Khvormuj in Bushehr province.

Most notably, three explosions were heard near the northwestern city of Tabriz — the first time this city has come under direct US attack since the conflict began. Air defenses were also activated in the southeastern cities of Konarak and Chabahar in Sistan and Baluchestan province. Analysts note that the expansion of strikes to Tabriz marks a significant geographic escalation, extending from the southern coast deep into Iran's northwestern interior.

US Central Command (CENTCOM) stated the latest wave of strikes, beginning at 7:00 p.m. ET Saturday (07:00 Beijing time Monday), was designed to "further degrade Iran's ability to threaten commercial shipping in the Strait of Hormuz."

🇸🇦 Iran Directly Strikes Saudi Arabia for First Time in 4 Months

Iran launched its first direct attacks on Saudi Arabia in four months on July 19-20. A ballistic missile targeted Prince Sultan Air Base in Al-Kharj — the primary US air operations hub in Saudi Arabia housing approximately 2,300 American military personnel. The Saudi Defense Ministry confirmed the missile was intercepted.

Simultaneously, a Shahed-series drone penetrated Saudi air defenses and struck the SAMREF refinery at Yanbu on the Red Sea coast, a joint venture between Saudi Aramco and ExxonMobil with a processing capacity of 400,000 barrels per day. Yanbu serves as Saudi Arabia's primary alternative export corridor bypassing the Strait of Hormuz — the East-West Pipeline has been routing up to 7 million barrels per day to the port since March.

The dual strikes — targeting both the kingdom's military infrastructure hosting US forces and its economic lifeline — represent a significant strategic escalation. Saudi authorities issued shelter-in-place alerts for both Al-Kharj and Yanbu residents, with damage at the refinery described as "under assessment."

🛩️ IRGC Claims to Down US MQ-9 Drones, Intercept Cruise Missile

Iran's Islamic Revolutionary Guard Corps (IRGC) claimed to have shot down a US MQ-9 Reaper reconnaissance drone in southern Iran while it was conducting surveillance over operational areas, according to the semi-official Tasnim news agency. The IRGC also claimed its aerospace forces downed another MQ-9 over Ahvaz in southwestern Iran and successfully intercepted a US cruise missile in western Iran.

IRGC Quds Force Commander Brigadier General Esmaeil Qa'ani vowed that the Resistance Front would continue its path under the leadership of Supreme Leader Mojtaba Khamenei, who called on Iranians to maintain "sacred unity" amid the escalating conflict.

📉 Poll: 69% of Americans Disapprove of Trump's Iran War Handling

A Washington Post/Ipsos poll reveals that only 29% of Americans approve of President Trump's handling of the Iran war, while 69% disapprove. Just 28% of US respondents believe the war is worth fighting, with 68% holding the opposite view. An Economist/YouGov poll shows nearly half of American respondents expect the war to last a year or more.

Trump's overall approval rating has fallen to 37%, with a 61% disapproval rate. The killing of three US service members in two days — bringing total US combat deaths to 17 with over 420 wounded — is rapidly eroding public confidence in the administration's war strategy. Senator Jeanne Shaheen (D-NH) wrote on social media: "The President must end this disastrous war through negotiations."

The Atlantic published an editorial titled "Trump Owes Americans a Better Explanation on Iran," stating that Washington's war objectives have become "blurred and chaotic."

🇯🇵 Japan Defense Minister Calls to Re-examine "Three Non-Nuclear Principles"

Beyond the Middle East crisis, dangerous developments are also emerging in the Asia-Pacific security landscape. Japanese Defense Minister Shinjiro Koizumi publicly called for a debate on nuclear weapons on July 17, arguing that Japan must "discuss various policy options with a sense of crisis." The Japanese government plans to include measures to strengthen America's "extended nuclear deterrence" in its year-end revision of the "Three Security Documents."

The "Three Non-Nuclear Principles" — not possessing, not producing, and not permitting the introduction of nuclear weapons — have been Japan's foundational nuclear policy since their adoption by the Diet in 1971. Since Prime Minister Sanae Takaichi took office in October 2025, at least 82 prefectural and municipal assemblies across 25 prefectures have submitted opinions urging the government to uphold the principles. The Nagasaki Prefectural Assembly emphasized that as the only nation to have suffered atomic bombings, Japan has a responsibility to convey the threat of nuclear weapons to the world.

🌍 Week Ahead

Brent crude has surpassed $91/barrel, and WTI has broken $85, hitting a one-month high. Key focuses include whether Trump will authorize even larger "devastating strikes" following the recent US casualties, whether Iran will launch further attacks on Saudi Arabia and Israel, and whether Gulf mediation efforts can restart negotiations.
