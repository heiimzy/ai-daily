⚡ Tencent Unveils Full-Stack Embodied AI, Launches WorkBuddy on HarmonyOS, Sees 68x Surge in Hy3 Model Usage

On Day 2 of the 2026 World Artificial Intelligence Conference (WAIC 2026) in Shanghai, Tencent delivered its most comprehensive AI lineup to date. From foundational embodied AI models to enterprise agent platforms, from personal productivity assistants to cross-border AI solutions, the tech giant filled key gaps across robotics, office productivity, and globalization — signaling a clear pivot from "Q&A interaction" to "task-based collaboration."

Notably, Tencent's newly restructured Hunyuan Hy3 model saw its total API call volume surge more than 68x in its first week compared to its predecessor Hy2, claiming the top spot on OpenRouter's global model leaderboard — a metric that underscores the accelerating adoption of Tencent's AI stack.

Full-Stack Embodied AI: Three Specialized Models + TairosAgent Framework

Embodied AI is widely regarded as the next strategic frontier after large language models. At WAIC, Tencent for the first time systematically showcased a four-layer embodied AI architecture spanning cloud infrastructure, model layer, platform layer, and application layer — completing the full chain from compute power to cognition to real-world deployment.

At the model layer, Tencent launched the Hy-Embodied series of three specialized models forming a tiered robotics capability matrix:

  • Hy-Embodied-RxBrain-1.0 — The robot's cognitive "brain," pioneering the deep synergy of text reasoning and visual imagination in a continuous cognition sequence for autonomous task planning and execution
  • Hy-Embodied-VLM-1.0 — A lightweight visual perception model achieving flagship-level performance at just 1/10 of the computing cost, dramatically reducing robot vision deployment expenses
  • Hy-Embodied-VLA-0.5 — A unified vision-language-action model trained on over 10,000 hours of high-precision data, adaptable across robotic arms, wheeled platforms, and humanoid robots

All three models have already been deployed in real industrial scenarios, covering guided tours, elderly care, and commercial navigation. With a hardware-software integrated fast-adaptation capability, the same model set supports multi-form robot mass deployment.

Complementing the models, Tencent released the TairosAgent embodied agent framework and Apexio native agent. TairosAgent integrates perception, decision-making, and motion control into a complete system, enabling robots to form a unified loop of continuous sensing, deciding, and acting. The Tairos (T-Luosi) platform, first launched in 2025, has been upgraded with open-source models, agents, and development tools.

"True intelligence requires connecting language, vision, spatial cognition, body control, and environmental feedback in a closed loop of perception, embodiment, and action." — Zhang Zhengyou, Chief Scientist, Tencent Robotics X Lab

WorkBuddy Standalone App: First Universal AI Agent on HarmonyOS

In the AI agent space, Tencent launched differentiated products for individuals and enterprises. The most notable release is WorkBuddy, which debuted as a standalone app simultaneously on iOS, Android, and HarmonyOS — making it the first universal productivity AI agent to support Huawei's Harmony ecosystem.

Since its launch in March 2026, WorkBuddy has undergone rapid iteration and is now one of China's most popular office AI tools. It autonomously decomposes complex tasks and coordinates multiple tools for content creation, data analysis, and project management — evolving from a simple Q&A bot into a full-fledged task collaboration platform.

For enterprises, Tencent Cloud launched the ADP 4.0 Overseas Edition, an enterprise-grade agent development platform, alongside a "Ten Industries, Hundred Scenarios" ecosystem initiative. The platform already covers 30+ industries with deployments in intelligent customer service, knowledge management, and content production. The new edition features comprehensive globalization upgrades: integration with LINE, Telegram, and other overseas social channels; multi-timezone and multi-language auto-adaptation; compatibility with multiple international LLMs; and full connectivity with Google Workspace and other SaaS platforms.

Tencent Lefeng also unveiled an enterprise knowledge integration upgrade supporting full-library import, incremental sync, and real-time retrieval — ensuring AI agents can read and understand enterprise-specific knowledge.

AI for Overseas Expansion: 22 "Country Specialists" Guide Globalization

In collaboration with the China Academy of Information and Communications Technology (CAICT), Tencent launched AI Navigator+, an intelligent agent built on WorkBuddy for cross-border businesses. It integrates policy, commercial, and cultural knowledge from seven countries including Thailand, Singapore, and the UAE, featuring 22 "country specialist" models that provide localized decision support across legal, tax, and market access domains.

The system operates through WorkBuddy's "Global Development Zone," which is now fully live. Enterprise users can interact with AI Navigator+ in natural language to receive compliance advice, market analysis, and business strategy — substantially reducing information barriers and decision costs for overseas expansion.

AI Across All Verticals: Marketing, Gaming, and Beyond

Tencent's AI push extends beyond enterprise tools. The company's marketing division showcased the AIGC platform "Tencent Miaosi" (Wonderful Ideas) and the AIM+ intelligent advertising suite, shifting creative production from one-off custom work to scalable generation, and moving ad placement from human-dependent operations to AI-driven real-time optimization.

In gaming, Huya (a Tencent-affiliated live streaming platform) released the VAM real-time multimodal digital human model, capable of generating a fully interactive virtual persona from a single static image — marking a shift from scripted digital humans to real-time interactive avatars for live streaming and events.

During the conference, Tencent Research Institute and Tencent Cloud Intelligence jointly published the "2026 Top Ten AI Trends" report, mapping key directions from model evolution to industrial restructuring.

Strategic Implications: A Pivotal Moment for Tencent AI

Tencent's WAIC 2026 showcase signals a fundamental shift in its AI strategy — moving beyond parameter counts and benchmark rankings to compete on "full-stack capability." The Hunyuan Hy3 model, with only 21B activated parameters, matches models several times its size in real-world blind tests, outperforming domestic competitors like GLM5.1. The 68x surge in Hy3 API calls in its first week, topping OpenRouter's global leaderboard, directly confirms the market's intense demand for high-performance, cost-effective AI models.

"AI applications are evolving from Q&A interaction to task-based collaboration — from individuals to teams to entire organizations," said Lin Songtao, Tencent Vice President. "We aim to build models and platforms that make AI a productivity tool everyone can use and every organization can leverage."

With embodied AI, enterprise agents, and personal AI assistants now moving from vision to reality, Tencent is rapidly translating its massive AI ecosystem into products that span the full spectrum from digital services to physical-world automation.
