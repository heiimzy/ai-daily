⚡ July 12 World Politics: Oman Summons Iranian Ambassador in Unprecedented Protest, Qatar Reports 3 Injured Including Child, CENTCOM Declares Southern Route Open for Free Navigation

The US-Iran standoff over the Strait of Hormuz entered its third day on Sunday with significant new diplomatic fallout, military operations, and humanitarian consequences. Oman issued a rare formal protest to Iran's ambassador after drone strikes hit its territory, Qatar confirmed three civilians including a child were wounded by falling debris from intercepted Iranian missiles, and the US Central Command (CENTCOM) completed its third round of strikes this week — hitting over 300 targets across three consecutive nights of operations.

Oman Summons Iranian Ambassador in Rare Diplomatic Rebuke

Oman took the extraordinary step of summoning Iranian Ambassador Mousa Fereidoun on Sunday to deliver a formal protest note over drone strikes targeting Omani territory. The Foreign Ministry expressed "profound dismay at these irresponsible acts," emphasizing the need to respect state sovereignty, good neighborliness, and non-interference in internal affairs.

Oman's state news agency reported that Iranian drones struck multiple sites in the Musandam and al-Batinah governorates. Musandam is Oman's strategic exclave jutting into the Strait of Hormuz, making it uniquely vulnerable to spillover from the conflict. The attack came just one day after Oman hosted Iranian Foreign Minister Abbas Araghchi for talks on maritime security in the very same waterway.

During Saturday's talks in Muscat, Oman had proposed a two-corridor framework: unrestricted navigation through the southern corridor in Omani territorial waters, and Iranian-managed passage through the northern corridor with prior approval but no tolls. The Iranian delegation returned to Tehran for consultations without committing to the proposal. Hours later, Iran's IRGC struck a vessel in the southern corridor and announced the closure of the strait.

Analysts note that Oman — historically the most trusted mediator between Washington and Tehran — issuing a public protest against Iran marks a significant deterioration in bilateral relations and complicates future mediation efforts.

Qatar: 3 Civilians Wounded, Mediation Role Under Threat

Qatar's Ministry of Interior confirmed that three people, including a child, were injured by falling shrapnel from intercepted Iranian ballistic missiles targeting the Al Udeid air base — home to US Central Command's forward headquarters. Qatar's Foreign Ministry issued a strongly worded condemnation, calling the attacks a "dangerous escalation" that "undermine political and diplomatic efforts aimed at achieving security and stability in the region."

The attack places Qatar's mediation role in serious jeopardy. Doha has previously stated it would not serve as a mediator while under attack. Qatar has been central to US-Iran ceasefire negotiations and hosted multiple rounds of talks, including the June talks that produced the now-collapsed Memorandum of Understanding. The Interior Ministry raised the security threat level to high and urged residents to remain in safe locations.

"The continuation of these aggressions represents a dangerous escalation that will complicate efforts aimed at containing tensions, undermine political and diplomatic endeavors aimed at achieving security and stability in the region, and holds the Islamic Republic of Iran fully legally responsible." — Qatar Foreign Ministry Statement

CENTCOM Completes Third Wave: 300+ Targets Struck

US Central Command confirmed it completed a third round of strikes against Iran on Saturday at 7:15 PM ET, hitting approximately 140 Iranian military targets. Combined with two previous rounds, more than 300 targets have been struck across three nights, including missile and drone launch sites, naval capabilities, ammunition storage facilities, communication networks, and coastal surveillance locations.

Defense Secretary Pete Hegseth posted on X: "Iran made a poor choice. Now they pay."

In a significant development, the Joint Maritime Information Center (JMIC) issued a formal advisory Sunday declaring that the southern route through the Strait of Hormuz remains open for commercial traffic. "The Strait of Hormuz is an international waterway and is not subject to coercion or control by any single nation," the advisory stated. US Naval Forces Central Command confirmed the southern route has been expanded for two-way traffic and continues to coordinate safe passage for commercial vessels. Since early May, US forces have facilitated the transit of more than 800 commercial vessels and 400 million barrels of crude oil through the waterway.

Iranian Cities Hit in Latest Strikes

Iranian state media reported US airstrikes across multiple southern cities and provinces. Officials from Bushehr province confirmed strikes on five cities including Asaluyeh, Bandar Deyr, Bushehr city, Dashti and Tangestan. Explosions were reported in Bandar Abbas, Sirik, Jask, and on Qeshm Island — home to an underground Iranian missile base. Iran's Health Ministry spokesperson Hossein Kermanpour said the death toll over four days of strikes has reached 19, with more than 100 wounded, including civilians.

Iran Strikes Back Across Five Gulf States

In retaliation, Iran's Islamic Revolutionary Guard Corps (IRGC) launched coordinated strikes against US military facilities across five Gulf states plus Jordan:

  • Qatar's Al Udeid Air Base: Ballistic missiles destroyed a fighter jet maintenance center and command-and-control facility
  • Oman's Port of Duqm: Logistics support centers and aircraft carrier refueling platforms targeted
  • Kuwait: Explosive drones struck Patriot air defense systems, ammunition depots and radar sites
  • Bahrain: Drone attacks targeted US communications systems and radar stations
  • Jordan's Prince Hassan Air Base: Command-and-control center and MQ-9 drone hangars destroyed

The UAE confirmed its defense systems were engaging Iranian missiles and drones, while air raid sirens sounded across Bahrain. Kuwait's military reported intercepting aerial targets in its airspace.

Iran's Parliament Speaker: 'Era of One-Sided Deals is OVER'

Iran's Parliament Speaker Mohammad Baqer Qalibaf posted on X Sunday: "The era of one-sided deals is OVER. We told you: keep your word or pay the price. Reality is knocking." He attached a screenshot of Article 5 of the June MoU, which stipulates that navigation through the Strait of Hormuz must follow "Iranian arrangements."

Iran's Foreign Ministry Spokesperson Esmail Baqaei confirmed that Iran has rejected US requests for direct negotiations. "Iran will only fulfill its commitments on a reciprocal basis and will continue to insist on 'commitments-for-commitments,'" he told state television.

Iraq PM to Visit Washington Monday

As the crisis deepens, Iraq's Prime Minister is scheduled to visit Washington on Monday for talks expected to focus on deepening bilateral ties and securing new oil and gas agreements. Iraq, OPEC's second-largest producer, has been severely impacted by the Strait of Hormuz closure, which has crippled its primary oil export route and slashed government revenues.

Meanwhile, Pakistan's Deputy Prime Minister and Foreign Minister Ishaq Dar spoke by phone with his Iranian counterpart Abbas Araghchi, urging "de-escalation and restraint as agreed in the Islamabad MoU in June 2026." Saudi Arabia also expressed grave concern over the escalation.

India: 10 Crew Rescued, 1 Missing

India's Ministry of External Affairs confirmed that 10 of the 11 Indian nationals aboard the stricken M/V GFS Galaxy — the Cyprus-flagged container ship attacked by Iran in the Strait of Hormuz — have been rescued. One Indian national remains missing. India condemned the attack on commercial shipping and called for immediate de-escalation and restoration of safe navigation through international waterways.

Outlook

The Strait of Hormuz crisis has reached its most dangerous point since the February 2026 outbreak of war. The core dispute over control of the waterway remains unresolved. Oman's mediation is undermined by the attack on its territory, Qatar's role is compromised, and Iran has hardened its position. With Trump declaring the ceasefire "over" but leaving the door open for talks, and Iran's new Supreme Leader Mojtaba Khamenei vowing revenge for his father's killing, the risk of further escalation remains exceptionally high in the days ahead.
