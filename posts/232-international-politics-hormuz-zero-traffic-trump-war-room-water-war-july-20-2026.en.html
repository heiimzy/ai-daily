⚡ July 20 International Politics Deep Dive: Strait of Hormuz Traffic Hits Zero, Third US Soldier Killed in Iraq, Trump Holds Emergency Situation Room Meeting

The US-Iran conflict entered a perilous new phase over the weekend of July 18-19, with the Strait of Hormuz grinding to a complete halt, a third American service member killed in a drone ordnance accident in Iraq, and President Trump convening an emergency Situation Room meeting to discuss "devastating strike" plans that may include ground forces. The conflict has also taken a dark new turn with water infrastructure becoming a direct target of military operations — a dangerous escalation that threatens the survival of entire communities.

Strait of Hormuz Traffic Falls to Zero: IRGC Warns Any Transit Will Be Struck

Iran's Islamic Revolutionary Guard Corps (IRGC) announced on Sunday that traffic through the Strait of Hormuz has dropped to zero, with no vessels currently transiting the strategic waterway. A source from the IRGC Navy told Iran's Tasnim news agency that any attempt to cross the strait will be met with Iranian force.

"As long as the US continues its hostile and provocative behavior, the Strait of Hormuz will remain closed, and Iran will not issue passage permits to any vessel," the source stated.

The IRGC previously declared the strait closed on July 12, but the "zero traffic" announcement marks the first time the blockade has been fully enforced. The waterway, which normally handles about one-fifth of global oil supply, has seen traffic plummet from roughly 55 vessels per day to single digits, and now to zero.

The IRGC warned that vessels attempting to transit via "unsafe routes" with US backing would "certainly face accidents." The Guard previously claimed it intercepted four vessels attempting unauthorized passage, with two oil tankers reportedly striking naval mines.

Third US Service Member Killed in Iraq: Death Toll Reaches 17

US Central Command (CENTCOM) confirmed on Sunday that a US service member was killed in northern Iraq during a controlled detonation operation of unexploded ordnance from a downed Iranian one-way attack drone. A second service member was wounded in the incident.

This brings the total number of US service members killed since the US-Israel campaign against Iran began on February 28 to 17, with more than 420 wounded. The announcement follows the deaths of two US service members in an Iranian ballistic missile and drone attack on Jordan's Muwaffaq Salti Airbase on Friday, with a third still listed as missing in action.

CENTCOM stated that unidentified remains have been recovered during the search for the missing soldier and are undergoing DNA identification.

Trump's Emergency Situation Room Meeting: 'Devastating Strike' Plans Under Discussion

As the eighth consecutive night of US airstrikes on Iran concluded, President Trump convened an emergency meeting in the White House Situation Room on Saturday. According to multiple media reports, attendees included Vice President Vance, Secretary of State Rubio, Secretary of Defense Hegseth, Chairman of the Joint Chiefs of Staff General Caine, CIA Director Ratcliffe, and Special Envoy Witkoff.

The high-level meeting itself signals the gravity of the situation. The core agenda was described as a "new plan for devastating strikes on Iranian strategic targets." Trump warned beforehand that "next week it will be power plants, bridges" unless Iran returns to negotiations, and for the first time openly stated he does not rule out deploying ground forces to Iran.

Iran dismissed the threats. Deputy Foreign Minister Kazem Gharibabadi stated: "Iran has never left the negotiating table. It was the US that tore up the Islamabad Memorandum of Understanding. Iran will never be the first to bow and request negotiations with America." Major-General Ali Abdullahi, commander of Iran's Khatam al-Anbiya Central Headquarters, warned that "any greed, bullying, and barbaric act will be met with a decisive and devastating response from Iran's armed forces."

Water Infrastructure War: US Destroys Iran Desalination Plant, Iran Retaliates on Kuwait

The weekend marked a dangerous turning point in the conflict as water infrastructure became a direct target for the first time.

CENTCOM confirmed that US warplanes struck multiple targets in Iran's Hormozgan province during Saturday night's airstrikes. Iran's official IRNA news agency reported that the Bonji desalination plant on Qeshm Island — a strategic location at the mouth of the Strait of Hormuz — was completely destroyed, cutting off drinking water to at least 10,000 residents. A key bridge connecting Bandar Abbas port city was also severely damaged.

In retaliation, Iran's IRGC targeted water infrastructure in Kuwait for the second consecutive day. Kuwait Petroleum Corporation confirmed that a major oil facility and a critical desalination plant were hit by Iranian ballistic missiles and drones, sparking large fires.

Kuwait's fire department issued a stark warning: with 90% of the desert nation's drinking water dependent on seawater desalination, the sustained attacks on power and desalination units have pushed multiple plants offline, threatening the country's very survival. The Gulf Cooperation Council (GCC) has formally characterized these strikes on civilian infrastructure as "war crimes."

IAEA Investigates Nuclear Plant Strike as Iran's Internal Power Struggle Intensifies

The International Atomic Energy Agency (IAEA) confirmed on Sunday it is investigating reports that a US airstrike targeted the under-construction Darkhovin nuclear power plant in southwestern Iran. IAEA Director General Rafael Grossi appealed for military restraint, emphasizing that nuclear facility security is inviolable. Iran's Atomic Energy Organization condemned what it called a "terrorist attack" on the construction site.

The collapse of the ceasefire has also deepened Iran's internal political crisis. The death of Supreme Leader Ali Khamenei and the continued absence of his successor Mojtaba Khamenei from public view has emboldened hardline factions. According to CNN, hardline parliamentarians have publicly accused President Pezeshkian, chief negotiator Ghalibaf, and Foreign Minister Araghchi of attempting a "political coup" by sidelining parliament and diminishing the supreme leader's authority.

"They are trying to elevate the role of the Supreme Council for National Security while diminishing the role of the supreme leader and parliament. This is the political coup they have designed and are carrying out step by step." — Hardline Parliamentarian Kamran Ghazanfari

The US State Department has issued a worldwide travel alert for Americans abroad, citing heightened tensions in the region. Analysts note that both sides still retain off-ramps — Iran has not entirely closed diplomatic channels, and Trump faces midterm election considerations at home. But each round of escalation brings the conflict closer to an all-out war with unpredictable consequences.
