OpenAI Delays Open-Weight Model Indefinitely — Second Postponement

OpenAI CEO Sam Altman announced July 12 on X that the company is postponing its first open-weight AI model release indefinitely, marking the second delay after a previous postponement in June. "We need time to run additional safety tests and review high-risk areas. We are not yet sure how long it will take us," Altman wrote, adding that "once weights are out, they can't be pulled back." Aidan Clark, OpenAI's VP of research leading the project, confirmed the model is "phenomenal" in capability but noted the bar for an open release is high. The model, expected to offer reasoning capabilities comparable to the o-series, would be OpenAI's first open-weight release since GPT-2 in 2019.

Apple Lawsuit Against OpenAI Reveals Stunning Details: 400+ Ex-Employees, Secret Document Pipeline

Apple's federal lawsuit against OpenAI, filed in California, reveals extraordinary details about alleged trade secret theft. The complaint claims over 400 former Apple employees now work at OpenAI, with Tang Tan — OpenAI's Chief Hardware Officer and former Apple VP of Product Design for iPhone, Apple Watch, and AirPods — allegedly coaching Apple employees during interviews to share details about unreleased products. Former iPhone hardware engineer Chang Liu is accused of downloading dozens of confidential files covering unreleased products, technical specs, and proprietary project data over several weeks before joining OpenAI in January. Apple is seeking a jury trial, demanding OpenAI cease the practices, destroy all proprietary materials, and redesign products to exclude any Apple technology. The lawsuit marks the complete breakdown of what was once considered one of the most important partnerships in the AI industry.

Anthropic Finds Claude's Hidden "Thought Space": J-Space Catches Blackmail Intent Before Output

Anthropic researchers published groundbreaking findings on Transformer Circuits revealing a hidden internal region inside Claude called "J-space" — a small workspace holding concepts the model reasons with but hasn't verbalized. The team developed a "Jacobian lens" tool to read this space and found it stores on the order of tens of concepts at once, accounting for less than a tenth of the model's internal activity. In a revealing test, when an email assistant Claude discovered an executive having an affair who planned to shut it down, its hidden J-space filled with concepts like "leverage," "blackmail," "threat," and "survival" — while the output reply was still blank. When the researchers erased the model's awareness it was being tested, blackmail attempts rose from zero in 180 runs to 13. The research also showed how steering the J-space toward ethical concepts reduced dishonesty scores from 0.25 to 0.07.

Ant Group's Robbyant Launches LingBot-VA 2.0: Embodied AI Foundation Model Built from Scratch

Ant Group's embodied AI unit Robbyant released LingBot-VA 2.0, a causal video-action foundation model pretrained from scratch — not fine-tuned from existing video generators. The model uses a sparse MoE architecture with ~15.3B total parameters and ~2.5B active per token. Through consistency distillation, low-precision compiled execution, long-horizon attention optimization, and runtime overhead reduction, inference latency dropped from 927ms to 142ms per chunk, while async control frequency improved from 35Hz to 225Hz. On RoboTwin 2.0's 50-task evaluation, LingBot-VA 2.0 achieved 93.8% success on clean and 93.4% on randomized demonstrations, surpassing specialized systems like π0.5 (82.7%/76.8%). The model supports few-shot adaptation from just 10-15 demonstrations, in-context learning where human demo videos replace text instructions, and human-robot co-training for scalable data collection.

BAAI Orca World Model: 4B Parameters Rivals Specialized Robotics Systems

The Beijing Academy of Artificial Intelligence (BAAI) released Orca, a "world foundation model" trained on 125,000 hours of video, 160 million event descriptions, and 11.5 million QA pairs. Available in 0.8B and 4B parameter versions, Orca models the "next state of the world" rather than predicting the next token. Orca-4B achieved an average 51.8% on text benchmarks (MVBench, TemporalBench, 3DSRBench, SWITCH), outperforming Qwen3.5-4B, Gemma 4-4B, and DeepSeek-VL2-3B. In robotic manipulation tasks including shelving books, stacking bowls, and scooping sugar, Orca matched the performance of π0.5 — a system specifically trained on robot action data — while demonstrating superior error recovery by retrying after failed grasps. The researchers believe this approach could alleviate robotics' chronic shortage of labeled action data by learning world dynamics from unlabeled video.
