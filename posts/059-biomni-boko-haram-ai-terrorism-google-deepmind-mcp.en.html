🧬 Stanford's Biomni Published in Science: An AI Co-Scientist Transforming Biomedical Research

A team led by Stanford professor Jure Leskovec has unveiled Biomni — a general-purpose biomedical AI agent — in a paper published in Science. Unlike ordinary chatbots, Biomni integrates 150 specialized biomedical tools, 105 software packages, and 59 databases spanning all 25 biomedical sub-domains defined by bioRxiv. Users describe a research problem in natural language — e.g., "Why are these patients responding differently to this drug?" — and Biomni autonomously reads the literature, forms hypotheses, selects datasets, writes analysis code, interprets results, and suggests next-stage experiments. In one real-world test, a user uploaded 458 files of continuous glucose monitoring, food intake, and physical activity data. Biomni cleaned and unified the data, generated visualizations, and identified patterns in just 35-40 minutes — work estimated to take a human over 60 hours. Across 400+ real-world research tasks, Biomni achieved expert-level accuracy. Remarkably, over 10,000 labs worldwide are already using the prototype, making it the most widely deployed AI co-scientist in biomedicine. Lead author Kexin Huang — a Stanford CS PhD who co-founded Phylo to commercialize the system — said: "Biomni is changing the way biologists work." Leskovec emphasized: "The hurdle in biomedical science is not intelligence or ideas; it is mechanics. Biomni can do this work in minutes."

⚠️ Groundbreaking Field Report: Boko Haram Systematically Uses Frontier AI for Attack Planning

The Center for the Analysis of Security and Policy (CASP) released its report — "'God Has Helped Us, and So Will AI': How the Terrorist Group Boko Haram Uses Frontier AI" — based on 57 in-person interviews with 27 former Boko Haram members in northeast Nigeria conducted in 2025-2026. This is the first on-the-ground evidence of AI use by an active terrorist organization, and the findings are alarming. Both factions — Islamic State West Africa Province (ISWAP) and JAS — systematically use ChatGPT, Claude, Gemini, Grok, Meta AI, and DeepSeek for every stage of military activity: attack planning, explosive device design, weapons troubleshooting, and operational security. The Islamic State deployed seven instructors to ISWAP in 2024 specifically to provide AI training alongside drone operations and IED assembly. One former ISWAP commander said: "You type in the question or use your voice — like 'How can I build a bomb?' — and it tells you how. It is like a human robot! We used it a lot." Within roughly two years of ChatGPT's release, both factions progressed from initial exposure to dedicated AI units and full operational integration. Security safeguards were described as "manageable rather than prohibitive." One commander noted that trained members "know how to bypass the restrictions." The AI Incident Database has recorded 283 cases of "malicious actors and misuse." The report warns that terrorist AI adoption has been significantly underestimated in both scope and speed.

🔧 Google DeepMind Upgrades Agent Platform: Background Execution and MCP Become Standard

Google DeepMind has rolled out four significant features to Gemini API Managed Agents. The headline addition is Background Execution — agents can now run asynchronously without maintaining an open HTTP connection, eliminating the need for developers to sustain persistent connections during long-running tasks like document analysis or multi-step data pipelines. The second feature is direct remote MCP server connectivity, giving agents access to internal databases or third-party APIs through Anthropic's open standard. MCP has crossed 13,000+ servers as of June 2026 and was endorsed by Anthropic, OpenAI, and Block under the Linux Foundation on July 8, with Google Cloud launching its own MCP server the same day and AWS following on July 7. The third addition lets developers use custom functions alongside built-in sandbox tools. The fourth — and likely the most impactful for production deployments — allows credentials (tokens, etc.) to be refreshed between interactions without losing the sandbox state, solving a persistent pain point in enterprise agent deployments. The update directly competes with Anthropic's Claude Code, and Google's decision to adopt a competitor's protocol rather than pushing its own signals that MCP has become the de facto agent connectivity standard. Analysts note that the background execution feature mirrors the success pattern of AWS Step Functions, while credential refresh is the "sleeping killer feature" — agent state management has been the number one operational headache in production systems.
