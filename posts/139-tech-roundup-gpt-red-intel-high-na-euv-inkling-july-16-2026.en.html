⚡ July 16 Tech Roundup: OpenAI Launches GPT-Red Self-Attacking AI, Intel Ships First High-NA EUV Volume Chips, Mira Murati's Thinking Machines Debuts Inkling

1. OpenAI Unveils GPT-Red: Teaching AI to Hack Itself

On July 15, OpenAI announced GPT-Red, an internal AI model designed specifically to attack the company's other models — an "AI super-hacker" built to find vulnerabilities at scale. The breakthrough safety tool uses self-play reinforcement learning to discover and exploit security flaws, then feeds those discoveries back into production model training.

GPT-Red's core innovation is its self-play training framework: one model plays the attacker while defender models try to resist prompt injection attacks. As defenders get stronger, GPT-Red is forced to invent more sophisticated attacks. OpenAI says GPT-Red's training consumed "an unprecedented amount of compute dedicated purely for improving safety."

Crushing Human Performance

In independent benchmark tests, GPT-Red found successful attack paths in 84% of scenarios against GPT-5.1, compared to just 13% for human red-teamers. When OpenAI replicated a 2025 experiment where human testers had tried to find weaknesses in GPT-5, GPT-Red outperformed the humans by a wide margin.

More significantly, GPT-Red discovered a previously unknown attack class — "Fake Chain-of-Thought" attacks. This technique targets the reasoning traces that modern LLMs use, planting false entries in a model's internal "working memory" to trick it into trusting inaccurate information. Against GPT-5.1, these attacks succeeded over 95% of the time; after GPT-Red-hardened training, they now succeed on fewer than 10% of attempts against GPT-5.6 Sol.

GPT-5.6 Sol Becomes Most Robust Release

OpenAI used GPT-Red's findings directly in GPT-5.6 Sol's training. The result: 6x fewer failures on OpenAI's hardest direct prompt injection benchmark compared to the best production model from four months ago. On GPT-Red's own attacks, GPT-5.6 Sol fails on only 0.05% of direct prompt injections. Multiple indirect prompt injection benchmarks have been fully saturated, with the model resisting over 97% of attacks.

GPT-Red will remain strictly internal due to its deliberately trained offensive capabilities. OpenAI believes it's not easily replicable — it required over a year of development and frontier-scale compute resources.

"The risk surface grows and the blast radius also grows." — Nikhil Kandpal, OpenAI research scientist and GPT-Red co-creator

2. Intel Ships World's First High-NA EUV High-Volume Chips

ASML announced on July 15 that Intel Foundry has begun high-volume manufacturing using its High-NA EUV lithography technology, becoming the first company to ship commercial logic chips produced with this cutting-edge tool. This milestone marks a new era in semiconductor manufacturing.

Panther Lake Leads the Way

Intel is using ASML's High-NA EUV equipment on its Intel 18A process to produce select layers of its Core Ultra Series 3 processors (codename Panther Lake). These chips are dual-qualified at Intel's Oregon fab — the same layer can be exposed on either existing 0.33 NA NXE scanners or the 0.55 NA EXE scanners, with interchangeable wafers at matched yields.

High-NA EUV increases the optical system's numerical aperture from 0.33 to 0.55, enabling finer circuit patterns in a single exposure and reducing reliance on complex multi-patterning techniques. This improves manufacturing precision and simplifies the process — critical for the advanced chips powering the AI era.

A $400 Million Per Machine Milestone

Each High-NA EUV tool costs approximately $400 million — double the price of standard EUV systems. ASML plans to significantly expand production capacity, increasing low-NA EUV output from ~65 units per year by 30% in 2027 and another 30% in 2028, with similar expansions for immersion DUV systems.

Notably, TSMC plans to continue using low-NA EUV through 2029, giving Intel a potential competitive advantage in advanced process nodes.

"This milestone reflects the close technical collaboration between Intel and ASML and shows how High NA EUV can be integrated into advanced semiconductor manufacturing at scale." — Naga Chandrasekaran, EVP and GM of Intel Foundry

3. Mira Murati's Thinking Machines Lab Debuts First Open Model Inkling

Thinking Machines Lab, founded by former OpenAI CTO Mira Murati, released its first model on July 15 — Inkling, with fully open weights under Apache 2.0 license. The launch marks a significant milestone in the industry's shift toward customizable AI.

A 975B-Parameter "Medium-Sized Beast"

Inkling is a mixture-of-experts transformer with 975 billion total parameters but only about 41 billion active per token. It supports a context window of up to 1 million tokens and was pretrained on 45 trillion tokens spanning text, images, audio, and video. A lighter sibling, Inkling-Small, offers 276B total / 12B active parameters and matches or beats Inkling on several benchmarks.

"Not the Strongest, But You Can Reshape It"

Thinking Machines is refreshingly honest: the company states upfront that Inkling "is not the most performant model available today, closed or open." On Humanity's Last Exam, Inkling scores 30.0% vs. Claude Fable 5's 53.3%; on SWE-Bench Verified, 77.6% vs. 95.0%.

But raw capability isn't the pitch — it's customizability. Through the Tinker platform, organizations can fine-tune Inkling and fully own the result. In a partnership with hedge fund Bridgewater, an open model trained on financial data scored 84.7% on financial reasoning tests, beating top proprietary models at a fraction of the cost.

A Shift in AI Industry Models

Inkling's launch comes amid growing反思 about reliance on closed models. Microsoft CEO Satya Nadella recently warned that companies using closed models "pay twice" — once in fees and again by surrendering the knowledge baked into their prompts. Meanwhile, Chinese open-weight models now account for 41% of Hugging Face downloads and occupy the top six spots on OpenRouter.

Thinking Machines went from founding to first model in roughly 9 months (OpenAI took about 5 years, Anthropic about 3). The lab has raised $2 billion at a $12 billion valuation. Inkling is available completely free; Thinking Machines monetizes through the Tinker fine-tuning platform.
