⚡ July 22 Financial Roundup: Dow Surges 384 Points to Break 3-Day Slide, Brent Breaks $91 on Hormuz Crisis, Q2 Earnings 88% Beat Rate

Wall Street Rebounds Broadly: Dow Leads with 384-Point Rally, Nasdaq Jumps 1.3% on Chip Recovery

U.S. stock markets staged a broad rally on Tuesday, July 21, with the Dow Jones Industrial Average surging approximately 384 points (+0.74%) to close at 52,223.54, snapping a three-day losing streak in decisive fashion. The S&P 500 advanced 62.50 points (+0.84%) to 7,505.78, while the Nasdaq Composite jumped 336 points (+1.32%) to 25,844.05 — recovering much of Monday's selloff in a single session.

The driving force behind Tuesday's rally was the continued strength of Q2 earnings season. According to FactSet data, nearly 88% of the roughly 66 S&P 500 companies that have reported so far have exceeded profit estimates — a beat rate well above historical averages. Key highlights included:

  • 3M surged more than 9%: The industrial conglomerate delivered a comprehensive Q2 beat across both revenue and profit lines, becoming the single largest positive contributor to the Dow's gain
  • General Motors rose 3%: Both revenue and profit cleared Wall Street expectations, signaling continued strength in U.S. consumer spending
  • Semiconductor stocks rebound sharply: The Philadelphia Semiconductor Index rose over 3% in early trading before closing up 0.6%. Marvell Technology gained 3%+, Intel rose 2%+, Broadcom and Micron each added ~2%. The chip sector had lost over 20% in the prior week on AI capex concerns

Major earnings reports still to come this week include Alphabet, IBM, and Tesla — results that will provide critical signals on the health of the AI-driven tech megacap complex. This week also represents the final full trading week before the July 29 FOMC meeting.

Brent Crude Breaks $91: 10th Night of US-Iran Strikes, Hormuz Traffic Collapses to Single Digits

Oil prices continued their relentless climb. Brent crude futures rose 2.31% to $91.28 per barrel on Tuesday, reaching a five-week high not seen since June 10. WTI crude gained 2.39% to $84.45 per barrel. Over the past month, Brent has surged 17.18% and is up 33% year-over-year.

The rally is being driven by a confluence of supply disruptions across multiple chokepoints:

  • 10th consecutive night of US strikes on Iran: CENTCOM confirmed another wave of strikes against Iranian targets. President Trump vowed Iran "will pay" for the deaths of three American service members
  • Strait of Hormuz nearly paralyzed: According to Kpler shipping data, only 4 commercial vessels transited the strait on Sunday, down from a pre-conflict average of 130-140 per day. LSEG data confirms traffic has fallen to single digits
  • Houthis declare maritime blockade of Saudi Arabia: Yemen's Houthi movement threatened to block Saudi Red Sea ports, endangering the kingdom's alternative export route via Yanbu that bypasses Hormuz
  • CPC pipeline disrupted: Drone strikes on the Caspian Pipeline Consortium terminal on Russia's Black Sea coast halted exports from Kazakhstan, one of the world's largest crude suppliers (1.58 million barrels per day at risk)

Amrita Sen, founder of Energy Aspects, warned that a sustained reduction in Hormuz traffic combined with depleted global inventories could push oil prices above $100 per barrel. Diesel crack spreads remain elevated near $70/barrel, signaling severe downstream cost pressure.

"Brent breaking above $90/bbl marks the transition from geopolitical risk premium to a live supply shock. This is not markets reacting to headlines — this is the data signal of tankers not moving." — Stockwire Market Analysis

Fed Blackout Period: 86% Probability of July Hold, September Hike Bets Rising

The Federal Reserve has entered its pre-meeting blackout period ahead of the July 29 FOMC decision. According to CME FedWatch, markets are pricing an 85.6% probability of rates remaining unchanged in July, with only 14.4% pricing a 25bp hike. However, the outlook becomes more hawkish for September — the probability of no change drops to 38.5%, a 25bp hike is priced at 53.5%, and there is a 7.9% chance of a cumulative 50bp increase.

The interest rate environment is exerting structural pressure on markets:

  • 2-year Treasury yield at 4.24%, a 16-month high reflecting near-term hike pricing
  • 10-year Treasury yield at 4.51%, up 5bp, compressing multiples for high-duration growth stocks
  • Dollar Index above 101, weighing on emerging market assets and gold

Deutsche Bank has issued a stark warning in its latest report, raising its inflation forecasts and now projecting two rate hikes in 2026 (September and December, totaling 50bp), which would push the fed funds rate to 4.1%. The bank warns that the "Higher for Longer" environment needs to be aggressively repriced, creating a direct shock to fixed-income markets.

Political pressure on the Fed continues to mount. The White House has been pushing aggressively for rate cuts, but newly appointed Fed Chair Kevin Warsh has repeatedly emphasized the central bank's commitment to price stability. Fed Governor Christopher Waller's recent comments delivered a pointed response: "We are not going to keep rates down just to help the government finance its deficits."

Gold Struggles at $4,000, Commodity Divergence Intensifies

Gold futures continue to wrestle with the psychologically important $4,000/oz level. COMEX gold edged down 0.15% to $4,012.8/oz on Tuesday, while silver rose 0.66% to $56.7/oz. The Kitco survey shows 79% of Wall Street analysts are bearish on gold in the near term, and HSBC has lowered its annual gold forecast to $4,560/oz. Gold's traditional safe-haven bid is being suppressed by the strong dollar and rising real yields.

Other commodities show a diverging picture. Copper prices weakened on demand concerns from China, while agricultural products firmed on supply anxiety. U.S. gasoline prices have returned above $4/gallon (AAA data), with diesel crack spreads near $70/barrel — reflecting the pass-through of energy costs to the broader economy.

Week Ahead: Key Events to Watch

Several important data points remain on the calendar for the rest of the week:

  • Thursday (July 23): Initial jobless claims (consensus 212K vs 208K prior)
  • Friday (July 24): S&P Global July flash Manufacturing PMI (est. 54.5 from 53.9) and Services PMI (est. 51.0 from 51.2), June new home sales
  • Next week (July 29): FOMC rate decision — Kevin Warsh's second meeting as Chair
  • Earnings this week: Alphabet, Tesla, IBM quarterly reports

For investors, the current market is defined by a two-front battle: strong earnings fundamentals providing a floor, while surging oil prices and Fed uncertainty cap upside. Most market observers expect range-bound trading in the near term, with directional breakout dependent on next week's FOMC decision and heavyweight earnings results.
