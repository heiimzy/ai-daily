⚡ July 15 Financial Roundup: KOSPI Surges 7% to 7,300 with Buy Sidecar Triggered, ASML Beats and Raises Full-Year Outlook, Oil Hits $86 on Three-Day Rally

1. KOSPI Rockets 7%, Foreign Investors Pour 1 Trillion Won Into Korean Market

South Korea's KOSPI index staged a historic rally on Wednesday, surging 6.86% (470 points) to 7,326.96 as of 11:20 a.m. KST, reclaiming the 7,300 level in a powerful rebound. The tech-heavy KOSDAQ index also jumped 4.66% to 820. Both indices triggered buy-side circuit breakers within 17 minutes of opening — the 36th such activation this year.

Foreign investors led the charge, with net purchases of 1.02 trillion won ($693 million), while institutions bought a net 413.9 billion won. Retail investors, by contrast, were net sellers of 1.46 trillion won, in a classic "smart money buying the panic dump" pattern.

In individual stocks, SK Hynix surged 11.34% to 21.37 million won, following a 27.29% explosion in its US ADRs overnight. Samsung Electronics gained 6.18%, Hanmi Semiconductor jumped 18.0%, and SK Square rose 16.9%. All 17 industry sectors closed in positive territory, with electrical & electronics (+8.57%) and machinery (+6.58%) leading the gains.

The won strengthened, with the USD/KRW exchange rate falling 6.0 won to 1,487.0, as foreign capital flooded into local equities.

2. ASML Crushes Q2 Estimates, Raises Full-Year Forecast for Second Time

In a major boost for the global semiconductor sector, Dutch lithography giant ASML reported better-than-expected Q2 2026 earnings on Wednesday, raising its full-year sales guidance for the second time this year. The company cited surging demand for extreme ultraviolet (EUV) lithography machines driven by AI chipmaker expansion plans.

ASML shares rose 5.1% in Amsterdam trading following the announcement. The Philadelphia Semiconductor Index (SOX) had already surged 314 points on Tuesday, with Nvidia (+4.06%), Micron (+4.92%), and Intel (+4.51%) all posting strong gains. The Nasdaq Composite closed up 0.90% at 26,107.

"ASML's second guidance revision is the strongest confirmation signal yet that the semiconductor capex cycle is far from peaking," one analyst noted.

3. Oil Prices Rally for Third Day, Brent Hits $86.19 on Escalating US-Iran Conflict

Crude oil prices extended gains for a third consecutive session on Wednesday, as the US-Iran conflict showed no signs of de-escalation. US CENTCOM launched another 7-hour wave of airstrikes against Iranian military targets near the Strait of Hormuz overnight Tuesday. President Trump told Fox News that the US would strike Iranian bridges and power plants next week if Tehran refuses to return to negotiations.

Brent crude rose 1.72% to $86.19 per barrel — its highest since June 12 — while WTI gained 1.4% to $80.40. Brent had already surged 11% over the previous two sessions. Iran's Islamic Revolutionary Guard Corps claimed drone attacks on US positions at Jordan's Azraq Air Base and struck weapons storage facilities in Bahrain and Kuwait. A Kuwaiti naval vessel was hit, wounding 4 crew members.

Analysts at Nuvama Institutional Equities warned that an extended closure of the Strait of Hormuz — through which roughly 20 million barrels per day of crude flow — could send oil prices surging to between $110 and $150 per barrel.

4. Dollar Index Slips Below 101, All Eyes on PPI and Warsh Day 2

The US Dollar Index (DXY) continued its post-CPI slide, trading 0.12% lower at 100.80 during Asian hours on Wednesday. The June CPI data released Tuesday showed a 0.4% monthly decline — the steepest since April 2020 — cooling the annual rate to 3.5% and slashing July rate hike probabilities from 41.7% to 16.6%.

Markets now turn to two critical events later today:

  • US June PPI data (due 12:30 GMT) — Wholesale inflation data will be a key input for the Fed's preferred PCE gauge. Markets are pricing a 63% probability of a rate hike by September.
  • Fed Chair Warsh Day 2 testimony — Warsh appears before the Senate Banking Committee after a hawkish House debut on Tuesday, where he declared "zero tolerance" for inflation and announced five internal task forces. Investors will watch for any softening in tone.

In currency markets, EUR/USD rose to 1.1435, GBP/USD held at 1.3401, and USD/JPY eased to 162.09. The offshore yuan traded at 6.7735 per dollar.

5. Gold Retreats to $4,024 as Risk Appetite Improves

Gold prices edged lower on Wednesday as improving risk sentiment diverted capital away from safe-haven assets. Spot gold fell 0.7% to $4,023.67 an ounce, after briefly touching $4,100 on Tuesday following the CPI release. Silver slipped 0.46% to $58.83.

The equity rally across Asia — with the KOSPI soaring 7%, Nikkei +0.9%, ASX 200 +0.45% — drove a rotation out of precious metals into risk assets. However, ongoing Middle East tensions provided a floor under gold prices. The 10-year Treasury yield settled at 4.58%, down 4 basis points, while the 2-year yield dropped 8 bps. Bitcoin rallied to $64,583, a three-week high.
