⚡ July 23 International Politics Roundup: US Strikes Iran for 11th Night — Bushehr Hit, Tehran Air Defenses Activated; Trump's 'One Bridge Per Ship' Deterrence; Rubio Says Iran 'Not Serious' at ASEAN; Lavrov-Rubio Meet in Manila Today; EASA Warns Against Jordan Airspace; Netherlands to Ban Israeli Settlement Imports

I. 11th Night of Strikes: US Expands to Bushehr Province, Tehran Air Defenses Activated

U.S. Central Command completed its 11th consecutive night of strikes on Iran late Tuesday, with the campaign expanding to new geographic territory for the first time. American forces struck targets in Bushehr Province, home to Iran's only civilian nuclear power plant. According to Iran's IRNA news agency, the city of Jaghadak in Bushehr province was hit, with a power substation near the nuclear plant damaged.

Tehran residents reported multiple explosions in the early hours of Wednesday as Iran activated its air defense systems over the capital for the first time in the current campaign, Iran's semi-official Fars news agency confirmed. The Islamic Revolutionary Guard Corps (IRGC) claimed its air defense forces shot down a U.S. MQ-9 Reaper drone over Bushehr province.

"If the Americans attack Iran's bridges or power plants, Iran will respond by striking bridges and infrastructure in the region, including energy facilities in which the United States has interests." — Tasnim News Agency, quoting an Iranian military source

In retaliation, Iran's military launched drone and missile strikes against U.S. facilities in Jordan, Bahrain, and Kuwait in the early hours of Wednesday. Iran said it struck accommodation and storage facilities at Al Azraq Air Base in Jordan, equipment warehouses and aircraft maintenance hangars at Sheikh Isa Air Base in Bahrain, and Camp Doha in Kuwait.

Trump's 'One Bridge Per Ship' Deterrence Escalation

President Donald Trump issued his latest deterrent threat on Truth Social: "From this point forward, any time the Islamic Republic of Iran shoots at a ship in the Strait of Hormuz, whether it be by Missile, Rocket, Drone, or any other device or weapon, the United States will bomb and destroy ONE BRIDGE OR POWER PLANT, including those located next to, or in, the Capital City of Tehran."

This marks the first time Trump has explicitly threatened civilian infrastructure in and around the Iranian capital. International law experts noted that strikes on civilian infrastructure could constitute war crimes. Trump later departed for Dover Air Force Base to attend a dignified transfer ceremony for three U.S. service members killed in the conflict.

Since the June ceasefire memorandum collapsed on July 8, the U.S. has conducted 11 consecutive nights of strikes. The Pentagon has disclosed that the total cost of the Iran war has reached $37.5 billion. Nearly 100 U.S. service members have been injured in Iranian retaliatory strikes this month, with a total of 17 killed since the conflict began on February 28.

II. Rubio at ASEAN: Iran 'Not Serious' About Talks

U.S. Secretary of State Marco Rubio delivered his most comprehensive statement yet on the Iran crisis while attending ASEAN foreign ministers' meetings in Manila on Wednesday. Rubio said the U.S. remains open to diplomacy but that Tehran is "not serious" about negotiations.

"If they're serious, we're serious. If they're not, then we will do what's necessary to protect our interests, and also the interests of our allies." — Secretary of State Marco Rubio in Manila

Rubio specifically rejected Iran's demand for control over Strait of Hormuz shipping, calling it a "dangerous precedent" that could be replicated worldwide, including in the South China Sea — a pointed reference to ongoing maritime disputes involving China and ASEAN members.

On the sidelines of ASEAN, Rubio also met with Chinese Foreign Minister Wang Yi to discuss President Xi Jinping's planned visit to the U.S. The two sides agreed to hold U.S.-China AI talks in September. Meanwhile, new UK Foreign Secretary Ed Miliband made his international debut at the ASEAN gathering, calling on all parties to "work intensively to de-escalate this conflict."

III. Lavrov-Rubio Meeting Confirmed: Face-to-Face in Manila on July 23

Russian Foreign Minister Sergey Lavrov confirmed on Wednesday that he will meet U.S. Secretary of State Marco Rubio on the morning of July 23 in Manila, on the sidelines of the ASEAN foreign ministers' meeting. This will be their first face-to-face meeting since September 2025.

Lavrov said he plans to press Rubio on President Trump's prediction of an "imminent settlement" to the Ukraine war. The two diplomats are expected to discuss bilateral relations, the Ukraine conflict, and Middle East security. Rubio emphasized that as the world's two largest nuclear powers, the U.S. and Russia cannot stop communicating.

Separately, Russian and Chinese deputy foreign ministers held consultations in Beijing on July 22, jointly calling for an immediate cessation of hostilities between the U.S. and Iran and a return to the political track based on the Islamabad Memorandum of Understanding.

IV. EASA Warns Against Jordan Airspace; Netherlands to Ban Israeli Settlement Imports

The European Union Aviation Safety Agency (EASA) issued a safety warning on July 23 advising all airlines to avoid Jordanian airspace through the end of August due to the risk of missile fire between Iran and Israel. Jordan has intercepted multiple Iranian missiles in recent days, with air raid sirens sounding in Aqaba.

On the same day, the Netherlands announced it would formally ban imports of products manufactured in Israeli settlements in occupied territories, starting in September. It becomes the first EU member state to implement such a ban. Israel strongly condemned the decision.

V. DP World Builds New Terminals on UAE East Coast to Bypass Hormuz

Global port operator DP World announced on July 22 that it will develop two new container terminals in Fujairah on the UAE's eastern coast, aiming to reduce dependence on the Strait of Hormuz, which Iran has effectively blockaded. The new terminals will boost DP World's total UAE capacity from 19.4 million TEUs to nearly 22 million TEUs, while significantly expanding general cargo and Ro-Ro capability.

Shipping data shows just four commercial vessels crossed the Strait of Hormuz on Tuesday, down from seven the previous day, with no very large crude carriers or LNG tankers visible in traffic counts.

VI. Iran Executes Man Accused of Aiding U.S. and Israel

Iranian state media reported on Wednesday that authorities executed a man accused of providing intelligence to the United States and Israel during January protests. The execution signals Tehran's determination to maintain internal security control amid ongoing external military pressure.
