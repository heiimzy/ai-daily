⚡ July 18 International: 2 US Troops Killed in Iran Strikes, Tehran Threatens Full-Scale Offensive, Rial Crashes to Record Low

🔴 Major Escalation: 2 US Troops Killed, 1 Missing — First Direct Iranian Fire Casualties

U.S. Central Command (CENTCOM) announced on Saturday, July 18, that two U.S. service members were killed and one remains missing following an Iranian attack on a base in Jordan — marking the first U.S. combat deaths from direct Iranian fire since the opening days of the war on February 28, 2026.

The casualties occurred on Friday as U.S. and partner forces defended against Iranian ballistic missile and drone attacks, according to a CENTCOM statement. Four other service members who were medically evacuated to Jordanian hospitals have since been discharged. Since the war began, 16 U.S. service members have been killed and over 430 wounded.

"More than 50,000 American service members are operating across the Middle East and remain vigilant, lethal, and ready," CENTCOM said. Previous U.S. casualties in the conflict had largely resulted from indirect attacks such as rockets and IEDs — this direct loss of life from Iranian fire represents a significant escalation in the human cost of the confrontation.

⚡ Iran Threatens 'Full-Scale Offensive': 'No Political Border Will Be Safe'

Major General Mohsen Rezaei, a senior military advisor to Supreme Leader Mojtaba Khamenei and former head of Iran's Islamic Revolutionary Guard Corps (IRGC), issued a stark warning on Saturday: if U.S. strikes continue for another 2-3 days, Iran's armed forces will transition from deterrence and retaliation to full-scale offensive operations.

"If U.S. strikes continue for several more days, we will move into a phase of full-scale offensive operations," Rezaei told Iranian state media IRIB. "Iran will no longer limit itself to retaliatory, like-for-like responses... no political border will be safe." He emphasized that America's "fight-and-talk" strategy of attrition has reached a dead end and that future Iranian attacks will be more intense.

Iran has thus far targeted U.S. allies in the Gulf region (Kuwait, Bahrain, Jordan) rather than directly striking American military installations. The threat of a "full-scale offensive" signals a potential paradigm shift in which U.S. bases and personnel themselves become more direct and larger-scale targets.

🔥 Infrastructure War Escalates: Both Sides Strike Power Plants, Bridges, and Desalination Facilities

On July 18, the U.S.-Iran battlefield expanded beyond purely military targets to include infrastructure — raising concerns of war crimes under international humanitarian law.

Seventh Night of U.S. Strikes: 'Military Logistics Infrastructure' Targeted

CENTCOM announced the completion of its seventh consecutive night of strikes on Iran, with targets explicitly described for the first time as including "military logistics infrastructure" — along with surveillance sites, underground weapons storage, and maritime capabilities. The U.S. military said the operations were conducted "at the direction of President Donald Trump to continue degrading Iran's military capabilities."

Iranian state media reported explosions in multiple locations across southern Hormozgan province, including three blasts in the Sirik area, as well as strikes in Yazd and Ahvaz. At least five bridges were destroyed in Bandar Khamir and a train station hit, killing seven civilians, according to Iranian officials. A control tower at Chabahar Port — described by the Pentagon as part of the IRGC's maritime surveillance network — was also destroyed, with Defense Secretary Pete Hegseth sharing footage of the tower collapsing.

A White House spokesperson told the BBC the U.S. had "carried out strikes exclusively on military targets, including military logistics infrastructure," denying Iranian allegations of attacks on civilian infrastructure.

Iran Retaliates: Kuwait Power Plant Hit Again, 20 Villages Lose Water

In response to U.S. strikes, Iran's IRGC launched a fresh wave of attacks on Saturday targeting U.S. allies across the Gulf:

  • Kuwait: A power generation and water desalination plant was struck for the second consecutive day, causing a large fire that knocked out multiple generation units; Kuwait Petroleum Corporation (KPC) confirmed one of its oil facilities was hit by "repeated brutal Iranian attacks," causing several injuries and significant material damage; Kuwait International Airport suspended operations, with Kuwait Airways rescheduling most flights; Kuwait's military said it had intercepted 32 drone attacks since Thursday
  • Bahrain: The U.S.-used Sheikh Isa Air Base was attacked, with Iran claiming to have destroyed a U.S. drone depot and Bahrain's main AI center; Bahrain's air defense forces said they had "thwarted" multiple waves of attacks
  • Jordan: The military said it intercepted 10 Iranian ballistic missiles; Iran's IRGC claimed to have destroyed at least 2 U.S. fighter jets at the Azraq base
  • Saudi Arabia: Early warning alerts were triggered in Al-Kharj (home to a U.S. military base) and Yanbu (a key oil export terminal) — the first reported Iranian attack on Saudi soil in three months

On the Iranian side, U.S. strikes destroyed the pumping station and power transformer at the Bonji desalination plant in Jask County, leaving approximately 10,000 people in 20 villages without drinking water. Iran's Health Ministry reported that U.S. airstrikes have killed at least 50 people and wounded more than 500 since July 6.

UN Secretary-General António Guterres expressed grave concern over the escalation, particularly attacks on civilian infrastructure.

💸 Iranian Rial Crashes to Record Low: 1.928M to USD as Inflation Hits 88.6%

The resumption of hostilities has dealt a devastating blow to Iran's economy. On Saturday, the Iranian rial plunged to an all-time low of 1.928 million to the U.S. dollar, depreciating over 8% from approximately 1.78 million a week earlier. Washington's reimposition of a naval blockade on Iranian ports and revocation of sanctions waivers on oil exports have cut off Tehran's already fragile foreign exchange earnings.

Iran is grappling with 88.6% annual inflation and food inflation nearing 134%. Iranian officials say the conflict has caused approximately $270 billion in damage to civilian infrastructure, industries, and petrochemical facilities. Economists warn that if the war continues, the rial could breach the 2 million mark, potentially triggering severe social unrest.

✈️ U.S. Rushes Tanker Aircraft to Israel: Signal of Widening Campaign

According to Israeli Channel 12 and Axios, citing multiple U.S. and Israeli sources, the U.S. government has formally notified Israel that it will deploy dozens of aerial refueling tankers to the country. The tankers will arrive at a rate of 5-8 per day and will not land at Ben Gurion International Airport. Reports indicate President Trump appears intent on escalating the conflict, seeking to inflict sufficient destruction to force Iran to reopen the Strait of Hormuz and accept U.S. demands on nuclear issues.

The U.S. currently maintains approximately 30 tanker aircraft at Ben Gurion Airport and a similar number at Ramon Air Base in southern Israel. The deployment of additional tankers typically precedes large-scale air operations — notably, the U.S. conducted a similar tanker buildup before launching the initial joint U.S.-Israeli strikes on Iran in February 2026. The move has raised international concern that the conflict could further escalate to include strikes on Iran's nuclear facilities.

On the same day, Iran's IRGC Navy claimed to have intercepted 4 vessels attempting to transit the Strait of Hormuz in the past 24 hours, and alleged that 2 oil tankers had exploded and caught fire after hitting mines south of the strait — claims swiftly denied by CENTCOM as "false." MarineTraffic data showed only approximately 6 ships transiting the strait in the past 24 hours, effectively paralyzing one of the world's most critical energy chokepoints.
