⚡ July 15 Tech Roundup: PKU Neuromorphic Chip Hits 2.12ms in Science, DongFangSuanXin DF1000 AI Chip Launches, Intel Nova Lake Shifts 90% to 18A, Samsung Wins Anthropic 2nm Order

1. PKU/CAS Unveils World's First Sub-10ms Neuromorphic Chip in Science

A team led by Professor Yang Yuchao at Peking University's School of Integrated Circuits, in collaboration with Professor Song Zhitang at the CAS Shanghai Institute of Microsystem and Information Technology, published a groundbreaking result in the journal Science — the world's first sub-10-millisecond neural dynamical system (NDS) chip based on phase-change memristors. The chip achieves a single-step computation latency of just 2.12 milliseconds, pushing neuromorphic hardware into the millisecond era for the first time.

Fabricated on a 40nm process, the chip integrates an in-memory computing array with a step-size drift array covering only 0.28 square millimeters. It runs at 50 MHz with just 9 pipeline stages per integration step, achieving the 2.12ms latency milestone.

Performance Leap

Experimental results show the system outperforms state-of-the-art ASIC accelerators by 3.82x to 36.27x on identical neural dynamics tasks, while consuming 11.75x to 24.73x less power. On high-fidelity brain modeling tasks — including cortical surface reconstruction — the chip achieves a 50.38x to 478.18x speedup over Nvidia's A100 GPU.

A Science perspective article noted that the platform "enables high-precision computation at millisecond latency, paving the way for real-time computing in clinical imaging, robotics, and embodied intelligence."

Medical AI Applications

The chip enables real-time applications including digital brain twins, intraoperative neuronavigation, real-time cortical reconstruction, and neurodegenerative disease diagnosis. For conditions like Alzheimer's and Parkinson's, subtle changes in brain structure and function carry significant clinical value. The technology provides a new path for early screening, disease monitoring, and personalized intervention.

2. DongFangSuanXin Launches DF1000: Software-Defined 3D Near-Memory Computing AI Chip

Chinese high-end AI chip company DongFangSuanXin (东方算芯) held a launch event in Shanghai, unveiling its first software-defined near-memory computing 3D AI chip — the DF1000 — alongside a complete product portfolio spanning from accelerator cards to AI computing clusters.

Architectural Innovation Overcoming Process Constraints

The DF1000 achieves 520 TFLOPS @ BF16 on a mature process node through architectural innovation rather than process scaling. It employs a software-defined computing architecture supporting a three-level dataflow programming paradigm, improving efficiency through spatial parallelism and time-division multiplexing of hardware resources.

On the memory front, the chip uses 3D hybrid bonding technology with wafer-level stacking to deliver 6.4TB/s memory bandwidth and 900GB/s Scale-up interconnect bandwidth. This approach aims to fundamentally solve the "memory wall" problem while reducing dependency on advanced overseas manufacturing and HBM supply chains.

Complete Product Matrix

  • Dianfeng DF1000 Accelerator Card — Single-card AI inference/training
  • Qingyuan QY100 Server — Data center deployment
  • Tuoyu TY64 Supernode — 64-card liquid-cooled computing node
  • Huishu HS128 Computing Cluster — Large-scale distributed training platform

"What truly determines system capability is not just how small the transistor is, but whether the computing architecture, storage system, chip interconnect, and software stack can work together," said Dr. Wei Shaojun, Chairman of DongFangSuanXin. The DF1000 represents China's latest effort to achieve performance breakthroughs through architectural innovation rather than reliance on advanced foreign processes.

3. Intel's Nova Lake Foundry Reversal: 90% of Compute Modules Return to 18A

Intel plans to reverse its foundry strategy for the next-generation Nova Lake processor, shifting the majority of compute module production from TSMC back to its own fabs — raising self-production from the originally planned 30-40% to 80-90%, according to Chinese tech media reports.

Nova Lake, part of the Core Ultra 400 series targeted for late 2026, uses a modular tile architecture. Intel had previously planned a dual-foundry strategy with 60-70% of compute modules at TSMC's 2nm process and the remainder on自家18A. That ratio has now been inverted.

18A Yield Turnaround

Intel has resolved early 18A yield challenges after an extended technical攻坚 effort. Current 18A yields are on a stable upward trajectory, enabling timely mass production with competitive costs — the core confidence behind Intel's aggressive shift back to in-house production.

Intel currently operates two 18A production lines: Fab 52 at its Octillo campus in Arizona, and another fab in Hillsboro, Oregon, with a combined monthly capacity of approximately 30,000 wafers. For Nova Lake, Intel's challenge has shifted from yield issues to capacity constraints. This foundry reversal marks the first time since Intel's IDM 2.0 strategy that its advanced in-house process has taken back dominance in a truly high-volume product.

4. Samsung Reportedly Wins Anthropic 2nm AI Chip Order, Tesla AI5 Tape-Out Completed

Samsung Electronics' foundry division has reportedlydecided to take on Anthropic's custom AI chip manufacturing order, using a 2nm process, according to Korean media outlet NewsWorks and multiple tech publications. If the order is confirmed, it would help Samsung expand its advanced process customer base and potentially lift its long-struggling foundry business back to growth.

When Anthropic completed its Series H funding in May, it listed Samsung, SK Hynix, and Micron as "strategic infrastructure partners." Since Samsung is the only one among the three memory makers that also offers non-memory chip foundry services, industry sources believe Samsung is the most likely candidate for Anthropic's custom AI chip production.

Tesla AI5 Chip Progress

Samsung has also reportedly secured Tesla's AI5 chip order. Samsung foundry senior engineer Kim Jeong-gon revealed on LinkedIn that the Tesla AI5 chip — co-developed with Samsung — has entered the tape-out (design finalization) stage. The chip is expected to be manufactured on a 2nm process at Samsung's Taylor, Texas fab and will be deployed in Tesla's latest products.

Industry analysts note that the trend of AI companies building custom chips has become irreversible. From OpenAI's "Tigris" project and Google's TPU to Microsoft's Maia, Meta's MTIA, and now Anthropic's partnership with Samsung, the era of AI companies solely relying on Nvidia GPUs is giving way to a new age of custom silicon. This battle for computing sovereignty is reshaping the competitive dynamics of the entire AI industry chain.
