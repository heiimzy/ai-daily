⚡ July 17 Financial Roundup: TSMC's Beat Becomes a Bearish Signal, Global Chip Stocks Routed, KOSPI Crashes 6.37%, Nasdaq Falls 0.84%, CXMT Launches Mega IPO

1. Global Semiconductor Bloodbath: TSMC's Record Earnings Trigger "Sell the News"

On Thursday, July 16 (ET), the global semiconductor sector experienced one of its worst single-day selloffs of the year, triggered paradoxically by what should have been good news. Taiwan Semiconductor Manufacturing Co. (TSMC) reported record Q2 2026 results — net profit surged 77% to a record $22 billion, revenue hit $40.2 billion (+36% YoY), and gross margin improved to 67.7%, beating guidance. Yet the market's focus was squarely on the company's dramatically raised capital expenditure outlook.

TSMC lifted its 2026 capex guidance to $60-64 billion, up sharply from the prior $52-56 billion range, and announced an additional $100 billion investment in Arizona, bringing its total U.S. commitment to $165 billion. Management explicitly warned that second-half 2026 gross margins would come in below H1 levels due to the 2nm node ramp and overseas fab construction costs.

The Philadelphia Semiconductor Index (SOX) plunged over 4%, with all 30 constituents in the red. Goldman Sachs analysts described the selloff as a "free cash flow repricing event" — every incremental capex dollar compresses near-term shareholder returns, forcing multiple compression at elevated valuations.

Memory Chip Stocks Suffer the Worst Damage

  • SanDisk (SNDK): -10.80%
  • SK Hynix ADR (SKHY): -8.21% (Seoul-listed shares -11.53%)
  • Micron Technology (MU): -6.00%, lost its $1 trillion market cap
  • Marvell Technology (MRVL): -8.12%
  • Western Digital (WDC): -8.7%
  • AMD (AMD): -3.5%
  • Intel (INTC): -1.9%
  • NVIDIA (NVDA): -1.3%

In stark contrast, the Magnificent Seven rallied across the board: Apple (+1.31%) hit another all-time high, Microsoft (+1.08%), Amazon (+0.4%), and Google (+0.36%) all gained as capital rotated out of hardware names into mega-cap tech with more liquid profiles.

2. KOSPI Crashes 6.37%: BOK Surprise Rate Hike Meets Regulatory Hammer on Leveraged ETFs

Asia's worst casualty on July 16 was South Korea. The KOSPI index collapsed 6.37% to close at 6,820.60, breaching the psychologically critical 7,000 level and triggering a sell-side sidecar mechanism — a 5-minute suspension of program sell orders after KOSPI 200 futures fell over 5%.

Multiple factors converged to produce the meltdown:

  • The Bank of Korea unexpectedly raised its benchmark rate by 25bp to 2.75%, its first rate hike since January 2023, aimed at combating Iran-conflict-driven inflation
  • South Korea's Financial Supervisory Service banned new single-stock leveraged ETF listings and tripled margin requirements to 30 million won (approx. $20,200), accepting only cash collateral
  • U.S. Commerce Department pressure on Samsung and SK Hynix to relocate 40-50% of capacity to America, met with strong resistance from the Presidential Office

Individual stock carnage: Samsung Electronics -8.77%, SK Hynix -11.53%. Foreign investors net sold 1.38 trillion won, institutions dumped 2.37 trillion won, while retail buyers bought 3.66 trillion won but could not stem the tide. Samsung and SK Hynix combined with 16 related leveraged ETFs accounted for 96.3% of total KOSPI trading volume at 28.3 trillion won ($20.5 billion).

Japan's Nikkei 225 also plunged 1,916 points (-2.79%) to 66,835. Kioxia -15%, SoftBank Group -6.3%, Advantest -5.9%, Tokyo Electron -4.5%.

3. A-Share Markets Slide: Sci-Tech 50 Plunges 4%, CXMT Launches Record IPO Subscription

China's A-share markets fell broadly on Thursday, tracking the global semiconductor rout. Key closing levels:

  • Shanghai Composite: -1.85% to 3,882.41, lost the 3,900 level
  • Shenzhen Component: -1.97% to 14,488.65
  • ChiNext (Growth Enterprise): -2.95% to 3,692.46
  • Sci-Tech 50 (STAR Market): -4.02% to 2,110.36

Total turnover contracted to 2.42 trillion yuan, the lowest since mid-April, with over 2,800 stocks declining. Memory chip concept stocks led the losses: De Ming Li hit limit-down for a second consecutive day; Changdian Technology, Zhaoyi Innovation, and several others also hit limit-down. Montage Technology A-shares fell over 16% and Hong Kong shares tumbled over 23% following news of a South Korean prosecutor raid on its local office.

The session's highlight was ChangXin Memory Technologies (CXMT) launching its STAR Board IPO subscription. Priced at 8.66 yuan per share with 6.688 billion shares offered, the IPO could raise up to 66.6 billion yuan ($8.55 billion), making it the year's largest A-share IPO. The online subscription rate hit 0.47%, the highest of any 2026 A-share new issue. Analysts project CXMT's post-listing market cap could reach ~3 trillion yuan, with each winning lot potentially yielding ~20,000 yuan profit.

4. US Markets Diverge: Dow Edges Up, Nasdaq Slides 0.84%

U.S. equity markets showed sharp sector divergence on Thursday. The Dow Jones Industrial Average managed a modest 0.17% gain to 52,745.56, buoyed by UnitedHealth which surged over 7% after reporting Q2 revenue of $112 billion (beating by $1.14B) and adjusted EPS of $6.38 (beating by $1.46).

The S&P 500 fell 0.23% to 7,554.93 and the Nasdaq Composite dropped 0.84% to 26,048.62, dragged down by the semiconductor rout.

Economic data: U.S. June retail sales rose 0.2% month-over-month, in line with expectations and marking a fifth consecutive monthly gain, signaling consumer resilience despite elevated prices.

5. Oil Holds High, Gold Under Pressure, Yields Ease

Geopolitical risk from the escalating U.S.-Iran conflict continues to underpin energy prices:

  • Brent crude: $84.95/barrel
  • WTI crude: $80.41/barrel
  • Gold: -0.42% to $4,044/oz; Silver -2.83% to $57.11
  • 10-year Treasury yield: 4.55% (down ~4bp); 2-year: 4.13% (down ~6bp)
  • Dollar Index (DXY): 100.49, hitting a near-four-week low

The CME FedWatch tool now prices only a 10.2% probability of a July rate hike, down from 31% a week ago. Fed Chair Warsh, in his second day of Senate testimony, maintained a hawkish tone — "the inflation task is not finished" — but markets chose to focus on the two consecutive days of cooling CPI and PPI data.

6. The Great Rotation: $3.2 Trillion Shifts Between Mag 7 and Semiconductors

According to Strategas data, approximately $3.2 trillion in market value has shifted between the Magnificent Seven and semiconductor stocks in July alone. Semiconductor ETF daily trading volume has surged to over $40 billion, up from $9 billion a year ago. The S&P 500 has been trapped in a narrow 7480-7580 range since early May as the opposing moves in these two mega-sectors have essentially neutralized each other.

Warren Buffett this week described the current market as a "gambling mania where value targets are hard to find." Goldman Sachs' High Beta Momentum Index has retreated 24% month-to-date, its worst monthly performance since April 2009. With Q2 earnings season entering full swing, the upcoming reports from Netflix, Tesla, and other tech giants will be critical in determining whether the market breaks out of its two-month consolidation.
