⚡ July 17 Tech Roundup: Google Gemini 3.5 Pro Delayed Again, WAIC 2026 Opens in Shanghai, Nubia Debuts World's First AI Agent Phone, Xiaomi Open-Sources Robot Model

1. Google Gemini 3.5 Pro Delayed for Months as Coding Performance Falls Short

Google's flagship AI model Gemini 3.5 Pro has fallen months behind schedule as the company works to improve the model's capabilities, particularly in code generation, according to sources familiar with the matter reported by Caixin via Sina Finance at 2:43 AM and 3:31 AM CST on July 17.

Ten current and former employees revealed that the delay has frustrated engineers, AI researchers, and management, many of whom worry that Google may lose its market edge as competitors Anthropic and OpenAI ship models that outperform Gemini on key benchmarks.

Root Causes of the Delay

Google had already pushed the Gemini 3.5 Pro launch from June to July, but the latest reports suggest further delays of weeks or even months. An update to Gemini's training data last month aimed at improving coding performance yielded disappointing results. Google's stock fell 3.2% on Thursday in response to the news.

Internal sources point to a complex approval process involving multiple stakeholders and the challenge of integrating AI into Google's vast product ecosystem including Search, Maps, and YouTube. One former employee described the difficulty of aligning business units as "trying to boil the entire ocean."

Competitive Landscape

OpenAI and Meta have both recently released new models that lead Google in coding capabilities. AI researchers note that while Gemini's biggest advantage is its ability to leverage Google Search data, Anthropic and OpenAI currently lead in building the most capable frontier models.

Google stated: "We are accelerating the launch of multiple models while maintaining high cost-effectiveness." However, market reaction suggests investor unease about Google's position in the AI race. The delayed model was rumored to feature a 2-million-token context window and a Deep Think reasoning layer — capabilities that would be highly competitive if and when they ship.

2. WAIC 2026 Opens in Shanghai: President Xi Attends, 1,100+ Companies Exhibit

The 2026 World Artificial Intelligence Conference (WAIC) kicked off on July 17 in Shanghai, running through July 20 across four venues with a combined exhibition area exceeding 100,000 square meters. President Xi Jinping attended and delivered a keynote speech — his first appearance at WAIC since the conference began in 2018 — signaling Beijing's elevation of AI leadership to a top-tier national priority.

Conference Highlights

  • Unprecedented scale: 1,100+ companies showcasing 3,000+ technologies, with over 300 AI products making global debuts
  • Presidential attendance: Xi Jinping's presence marks AI as a strategic priority in the US-China tech rivalry
  • AI agent phone launch: Nubia's world-first AI agent phone NaviX Ultra debuts on opening day
  • Robot showcase: Multiple manufacturers exhibit humanoid robots and embodied AI products

The Nubia booth became the center of attention on the first day. Analysts note that WAIC 2026 arrives at a critical moment in global AI competition, with the US-China dual-superpower dynamic defining the year's narrative.

3. Nubia NaviX Ultra: World's First AI Agent Phone Makes Official Debut

Nubia officially unveiled the NaviX Ultra at WAIC 2026 on July 17 — the world's first AI agent phone. The device is powered by ByteDance's Doubao AI assistant, marking a significant shift from traditional voice assistants to autonomous AI agents capable of proactive task execution.

Key Specifications

  • Processor: Snapdragon 8 Gen 4 (3nm, all-big-core architecture)
  • Display: 6.78-inch OLED, 1.5K resolution, 120Hz refresh rate
  • Camera: Quad 50MP setup (front + triple rear)
  • Battery: 7,000mAh with 90W wired fast charging, supports wireless charging
  • Colors: Black, White, Blue, Pink

AI Features

The NaviX Ultra is built around four core principles: "understands, executes, remembers, and stays secure." AI is deeply integrated at the system level, enabling cross-app task execution. The phone features a dedicated orange AI button for one-click activation and switching of the AI agent.

Nubia President Ni Fei explained that current AI phones merely "stack features" — users still need to jump between multiple apps to accomplish one task. The next phase of AI phones will be "native agent" architecture, where the phone proactively understands user needs and completes tasks autonomously rather than requiring users to adapt to the phone's logic.

The device integrates a dedicated AI agent engine at the Android system level, with system-level simulated touch control permissions, enabling accurate understanding of multi-turn complex commands and autonomous cross-app operations.

4. Xiaomi Open-Sources Xiaomi-Robotics-U0: First Unified Embodied Generation Model

Between July 15 and 17, Xiaomi Technology released a series of breakthroughs in embodied AI. Following the Xiaomi-Robotics-1 foundation model on July 15, Xiaomi announced the open-source release of Xiaomi-Robotics-U0 — the industry's first unified solution covering four core embodied generation tasks.

Technical Highlights

  • Scale: 38-billion-parameter multimodal autoregressive model
  • Speed: Self-developed FlashAR+ inference acceleration delivers 83x efficiency gains over traditional autoregressive architecture
  • Breakthrough: 1024×1024 image generation reduced from 400+ seconds to 5.44 seconds
  • Open source: Full code and weights publicly available for developers

Xiaomi's robotics team released consecutive announcements from July 14 to 17, gradually building a closed loop of "hardware — data — model." The endgame for embodied AI is an integrated system of software, hardware, and data. Xiaomi's push provides China's massive robotics supply chain with a clear industrial path forward.
