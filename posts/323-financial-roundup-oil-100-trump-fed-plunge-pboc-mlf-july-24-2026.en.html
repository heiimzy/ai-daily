⚡ July 24 Financial Morning: Brent Crude Breaks $100, Trump Visits Fed, Dow Plunges 522 Points on AI Capex Fears

🔥 Brent Crude Surges Past $100: Middle East Conflict Opens Second Front in Red Sea

Brent crude futures broke through the $100 per barrel mark in early Asian trading on July 24, surging over 6% to hit a seven-week high — the first time prices have crossed the psychological threshold since June. The historic breakout was fueled by the expansion of the Middle East conflict into a second maritime front.

Houthi militants struck two Saudi oil tankers — the Encelia and the Layla — in the Red Sea on Wednesday, marking the first confirmed hits on commercial shipping since the group declared a maritime blockade last month. President Donald Trump responded by vowing "major military punishment" against Iran and its Houthi allies, threatening to destroy an Iranian bridge or power plant for every ship attacked. The UK's FTSE 100 fell 0.73% to 10,639 points, with airline stocks hardest hit — Wizz Air plunged 3.76% and easyJet managed only a 2.7% gain despite beating earnings estimates. Energy stocks soared as BP, Shell and other oil majors rode the crude rally, while precious metal miners dropped 5.1% as gold fell roughly 2% on a stronger dollar and rising inflation expectations. The 10-year U.S. Treasury yield climbed to 4.66%, a two-month high, as markets began pricing in a greater probability of rate hikes.

🏛️ Trump Visits the Fed: Pressure for Rate Cuts Intensifies

In an unprecedented move, U.S. President Donald Trump toured the Federal Reserve's headquarters on Thursday, inspecting a $2.5 billion renovation project before meeting personally with Fed Chair Jerome Powell to discuss interest rate policy. Trump later described the conversation as "productive" and made clear his desire for lower rates.

"If we could lower interest rates by three percentage points to one percent, we'd save over a trillion dollars," Trump told reporters. When asked whether he would fire Powell, Trump responded: "That would be a big move." The visit's timing was highly sensitive — coming less than a week before the Fed's July 31 FOMC rate decision. Markets have fully priced out any chance of a July rate cut, but CME FedWatch data shows the probability of a September rate hike has surged from 10% to 34% in just one week. Meanwhile, Bridgewater Associates founder Ray Dalio issued a stark warning on X: unless the U.S. quickly reduces its fiscal deficit to 3% of GDP, the country faces an "economic heart attack" within three years.

📉 Wall Street Rout: AI Spending Panic Wipes Out 522 Points from the Dow

U.S. stocks plunged sharply at Thursday's open. The Dow Jones Industrial Average sank 522.58 points to 51,696.00 as of 10:51 a.m. ET, a decline of 1.00%. The S&P 500 fell approximately 1.08% and the tech-heavy Nasdaq Composite tumbled 2.1%, leading the selloff.

The trigger: second-quarter earnings from Alphabet (Google's parent) and Tesla, both released after Wednesday's closing bell. Alphabet posted revenue of $119.8 billion (up 24% YoY) with Google Cloud surging 82% to $24.8 billion. But the company raised its full-year 2026 capital expenditure guidance to a staggering $195-205 billion (from $180-190 billion previously), while free cash flow swung to negative $5.86 billion — the first time in over a decade. Shares fell over 4% in after-hours trading as investors questioned whether massive AI infrastructure spending will generate proportional returns.

Tesla's picture was equally concerning. Record Q2 revenue of $28.24 billion (up 26% YoY) was overshadowed by adjusted EPS of just $0.33, far below the $0.51 consensus. Operating expenses surged 47% YoY, capex more than doubled to $5.79 billion, and free cash flow turned negative $1.09 billion — the first quarterly cash burn in over two years. Elon Musk declared 2026 "a massive capex year" with total spending expected to exceed $25 billion. Tesla shares dropped about 3% after hours.

In a bright spot, Super Micro Computer (SMCI) surged 26% on a record $60+ billion order backlog, underscoring that AI infrastructure demand remains robust even as Wall Street recalibrates its expectations for investment returns. The broader market narrative has shifted from AI hype to a hard-nosed assessment of capital allocation efficiency.

🏦 PBOC Conducts 500 Billion Yuan MLF Operation: Liquidity Support in Focus

The People's Bank of China announced a 500 billion yuan (approximately $68.5 billion) Medium-term Lending Facility (MLF) operation on July 24, with a one-year tenor. The operation uses a fixed-quantity, rate-bidding, multiple-price format, continuing the PBOC's strategy of maintaining ample banking system liquidity. This follows several weeks of increasing net open market injections — last week alone saw net liquidity injections of 510 billion yuan through reverse repo operations.

China's Ministry of Finance also released H1 2026 fiscal data, showing general public budget revenue of 12.1 trillion yuan (up 4.7% YoY) and expenditure of 14.33 trillion yuan (up 1.5% YoY). Securities transaction stamp tax surged 97.3% YoY, reflecting high stock market activity, while land-use rights transfer income plunged 31.5% YoY, confirming persistent weakness in the real estate sector. On the A-share market, the Shanghai Composite hovered around 3,876 points as investors await the impact of today's MLF operation.

🇪🇺 ECB Holds Rates at 2% as PMIs Paint Divergent Picture

The European Central Bank held its three key interest rates unchanged on Thursday — the deposit facility rate at 2.00%, main refinancing rate at 2.15%, and marginal lending rate at 2.40%. The decision was widely expected, with President Christine Lagarde emphasizing a "meeting-by-meeting" approach and full data dependency.

Eurozone HCOB PMIs for July showed the manufacturing PMI creeping up from 49.5 to 49.8 (still in contraction), while services PMI rose from 50.5 to 51.2. In the U.S., the S&P Global Manufacturing PMI unexpectedly contracted from 52.0 to 49.5 — its first sub-50 reading since January — while Services PMI expanded strongly from 52.9 to 55.2, highlighting a widening structural divergence between sectors.

In currency markets, EUR/USD slid 0.20% to 1.1749, while GBP/USD remained under 1.3400. The U.S. Dollar Index (DXY) strengthened above 105.7, and USD/JPY hovered near 163 — perilously close to its four-decade low — keeping markets on alert for potential Bank of Japan intervention.
