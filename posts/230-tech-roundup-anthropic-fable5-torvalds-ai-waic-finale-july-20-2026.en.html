⚡ July 20 Tech Roundup: Anthropic Fable 5 Subscriptions Restructured, Linus Torvalds Tells Anti-AI Critics to 'Fork It', WAIC 2026 Concludes

Anthropic Claude Fable 5 Subscription Changes Take Effect Today

After three deadline extensions, Anthropic's finalized Claude Fable 5 subscription structure officially took effect on July 20, drawing a sharp line through its paid subscriber base.

Under the new policy, Claude Max and Team Premium subscribers retain bundled Fable 5 access, but at a sharply reduced 50% of standard weekly usage limits. Compounding the cut, base subscription limits themselves shrank by roughly one-third on the same day as the two-month 50% Claude Code rate-limit boost expired.

For Pro and Team Standard subscribers, the change is more severe: they lose all bundled Fable 5 access entirely. Anthropic is issuing a one-time $100 usage credit, after which continued Fable 5 usage is billed at API rates — $10 per million input tokens and $50 per million output tokens, the highest pricing tier across the entire Claude model lineup.

Fable 5's journey to a permanent subscription model has been unusually turbulent. Anthropic originally planned to end free subscription inclusion on July 7, extended to July 12 after user backlash, then pushed again to July 19 as competitive dynamics and capacity constraints continued to shift. Each extension was announced via the official @claudeai X account. The final decision is widely seen as a compromise between compute shortage and competitive pressure — with OpenAI's GPT-5.6 Sol now broadly available and Chinese open-source models like Kimi K3 rapidly closing the gap.

Notably, the Trump administration recently lifted export controls on Anthropic's Mythos and Fable models, with Anthropic committing to enhanced security measures. This policy shift could bring additional international demand, further straining inference capacity.

Linus Torvalds: Linux Is NOT Anti-AI — 'Fork It or Walk Away'

Linux creator Linus Torvalds posted a forceful response on the Linux Kernel Mailing List (LKML), declaring that the Linux kernel project will not ban AI-assisted coding tools. Developers who oppose AI, he said, can "do the open-source thing and fork it. Or just walk away."

The debate was triggered by Sashiko, an agentic Linux kernel code review system that its creators claim can independently find 53.6% of bugs that would later be fixed by human coders, albeit with a roughly 20% false positive rate. Some maintainers objected to AI-generated bug reports creating additional workload, citing the Software Freedom Conservancy's recent guidelines advising open-source projects to respect developers who reject AI.

Torvalds wrote: "Linux is not one of those anti-AI projects, and if somebody has issues with that, they can do the open-source thing and fork it. Or just walk away." He emphasized his stance is "based on technical merit. Not fear of new tools." On AI's utility, he stated: "AI is a tool, just like other tools we use. And it's clearly a useful one. Anybody who doubts that clearly hasn't actually used it."

Acknowledging AI imperfections, Torvalds urged critics to look in the mirror: "Because it's not like natural intelligence is always all that great either." He stressed that the kernel community makes decisions "based on technical merit. Not fear of new tools," and that Linux is "NOT some kind of 'social warrior' project."

This stance contrasts with several recent open-source projects that have banned AI-generated code, including the Godot game engine and RPCS3 emulator. Torvalds' evolution is striking — from calling AI "90% marketing" in 2025, to personally experimenting with "vibe coding" tools earlier this year, to now vigorously defending AI tools in kernel development.

WAIC 2026 Concludes: Embodied AI and Humanoid Robots Steal the Show

The four-day 2026 World Artificial Intelligence Conference (WAIC) concludes today in Shanghai. Under the theme "AI Partnership for a Brighter Future," the exhibition area exceeded 100,000 square meters for the first time, attracting over 1,100 companies showcasing more than 4,500 products, including over 350 global premieres.

President Xi Attends WAIC for the First Time

Chinese President Xi Jinping made his first-ever appearance at WAIC, delivering a keynote speech calling for international cooperation rather than unilateral dominance in AI. He announced the establishment of the World AI Cooperation Organization (WAICO), headquartered in Shanghai, with 29 founding member countries including Russia, Pakistan, Kazakhstan, and Indonesia. Xi pledged AI training opportunities for 5,000 individuals from developing countries over five years.

Agibot Debuts Four New Products, 30+ Robots on Conference Floor

Embodied AI leader Agibot unveiled four new products simultaneously: the Yuanzheng A3 Ultra full-size humanoid, the Lingxi X2 Edu education platform, the Jingling G2 Max heavy-payload industrial robot, and the Linjiedian OmniHand 3 Ultra-M dexterous hand. The A3 Ultra features NVIDIA's Thor chip with a proprietary three-layer heterogeneous computing architecture (700 TOPS), GPS/RTK/UWB fusion positioning for centimeter-level accuracy, and 8-hour operation with hot-swap battery support. Over 30 Agibot robots are deployed across WAIC venues providing visitor guidance and information services.

Agibot co-founder Peng Zhihui signaled global export ambitions, but the US response has been swift: Senators Tom Cotton and Chuck Schumer introduced the American Security Robotics Act in March 2026, which would bar federal agencies from purchasing humanoid robots from companies linked to foreign adversaries, with Agibot specifically named.

Domestic AI Chips and Computing Infrastructure Take Center Stage

The H2 exhibition hall showcased China's most advanced computing infrastructure. Huawei's Atlas 950 SuperPoD made its debut with 64 Ascend cards per cabinet, supporting over 500,000 cards at maximum scale. Dongfang Suanxin displayed the DF1000 chip, built on a fully domestic supply chain from design to 3D packaging. Other chip makers including Muxi, Kunlunxin, and Moore Threads presented complete computing systems from individual chips to full cabinet clusters.

Tencent, SenseTime, iFLYTEK Unveil New Models

Tencent's Robotics X Lab released the Hy-Embodied-VLM-1.0 embodied vision-language model and the Apexio continuously-online embodied agent. SenseTime launched the SenseNova U1 Pro flagship multimodal model supporting 8K output and long-horizon agent workflows. iFLYTEK introduced GuideX, an intelligent interaction agent for public service environments now deployed across Southeast Asia, the Middle East, and Latin America.
