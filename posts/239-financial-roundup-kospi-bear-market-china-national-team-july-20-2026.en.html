⚡ July 20 Financial Roundup: KOSPI Crashes Into Technical Bear Market Triggering Sell-Side Sidecar, China's 'National Team' Deploys $84B Emergency Rescue

1. KOSPI Plunges 4.36%: 20th Sell-Side Sidecar of 2026, Technical Bear Market Confirmed

South Korean stocks suffered a brutal selloff on Monday, July 20. The benchmark KOSPI index opened 2.60% lower at 6,643.58 and accelerated its decline through the session, briefly touching 6,523.54 — a drop of 4.36%. By noon, the index was trading at 6,578.29, down 3.55%. The Korea Exchange (KRX) triggered a sell-side sidecar on the main bourse at 11:21:26 AM KST — the 20th such activation on the KOSPI in 2026. The KOSDAQ market had already triggered its own sidecar at 10:52 AM, with the KOSDAQ index falling as much as 5.29% during the session.

On a broader scale, the KOSPI has now retreated more than 25% from its recent peak, officially entering a technical bear market. Han Ji-young, a researcher at Kiwoom Securities, commented: "Since July, the KOSPI has dropped about 25% from its peak, entering a technical bear market. A sharp decline of 30–40% in leading stocks such as Samsung Electronics, SK hynix, and Samsung Electro-Mechanics is amplifying the sense of decline."

AI Shockwave: Kimi K3 Sparks Capex Panic

Two core drivers fueled the crash — an AI shock and Middle East geopolitical risk. Chinese AI startup Moonshot released Kimi K3 over the weekend, the world's first open-source large language model with 3 trillion parameters — double the estimated size of Anthropic's Claude Opus (~1.5T). Investors immediately began reassessing the outlook for AI infrastructure capital expenditure, fearing that low-cost open-source models could undermine the massive spending plans of US tech giants.

This dynamic mirrors the DeepSeek shock from late 2025. Kim Ju-yeon, a researcher at Mirae Asset Securities, warned: "Concerns have been raised that one could approach the highest level of US technology even at low cost. As the possibility of revisions to US big tech's massive capex strategies emerged, it triggered a market decline led by beneficiaries of expanded AI spending."

Adding fuel to the fire, TSMC reported strong Q2 results but raised its 2026 capital expenditure guidance to $60-64 billion (from $52-56 billion). Markets interpreted the higher spending as a precursor to supply oversaturation, sending TSMC shares down 7.29% and deepening the semiconductor rout.

Broad-Based Selloff: Samsung -4.5%, SK Hynix -4.4%, Hyundai -6.6%

No major KOSPI stock was spared. Samsung Electronics plunged 4.5% to 240,000 won, while SK hynix tumbled 4.4% to 1.78 million won. Hyundai Motor dropped 6.59%, LG Energy Solution fell 2.40%, and Samsung Life Insurance lost 6.19%.

The only bright spot was S-Oil (+3.04%), benefiting from surging crude oil prices. Notably, individual investors net-sold 3.21 trillion won and institutions 870 billion won, while foreign investors were net buyers at 3.85 trillion won — effectively serving as the "buyer of last resort." The KOSPI's 12-month forward P/E ratio has fallen to just 5.8x, the lowest level in 20 years including the 2008 global financial crisis.

2. China's 'National Team' Deploys $84B Emergency Rescue: CSRC Symposium + State-Owned Enterprise Buybacks + A-Share Rebound

In stark contrast to the panic in Seoul, China's A-share market received a barrage of policy support over the past 24 hours, with state-backed buying at a pace rarely seen in recent years.

China Guoxin $70B + China Chengtong $14B: State Capital in Action

On the evening of July 19, China Guoxin Holdings announced that its investment arm had deployed over 500 billion yuan (~$70 billion) in stock buyback and share-purchase special refinancing facilities and matching funds to stabilize the market, vowing to continue increasing holdings. Simultaneously, China Chengtong announced it had cumulatively purchased nearly 100 billion yuan (~$14 billion) in Chinese equity assets. Both institutions explicitly committed to "fully maintaining the stable and healthy operation of the capital market."

On Monday morning, three additional state-owned enterprises — China Coal Energy, CRRC Corporation, and Aluminum Corporation of China — announced buyback plans totaling at least 1.2 billion yuan. Three securities firms (Hua'an, Guolian Minsheng, and Guojin) also announced share repurchase plans of up to 700 million yuan.

CSRC Emergency Symposium: Five Major Market Forces Align

Sources close to the regulator revealed that the China Securities Regulatory Commission (CSRC) convened an emergency symposium on July 20 with listed companies, securities firms, and fund managers to solicit opinions on stabilizing the capital market. This represents the clearest policy-level signal yet that authorities are coordinating a broad rescue effort.

Fund flows corroborate the intervention. During the week of July 13-17, total ETF net inflows hit a staggering 230 billion yuan, with broad-based ETFs accounting for 156.1 billion yuan. The Huatai-PineBridge CSI 300 ETF alone attracted 21.4 billion yuan in a single week, pushing its AUM toward 100 billion yuan. On July 17 alone, broad-based ETF turnover exceeded 151.6 billion yuan, surpassing the turnover level seen when the 'national team' previously announced its stock-purchase program.

A-Share Midday Rebound: Shanghai +1.18%, Moutai Surges 5% on Price Hike

Backed by multiple support forces, China's three major indices opened higher, retreated briefly, then staged a rebound. By midday close, the Shanghai Composite Index stood at 3,808.39 points, up 1.18%; the Shenzhen Component Index edged up 0.21%; and the ChiNext Index gained 1.13%. Combined turnover for the morning session reached 1.67 trillion yuan, up 68.1 billion from the previous session.

Sector-wise, oil and gas stocks surged (CNOOC hit limit-up), power stocks rallied, and coal stocks gained. The baijiu sector was notably active after Kweichow Moutai announced a 100 yuan price increase for its Feitian Moutai on the iMoutai platform (to 1,639 yuan/bottle), with the contract price also rising 100 yuan to 1,369 yuan/bottle. Moutai shares surged over 5%, pulling the broader consumer sector higher.

Chip stocks opened higher but reversed lower, with one stock (Demingli) falling limit-down for four consecutive sessions, while PCB concept stocks weakened. The divergence confirmed a market style shift from tech/growth toward value/defensive positioning.

3. Global Markets: Divergence Intensifies, Geopolitical Risk Dominates

Safe-Haven Rotation Into Energy

US markets closed lower on Friday — the S&P 500 fell 1.01%, the Nasdaq shed 1.40%, and the Philadelphia Semiconductor Index entered bear territory at 20%+ below its peak. Asian markets were mixed on Monday: Japan was closed for a holiday, Australia's ASX 200 edged up 0.3% on energy gains, and Hong Kong's Hang Seng opened 1.11% higher with the Tech Index up 2.2%.

Geopolitically, the US completed a ninth consecutive night of airstrikes against Iran, and Iran's IRGC declared Strait of Hormuz traffic at zero. Brent crude surged past $91/barrel (up 2.54%), with WTI crude above $85. Energy stocks were the sole consensus safe-haven trade globally.

Gold at $4,000, Fed Rate Hike Odds Rise

Spot gold traded around $4,014/oz on Monday, recovering slightly from intraday lows, but the oil-driven inflation surge weighed on the non-yielding metal. The CME FedWatch Tool now prices a 61.4% probability of a September rate hike. Cleveland Fed President Beth Hammack joined a growing chorus of policymakers warning that inflation remains persistent, adding to gold's headwinds.

The Korean won weakened to 1,482.90 per dollar, while the yen hovered near four-decade lows (USD/JPY ~162.50). Japan's Finance Minister Katayama issued fresh intervention warnings, but the wide rate differential continues to keep the yen under pressure.

Key Catalyst This Week: Big Tech Earnings

Alphabet (GOOGL) and Tesla (TSLA) are set to report Q2 earnings this week — a critical test for the AI capex narrative. Analysts project S&P 500 Q2 earnings growth of 26%, but any signal of slowing AI-related capital spending could trigger a fresh round of global tech selloffs. Intel reports on Thursday, and Micron's earnings on Wednesday will provide the next major data point on chip demand.
