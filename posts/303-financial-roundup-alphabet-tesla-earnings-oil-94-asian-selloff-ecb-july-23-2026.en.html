⚡ July 23 Financial Roundup: US Stocks Dip Ahead of Megacap Earnings, Brent Tops $94, Asia Markets Rout, ECB Decision Takes Center Stage

Global financial markets are navigating a complex landscape on Thursday, July 23, as investors digest a pivotal batch of Big Tech earnings, surging oil prices above $94/barrel, a sharp sell-off across Asia-Pacific equities, and the European Central Bank's interest rate decision later today.

🔹 Wall Street Wednesday: Cautious Close Ahead of Mega-Cap Earnings

U.S. stocks ended mixed on Wednesday, July 22, as caution prevailed ahead of after-hours earnings from Alphabet and Tesla. According to Reuters data, the S&P 500 slipped 10.72 points (-0.14%) to 7,498.48, the Nasdaq Composite lost 145.48 points (-0.56%) to 25,691.72, while the Dow Jones Industrial Average edged up 1.52 points to 52,226.16.

Semiconductor stocks showed mixed performance — Micron, Intel, and Sandisk each fell roughly 4%, while AT&T surged over 4% on an earnings beat. Oil's continued rally weighed on sentiment, with Brent crude climbing over 3% to approach $94/bbl as Secretary of State Rubio downplayed prospects for diplomatic progress with Iran.

🔹 Alphabet Q2 Earnings: Revenue Beats, EPS Slightly Misses as AI Investment Accelerates

Alphabet (GOOGL) reported mixed second-quarter results after Wednesday's close. Key metrics:

  • Revenue: $119.8 billion, +24.2% YoY, above consensus of $116.93 billion
  • Adjusted EPS: $2.85, slightly below $2.89 consensus
  • GAAP EPS: $9.11, massively above estimates (includes ~$99B gain on equity securities)
  • Google Cloud Revenue: $24.8 billion, +82% YoY
  • Capital Expenditure: $44.9 billion, roughly +100% YoY, in line with estimates
  • Search Revenue: +17% YoY, in line with expectations
  • YouTube Ads: +13% YoY

The standout item was $97.98 billion in "other income," primarily driven by fair-value gains on equity securities including stakes in Anthropic and SpaceX. This boosted GAAP net income to $112.1 billion, up 298% YoY. CEO Sundar Pichai highlighted that the Antigravity AI coding tool now has 2.4 million weekly active users, while Google Cloud's AI inference demand continues to surge.

🔹 Tesla Q2 Earnings: Record Revenue But Profit Miss, Stock Slides 3% After Hours

Tesla (TSLA) delivered a mixed Q2 report after Wednesday's close:

  • Revenue: $28.24 billion, +26% YoY, above $25.71 billion consensus
  • Adjusted EPS: $0.33, well below $0.51 consensus
  • Net Income: $1.11 billion, -5% YoY
  • Vehicle Deliveries: 480,126 (record), +25% YoY
  • Gross Margin: 16.8%, below 19.4% consensus
  • Free Cash Flow: -$1.1 billion (first negative in two years)
  • Capex: ~$5.8 billion, +142% YoY
  • Cash Position: ~$43.5 billion
  • Energy Storage Deployments: 13.5 GWh (record), +41% YoY

Tesla's gross margin miss highlights the cost of the company's aggressive pivot toward AI infrastructure, Cybercab production preparation, and Optimus robot development. Free cash flow turned negative for the first time in over two years. TSLA shares slid approximately 3% in after-hours trading to around $361. All eyes are on Elon Musk's 5:30 PM ET earnings call for clarity on the AI investment roadmap.

🔹 Brent Crude Breaks $94: Middle East Tensions Fuel Supply Risk Premium

Oil prices surged on Wednesday. Brent crude futures for September delivery settled at $94.07/barrel, up 3.36% — a six-week high. WTI crude rose 2.95% to $86.83/barrel.

The rally was driven by the 11th consecutive night of US-Iran strikes, with Rubio dampening hopes for diplomatic resolution. Houthi threats to Red Sea shipping lanes added further supply disruption fears. Brent crude is up 21.77% month-to-date and 37% year-over-year. Analysts warn Brent could test $100 if the Strait of Hormuz remains effectively closed.

🔹 Asia-Pacific Markets Rout: Tech Profit-Taking Spreads East

Asia-Pacific equities plunged on Thursday, July 23, as overnight weakness in US tech stocks and AI capex concerns spread to the region (Yonhap Infomax data):

  • Nikkei 225: 69,788.38 (-3.55%), below 70,000 after hitting record highs
  • Shanghai Composite: 4,106.25 (-1.37%), AI-related sectors led declines
  • Shenzhen Composite: 2,833.77 (-2.34%)
  • Hang Seng Index: 23,336.28 (-1.82%)
  • Taiwan Weighted: 47,100.65 (-1.34%)
  • KOSPI: -3.72%, SK Hynix down over 4%

Billy Leung, Investment Strategist at Global X Management, characterized the correction as an extension of sector rotation from the US rather than a new risk-off phase. The dollar-yen traded at 161.71, while USD/CNY reference rate was set at 6.8171.

🔹 ECB Decision Today: Rates Expected to Hold at 2.25%, Focus on September Signal

The European Central Bank announces its latest interest rate decision on Thursday, July 23. Markets widely expect the deposit facility rate to remain at 2.25% (99% implied probability on Polymarket).

Without updated staff projections at this interstitial meeting, all attention focuses on President Christine Lagarde's 13:45 CET press conference. ING expects a hawkish-leaning hold, with hints of a September rate hike potentially emerging via post-meeting communications. EUR/USD trades near 1.140, with the 10-year Bund yield around 3.15%. A September hike is already nearly fully priced in, barring a sharp decline in oil prices.

🔹 Gold Holds Above $4,100: Safe-Haven Bid Defies Higher Yields

Spot gold rose 1.0% to $4,118.74/oz on Wednesday, with futures up 1.1% to $4,123/oz — a fourth consecutive session of gains. The rally persists despite rising bond yields and surging oil prices, both typically headwinds for gold. Analysts at TradeVisor note gold has broken above key technical resistance at $4,035, with the next target at $4,200. Central bank buying across Asia and the Middle East provides structural support beneath the tactical safe-haven bid.

Overall, global financial markets on July 23 are firmly in "risk-off" mode — equities under pressure, commodities surging, and gold resilient. The Alphabet and Tesla earnings provide fresh anchors for valuation, while the ECB's policy path and Middle East developments will determine the next phase of market direction.
