⚡ WAIC 2026: Humanoid Robot 'Mass Production Era' Begins — 300+ Robots Work Across Venue, Domestic Supply Chain Hits 75%

From Acrobatics to Real Work: The Biggest Shift at WAIC 2026

SHANGHAI, July 19, 2026 — At the 2026 World Artificial Intelligence Conference (WAIC), the embodied AI exhibition hall has undergone its most visible transformation yet. Where dancing robots and martial arts demonstrations once dominated the floor, visitors now see palletizing, material handling, assembly, and inspection tasks being performed in real industrial settings. The conference, running July 17-20 at the Shanghai World Expo Center under the theme "AI Partnership for a Brighter Future," has drawn over 1,100 companies showcasing more than 3,000 exhibits, including over 300 global debuts.

According to official figures, WAIC 2026 has elevated embodied intelligence to a core track alongside intelligent computing for the first time. The number of embodied intelligence exhibitors surged from 80 last year to over 200. More than 208 embodied intelligence terminals and over 300 physical robots are on display. Peng Zhihui, co-founder of AgiBot, noted: "Compared to last year, the most obvious shift at WAIC 2026 is that embodied AI is moving from 'showing off capabilities' and 'demonstrating skills' over the past two years to true deployment and productivity release."

300 Robots as Venue Staff: World's First Large-Scale Commercial Humanoid Deployment

Perhaps the most striking change at this year's WAIC is that dozens of humanoid robots have become actual "volunteers" — no longer confined to display booths, they roam across the venue's corners, providing guided tours, answering inquiries, and managing unmanned retail kiosks. This marks the first time full-sized humanoid robots have been deployed at scale for commercial service in a major exhibition. The robots, primarily provided by AgiBot, offer continuous navigation and information services throughout the expo.

"In the past, people would ask: Can the robot dance? Can it do a backflip? Now people ask: Can the robot work continuously for 24 hours? Can it repeat a movement ten thousand times? Can the function be reused for another standardized product?" said Hu Chunxu, Vice President of Developer Ecosystem at Digua Robot, describing the industry's shifting focus.

Leju Robotics set up a 1:1 replica production line in its "Brick-Moving Zone." Three humanoid robots tirelessly perform palletizing, depalletizing, and small-part loading operations — when boxes shift out of alignment, the robots automatically correct their posture without any human intervention. These solutions have been deployed in factories for 3-4 months, serving over ten leading enterprises including FAW, ZTE, and Changhong.

2026: The 'Year of Mass Production' for Humanoid Robots

According to MIIT forecasts, annual humanoid robot production is expected to exceed 100,000 units this year. In 2025, global humanoid robot shipments were approximately 20,000 units. 2026 is widely recognized by the industry as the "Year of Mass Production" — not the "Year of Demonstrations" or "Year of Fundraising" but the year when "physical machines roll off production lines, customers sign receipts, and production lines run continuously."

AgiBot has set its WAIC theme as "The Year of Deployment," introducing multiple deployment-focused products including the Expedition A3 Ultra, Spirit G2 Max, and Lingxi X2 EDU. The Expedition A3 Ultra features a 700 TOPS onboard computing platform and was named WAIC's "Treasure of the Hall." AgiBot also launched the AgiGO-2 proprietary embodied foundation model with Action Thought Chain and asynchronous dual-system architecture. Its GE-2 world model ranked first overall in the 2026 World Arena World Model competition.

Perhaps the most significant supply chain signal: the domestic production rate of core components for humanoid robots has now exceeded 75%. Leju's Kuafu series humanoid robots have achieved a domestic component rate exceeding 95%. From underlying computing chips and actuated joints to multimodal perception sensors, a complete, independently controlled domestic supply chain is taking shape. This marks the transition of humanoid robots from "experimental prototypes assembled from scattered imported parts" to "mature industrial products ready for mass production and standardized delivery."

Six Robots Build 81,920-Brick Great Wall in 15 Hours

One of the most compelling embodied AI demonstrations at WAIC 2026 was Dexmal's "Six Robots Build the Great Wall" challenge. Four desktop robots and two wheeled humanoid robots worked collaboratively to assemble a 3.5-meter-long, 1.5-meter-wide, 1.1-meter-tall Great Wall model from 81,920 miniature building blocks over 15 hours.

The challenge tested three notoriously difficult capabilities: sub-millimeter precision assembly (each block required alignment to within 0.1-1 mm), heterogeneous multi-robot coordination (six robots sharing the same physical workspace without remote control), and 15-hour continuous operation with sustained accuracy. The "brains" behind the operation was Dexmal's DM0.5, an open-world Vision-Language-Action foundation model featuring up to 60 seconds of historical visual context, embodied chain-of-thought reasoning, and trajectory alignment. On the standardized RoboChallenge Table30 V2 benchmark, DM0.5 achieved a 43% overall success rate, the best published result.

Rideable Quadruped Robots Steal the Spotlight

Another major attraction at WAIC 2026 was the emergence of rideable quadruped robots. A riding robot developed by Chongqing Haochen Embodied Intelligence Technology, with technical support from AgiBot subsidiary AGIQUAD, opened for global pre-orders at the event with September delivery planned. The robot reaches 15 km/h top speed, carries 75 kg payload, travels 20 km per charge, and climbs 25-degree slopes with all-terrain capability and voice interaction.

DaxAI Robot's Qiji X1 All-Terrain Intelligent Mount-Quadruped Cyber Horse, billed as the world's first AI-powered smart riding quadruped, also drew huge crowds. It features a 300 kg dynamic payload, 40 km range, and 10 km/h top speed, designed for premium off-road leisure riding across forests and grasslands. Hangzhou-based DEEP Robotics unveiled a limited-edition robot horse celebrating the Year of the Horse 2026, weighing 30 kg and carrying up to 50 kg on the same rugged platform used in industrial inspection and emergency rescue.

Industry observers noted that quadruped robots are being repositioned from "tools" to "partners," expanding from industrial inspection and emergency rescue into outdoor leisure and human-robot collaboration scenarios.

VLA vs World Models: Two Parallel Technical Paths

At the technical level, WAIC 2026 clearly presented two parallel development paths: VLA (Vision-Language-Action) models and World Models. AgiBot has adopted a dual-track strategy: using its proprietary GO-2 embodied foundation model as the decision-making core with Action Chain-of-Thought and asynchronous dual-system architecture, while continuously iterating its GE-2 world model to upgrade from virtual simulation to real-world autonomous learning. The company also launched Genie Evolver 1.0, a real-world closed-loop reinforcement learning framework representing a third differentiated approach.

Pudu Robotics showcased its "One Brain, Multiple Embodiments" Physical Agent architecture at WAIC, with its PUDU D7 industrial-grade semi-humanoid robot making its first offline public appearance. The system features a physical VLM module, Physics Intuition Model (PIM), and action-expert modules enabling cross-form skill migration. Moore Threads presented its three "AI Factory" deployments — Model Training Factory, Token Production Factory, and Agent Production Factory — along with the MTT C256 supernode achieving 128-card full interconnect in a standard single-width cabinet.

The Year of Embodied Data: 100x Growth Creates New Unicorns

Another emerging trend at WAIC 2026 is the significantly increased presence of upstream data collection companies. Lightwheel AI (a physical AI data and evaluation infrastructure company), exhibiting for the first time, has exceeded a ¥15 billion valuation in 2026, becoming the world's first embodied data独角兽 (unicorn). Qianjue Robotics showcased X-TouchMind V1, the industry's first embodied haptic model, along with a full-hand tactile solution featuring fingertip touch sensors and electronic skin modules for real-time contact position, force magnitude, and slip detection.

Industry estimates suggest that data and evaluation demands in embodied AI have grown exponentially this year, with overall scale reaching 100-1,000 times last year's level, jumping from thousands of hours to millions of hours. Data collection, simulation, and evaluation infrastructure are becoming the true differentiators in model capability.

From AI chips to foundation models, from data acquisition to commercial deployment, WAIC 2026 sends a clear signal: China's embodied AI industry has fully transitioned from technology validation into大规模 (large-scale) commercial deployment. As one exhibitor put it: "They won't completely replace people, but they can free many workers from dangerous environments and help unlock more of human potential."
