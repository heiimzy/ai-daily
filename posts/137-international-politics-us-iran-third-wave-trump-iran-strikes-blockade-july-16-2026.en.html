⚡ July 16 International Politics: US Launches Third Wave of Airstrikes on Iran in 24 Hours, Trump Threatens Power Plants, IMF Warns of Energy Crisis

The military conflict between the United States and Iran escalated dramatically on Wednesday, July 15, into Thursday, July 16. US forces launched a third wave of airstrikes in 24 hours — their first daylight strikes in the current phase — while reinstating a full naval blockade of Iranian ports. President Trump threatened to bomb Iranian power plants and bridges next week if Tehran does not return to negotiations. The International Monetary Fund warned that the prolonged closure of the Strait of Hormuz poses a severe threat to global energy supplies and economic growth.

Three Waves of Strikes in 24 Hours, First Daylight Assault

US Central Command (CENTCOM) confirmed a third wave of airstrikes against Iran on Wednesday. The first wave was completed at 2:00 a.m. GMT, the second at 11:30 a.m. GMT, and the third was launched at 7:00 p.m. local time — marking the first time US forces conducted large-scale daylight strikes in the current phase of the conflict.

CENTCOM stated on X that the strikes target Iranian military capabilities used to threaten vessels freely transiting through the Strait of Hormuz, an international waterway vital to global commerce. A 90-minute precision strike operation destroyed Iranian coastal defense systems and cruise missile storage and launch sites on Greater Tunb Island.

Iranian state media reported explosions in the southern cities of Ahvaz and Chabahar, as well as in Bandar Abbas, Bushehr, and Qeshm Island. Iran's army said 13 US missiles struck Bambour Garrison in Iranshahr, killing seven soldiers from the 388th Brigade and wounding several others.

Trump: Power Plants and Bridges Next Week, IRGC May Be 'Wiped Out'

In a Fox News interview, President Trump vowed to escalate the campaign significantly. We're going to hit them very hard tonight. We're going to hit them very hard tomorrow night. We're going to hit them very hard the night after. And then next week, it gets really bad for them, because next week comes the power plants, next week comes the bridges, he said.

When asked by Fox Business whether he might wipe out the Islamic Revolutionary Guard Corps (IRGC) like ISIS, Trump replied: Yeah, it does. We will see what's happening. He also revealed that Iran had reached out for talks, saying They want to meet. They always want to meet.

Trump told reporters aboard Air Force One: I don't like giving deadlines, but they pretty much know. They know the story. They'd better behave.

IRGC Strikes Back: US Bases Hit in Bahrain, Kuwait, and Jordan

In retaliation for the US airstrikes, the IRGC announced the third phase of Operation Nasr-2, launching coordinated missile and drone attacks on US military facilities across the region:

  • Bahrain: The IRGC claimed to have struck the US Fifth Fleet headquarters, fuel depots, and military equipment warehouses in Juffair. Air raid sirens sounded across the kingdom as Bahrain's defense forces intercepted incoming aerial attacks.
  • Kuwait: The main US logistics and support center at Mina Abdullah was targeted. Kuwait's military confirmed intercepting 1 ballistic missile, 5 cruise missiles, and 33 drones launched from Iran, with four soldiers wounded.
  • Jordan: The IRGC launched a second attack on Azraq Air Base, which hosts US F-15, F-16, and F-35 fighter jets. Jordan's military said it intercepted and shot down three Iranian ballistic missiles.

Full Blockade on Hormuz, Tanker Struck

The US formally reinstated its naval blockade of Iranian ports on Tuesday. CENTCOM reported that two commercial vessels attempting to breach the blockade had been intercepted. The US military also struck an unladen oil tanker attempting to sail toward an Iranian port, saying it had ignored multiple warnings in violation of the blockade.

The IRGC countered by declaring the Strait of Hormuz remains closed to all vessels, firing warning shots at two commercial ships attempting to transit what Iran considers its territorial waters. The IRGC further warned it could close all other energy export corridors that benefit the United States and its allies, stating: Regional energy exports are either shared by all, or denied to all.

According to shipping intelligence firm Kpler, just 22 ships crossed the strait on July 9, compared to 147 crossings daily before the war — an 85% collapse in traffic.

IMF Warns of Global Economic Impact

Christian Mumssen, director of strategy at the IMF, warned that a prolonged disruption to energy supplies would clearly affect the global economic outlook. Many countries face severe pressure from surging energy prices.

Brent crude closed at $86.19 per barrel on Wednesday, up 15% in a week, the highest since mid-June. US WTI crude rose to $80.40 per barrel. Rising gasoline prices in the US have become a politically sensitive issue ahead of the November midterm congressional elections.

The IMF had warned in June that extending the conflict beyond mid-July posed significant risks, as countries had largely depleted their strategic oil reserves to cushion the impact on consumers.

Casualties Mount

Iranian government spokesperson Fatemeh Mohajerani said at least 30 civilians had been killed in southern Iran by recent US strikes. Health Ministry spokesperson Hossein Kermanpour reported that over 260 people had been wounded in the latest overnight strikes, with 222 discharged after treatment.

US military fatalities in the Iran war now stand at 14, the latest being a Navy pilot killed in a helicopter crash over the Arabian Sea in early July.

Lebanon-Israel Talks in Rome Yield Progress

Under US mediation, Lebanon and Israel concluded two days of face-to-face talks in Rome — their sixth round of negotiations since the war expanded in March. A US official described the talks as productive and positive, with both sides agreeing on the structure and guidelines for pilot zone implementation in southern Lebanon. The framework includes Hezbollah's disarmament, Israeli withdrawal, and the deployment of Lebanese troops. A US official said the first pilot zone could launch within days.

However, major explosions were reported in the strategic southern Lebanese town of Khiam early Wednesday morning, underscoring that military hostilities continue alongside diplomatic efforts.
