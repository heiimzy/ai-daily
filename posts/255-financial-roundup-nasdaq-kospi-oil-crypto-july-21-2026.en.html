⚡ July 21 Financial Roundup: Nasdaq Tops 21,000, KOSPI Crashes 4.5% on AI Leverage Unwind, China Injects $60B

🇺🇸 Wall Street: Nasdaq Breaks 21,000 for First Time as Earnings Season Exceeds Expectations

U.S. stock indexes closed mixed on Monday, July 20 (early July 21 Beijing time), but both the Nasdaq Composite and S&P 500 hit intraday all-time highs. The Nasdaq Composite breached 21,000 for the first time in history, while the S&P 500 briefly touched a record before paring gains.

The Dow Jones Industrial Average fell 0.57% to 51,848.96, the S&P 500 edged down 0.18% to 7,444.19, and the Nasdaq Composite slipped 0.04% to 25,510.27.

85% of Companies Beat Earnings Estimates

According to FactSet, more than 85% of the 62 S&P 500 companies that have reported so far exceeded earnings expectations. FactSet VP John Butters noted that the "Magnificent Seven" tech giants are expected to post 14% year-over-year earnings growth in Q2, far outpacing the 3.4% average for the other 493 S&P 500 companies. Goldman Sachs raised its 12-month S&P 500 target to 6,900, citing a weakening dollar that could boost earnings by 2-3%.

Verizon Communications surged more than 4% after beating Q2 profit estimates and raising its full-year guidance. Block (formerly Square) jumped 7% ahead of its S&P 500 inclusion on July 23. Alphabet gained over 2% on reports of its Gemini-integrated server chip.

Treasury Secretary Bessent: Fed Should Cut If Inflation Cools

Treasury Secretary Scott Bessent said Monday that if inflation data comes in low, the Fed should cut interest rates, which would unlock the mortgage market. Barclays strategists said the odds of Fed Chair Powell being fired remain very low. Markets are now focused on this week's economic data, including June existing home sales on Wednesday and weekly jobless claims on Thursday.

🇰🇷 South Korea: KOSPI Plunges 4.5% as AI Leverage Unravels

South Korea's stock market rout deepened on Monday, with the KOSPI falling another 4.5% toward the 6,250 support level, breaking below its 100-day moving average. The KOSDAQ index crashed more than 5%. The selloff is now widely recognized as a textbook case of how leverage-fueled rallies can reverse violently.

Options Imbalance Triggers Mechanical Selling

JPMorgan data shows that SK Hynix's call/put ratio had hit an extreme 27x, making it the poster child of crowded AI trades. Goldman Sachs estimates that every 5% move in the index triggers approximately $4.7 billion in market-maker rebalancing — roughly 13% of daily KOSPI turnover. For SK Hynix and Samsung Electronics, the rebalancing impact exceeds 20% of their respective daily volumes.

Goldman characterizes this not as a structural breakdown of the AI investment thesis, but as a liquidity-driven position reset. Long-term fundamentals like data center capex remain intact, but mechanical selling pressure must fully dissipate before stability returns.

Retail investors have been hit hardest. The 2x leveraged SK Hynix ETF has fallen back to late-April levels, requiring a 250% gain to break even for those who bought near the top — a stark reminder of the compounding decay inherent in leveraged products during volatile markets.

"When a rally is fueled by leverage and derivatives, the reversal is amplified by the same forces in the opposite direction." — Wall Street CN

🇨🇳 China: Regulator Pledges Market Stability as State Funds Deploy Over $60 Billion

China's securities regulator, CSRC Chairman Wu Qing, convened an investor symposium on July 20, vowing to "fully maintain stable market operations." Three major positive signals emerged in market liquidity.

State-Owned Giants Inject $60B+

China Guoxin announced that its investment arm had deployed over 500 billion yuan (~$70 billion) in share buybacks and market stabilization. China Chengtong simultaneously announced large-scale purchases of Chinese equities totaling nearly 10 billion yuan. Multiple state-owned companies — including Aluminum Corp of China, CRRC, China Coal Energy, SDIC Power, and China Three Gorges — announced controlling shareholder buyback plans.

Fund Self-Purchases and Record ETF Inflows

Boshi Fund announced a 50 million yuan self-purchase of its own equity funds, the first mutual fund to do so in July. Private fund data shows six firms announced 412 million yuan in self-purchases since the start of July. On July 20, three major broad-based ETFs (KCB50, ChiNext, CSI 300) saw combined turnover exceeding 60 billion yuan. Last week (July 13-17), equity ETFs saw net inflows exceeding 200 billion yuan.

Citi upgraded China to "overweight." Multiple insurance giants — Ping An, PICC, CPIC — jointly announced they would continue increasing equity and ETF allocations. China's Shanghai Composite closed +0.85% on July 20, while Hong Kong's Hang Seng Index surged 2.36%.

🛢️ Oil: Brent Returns Above $90 as Houthis Blockade Saudi Arabia

Geopolitical tensions in the Middle East continued to escalate, pushing oil prices sharply higher. Brent crude futures gained 2.69% to $90.47/barrel, while WTI crude rose 2.51% to $83.83/barrel.

Yemen's Iran-aligned Houthi rebels announced a "navigation ban" on Saudi Arabia on July 20, opening a new front in the US-Iran conflict. A senior Iranian official indicated mediators have proposed a 10-day ceasefire to de-escalate tensions. Analysts note that normalization of Strait of Hormuz shipping will take time, keeping oil prices elevated in the near term.

₿ Crypto: Trump Signs GENIUS Act Stablecoin Bill, Ether Hits 2026 High

President Trump signed the GENIUS Act stablecoin regulation bill into law on Friday, marking a major legislative victory for the crypto industry. The bill passed the House 308-122 and prohibits interest or yield payments on regulated stablecoins.

Ether surged to $3,795, hitting a 2026 high, as Deutsche Bank analysts noted the stablecoin interest ban is driving capital toward ETH. Solana also rallied sharply, with the ProShares Ultra Solana ETF gaining 17.2%. Bitcoin edged up 0.4%, trading in the $64,000-$66,000 range, still 3% below its all-time high of $123,153 set last week.

🇯🇵 Japan: Political Turmoil Weakens Yen as Coalition Loses Upper House Majority

Japan's ruling coalition lost its upper house majority in weekend elections, further weakening PM Shigeru Ishiba's grip on power. The yen weakened to near 40-year lows, with USD/JPY approaching 163. Japan's Finance Minister warned of "bold action against excessive speculative moves," though markets remain skeptical about intervention effectiveness given past ¥7+ trillion spend.
