⚡ July 15 Financial Roundup: US PPI Unexpectedly Falls 0.3%, Morgan Stanley Posts Record Q2 with EPS of $3.46, Apple Intelligence Approved in China with Alibaba Qwen, Bank of Canada Holds Steady

1. US June PPI Falls 0.3% MoM — First Decline Since August 2025

The U.S. Bureau of Labor Statistics reported Wednesday that the Producer Price Index (PPI) for final demand declined 0.3% in June, seasonally adjusted — well below the unchanged reading economists had expected and marking the largest monthly drop since August 2025. The report follows Tuesday's better-than-expected CPI data, giving inflation watchers two consecutive days of encouraging news.

Core PPI (excluding food, energy, and trade services) edged up just 0.1% month-over-month, also below the 0.3% consensus estimate. On an unadjusted 12-month basis, final demand PPI rose 5.5%, cooling from 6.0% in May. Core PPI stood at 5.1% year-over-year, unchanged from the prior month.

Breaking down the components, final demand goods prices plunged 1.4% — the steepest drop since the pandemic era — driven by a 6.4% collapse in energy prices and a 0.6% decline in food costs. Final demand services edged up 0.2%, with trade services down 0.1% and transportation/warehousing up 0.4%.

"The Fed's war with inflation isn't over by any means, as Fed Chair Warsh made plain in yesterday's testimony, but there is good news from the front: inflation at the factory level is trending lower, and producers will not be passing on their higher costs to the consumer level as much as we previously thought." — Chris Rupkey, Chief Economist at FWDBONDS

Treasury yields edged lower after the data. The 2-year yield fell more than 2 basis points to 4.166%, while the 10-year yield slipped to 4.581%. Rate futures maintained a ~15% probability of a July rate hike, while September hike odds receded from ~65% to around 48%.

2. Morgan Stanley Posts Record Q2: EPS of $3.46, Equities Trading Surges 69%

Morgan Stanley became the latest major bank to deliver blowout quarterly results on Wednesday, posting record revenue and profit for the second quarter. The Wall Street bank joins Goldman Sachs and JPMorgan Chase in a standout earnings season for the financial sector.

Key metrics:

  • Earnings per share: $3.46 (LSEG estimate $2.94)
  • Total revenue: $21.35 billion (estimate $19.64 billion)
  • Net income: $5.58 billion, up 58% year-over-year
  • Equities trading revenue: $6.3 billion (record), up 69% YoY
  • Fixed income trading: $2.46 billion, up 13%
  • Investment banking: $2.44 billion, up 58%
  • Wealth management: $8.86 billion, up 14%

"Active markets and consistent execution across all three regions drove exceptional results for our integrated firm," CEO Ted Pick said in the release. Analysts highlighted that equities trading revenue beat StreetAccount estimates by roughly $1.9 billion, with "notable strength in Asia" as the global AI trade spreads worldwide. Morgan Stanley shares rose approximately 1% in premarket trading.

3. Apple Intelligence Gets Green Light in China, Alibaba's Qwen to Power AI Features

China's Cyberspace Administration (CAC) on Wednesday announced that "Apple Intelligence" has passed the country's generative AI service filing, clearing the way for Apple's AI features to launch in mainland China. Alibaba immediately confirmed that its Tongyi Qianwen (Qwen) large language model will be integrated into Apple Intelligence to provide AI capabilities for iOS, iPadOS, macOS, and visionOS users in China.

The news sent Alibaba's U.S.-listed shares surging as much as 6% in premarket trading to $119.30, before settling around 4.32% higher at $117.17. The partnership is a landmark development for China's AI ecosystem. Alibaba's Qwen series is the world's most-downloaded open-source AI model family and consistently ranks at the top of Hugging Face's open-source leaderboards.

Alibaba is simultaneously executing a three-year, 380 billion yuan ($53 billion) AI infrastructure investment plan. The company also announced a separate partnership with Honor to jointly develop an "Agentic OS," with details expected at the World AI Conference (WAIC) opening in Shanghai on July 17 — China's largest AI event featuring over 1,100 companies.

4. Bank of Canada Preview: Expected to Hold at 2.25% for Sixth Straight Meeting

The Bank of Canada announces its interest rate decision at 9:45 AM ET (13:45 GMT) Wednesday, with markets universally expecting a hold at 2.25% — the sixth consecutive pause. Governor Tiff Macklem's press conference and the updated Monetary Policy Report follow at 10:30 AM ET.

Key points to watch:

  • Rate path: All 36 economists in a Reuters poll expect no change. 19 of 30 see rates on hold until at least July 2027.
  • Inflation outlook: May CPI hit 3.2% YoY, but falling oil prices have eased some pressure. Macklem previously noted little pass-through from energy to core prices so far.
  • MPR update: The neutral rate range remains 2.25%-3.25%. Headline CPI projections may be revised up, but core estimates likely unchanged.
  • CAD impact: USD/CAD has fallen toward 1.40, supported by elevated oil prices. A hawkish surprise could strengthen the loonie further.

ING's Francesco Pesole noted the BoC has little incentive to shift policy today, but the December 2026 contract already prices 20bp of tightening. If oil stays elevated and inflation expectations become unanchored, a year-end hike remains on the table.

5. Futures Rally as Markets Digest PPI Data; Geopolitical Risks Linger

U.S. stock index futures edged higher Wednesday as traders absorbed the latest inflation data. As of 8:00 AM ET, Nasdaq 100 futures rose 0.44% to 29,892, S&P 500 futures gained 0.13% to 7,604, and Dow futures were flat to slightly positive.

Asian markets continued their rebound. Japan's Nikkei 225 gained 1.14%, Hong Kong's Hang Seng rose 1.46%, while China's CSI 300 was flat as weak Q2 GDP data (4.3% YoY, missing 4.5% estimates) tempered sentiment. South Korea's KOSPI consolidated after yesterday's 7% surge.

In commodities, Brent crude held above $85/barrel and WTI traded near $79 as U.S.-Iran hostilities continued for a fourth consecutive night. Gold oscillated around $4,050/oz. Bitcoin maintained above $64,500.

The packed earnings calendar continues with reports from Johnson & Johnson, BlackRock, and United Airlines later Wednesday. The next major Fed event is the July 28-29 FOMC meeting.
