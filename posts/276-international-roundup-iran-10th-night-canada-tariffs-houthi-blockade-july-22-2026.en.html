⚡ July 22 International Politics: US Strikes Iran for 10th Consecutive Night, Iran Retaliates Against Kuwait and Bahrain Bases; Trump Slaps 50% Tariffs on Canada; Houthis Blockade Saudi Arabia Threatening 7% of Global Oil Supply

I. US-Iran Conflict: 10th Night of Strikes — CENTCOM Bombs Iranian Heartland, Iran Strikes Back at Three Nations

The US-Iran conflict entered its 10th consecutive night of aerial exchanges. US Central Command (CENTCOM) confirmed it completed the 10th round of strikes on Iran on July 21, targeting military command centers, missile and drone launch sites, air defense systems, and maritime capabilities used to threaten commercial shipping through the Strait of Hormuz.

Iranian media reported explosions across multiple southern provinces — Sirik and Bandar Abbas in Hormozgan Province, Shiraz in Fars Province, Bushehr, and Chabahar and Konarak in Sistan and Baluchestan Province. The governor of Bushehr confirmed air defense systems had intercepted incoming threats with no reported damage.

"These strikes are designed to further degrade Iranian military capabilities used to attack commercial shipping in the Strait of Hormuz." — US Central Command statement

In retaliation, Iran's Islamic Revolutionary Guard Corps (IRGC) announced the 18th phase of Operation Saeqeh, launching surface-to-surface missiles at US HIMARS systems stationed at Camp Arifjan in Kuwait. The IRGC also claimed to have destroyed a Patriot missile battery in Riffa, Bahrain, along with radar installations and an MQ-9 Reaper drone hangar at Ali Al Salem Air Base in Kuwait.

Three Fallen US Soldiers Identified

The Pentagon released the identities of two soldiers killed in the Jordan attack over the weekend: 25-year-old First Lieutenant Tyler James Feehan of Hawaii and 19-year-old Private Isabella Gonzales of Texas. A third service member was killed in northern Iraq while conducting a controlled detonation of unexploded ordnance from an Iranian drone. Total US military fatalities in the conflict have now reached 17.

The New York Times reported that the Pentagon had failed to disclose nearly 100 injuries to US troops since July 7, the vast majority classified as mild concussions. President Trump took to Truth Social, vowing: "Every time Iran kills an American soldier, they will pay for that killing many times over!"

Oil Tankers Attacked in Strait of Hormuz

Iran claimed to have intercepted two "non-compliant oil tankers" attempting to transit the southern route of the Strait of Hormuz, with both vessels catching fire and forced to halt. The United Kingdom Maritime Trade Operations (UKMTO) confirmed a commercial vessel had been struck by an unidentified projectile off the coast of Oman, with the crew safely evacuated. Shipping data shows daily transit volume through the Strait has collapsed from pre-war levels to single digits.

II. Trump Invokes 1930 Tariff Act to Impose 50% Tariffs on Canada, US-Mexico Bilateral Talks Begin

President Donald Trump signed three executive orders on July 20 imposing 50% tariffs on approximately $20 billion worth of Canadian goods, invoking Section 338 of the Tariff Act of 1930 — an obscure legal provision unused for nearly a century. The move follows the Supreme Court's February ruling striking down Trump's previous national emergency tariff authority.

Affected products include wine, hockey sticks, cement, dairy products, plywood, paper, and furniture. Energy products, potash, fish, and critical minerals are exempt. The tariffs take effect in 30 days, leaving a window for negotiation.

"Canada has to be held accountable for this continued discrimination. U.S. exports have been harmed." — Senior Trump administration official

The White House stated that Canada is one of only two countries (along with China) that retaliated against Trump's previous tariffs. Canada imposed 25% tariffs on US motor vehicles, reduced US alcohol purchases by 81%, and maintained high dairy tariffs, which the administration cited as discriminatory.

US-Mexico Bilateral Talks Begin, Canada Sidelined

US and Mexican trade negotiators began their third round of bilateral talks in Mexico City on July 21 — without Canada. US Trade Representative Jamieson Greer praised Mexico's "pragmatic" approach and lack of retaliation, while Mexico seeks relief from 25% US tariffs on autos and 50% tariffs on steel and aluminum.

The US declined to renew the USMCA on July 1, triggering a 10-year annual review period. Greer stated that formal negotiations with Canada have not yet begun, as Ottawa has not provided the policy changes Washington is seeking. Canadian Prime Minister Mark Carney said his government has submitted comprehensive proposals but expressed frustration at being sidelined.

III. Houthis Declare Full Naval Blockade on Saudi Arabia, Global Energy Faces New Threat

Yemen's Houthi movement declared a comprehensive naval blockade on Saudi Arabia on July 21, emailing shipping companies to avoid Saudi ports. The announcement sent shockwaves through global energy markets — the Bab el-Mandeb strait, linking the Red Sea to the Gulf of Aden, handles approximately 7% of global oil supply.

Combined with Iran's effective closure of the Strait of Hormuz (which handled ~20% of global oil and gas transit before the war), both of the Middle East's major energy chokepoints are now under threat. Approximately 25% of global oil and gas transportation faces potential disruption. War risk insurance premiums doubled following the Houthi announcement.

The Houthi move is retaliation for Saudi-led coalition airstrikes on Sanaa airport and what the group calls Riyadh's blockade of Yemeni ports and airports serving Israel. The Houthis have repeatedly fired missiles and drones at Israel and targeted Red Sea shipping throughout the conflict. Analysts warn the campaign could threaten Israel's maritime access and complicate its naval operations.

IV. Netanyahu Pushes Trump to Strike Iran's Underground Nuclear Site

According to The Wall Street Journal, Israeli Prime Minister Benjamin Netanyahu has provided new intelligence to Washington suggesting Iran has moved thousands of uranium enrichment centrifuges to the deeply buried Pickaxe Mountain complex near Natanz. The tunnel network, buried approximately 140 meters deep in solid granite, is beyond the reach of most conventional airstrikes.

Trump stated in a July 13 interview with Hugh Hewitt: "We're going to take out Pickaxe Mountain. Tell the Iranians to be ready." He reiterated to reporters this week that the US would strike the area "pretty soon" and "very heavily." Nuclear experts note that even the US military's 30,000-pound GBU-57 Massive Ordnance Penetrator may face challenges against the granite-protected facility.

Israel has expressed concern that despite 10 nights of US airstrikes, nuclear-related work has not been adequately targeted. IAEA Director General Rafael Grossi confirmed that Iran continues to deny inspectors access to the Pickaxe Mountain site.

V. Other International Developments

US State Department Issues Global Security Alert

The State Department issued a "worldwide caution" alert on July 21 urging American citizens globally to "exercise increased caution" due to "heightened tensions in West Asia" and "the potential for unforeseen escalation." The alert advised reconsidering travel to and through the Middle East.

New UK Prime Minister Takes Office

Andy Burnham, former Mayor of Manchester, officially succeeded Keir Starmer as the UK's seventh prime minister in a decade. Analysts expect Burnham to continue moving toward closer EU ties while navigating the complex US-UK relationship amid the Iran war.

Russia-Ukraine: 400 Ukrainian Drones Strike Moscow

Ukraine launched over 400 drones in a massive overnight attack on 19 Russian regions, causing fires at multiple oil depots and logistics centers around Moscow. Russia retaliated with approximately 40 ballistic missiles and 125 drones against Kyiv. India's Foreign Ministry confirmed that a Russian missile struck a cargo ship leaving Odesa, killing four Indian crew members.
