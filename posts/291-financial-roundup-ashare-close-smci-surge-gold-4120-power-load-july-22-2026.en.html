⚡ July 22 Financial Close: ChiNext Crashes 3.23%, Gold Breaks $4,120, SMCI Explodes 18% After Hours

Global financial markets saw sharp divergence on July 22 amid a complex mix of geopolitical tensions, earnings expectations, and sector rotation. China's A-shares closed with steep losses on the tech-heavy ChiNext board, while safe-haven gold surged past $4,120. U.S. futures edged lower but Super Micro Computer (SMCI) soared 18% after hours on record backlog figures. Here are the day's key financial developments.

China A-Shares Close Mixed: ChiNext Plunges 3.23%, Gold & Power Sectors Shine

China's A-share market closed with sharp divergence on Wednesday. The Shanghai Composite Index inched up 0.07% to 3,867.03, while the Shenzhen Component Index fell 1.42% to 14,061.44. The ChiNext (Growth Enterprise Index) plunged 3.23% to 3,566.73, and the STAR 50 dropped 2.26%. Total turnover reached 2.67 trillion yuan ($393 billion), with over 3,800 stocks declining.

Sector performance was heavily skewed toward defensive and resource stocks:

  • Precious metals led the rally: Shanjin International and Zhaojin Mining hit daily limits up. Chifeng Gold surged over 7%, with Zijin Mining following. Spot gold broke above $4,120/oz intraday, fueling the sector.
  • Oil & gas stocks gained: Zhongman Petroleum hit the daily limit. CNOOC rose alongside oil service names. Brent crude topped $92.66/barrel, a high since June 11; WTI reached $85.75.
  • Coal stocks advanced: Dayou Energy and Huadian Energy hit limits. Yanzhou Coal and Luan Environmental rose.
  • Power sector active: Jiuzhou Group surged 11%, and several power utilities hit daily limits including Huadian Liaoning and Huayin Electric.
  • Baijiu (white liquor) stocks edged up: Shede Spirits and Yingjia Distillery gained over 4%.

On the downside, PET copper foil stocks continued to slide, with Tongguan Copper Foil dropping over 11%. Film and entertainment stocks weakened. Robotics, gaming, and battery sectors all underperformed. Memory chip concept stocks reversed lower.

Market Outlook

CITIC Securities noted that the July pullback was technically driven by capital flows and positioning, not fundamental deterioration. State-backed funds and major insurers have signaled market entry, with broad-based ETFs seeing massive inflows. Analysts believe the correction is largely complete, potentially setting the stage for a new recovery cycle.

U.S. Pre-Market: Futures Dip, SMCI Soars 18% After Hours on Record $60B Backlog

U.S. stocks closed sharply higher on Tuesday, July 21, led by a powerful semiconductor rebound. The Dow Jones Industrial Average rose 385.38 points (+0.74%) to 52,224.64. The S&P 500 gained 0.89% to 7,509.20, and the Nasdaq Composite surged 1.29% to 25,837.21, snapping a three-day losing streak.

Key movers included:

  • Super Micro Computer (SMCI) surged approximately 18% in after-hours trading after disclosing preliminary results showing record backlog exceeding $60 billion.
  • Micron Technology jumped 12.2%, and NVIDIA rose 2% — together providing the strongest lift to the S&P 500.
  • AMD rose 8.11%, Applied Materials climbed 7.39%, and the VanEck Semiconductor ETF (SMH) surged 4.52%.

This week marks a critical earnings deluge: Alphabet (Google) and Tesla report Q2 results Wednesday after the bell, followed by Intel and IBM later in the week. Of the roughly 66 S&P 500 companies that have reported, nearly 88% beat earnings expectations. However, analysts caution that expectations have been significantly raised, and merely delivering decent results may not satisfy investors.

In overnight trading, U.S. stock futures edged lower. Nasdaq-100 futures fell 0.21%, S&P 500 futures slipped 0.11%, and Dow futures declined 0.10%. Rising oil prices continue to weigh on sentiment, with Brent crude climbing to a five-week high above $92/barrel.

Gold Surges Past $4,120 as Safe Havens Rally

Amid escalating geopolitical tensions, spot gold broke above $4,120 per ounce on Wednesday, gaining over 1% intraday. Spot silver held above $59/oz, gaining 0.43%. COMEX gold futures advanced 1.69% to $4,083.70, while COMEX silver futures surged 4.23% to $59.065.

Key drivers behind the gold rally:

  • U.S.-Iran conflict escalation: The U.S. completed its 11th consecutive night of strikes on Iran, with war costs reaching $37.5 billion. Trump reiterated threats to strike Iran's underground nuclear facility.
  • Red Sea blockade: Three Saudi crude tankers reversed course in the Red Sea after Houthi threats. Bab el-Mandeb Strait traffic dropped 34% in two weeks.
  • Federal Reserve outlook: A Reuters poll found most economists see a high probability of a Fed rate hike this year, though safe-haven demand currently outweighs rate concerns.
  • Technical buying: Gold's break above its short-term downtrend triggered short covering and fresh longs. The 4,080-4,100 zone has become the new battleground.

Analysts note that this rally is driven not by the traditional "geopolitical escalation → safe-haven" narrative, but rather by the reverse — "potential ceasefire → lower oil pressure → easing rate hike expectations" logic chain. A 10-day ceasefire proposal, if accepted, could further support gold by reducing energy-driven inflation concerns.

China's Power Load Hits Record High, Power Stocks Active

National Energy Administration data shows that China's power load hit an all-time high on July 10, reaching 1.518 billion kilowatts — 10 million kW above the previous record. Since the start of summer, power grids in the southern region and multiple provincial grids (Guangdong, Guangxi, Hainan, Ningxia, Gansu, Fujian, Shaanxi) have set over 20 new records cumulatively.

A-share power stocks were notably active on Wednesday. Jiuzhou Group surged over 11%, and multiple power utilities hit daily trading limits. Analysts highlight that sustained high temperatures driving electricity demand, combined with structural shifts from rising renewable energy penetration, make the power sector attractive for long-term allocation.

Separately, the Ministry of Transport announced the continuation of the old truck scrappage and replacement program for 2026, with subsidies for qualifying new energy trucks — reinforcing China's energy transition policy momentum.

Global Markets Snapshot

  • South Korea KOSPI: Closed up 3.56% at 6,747.95. Samsung Electronics surged 6.15%, SK Hynix gained 4.08%. Foreign and institutional net buying reached 1.67 trillion won ($1.13B).
  • Japan Nikkei 225: Rose 3.3% to 66,232, as the yen broke below 163 per dollar — the weakest level since 1986.
  • European markets: Stoxx 600 edged higher. UK's FTSE 100 added 0.6% as new PM Burnham chaired his first cabinet meeting.
  • Treasury yields: 10-year U.S. yield rose to 4.63% (two-month high), 30-year at 5.14%.
  • Bitcoin: Held near $66,300, consolidating gains with limited intraday volatility.

Overall, global markets remain caught between "geopolitical premium" and "earnings recovery" forces. Oil price trajectory, Fed policy expectations, and Big Tech earnings will be the key variables determining market direction in the coming weeks.
