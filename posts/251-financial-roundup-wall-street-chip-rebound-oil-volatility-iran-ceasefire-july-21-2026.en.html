⚡ July 21 Financial Roundup: Chipmakers Power Nasdaq's 200-Point Rally, Brent Crude's $91 Spike Reverses on Iran Ceasefire Hopes, China A-Shares Hit Year Highs

1. Wall Street Close: Chip Stocks Lead Recovery as Nasdaq Climbs 200 Points

U.S. stock indexes closed mixed on Monday (July 20) as a powerful rebound in semiconductor shares propelled the Nasdaq and S&P 500 to fresh all-time highs, while the Dow edged lower on geopolitical uncertainty and energy sector weakness.

The Dow Jones Industrial Average fell 128.52 points, or 0.25%, to 52,017.90. The S&P 500 gained 28.66 points, or 0.38%, to 7,486.35. The Nasdaq Composite surged 201.90 points, or 0.79%, to 25,722.14.

The chip rebound was the day's defining theme. The VanEck Semiconductor ETF (SMH) climbed over 2%, while the Philadelphia SE Semiconductor Index rose 1.9% — though it remains more than 20% below its late-June record high, technically in bear market territory. Micron Technology led the charge with a 5%+ gain, SanDisk surged 6.30%, Western Digital added 4.90%, and AMD rose over 3%.

Analysts attributed the rebound to dip-buying after last week's sharp selloff, which was driven by concerns over AI spending returns. LSEG data shows S&P 500 Q2 earnings growth is expected at 26% year-over-year, up from an earlier estimate of 23.7%.

2. Oil Markets: Brent Hits $91 Then Plunges as Iran Signals Ceasefire

Crude markets experienced a dramatic swing on Monday. Brent crude September futures surged 3.8% to an intraday high of $91.42 per barrel — a one-month peak — before reversing sharply to close 1.6% lower at $86.67. WTI crude followed a similar pattern, retreating from an $86.40 high to below $80.

The catalyst for the reversal came from Tehran. Shortly after midnight Monday, Iranian Foreign Ministry spokesman Esmaeil Baghai signaled openness to diplomatic resolution, stating that intermediaries continue to exchange information with Iran and that negotiations could proceed in accordance with national interests. According to Iranian sources, a proposal for a 10-day ceasefire has been put forward to revive last month's provisional peace agreement.

The price volatility unfolded against the backdrop of the ninth consecutive night of U.S. airstrikes on Iran. CENTCOM stated the strikes "are designed to further degrade military capabilities Iranian forces have used to attack commercial shipping in the Strait of Hormuz." Daniela Hathorn, senior market analyst at Capital.com, noted: "Traders are cutting some of the geopolitical risk premium that had been priced in recently. While the conflict is far from resolved, the prospect of renewed talks has eased immediate concerns about further oil supply disruptions."

Brent crude remains more than $16 above the lows hit in June when the US and Iran signed a Memorandum of Understanding to reopen the Strait of Hormuz, indicating the geopolitical risk premium remains substantial.

3. Key Corporate News: Alphabet Developing Gemini-Integrated Server Chip

Alphabet shares rose 2.9% Monday following a report that its Google unit is developing a custom server chip deeply integrated with the Gemini AI model, aimed at improving AI computing efficiency and alleviating capacity constraints. The news amplified the positive sentiment in the semiconductor sector.

The second-quarter earnings season reaches a crescendo this week, with Alphabet and Tesla reporting after Wednesday's close, followed by Intel and IBM. Markets will closely scrutinize these tech giants' AI capital expenditure plans and profit outlooks.

4. China A-Shares: All Three Major Indices Hit Year Highs on 1.7 Trillion Yuan Turnover

China's A-share market rallied across the board on Tuesday (July 21), with the Shanghai Composite and ChiNext both hitting fresh year-to-date highs. The Shanghai Composite gained 0.72% to close at 3,559.79, the Shenzhen Component rose 0.86% to 11,007.49, and the ChiNext added 0.87% to 2,296.88. More than 4,000 stocks advanced, with total turnover reaching 1.70 trillion yuan, significantly up from the previous session.

By sector, building materials (+6.06%), construction and decoration (+3.79%), and steel (+3.44%) led the gains. Hydropower concept stocks saw a wave of limit-up moves, with the Yalong River hydropower concept surging over 13%. Banking (-0.77%) and computer (-0.31%) sectors underperformed.

Capital flow data showed continued divergence. AI concept stocks, big data, and computing power sectors saw significant capital outflows, with AI concepts alone experiencing net outflows of 11.2 billion yuan. In contrast, the Belt and Road Initiative, state-owned enterprise reform, and wind energy concepts attracted capital inflows.

Bosera Fund's macro outlook noted that current A-share valuations have adjusted to reasonably low levels, with recent weakness mainly driven by South Korea's aggressive deleveraging and volatile overseas industry expectations. ETF funds have seen substantial inflows at key support levels — the highest since April 2025.

5. Other Notable Market Developments

SpaceX Stock Nears All-Time Low

SpaceX (SPCX) fell another 1.10% to $122.62 on Monday, approaching its all-time low of $122.12. The stock has now dropped nearly 23% since its record-breaking June IPO. According to IBTimes, investor concerns center on SpaceX's enormous capital requirements — an estimated $84 billion annually from 2027 — and continued Starship launch delays. Despite this, 27 of 28 analysts maintain a Buy rating, with a 12-month average price target of approximately $240.

Japan Political Turmoil: Ruling Coalition Loses Upper House Majority

Prime Minister Shigeru Ishiba's ruling coalition lost its majority in Sunday's upper house elections, sending the yen higher on Monday. USD/JPY traded in a narrow 162.00–162.80 range as markets had partially priced in worst-case scenarios. The political uncertainty compounds pressure from the Trump administration's August 1 tariff deadline, which could impose 30% tariffs on Japanese exports.

US Economic Data: Leading Indicators Edge Lower

The Conference Board's Leading Economic Index (LEI) slipped 0.2% to 99.1 in June, dragged by weakening consumer spending. However, the index declined only 0.3% in the first half of 2026 — a stark improvement from the 1.1% contraction in H2 2025. Strong corporate AI investment is expected to support economic activity amid improving inflation conditions.

Cryptocurrency Markets

Bitcoin held steady near $64,500, remaining more than 3% below its all-time high of $123,153. President Trump's signing of the GENIUS Act stablecoin regulation bill on Friday boosted crypto-linked stocks. Ethereum climbed to $3,795, approaching its year-to-date high.
