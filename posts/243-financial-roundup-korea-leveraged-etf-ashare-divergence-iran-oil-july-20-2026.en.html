⚡ July 20 Financial Closing Bell: South Korea Leveraged ETFs Crash 70% as Retail Nightmare Unfolds; A-Shares in Extreme Divergence; Iran Signals Possible Talks as Oil Volatility Surges

🔥 South Korea Leveraged ETF Crisis: Retail Investors Lose 14 Trillion Won in Chip Bet Meltdown

South Korean retail investors are experiencing a leveraged nightmare of historic proportions. CNBC published an in-depth report headlined "Give me my money back" Monday, detailing the catastrophic collapse of single-stock leveraged ETFs that has wiped out billions in retail wealth.

Since single-stock leveraged ETFs launched on May 27, Korean retail investors have purchased a net 14 trillion won ($9.4 billion) of them, compared with roughly 2 trillion won by foreign investors, according to KB Financial Group. But as Samsung Electronics and SK Hynix shares reversed sharply from June highs, these leveraged products have been decimated.

The KODEX SK Hynix Single Stock Leverage ETF — designed to deliver twice the daily move in SK Hynix shares — has crashed about 70% from its record high reached in June and is down roughly 50% from its debut, according to LSEG data. South Korean online trading forums were flooded with anguished posts: "I want to go back to before I started investing in stocks. Give me my money back," one investor wrote. "You're determined to kill me," said another.

Korea's central bank warned last month that leveraged stock investment by retail investors had climbed to a record high, driven primarily by margin borrowing and increasingly concentrated semiconductor positions. While the Bank of Korea said the buildup was unlikely to pose a systemic threat, it cautioned that leverage could magnify volatility during market corrections. Regulators tightened rules on Thursday, raising the minimum cash requirement to trade leveraged ETFs from 3 million won to 30 million won.

Oxford Economics data shows assets in the 25 largest leveraged Korea ETFs surged to roughly 30% of Korea-focused fund assets by June, up from about 15% at the start of 2026. The firm downgraded South Korea equities to neutral at the end of June, warning that leveraged positioning had grown significantly.

"Semis and memory is the most crowded global trade by institutional and retail positioning. It's over," said Thomas J. Hayes, chairman of Great Hill Capital. "You will see crowding out of semis and memory just as aggressively as 'crowding in' in coming months."

📉 A-Shares Close in Extreme Divergence: Shanghai Composite +0.85% but 200+ Tech Stocks Hit Limit-Down

China's A-share market experienced a rare divergence on Monday. The Shanghai Composite closed up 0.85% at 3,796.28, while the Shenzhen Component fell 0.71% to 13,610.23 and the ChiNext rose 0.42% to 3,443.10. However, more than 3,700 stocks declined across the market, with over 200 hitting the 10% limit-down — most of them in the technology sector.

Oil and coal stocks surged on the back of soaring crude prices, with CNOOC hitting the limit-up. The baijiu sector rallied as Kweichow Moutai gained nearly 6%. Meanwhile, electronic chemicals, components, and memory chip stocks collapsed across the board. CPO, PCB, and semiconductor equipment sectors led the declines. Total three-market turnover reached 2.72 trillion yuan, up 46.5 billion from the previous session.

China's state-backed "national team" continued its market stabilization operations. China Guoxin has deployed over 50 billion yuan ($7 billion) in special relending funds for share buybacks and supporting funds, while China Chengtong has purchased nearly 10 billion yuan in domestic stocks. Five state-owned enterprises including China Shenhua, CRRC, and Chalco announced buyback plans. ETF inflows hit 229 billion yuan last week, with broad-based products accounting for 156 billion yuan.

After the market close, CSRC Chairman Wu Qing issued a statement: "Investors are the foundation of the market. The CSRC will push forward with risk prevention, strengthened supervision, and high-quality development to maintain stable market operations."

🛢️ Iran Hints at Talks: Brent Crude Surges Past $91 Then Retreats to $88

Oil markets were highly volatile on Monday. Iran's Foreign Ministry spokesman Esmail Baghaei told reporters that negotiations with the US "could be pursued based on national interests," triggering a sharp pullback in crude prices. Brent crude for September delivery, which had surged past $91 in early Asian trading, retreated to around $88, erasing most of the day's gains. WTI crude also pulled back from above $84 to near $82.

The US military launched a ninth consecutive night of strikes against Iranian targets, with CENTCOM confirming strikes on military command centers, air defense and coastal surveillance sites, maritime capabilities, and missile launch sites. The UK Maritime Trade Operations agency confirmed a vessel was on fire off the coast of Oman. Iran's IRGC claimed to have mined and destroyed two tankers attempting to transit the Strait of Hormuz.

Amrita Sen, founder of Energy Aspects, warned that the substantial slowdown in shipping traffic through the Strait of Hormuz and depleted global inventories could push oil prices above $100 per barrel. "The market is still quite complacent despite the price increase we have seen," Sen told CNBC. Goldman Sachs estimates Gulf oil exports recovered to over 80% of pre-war levels after the June ceasefire, but those flows have since halted.

🏛️ Fed Rate Hike Odds Hit 61.4%, 30-Year Treasury Yield Breaches 5%

The oil-driven inflation scare is reshaping Federal Reserve expectations. The CME FedWatch Tool now prices a 61.4% probability of a September rate hike. The 30-year US Treasury yield breached the 5% threshold to reach 5.073%, the highest in recent memory.

Last week's softer CPI and PPI data had temporarily eased rate hike fears, but the oil shock has pushed markets toward a more hawkish stance. Futures now imply about 29 basis points of tightening by December. Gold is hovering around the $4,000 psychological level, with 79% of Wall Street analysts bearish on gold this week, according to the Kitco survey. HSBC has cut its 2026 gold price forecast to $4,560.

Bitcoin is trading in a narrow range between $64,000 and $65,000, with market sentiment turning cautious. Crypto Briefing notes that traders are increasingly turning to decentralized perpetual exchanges like Hyperliquid to bet on or hedge against oil price fluctuations.

Looking ahead, markets will focus on Alphabet and Tesla earnings on Wednesday, followed by the European Central Bank's interest rate decision on Thursday. With the Philadelphia Semiconductor Index confirming a bear market, tech giants' capex guidance will be critical in determining the market's near-term direction.
