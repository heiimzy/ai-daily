⚡ July 23 Financial Close: Brent Surges 4% to $97.84 as Houthis Attack Two Saudi Tankers in Red Sea; A-Share Indices Edge Higher; ECB Decision in Focus

🔥 Brent Crude Breaks $97 — Red Sea Opens New Energy Front

Brent crude futures surged 4% to $97.84 per barrel on Thursday, July 23 — the highest level since June 3 and a fifth consecutive day of gains. WTI crude climbed 3.22% to $89.63. Over the past month, Brent has skyrocketed 32.69%, marking a 41.43% year-over-year increase.

The immediate trigger: Yemen's Houthi militants attacked two Saudi oil tankers in the Red Sea. The UK Maritime Trade Operations (UKMTO) agency reported that a tanker caught fire after being struck about 70 nautical miles southwest of Al Shuqaiq, Saudi Arabia. The Houthis claimed responsibility, saying they had targeted the Saudi-flagged tankers Encelia and Layla with missiles and drones for violating their unilaterally declared maritime blockade.

This marks the first confirmed Houthi strike on oil tankers since the group announced a full naval blockade on Saudi Arabia. The Red Sea route via the Bab el-Mandeb strait connects to the Suez Canal and carries approximately 5 million barrels per day of oil — a critical alternative to the already compromised Strait of Hormuz.

"The energy market now has the dual-strait worry, with the Bab el-Mandeb Strait looking like it could join the Strait of Hormuz as a hot spot," said Tim Waterer, chief market analyst at KCM Trade.

🇺🇸 Trump Threatens Escalation: One Bridge or Power Plant Per Ship Attack

President Trump escalated rhetoric on Truth Social, vowing the US would destroy an Iranian bridge or power plant every time Tehran attacks a vessel in the Strait of Hormuz. Iran's Revolutionary Guards responded by warning that the southern route of the strait is mined and "completely closed."

The US military completed its 12th consecutive night of strikes on Iranian targets. Defense Secretary Pete Hegseth has disclosed the war cost has reached $37.5 billion. Meanwhile, Secretary of State Marco Rubio stated at the ASEAN foreign ministers' meeting that Iran has signaled willingness for talks but is "not serious."

The Brent three-month timespread widened to $9.26 per barrel — the deepest backwardation since May 22 — signaling acute near-term supply tightness. Paradoxically, EIA data showed US crude inventories rose by 2 million barrels to 411.7 million barrels in the week ended July 17, but geopolitical risks overwhelmed the bearish inventory signal.

🏛️ ECB Decision Tonight: Hawkish Hold Expected

The European Central Bank announces its July rate decision at 12:15 GMT (20:15 Beijing time), with markets pricing a greater than 99% probability of no change, keeping the deposit facility rate at 2.25%. All attention is on President Christine Lagarde's press conference at 12:45 GMT for signals on the September meeting.

The ECB raised rates by 25 basis points in June — its first hike of this cycle — in response to the Middle East energy shock. Since then, eurozone inflation has moderated somewhat, but oil prices have re-accelerated above $90. Markets are pricing in approximately 43 basis points (roughly two hikes) of total tightening for 2026, with a 73% probability of a September move.

Lagarde faces a delicate balancing act: maintaining a hawkish tone on inflation risks without over-validating market pricing. Analysts expect her to characterize the energy shock as "persistent" rather than "transitory," keeping the door open for September action. EUR/USD is trading near 1.14.
