⚡ July 15 Financial Roundup: KOSPI Surges Nearly 7% in Historic Revenge Rally, PPI Data and Warsh Day 2 Testimony in Focus, Goldman Says Chip Peak Premature, Lucid Plunges 55% Then Denies Bankruptcy, CICC Three-Way Merger Approved

🇰🇷 KOSPI Revenge Rally: 7% Surge in Biggest Single-Day Gain Ever

Asian markets staged a powerful rebound on July 15, with South Korea's KOSPI index surging 6.98% at the open to reclaim the 7,000-point level at 7,335 — the largest single-day percentage gain in the index's history. Japan's Nikkei 225 followed with a 1.36% rise to 68,666, as risk appetite returned across the Asia-Pacific region.

The catalyst: U.S. June CPI data came in at 3.5% year-over-year, well below the 3.8% expected, dramatically easing fears of an "ultra-hawkish" Fed rate hike at the July 28-29 FOMC meeting. The KOSPI had just crashed nearly 25% from its June 22 high of 9,114 to 6,800 in just 9 trading days, including an 8.9% single-day plunge on July 13 that triggered circuit breakers — making the technical rebound all the more explosive.

The KOSPI plunged from 9,114 to 6,800 in 9 sessions — a 25% crash. Then it bounced 7% in one morning. This is the largest single-day gain in the index's recorded history.

SK Hynix +9.4%, Samsung +5.9% Lead Chip Rebound

Semiconductor stocks led the charge. SK Hynix surged 9.41% to 2,093,000 won, reclaiming the 2 million won mark. Samsung Electronics jumped 5.89% to 278,500 won. In Japan, Kioxia advanced 7.15% while SoftBank edged up 0.33%.

The rally was amplified by SK Hynix's U.S. ADR which had surged 27% on Monday to $186 after Barclays initiated coverage with an Overweight rating. A massive premium persists: SK Hynix ADRs trade at roughly 27% above the Seoul-listed equivalent, implying a valuation gap of approximately $246 billion.

Goldman Sachs: Leveraged ETFs Amplified Volatility, Chip Cycle Not Peaked

Goldman Sachs published a report titled "KOSPI Tests Key Technical Support Levels" on July 14, identifying single-stock leveraged ETFs as the primary amplifier of Korea's market volatility. The report noted that 2x leveraged ETFs tied to Samsung and SK Hynix crashed over 30% in a single day, forcing managers to sell additional underlying shares to maintain target leverage ratios — creating a vicious cycle of falling prices triggering more selling.

However, Goldman explicitly refuted the view that the semiconductor cycle has structurally peaked. Earnings forecasts have not been revised downward, and capacity expansion may be delayed until H2 2028 amid supply shortages. The firm assessed the recent correction as a liquidity-driven position unwinding rather than a fundamental deterioration.

📊 Data Heavy Day: June PPI Release + Warsh Senate Testimony

Two critical macro events converge today (July 15):

  • June PPI Data (8:30 AM ET): Headline PPI expected -0.1% MoM (vs +0.3% prior), Core PPI expected +0.2% MoM.
  • Fed Chair Warsh Senate Testimony (10:00 AM ET): Day 2 of his semiannual Monetary Policy Report, this time before the Senate Banking Committee. On Day 1, Warsh struck a cautious tone, saying "mission accomplished is not my view" despite the cool CPI print.
CME FedWatch shows July rate hike probability crashed from 41.7% to 16.6% after CPI, but September odds remain elevated at 53%. A hotter-than-expected PPI reading could reverse the dovish repricing in hours.

🚗 Lucid Crashes 55% Intraday on Bankruptcy Rumors, Then Denies

Lucid Group (NASDAQ:LCID) experienced one of the most dramatic single-session collapses in recent memory on July 14. An industry media report revealed that the EV maker had hired AlixPartners — a firm famous for Chapter 11 bankruptcy restructuring — to evaluate strategic options including privatization or bankruptcy protection.

The stock plunged as much as 55-57% intraday to a record low of $2.17, triggering multiple trading halts. Lucid quickly issued a denial, calling the bankruptcy rumors "completely false" and stating AlixPartners was engaged only to improve operational efficiency. The stock recovered to close at $4.62, still down 16.15%. Trading volume hit 152.3 million shares, 665% above the 3-month average.

Lucid's fundamentals remain deeply challenged: $2.7 billion loss in 2025, another $1.1 billion loss in Q1 2026, -96% gross margin, and $1-1.5 billion quarterly cash burn. With $4.7 billion in cash as of March 2026, the central question persists: if not today, then when?

🏦 CICC Three-Way Merger Accepted by Chinese Regulators

China International Capital Corporation (CICC) announced on July 14 that its "three-in-one" restructuring plan to absorb Dongxing Securities and Cinda Securities via share swap has been formally accepted by the China Securities Regulatory Commission (CSRC). This is the first three-way merger in China's securities industry history.

CICC also released a strong H1 2026 earnings preview, projecting net profit of 7.708-8.227 billion yuan, up 78-90% year-over-year. The deal follows a wave of Chinese brokerage consolidation, including Dongfang Securities' acquisition of Shanghai Securities in May and Dongwu Securities' 83.68% stake purchase in Donghai Securities.
