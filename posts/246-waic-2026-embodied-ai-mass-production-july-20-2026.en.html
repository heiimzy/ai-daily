⚡ WAIC 2026 Closing Day: Embodied AI Transitions from Show to Real Work, Mass Production Era Officially Begins

July 20, 2026 — The four-day World Artificial Intelligence Conference (WAIC 2026) wrapped up in Shanghai today, marking a historic turning point for embodied AI. With a record exhibition area exceeding 100,000 square meters, the conference hosted over 1,100 companies showcasing 3,000+ cutting-edge科技成果, including 300+ global product debuts. The most dramatic transformation was in the embodied AI sector, where exhibitors surged from 80 last year to over 200, and more than 300 real robots were deployed for actual industrial tasks — dismantling pallets, loading materials, assembling components, and conducting inspections — rather than the dance performances and acrobatics that dominated previous years.

"The second half of embodied AI is no longer about technology — it's about deployment," declared Li Tong, founder and CEO of Keenon Robotics, capturing the consensus across the entire robotics exhibition hall. The message from WAIC 2026 was unmistakable: embodied AI is transitioning from "digital intelligence" to "physical intelligence," and 2026 is the dual元年 of mass production and real-world deployment.

Robots Stop Performing, Start Working: Real Production Lines Inside the Expo Hall

The most visible change at WAIC 2026 was the disappearance of martial arts demonstrations and dance routines from center stage, replaced by actual production scenarios. Companies moved their factory production lines directly onto the exhibition floor, where robots brewed coffee, transported materials, and performed precision assembly — all with zero human intervention.

Keenon Robotics demonstrated this shift with a compelling case study. Their robot-operated coffee shop at the NOWWA Coffee chain has gone into real commercial operation, serving actual customers who order through food delivery platforms. "We used to think performance wasn't real work, but if customers pay for it, it creates value," Li Tong explained, noting that Haidilao hot pot restaurants now use Keenon robots not just for food delivery but also for entertainment — a genuine revenue-generating application.

Geek+ unveiled its Gravity 4D unified embodied intelligence framework, designed for long-horizon tasks. The architecture features a "dual-brain" system: a cognitive brain (AR Transformer) that decomposes long-term tasks, and an action brain (Diffusion Transformer) that simulates physical outcomes. On the public LIBERO-Plus benchmark, incorporating 4D representations improved task success rates from 73.73% to 78.62%. Geek+'s operational network now spans 40+ countries with 1,700+ projects, serving 950+ international brands including Walmart and Adidas.

For the first time, WAIC achieved deployed-scale public service robotics. Zhiyuan Robotics, in partnership with Qingtian Rent, deployed 60 robots across the conference's three venues to provide navigation, information, and bilingual guiding services — marking the industry's first大规模 embodied AI deployment in a public service setting.

Mass Production Year Takes Shape: 10,000-Unit Production Line Agreements Signed

The most powerful signal from WAIC 2026 was that proving a robot can work is no longer enough — the critical differentiator is now mass production capability. 2026 is being hailed as the "Year Zero" for embodied AI mass production, and production capacity has become a dividing line between industry leaders and laggards.

On July 18, Lansheng Technology's subsidiary signed a landmark agreement with Shangwei New Materials for an initial 10,000-unit robot production line, covering precision component processing, joint module production, and full-unit assembly. This marks the transition of robot hardware manufacturing from sample customization and small-batch trial production to standardized mass production.

Industry-wide data from IDC shows global humanoid robot shipments approached 18,000 units in 2025, with Zhiyuan and Unitree leading the first tier at ~5,000 units each. Zhiyuan reached its 10,000th robot milestone in March 2026. Unitree's annual production capacity target has reached 190,000 units, while UBTech has raised its 2026 delivery target to 5,000 units. Zhishen Technology achieved cumulative production exceeding 15,000 units by June 2026, with single-month output reaching 5,000 units in June.

The technology demonstration hype cycle is giving way to commercial reality. As one industry observer noted: "When terminal companies ask 'where are my customers,' Lansheng answers 'how do we build them, build enough, build cheaply.' Together, these complete the commercial chain."

Qizhi Openmind Launches "Android for Robots": Universal Technical Platform Lowers Barriers

Among the dense crowd of humanoid robot exhibitors, newcomer Qizhi Openmind stood out by doing something entirely different — instead of showcasing robot acrobatics, it unveiled a universal technical base platform designed to solve what it sees as the fundamental bottleneck constraining embodied AI: the lack of a unified development, deployment, and iteration platform for all types of robots.

Drawing the analogy to smartphones, Qizhi's platform aspires to be the "Android of robotics" — a complete operating system and development ecosystem. Its core, Openmind OS, adapts to different brands and form factors of robot hardware while providing a unified runtime environment for models and applications. Industrial robotic arms, composite robots, and humanoid robots can all connect, enabling "develop once, deploy everywhere."

The development layer comprises three tools: HALO Skill Suit, a wearable system for capturing multi-modal operational data; Dayan Data Platform, handling data collection, curation, annotation, and training — reducing training cycles from months to weeks or days; and Modou IDE, which transforms robot development from code-based programming into visual low-code building accessible to workers without programming backgrounds.

Qizhi also unveiled HumanGPT, a human-centric whole-body full-modal world model developed in collaboration with Professor Chen Fei's team at the Chinese University of Hong Kong. HumanGPT learns from real human operational data and distills generalizable skill representations, creating a second data pathway for robot training that bypasses the expensive and slow bottleneck of real robot data collection. The product vision culminates in a robot "app store" — developers build skill applications, publish them, and robots download and run them instantly.

Intellifuture Unveils vivago R1: World's First Unlimited-Duration Content Creation Agent

On the closing afternoon of WAIC, Intellifuture officially launched vivago R1, the world's first multi-modal creation agent with unlimited-duration video generation and editing capabilities. This marks a transformation from single-point素材 generation to fully autonomous long-chain content creation.

vivago R1's core advantages are summarized as "long, long, stable." First, unlimited duration — it supports continuous generation of video at any length, producing several minutes of finished footage within 30 minutes, enabling short dramas, documentaries, brand films, and影视 content creation. Second, long-horizon thinking — it autonomously manages narrative logic, shot arrangement, and visual style consistency, solving the industry's persistent problems of visual inconsistency, character collapse, and narrative discontinuity. Third, stable generation — through AgentOS full-process orchestration, it achieves 85% effective content success rate, far above industry averages, dramatically reducing iteration time and cost.

In parallel, Intellifuture launched two major ecosystem initiatives: a Physical Intelligence Innovation Consortium with Feijiekesi et al., and a "Belt and Road" Token Export Alliance with China Brain-like AI and other institutions, advancing Chinese full-modal AI capabilities overseas.

The Paradigm Shift: AI Transitions from "Answering Questions" to "Completing Work"

WAIC 2026 sent a clear signal: the AI industry's core mission is shifting from "generating content" to "understanding the physical world and completing real work." Kunlun Tech Chairman Fang Han declared 2026 as the "Year of the World Model" during the conference, arguing that while the past two years were about generating text, images, video, and music, the new era demands AI that truly understands physical world dynamics and can interact with real environments in closed-loop feedback.

From exhibitor numbers doubling to 300+ robots working real jobs, from 10,000-unit production line agreements to multiple companies targeting mass production — every data point from WAIC 2026 confirms the same trend. The embodied AI industry has officially crossed the concept verification threshold and entered industrial competition. The competitive focus has shifted from hardware body capabilities to "brain" capabilities, and the core question is: who can first use AI to complete complex real-world tasks in industrial and service settings at scale?

When Keenon answers "who pays" with actual customer revenue, when Geek+ answers "think first, then act" with Gravity 4D, and when Lansheng answers "how to build enough" with 10,000-unit production capacity, the industry is collectively answering one fundamental question: robots exist to create commercial value. WAIC 2026 may well be remembered as the year embodied AI officially clocked in for work.
