⚡ July 16 International Politics Roundup: US Strikes Expand to Northern Iran, Iran Declares 'Existential War' After Voiding Peace Deal, Trump Considers Ground Operations, Threatens Nuclear Sites

1. US Strikes Northern Iran for First Time in Latest Escalation — Fifth Consecutive Day of Bombardment

The United States launched its fifth consecutive day of strikes against Iran early Thursday, July 16, dramatically expanding the geographic scope of its campaign. US Central Command (CENTCOM) confirmed a second wave of strikes began at 3:00 PM ET Wednesday (03:00 CST Thursday), targeting Iranian command centers, air defense sites, missile and drone capabilities, and coastal surveillance facilities.

Unlike previous days when strikes were concentrated along Iran's southern coast and near the Strait of Hormuz, the latest raids pushed significantly northward for the first time:

  • Semnan Airport hit — Located about 200 km east of Tehran, Semnan province is home to Iran's ballistic missile production and space program. Local authorities confirmed the main terminal building and a storage shed were damaged. No casualties were reported
  • Hamadan province attacked — Western Iran's Kabudarahang county was struck, with Iranian state media reporting multiple locations hit
  • Tehran air defenses activated — Air raid sirens sounded in southern and northeastern parts of the capital, with explosions reported near Parchin and Pakdasht — strategically significant military areas
  • Ahvaz cancer hospital evacuated — A pediatric cancer center in Ahvaz was forced to evacuate after nearby strikes, with families caring for children on surrounding streets
  • Southern coastal cities — Bandar Abbas, Qeshm Island, Sirik, Chabahar, Konarak, and Rask all came under continued bombardment

CENTCOM also confirmed a 90-minute precision strike earlier Wednesday against coastal defense systems and cruise missile storage and launch sites on Greater Tunb Island, a strategic point in the Strait of Hormuz. Three US officials told Reuters the strikes serve a dual purpose — forcing open the strait while simultaneously degrading Iranian defenses in preparation for potentially more complex military operations.

2. Iran Declares Peace Deal Void, Enters 'Existential War'

Iran's response matched the escalation in both tone and action. Foreign Ministry spokesperson Esmaeil Baghaei stated definitively on Thursday that Tehran has "no plans for negotiations" and is "focused on defense." He accused Washington of violating the June 17 memorandum of understanding (MoU) from the outset.

Iran's top negotiator and parliament speaker Mohammad Bagher Ghalibaf went further in a nationally televised address, declaring the peace deal effectively dead:

"If Iran cannot derive any benefit from the memorandum of understanding, we have no reason to adhere to it. Iran's national security depends on maintaining an Iranian order in the Strait of Hormuz. We are in an essential and existential war with America."

Ghalibaf simultaneously emphasized that negotiation — like armed resistance — remains part of Iran's "strategy of resistance," signaling a refusal to close diplomatic channels entirely despite the military escalation. The IRGC separately declared the Strait of Hormuz would remain closed "until the US ends its aggression" and warned it could shut "all other export corridors benefiting the US and its allies."

3. Iran Strikes US Bases in Bahrain, Kuwait, Jordan — 10th Phase of Retaliation

Iran's military launched the 10th phase of its retaliatory "Operation Lightning" in the early hours of Thursday, July 16:

  • Kuwait — Iran's army claimed to have destroyed an early warning radar, Patriot air defense system, and fuel storage tanks at Ali Al Salem Air Base. Kuwait confirmed intercepting 4 cruise missiles and 21 drones from Iran, reporting material damage but no casualties
  • Bahrain — Drone strikes targeted communication and radar systems at Sheikh Isa Air Base. Air raid sirens sounded across the island nation, with the Interior Ministry urging citizens to "remain calm and head to the nearest safe place"
  • Jordan — The IRGC claimed to have struck the Al-Azraq Air Base with Khyber-Shakan ballistic missiles, targeting the US command center and aircraft hangars housing F-15, F-16 and F-35 jets. Jordan's military said it shot down 8 incoming Iranian missiles
  • MQ-9 drone downed — Iran's IRGC Aerospace Force shot down a US MQ-9 Reaper drone over Andimeshk in southwestern Iran using a new air defense system

4. US Hellfire Missile Disables Oil Tanker Breaching Blockade

In the first maritime enforcement action since reimposing its naval blockade of Iranian ports, CENTCOM reported: A US aircraft fired Hellfire missiles into the smokestack of the Curacao-flagged oil tanker M/T Belma after it ignored multiple warnings while sailing toward Iran's Kharg Island oil export terminal. The vessel was rendered dead in the water and "is no longer transiting to Iran."

Two other commercial vessels were redirected after warnings during the first 24 hours of blockade enforcement. During the previous blockade (April 13–June 18), US forces sank 9 vessels and diverted over 140 ships. Iran's Deputy Foreign Minister Kazem Gharibabadi said the renewed blockade "has, in a way, dismantled the Islamabad memorandum."

5. Trump Considers Ground Operations — WSJ Reveals Pentagon Planning for Kharg Island, Nuclear Sites

The Trump administration is weighing a significant expansion of military operations against Iran, according to multiple US officials cited by The Wall Street Journal on July 16:

  • Top aides have held multiple briefings reviewing options including intensified airstrikes, deployment of ground troops to secure the Strait of Hormuz, and strikes on Iran's concealed nuclear facilities
  • In a Fox Business interview, Trump did not rule out "eliminating" the IRGC entirely when asked if the US could destroy the organization like ISIS: "That's possible. We'll see what happens."
  • Trump publicly threatened to strike "Pickaxe Mountain" — a fortified underground military facility near one of Iran's main nuclear sites
  • At the Pennsylvania Defense and Innovation Summit, Trump claimed Iran has contacted the US seeking talks, adding: "If they don't negotiate, we'll just finish it off"
  • Vice President JD Vance stated on The Joe Rogan Experience that the US would not deploy 150,000 ground troops for regime change, but did not rule out limited ground operations

Trump asserted the US has destroyed approximately 90% of Iran's weapons, missile launchers, drones, and military production capacity, and predicted Iran would be "defeated very soon," with oil prices potentially falling to $55/barrel once stability is restored.

6. Iran Releases US-Iranian Citizen Dena Karari as 'Gesture of Goodwill'

Amid the escalating hostilities, a notable diplomatic development emerged. Trump announced on Thursday that Iran had released US-Iranian citizen Dena Karari, who had been detained for nearly two years. Attorney Jared Genser confirmed Karari was safely out of Iran and en route to the United States.

Karari had been subjected to dozens of interrogations by Iran's Ministry of Intelligence and Security since December 2024 on charges of "collaboration with a hostile state" and espionage — charges Genser called "bogus." She ran the Children of Mehr Foundation, a nonprofit supporting impoverished Iranian children through private donations, and had been under a coercive exit ban but never formally imprisoned.

"The United States of America appreciates this gesture of Goodwill by Iran!" — President Donald Trump on Truth Social

Analysts view the release as Iran's attempt to preserve diplomatic room for maneuver even as military operations intensify, potentially signaling willingness to maintain backchannel communications.

7. Airlines Suspend Middle East Routes, Oil Tops $85 as Conflict Grinds On

The escalating conflict is producing tangible global economic impacts:

  • Aviation — Air Canada canceled flights to Tel Aviv and Dubai through October 24; Aegean Airlines suspended Dubai flights until August 31 and Erbil/Baghdad until September 30; Air France halted Beirut flights until August 2; KLM suspended Riyadh, Dammam and Dubai services; Cathay Pacific is planning to suspend Middle East routes
  • Oil — Brent crude rose for a fourth consecutive day, exceeding $85/barrel — more than 15% above pre-war levels. Iran signaled it may use Houthi allies to shut the Bab el-Mandeb strait, opening a second front against global energy supplies
  • Casualties — Iran's Health Ministry reported 35 civilians killed and over 300 wounded in recent US strikes. The Iranian army confirmed 7 soldiers died in a 13-missile barrage on the Bampur barracks in Sistan and Baluchestan province
  • US losses — One additional US service member was killed in Iran operations, bringing total US casualties to 14 dead and 414 wounded

The war, now in its fifth month since the US-Israeli preemptive strikes on February 28, has reduced daily transit through the Strait of Hormuz to just 21 vessels — down from a pre-war average of approximately 150. Global energy markets remain under severe strain as both sides show no signs of de-escalation.
