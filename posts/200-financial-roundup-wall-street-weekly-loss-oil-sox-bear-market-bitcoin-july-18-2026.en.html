⚡ July 18 Financial Evening: Wall Street Ends Week in Red, Oil Surges 16% Weekly, SOX Confirms Bear Market, Bitcoin Holds $64K

📉 Wall Street Close: All Three Indices Post Weekly Losses After Brief Rally

U.S. stocks closed lower across the board on Friday (July 17), capping a turbulent week. The Dow Jones Industrial Average dropped 406.55 points (-0.77%) to 52,146.42. The S&P 500 fell 1.01% to 7,457.69, while the Nasdaq Composite sank 1.4% to 25,520.24.

Weekly performance was broadly negative: the S&P 500 lost 1.6%, the Nasdaq slid 2.9%, and the Dow fell approximately 0.9%. Just two days earlier, the S&P 500 had climbed within 0.5% of its all-time high before AI and chip stocks triggered a sharp reversal.

European markets followed suit: ASMI and ASML fell 4.6% and 3.8% respectively, STMicroelectronics dropped 5%, and Infineon lost 4.2%. The pan-European Stoxx 600 closed 0.41% lower.

🔴 Philadelphia Semiconductor Index Enters Bear Market: 20.2% Plunge from Peak

The Philadelphia Semiconductor Index (SOX) fell as much as 6% intraday on Friday before paring losses to close 1.5% lower. The cumulative decline from its June 22 all-time high now stands at 20.2%, officially confirming a technical bear market. Global chip stocks have lost approximately $3.3 trillion in market capitalization over three weeks.

The trigger was Moonshot AI's Kimi K3 — a 2.8-trillion-parameter open-source model with a 1-million-token context window that scored 1,679 on the frontend code arena leaderboard, surpassing closed-source models from Anthropic and OpenAI. Markets fear that Chinese AI startups, closing the gap at dramatically lower costs, could undermine demand for premium chips — echoing the "DeepSeek moment" of January 2025.

"The market needs to become more comfortable with the ROI for hyperscalers, but that picture is complicated when you layer in valuations, sentiment, and flows on top of positive fundamentals." — Analyst comment

Key movers: Nvidia fell 2.2%, briefly dipping below a $4.8 trillion market cap. Applied Materials dropped 5.6%. Micron swung wildly between -5.8% and +3.2% before closing nearly flat. The VanEck Semiconductor ETF (SMH) has now fallen more than 17% this month alone, posting its third consecutive weekly decline.

🛢️ Oil Surges 16% Weekly: Brent Breaks $88 as Hormuz Transits Drop to Single Digits

Escalating U.S.-Iran conflict drove energy prices sharply higher. Brent crude settled at $88.10/barrel, up 4.6% on the day with a weekly gain of approximately 16% — the largest in months. WTI crude closed at $82.49/barrel, up 4.5% with a 15.5% weekly gain.

The U.S. military completed its seventh consecutive night of strikes against Iran, hitting military logistics infrastructure, underground weapons storage, and maritime capabilities for the first time. Iran's IRGC retaliated with "Operation Thunder" against U.S. bases in Kuwait, Bahrain, and Jordan. Kuwait's power and water desalination facilities were struck, forcing the shutdown of Kuwait International Airport. The Strait of Hormuz — which carries roughly 20% of global oil traffic — is seeing single-digit daily transits as the U.S. Navy enforces a full blockade on Iranian ports.

The impact is reaching consumers: the average U.S. regular gasoline price is approaching $4/gallon for the first time in a month. The 30-year fixed mortgage rate has risen to its highest level in nearly a year.

"The combination of tech concerns and inflation has really put a dent in the more buoyant narrative after the soft U.S. CPI report earlier this week." — Deutsche Bank Research

🍎 Apple vs. Nvidia: The Market Cap Crown Swaps Hands Briefly

Apple briefly surpassed Nvidia as the world's most valuable publicly traded company on Friday, hitting an all-time high of $333.74 per share with a market cap of approximately $4.91 trillion. This marked the first time since April 2025 that Apple has held the top spot.

The swap was driven more by Nvidia's decline than Apple's strength. Nvidia sank as much as 4.6% in early trading, pushing its valuation below $4.8 trillion. At roughly 20 times forward earnings, Nvidia is now trading at its cheapest valuation in about seven years — even lower than Hershey's 21x multiple. Apple shares barely moved through the session, closing up 0.1%.

Alphabet (Google) fell for a second consecutive day, down 2% on Friday after a 4.5% plunge Thursday. The company reportedly faces months-long delays in delivering its Gemini 3.5 Pro flagship model, with internal frustration growing over coding capability shortfalls.

📊 Notable Movers: Netflix -7.3%, Intuitive Surgical -14%, SpaceX at Record Lows

Netflix: Plunged 7.3% after Q2 revenue came in slightly below expectations. Forward guidance for Q3 also disappointed, amplifying concerns that growth is decelerating.

Intuitive Surgical: Despite beating Q2 earnings estimates, shares cratered 14.1% on concerns about slowing procedure growth following the expiration of enhanced ACA tax credits.

SpaceX: Dropped 5.4% to its lowest level since listing on the Nasdaq just over a month ago. Thursday's last-second abort of Starship Flight 13, combined with the broader AI stock rout, created a double headwind.

₿ Bitcoin Holds $64K: Crypto Fear & Greed Hits 25 'Extreme Fear,' Tether Freezes $131M in Iran-Linked Addresses

Crypto markets stabilized after a volatile week. Bitcoin recovered from a Friday low of $62,400 to trade near $64,000, up 1.78% over 24 hours. Ethereum recovered to approximately $1,844, outperforming Bitcoin with an 11% weekly gain.

However, sentiment remains deeply pessimistic: the Crypto Fear & Greed Index sank to 25 (Extreme Fear), continuing its decline from 32 the prior week. Total market capitalization stands at approximately $2.19 trillion, with Bitcoin dominance rising to 58.7%.

Key Developments

  • ETF flows: U.S. spot Bitcoin ETFs recorded several consecutive days of inflows. On July 16, net inflows reached $79.1 million, with Fidelity and Bitwise contributing over half. Ethereum ETFs saw net outflows of ~$28 million on the same day.
  • Tether freezes Iran-linked addresses: Tether froze 4 TRON-network wallets containing approximately $131 million in USDT tied to Iran's Islamic Revolutionary Guard Corps (IRGC) and central bank. The U.S. Treasury's OFAC simultaneously sanctioned multiple crypto wallets linked to Iran's central bank.
  • Strategy (formerly MicroStrategy): CEO Phong Le stated debt risks would only materialize if Bitcoin fell to $8,000-$10,000. The company's cash reserves have grown to approximately $3 billion.
  • Citigroup lowers Bitcoin target: Cut its 12-month price target from $112,000 to $82,000, acknowledging that geopolitical risks and ETF outflows have altered the baseline scenario.
  • E*TRADE launches crypto spot trading: Morgan Stanley's E*TRADE platform officially opened spot trading in Bitcoin, Ethereum, and Solana to its retail brokerage clients.
  • CLARITY Act hearing: The July 17 field hearing in New York drew zero Democratic members. The Senate's voting window narrows to just three weeks before the August recess.

🥇 Gold Battles at $4,000: Safe-Haven Appeal Suppressed by Inflation Fears

Gold edged up 0.77% to $4,023/oz on Friday, recovering from Thursday's dip below $4,000. However, the metal is headed for its largest weekly loss since June, down approximately 2.6% for the week.

Gold's traditional safe-haven bid has been muted during this conflict cycle. The core reason: rising oil prices fuel inflation expectations, raising the probability of a Fed rate hike — markets now price roughly 50% odds of a September hike. Gold, which pays no yield, becomes less attractive in a high-rate environment. The U.S. Dollar Index holding near 100.50 adds further pressure.

Technically, gold faces immediate support at $3,950-$3,960, with a break potentially testing $3,820. Resistance sits at $4,040-$4,060. Major Wall Street banks maintain year-end $5,000 targets, contingent on a decisive close above $4,060.

The People's Bank of China has now logged 20 consecutive months of gold accumulation, while Poland added approximately 82 tonnes to its reserves in 2026. Institutional buying provides a structural floor beneath the metal.

📋 Macro Data: Consumer Sentiment Beats Expectations, 10-Year Yield Eases

Friday's data showed U.S. consumer sentiment improving more than economists expected, while inflation expectations moderated. This is a positive signal for the Fed — anchored inflation expectations could prevent a self-reinforcing inflationary spiral.

The 10-year Treasury yield edged down to 4.55% from 4.57% on Thursday. Energy was the only S&P 500 sector to close in the green, reflecting the oil-driven rotation.

Looking ahead, all eyes are on the tech earnings super-week: Alphabet reports July 22, followed by Microsoft, Amazon, and Meta. The AI investment ROI debate will take center stage.
