⚡ July 12 12:00 Tech Update: Ant LingBot Drops 6 Embodied AI Models, Rebuilding Robotic Brains from Scratch

Ant Group's embodied intelligence subsidiary LingBot held a media briefing today where CEO Zhu Xing and Chief Scientist Shen Yujun delivered a remarkably sober assessment: current robotic brains may not have even reached the "GPT-1 moment" yet. The industry has not witnessed true intelligence emergence, and technical approaches remain far from converging.

The company unveiled 6 models spanning three technical paradigms — Vision-Language-Action (VLA), Video-Action (VA), and world models — with pretraining data totaling 60,000 hours covering 20 robot configurations across 17 manufacturers.

The "Door and Cat" Problem: The Gap Between Digital and Physical

Shen illustrated the core challenge with a vivid example. A cat sits behind an opaque glass door. A standard vision model can identify the cat and describe the scene. But for a robot to actually move toward the cat, merely "seeing" is not enough — it must understand that the glass door is a physical barrier, and that the cat is in an unreachable space until the door opens.

Digital models care about what is in the frame. Robots must judge distance, occlusion, contact relationships, and reachability. Semantic correctness is only the first step of a physical task.

Unlike video generation models serving content creation (such as Jimeng or Wanxiang), robotic time flows only forward. When a robot reaches for a cup, it cannot know whether someone will bump the table in the next second. The model must predict the next state based on current information and correct its actions as sensors return new data. Visual quality is irrelevant — predictions must be reasonable, fast, and convertible into action.

The team calls this approach "Embodied Native" and has trained the LingBot-VA 2.0 model from scratch. The architecture employs causal pretraining, sparse Mixture-of-Experts, and asynchronous inference design for high-frequency, closed-loop robot control.

Dual-Track Strategy: VLA + VA

The 6 released models span two technical tracks:

  • VLA Track: LingBot-VLA 2.0 series, better at understanding human language intent, lower inference resource consumption, easier to deploy. LingBot uses VLA to enter scenarios and validate data.
  • VA Track: LingBot-VA 2.0 series, exploring dynamic modeling and future prediction through causally pretrained physics world simulators.

Shen views these as different puzzle pieces rather than competing approaches. Over time, they may converge into a single model. The 6-model release reflects a strategy of deconstructing unsolved problems in robotic brains — the model count may actually decrease in the future.

The Data Odyssey: From 20,000 to 60,000 Hours

When asked how much data is enough, Zhu gave a frank answer: even 10 million hours might not be sufficient. Unlike autonomous driving, which operates within defined traffic rules, general-purpose robots must enter factories, warehouses, and homes, handling objects of different materials, adapting to different bodies, and dealing with failure states that cannot be predefined.

LingBot-VLA 2.0's pretraining data has grown from approximately 20,000 hours in the first generation to 60,000 hours, including 50,000 hours of robot trajectories and 10,000 hours of first-person human video, covering 20 robot configurations from 17 manufacturers. Action space has expanded from arms to include head, waist, mobile base, and dexterous hands.

Zhu compared pretraining to cultivating a well-rounded university graduate. When that graduate joins a bank as an accountant, they still need vocational training. Foundation models raise the ceiling of capability; post-training turns models into production tools.

Commercialization: Success Rate Over Speed

When a reporter brought up a warehouse case where a human takes 30 seconds by forklift while a robot takes over a minute, Zhu prioritized success rate over speed. If a robot fails several times in a row, the enterprise still needs to assign human backup, making deployment economically unviable. Only after success rate stabilizes will companies calculate cycle time, inference efficiency, and unit cost.

LingBot supports 20 robot configurations. After manufacturers connect, task-specific post-training is still required. What the "one brain, many bodies" approach truly saves is the cost of training from scratch each time the robot platform or scenario changes.

LingBot is exploring buyout, subscription, and customization pricing models with robot manufacturers. However, no externally verifiable customer cases, revenue figures, or cost models were disclosed.

LingBot-World-Infinity: Open-Source Causal World Model

Alongside the VLA and VA models, LingBot open-sourced LingBot-World-Infinity (LingBot-World 2.0) — a causal video generation model that functions as an interactive world simulator. Using MoBA attention and DMD distillation, it supports unlimited interaction duration without long-horizon drift.

Key technical features:

  • 14B parameter main model + 1.3B lightweight variant deployable on a single GPU
  • 720p 60fps real-time streaming generation (requires deployment stack)
  • "Director-Pilot" dual-agent architecture — VLM as director controlling semantic rules, DiT generator as pilot simulating physics dynamics
  • WASD keyboard control, semantic event injection, environment state switching

The model is available on Hugging Face under CC BY-NC-SA 4.0 license, providing a powerful world simulation tool for non-commercial research.

Industry Landscape: Embodied AI's "Model War" Phase

Zhu believes embodied AI is in an early "100-model war" stage similar to the LLM boom, which will likely converge to a few general foundation model providers. Robots entering homes at scale remains distant — comparing to Windows or Android is premature.

Digital-world agents proliferated rapidly as foundation model capabilities improved. Embodied intelligence may experience a similar capability spillover. But the physical world adds an unavoidable constraint: every judgment a model makes must ultimately be executed by a real body.

Ant Group provides LingBot with funding, talent, training infrastructure, data processing capabilities, and scenario ecosystems. LingBot builds its full-stack model system — from spatial perception, video generation, and interactive world models to VLA and VA — on this foundation, validating production readiness through robot manufacturer partnerships.
