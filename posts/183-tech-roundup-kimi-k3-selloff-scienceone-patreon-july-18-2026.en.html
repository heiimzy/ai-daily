⚡ July 18 Tech Roundup: Kimi K3 Triggers 'DeepSeek 2.0' Global Chip Rout, CAS Unveils ScienceOne Omni Surpassing GPT-5.5, Patreon Partners with Cloudflare to Block AI Crawlers

1. Kimi K3 Triggers 'DeepSeek 2.0': Global Chip Stocks Plunge, SOX Enters Bear Market

On July 17, Beijing-based Moonshot AI officially released Kimi K3, a 2.8-trillion-parameter open-weight model with a 1-million-token context window and native multimodal capabilities — making it the largest open-source AI model in the world. The model benchmarks close to Anthropic's Claude Fable 5 and OpenAI's GPT-5.6 Sol, outperforming all other tested systems including Claude Opus 4.8 and GPT-5.5 on most agentic and coding benchmarks.

The release triggered a "DeepSeek 2.0" moment in global financial markets. According to Bloomberg, the tech-heavy Nasdaq 100 Index plummeted 2.1% on Friday, while the S&P 500 fell 0.93%. Bourses in China, Japan, and Europe also declined. The New York Times reported that the sell-off was driven by "signs of increasing competition from China intensifying investor unease around eye-popping spending on AI infrastructure."

"US stocks plunged on Friday as the sell-off in semiconductor names gathered steam following the reveal of an artificial-intelligence model from a Chinese startup." — Bloomberg

The Philadelphia Stock Exchange Semiconductor Index (SOX) plunged 3.9%, entering a bear market — down more than 20% from its June peak. Memory chip stocks took the heaviest hits initially but staged an intraday rebound, with Sandisk recovering about 6%, SK Hynix 5%, and Seagate 3%.

Nvidia shares fell about 4.5%, dropping its market capitalization to approximately $4.81 trillion — allowing Apple (at $4.91 trillion) to surpass it as the world's most valuable public company for the first time since November 6, 2024. Other semiconductor stocks including AMD, Intel, Micron, Broadcom, and Qualcomm fell between 4% and 13%.

The market's concern centers on cost efficiency. Kimi K3 is priced at $3 per million input tokens and $15 per million output tokens — a fraction of Fable 5's $10/$50 and GPT-5.6 Sol's $5/$30. According to Artificial Analysis, K3's per-task cost averages $0.94, roughly half that of Claude Opus 4.8 at $1.80. The message to markets is clear: high-quality AI is becoming dramatically cheaper, challenging the investment thesis behind massive AI infrastructure spending.

2. Chinese Academy of Sciences Launches ScienceOne Omni: Scientific AI Model Surpasses GPT-5.5

At the ongoing World Artificial Intelligence Conference (WAIC 2026) in Shanghai, the Chinese Academy of Sciences (CAS) unveiled ScienceOne Omni — a major upgrade to the ScienceOne foundation model first launched at WAIC in July 2025. The model is designed to accelerate scientific discovery across disciplines by fusing broad AI capabilities with deep domain expertise.

ScienceOne Omni employs a progressive three-layer architecture: scientific data encoding, natural world knowledge alignment, and domain-specific task decoding. This enables a single model to handle cross-disciplinary data understanding, knowledge reasoning, scientific prediction, and content generation. It was trained on 8 million high-quality scientific reasoning samples covering more than 200 research tasks.

Evaluations across more than 60 professional research tasks showed ScienceOne Omni significantly outperformed Gemini-3.1-Pro and GPT-5.5 on most benchmarks, while achieving state-of-the-art results on domain-specific tasks including chemical property prediction, spectral-to-molecular structure prediction, and protein site forecasting.

  • Literature Review Agent: Generates professional-level reviews within 3 hours with 90% evidence attribution accuracy, having already produced nearly 40,000 reviews
  • Fluid Dynamics Simulation: AI-powered tool delivers results in under 10 seconds with less than 5% error
  • Astronomy: Spectral agent deployed at the National Astronomical Observatories improved rare celestial object identification by ~50%
  • Chemistry "Machine Scientist": Autonomously completes the full loop from literature mining to experimental synthesis, with 8 of the top 20 virtual candidates outperforming the previous best material

The model's entire computing architecture has been adapted to domestic Chinese chips, including Ascend and Hygon. ScienceOne Omni has been adopted by more than 50 CAS institutes, 30+ universities and research institutions, and is expanding internationally through UNESCO and the Alliance of International Science Organizations.

3. Patreon Partners with Cloudflare to Actively Block AI Training Crawlers

On July 17, Patreon announced it is working with Cloudflare to actively block AI training crawlers at the network level — marking a significant shift from passive requests to active enforcement in the battle over AI training data.

Since 2023, Patreon had relied on robots.txt files to request that AI crawlers not scrape its content. However, as AI scraping became more sophisticated, many crawlers simply ignored these requests. Patreon's Chief Product Officer Drew Rowny stated: "Consent shouldn't depend on whether a scraper chooses to behave."

Using Cloudflare's AI Crawl Control technology, Patreon can now actively identify and block AI training bots in real time. Testing showed that individual AI training crawlers' weekly access attempts fell from "thousands of attempts to zero" — clear evidence that the crawlers had been ignoring Patreon's robots.txt and scraping content without permission.

The company clarified that it will continue to allow indexing bots that organize information and direct users back to Patreon. The key distinction is between "indexing robots" and "training robots."

Cloudflare has recently introduced a suite of AI crawler management tools, including a "Pay Per Crawl" marketplace that lets websites charge AI bots for scraping, and a policy that blocks "mixed-use" crawlers by default on ad-supported pages. These moves are pushing the entire internet toward a model where AI training requires explicit consent rather than presumed permission.
