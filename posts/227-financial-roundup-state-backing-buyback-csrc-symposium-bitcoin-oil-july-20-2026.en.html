⚡ July 20 Financial Roundup: China State Giants Inject $8.4B+ into A-Shares, CSRC Holds Symposium, Bitcoin Holds $64,800

1. State-Backed Giants Inject Over 60 Billion Yuan into A-Shares

On the evening of July 19, Beijing time, two massive state-owned investment platforms announced major equity purchase programs, sending a powerful market-stabilization signal after a brutal week for Chinese equities.

China Chengtong Group and its subsidiaries Chengtong Capital and Chengyang Investment disclosed they have made nearly 10 billion yuan ($1.4B) in concentrated purchases of state-owned enterprise stocks and A-share ETFs. The group stated it "firmly believes in China's economic and capital market prospects" and will continue deploying自有资金 and stock buyback special loans for further purchases of SOE and tech-enterprise stocks.

China Guoxin Holdings announced that its investment arm has utilized stock buyback and share-increase special loans and matching funds totaling over 50 billion yuan ($7B). The entity will continue using policy tools alongside self-owned funds to purchase central enterprise stocks, vowing to "resolutely maintain the stable operation of the capital market."

Combined, the two state giants have injected over 60 billion yuan ($8.4B+). This builds on July 17's massive ETF inflows — CSI 300 saw 18.1 billion yuan, CSI A500 10.8 billion yuan, and STAR 50 8.5 billion yuan in a single day. Over the past week, over 100 billion yuan in stabilizing capital has flowed into the market.

2. CSRC to Hold Symposium with Listed Companies and Brokerages

China's Securities Regulatory Commission (CSRC) will convene a symposium on July 20 with listed companies, securities firms, and fund management institutions to solicit opinions on promoting the stable and healthy development of the capital market. This follows a series of stabilization measures initiated earlier this month.

The A-share market has suffered a severe correction since July. AI computing, CPO optical modules, and memory chip sectors have been hit hardest, with many stocks halving in value. On July 18 alone, 218 mutual funds recorded single-day losses exceeding 10%, while some quantitative私募 products saw weekly losses of 26%. The Shanghai Composite Index breached the key 3,190 support level, triggering widespread panic.

Multiple brokerages published weekend reports arguing the market is deeply oversold. Essence Securities noted that "the most intense selling phase has likely passed," while Industrial Securities pointed out that sharp intra-bull-market corrections are not unprecedented and do not signal an end to the cycle.

3. Private Equity Buyback Spree: Pingfanghe Leads with 100 Million Yuan

Against the backdrop of the market rout, several prominent private equity firms announced buyback programs over the weekend, signaling a belief that the bottom is near:

  • Pingfanghe Investment: 100 million yuan ($14M) self-purchase next week;
  • Yanhe Private Equity: 30 million yuan self-purchase over 10 trading days;
  • Hainan Xiwa: Affiliated entities and fund manager Liang Hong purchased 42 million yuan of their own products;
  • Xuanxin Asset: 10 million yuan self-purchase on July 17;
  • Zhiyuzhishan Investment: Over 30 million yuan completed in July.

PE industry analysts note that such concentrated self-purchases indicate fund managers believe current equity valuations offer a significant margin of safety, with systemic risks largely released.

4. Bitcoin Holds $64,800 as Weekend 'Shadow Market' Role Intensifies

Bitcoin traded near $64,800 through the weekend, with Polymarket pricing a 99.95% probability of BTC staying above $52,000. As the Strait of Hormuz crisis continues — daily transit has collapsed to just 8 tankers, with Brent crude holding above $85 — Bitcoin has become the only continuously traded global risk asset, amplifying its "shadow market" role for risks that have nowhere else to go during the weekend.

Analysts note that Bitcoin's 24/7 trading structure makes it the first market to absorb any geopolitical developments over the weekend. Short-term holder cost basis sits near $69,000, with the $64,000-$65,000 range forming near-term support. The softer-than-expected June US CPI (core at 2.6% YoY) has reduced the probability of a July Fed rate hike to approximately 15%, providing macro support for crypto.

"Bitcoin becomes a shadow market for risks that have nowhere else to go. Any new tanker attack, shipping suspension, or military strike could hit BTC hours before oil futures or equities can respond." — CryptoSlate

5. LPR Unchanged as Monetary Policy Enters Wait-and-See Mode

The People's Bank of China kept its benchmark lending rates unchanged on July 20, with the 1-year Loan Prime Rate (LPR) at 3.7% and the 5-year LPR at 4.45%, in line with market expectations. Huatai Securities commented that with LPR unchanged for two consecutive months, China's rate-cut window has likely closed, and the most accommodative phase of monetary policy may be over.

In the interbank market, liquidity remains slightly tight. The overnight Shibor stood at 2.0050%, with the 7-day rate at 2.3080%. The PBOC conducted 75 billion yuan in 7-day reverse repo operations at a maintained rate of 2.25%, achieving a net injection of 45 billion yuan. July tax payments continue to drain system liquidity, but the central bank's steady net injections signal a commitment to stability.

6. Oil Stays Elevated as Hormuz Crisis Intensifies

Brent crude futures held above $85/barrel, with WTI trading near $81. The Strait of Hormuz is seeing only 8 tanker transits per day — a fraction of the normal 20.9 million barrels/day. The US-Iran conflict has entered its 10th day with no signs of de-escalation. Iran has formally suspended the Islamabad Memorandum of Understanding, and market fears of a sustained supply disruption continue to build.

High oil prices transmit to financial markets through two channels: (1) raising inflation expectations and reducing the likelihood of Fed easing, and (2) increasing corporate production costs and suppressing risk appetite. Citi's US equity risk indicator has triggered 11.5 signals — the highest since 2008 — while global chip stocks have lost $3.3 trillion in market value over three weeks.
