⚡ July 12 Global Markets Weekly: S&P 500 at 5-Week High, Mag 7 Lags Behind Semis, Circle Wins National Trust Bank Charter, A-Share Volume Hits 3.76T

📊 US Stock Market Weekly: AI Drives S&P 500 to 5-Week High

U.S. stock indexes closed higher on Friday, July 10, with the S&P 500 gaining 0.42% to 7,575.39 — its highest close in five weeks. The Dow Jones Industrial Average rose 0.29% to 52,637.01, while the Nasdaq 100 advanced 0.33% to 29,825.11.

The week's rally was fueled by easing geopolitical tensions as US-Iran peace talks continued, persistent AI sector strength, and anticipation of a strong Q2 earnings season. Bloomberg Intelligence forecasts Q2 S&P 500 earnings to grow 23% year-over-year, approaching Q1's blowout 30% growth. AI infrastructure companies are expected to contribute nearly 60% of the index's Q2 earnings-per-share growth.

The 10-year Treasury yield rose 1.4 basis points to 4.565%. Gold edged down 0.77% to $4,120.20/oz, while WTI crude fell nearly 1% to $71.62/barrel as US-Iran peace talk hopes eased supply concerns. Bitcoin gained 1.71% to $63,899.14.

⚠️ Mag 7 Underperformance: Semi Index Surges 83% While Mag 7 Falls 1.9%

The most striking structural shift in U.S. markets this year is the dramatic underperformance of the Magnificent Seven. The Bloomberg Magnificent 7 Price Return Index fell 1.9% in the first half of 2026, while the S&P 500 gained 9.3% — an 11-percentage-point gap that marks the group's second-worst relative start to any year.

In stark contrast, the Philadelphia Stock Exchange Semiconductor Index has surged 83% year-to-date. Capital flows have rotated massively from mega-cap tech into the AI chip supply chain. SK Hynix jumped 13% in its Nasdaq debut, and Marvell Technology rose 9.6% after being added to the S&P 500. Morgan Stanley Wealth Management CIO Lisa Shalett warned semiconductors appear "meaningfully overbought" and advised diversifying into hyperscaler beneficiaries.

"The surge in backlogged orders and increasing pricing power among semiconductor manufacturers and memory suppliers has been remarkable, but we don't see it as sustainable. This isn't a forecast of the end of the cycle but rather an encouragement to diversify exposure to potential beneficiaries of the AI build-out, including some hyperscalers." — Lisa Shalett, CIO, Morgan Stanley Wealth Management

Analysts at Morgan Stanley, Goldman Sachs, and JPMorgan all argue the Mag 7 selloff has been excessive. Goldman Sachs partner Rich Privorotsky noted that hyperscalers "control the toll road, not just the vehicle."

The average Wall Street year-end target for the S&P 500 stands at 7,824.09. Should the Mag 7 continue to stagnate, the remaining 493 stocks would need to rise 6.8% by December. However, Wells Fargo's Samana notes the S&P 500 ex-Mag 7 is already up 14% YTD, suggesting the broader index can climb without mega-cap participation.

🏦 Fed Policy Watch: CPI Report and Warsh Testimony in Focus

The Federal Reserve held the federal funds rate steady at 3.5%-3.75% at its June FOMC meeting — the sixth consecutive hold since the start of 2026. The Fed's semiannual Monetary Policy Report, released July 10, confirmed that inflation "stepped up further this spring," attributing the rise to tariffs, war-related energy costs, and the rapid AI buildout.

Markets are pricing in a 32% probability of a 25-basis-point rate hike at the July 28-29 FOMC meeting. Two key macro events dominate the coming week:

  • July 14 (Tuesday) — June Consumer Price Index release, providing fresh inflation data
  • July 14-15 — Fed Chair Kevin Warsh's first semiannual Congressional testimony, scrutinized for rate path signals

June nonfarm payrolls added only 57,000 jobs, well below the 110,000 forecast, though the unemployment rate held steady at 4.2%. The mixed labor data leaves the Fed with limited room for decisive action.

The IMF's July World Economic Outlook projects 2026 global growth at 3.0%, noting that Middle East tensions are partly offset by strong AI demand. Global markets are walking a tightrope between AI-driven expansion and inflationary-geopolitical headwinds.

🇨🇳 China A-Shares: Post-Holiday Rally with 3.76T Yuan Volume

In the first trading session after the Dragon Boat Festival holiday, China's A-share markets staged a powerful rally. The Shanghai Composite jumped 1.78% to 4,163.10, the Shenzhen Component rose 2.13% to 16,372.50, the ChiNext surged 2.52% to a record 4,359.39, and the STAR 50 hit a new high of 1,948.93 (+1.96%).

The standout data point was trading volume: the three boards (Shanghai, Shenzhen, Beijing) recorded a combined 3.76 trillion yuan, the second-highest single-day volume in A-share history, trailing only the 3.99 trillion yuan record set on January 14, 2026.

The financial sector led the charge, with insurers and brokerages surging. New China Life, CSC Financial, and GF Securities hit their daily涨停 (10% upside) limits. The rally was supported by fiscal data showing securities transaction stamp tax revenue surged 88.8% year-over-year in the first five months of 2026, while listed brokerages reported 16.34% net profit growth in Q1.

Most brokerages are bullish on Q3. Guotai Junan Haitong Securities said the post-holiday removal of uncertainty opens a new upward window for Chinese equities, while Everbright Securities expects continued risk appetite recovery as overseas tensions ease.

🏛️ Circle Receives Final OCC Approval for National Trust Bank

On July 10, Circle Internet Group (NYSE: CRCL) announced it had received final approval from the U.S. Office of the Comptroller of the Currency to establish First National Digital Currency Bank, N.A., operating under the name Circle National Trust. Circle shares rose more than 4% on the news.

This marks the first federal regulatory approval of a national trust bank charter specifically for digital asset custody. Circle National Trust will initially provide fiduciary digital asset custody services for Circle and its affiliates, with potential future expansion to institutional clients. The trust bank may also eventually manage USDC reserves, bringing them under direct federal oversight.

"OCC approval to establish Circle National Trust marks a defining step in bringing blockchain technology and digital assets into the core of the U.S. financial system. Federal oversight of our trust bank sets a new standard for transparency, governance, and scale for Circle's infrastructure." — Jeremy Allaire, Co-Founder, Chairman & CEO, Circle

Circle originally submitted its OCC application in June 2025 and received conditional approval in December 2025. The company became the first BitLicense recipient from NYDFS in 2015 and the first global stablecoin issuer to comply with the EU's MiCA framework in 2024. Analysts view the national trust bank approval as a landmark step for crypto industry legitimacy.
