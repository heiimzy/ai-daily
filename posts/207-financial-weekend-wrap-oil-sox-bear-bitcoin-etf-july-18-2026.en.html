⚡ July 18 Financial Weekend Wrap: SOX Confirms Bear Market, Oil Surges 16% to $88, Bitcoin ETF Inflows for 4th Day, $2.5B Call Spread Targets $72K

1. US Stocks Post Weekly Losses, Chip Index Confirms Bear Market

Wall Street closed its worst week in a month on Friday, with all three major indices finishing in negative territory. The S&P 500 fell 1.01% to 7,457.69, recording a weekly loss of 1.6%. The Nasdaq Composite dropped 1.4% to 25,520.24, down 2.9% for the week. The Dow Jones Industrial Average shed 406.55 points (0.77%) to 52,146.42, posting a 0.9% weekly decline.

The Philadelphia Semiconductor Index (SOX) has officially entered bear market territory, falling more than 20% from its mid-June record high. The index had surged 105% between March and June before the selloff erased all those gains. The VanEck Semiconductor ETF (SMH) posted its third weekly decline in four weeks, down roughly 9% over that stretch.

The trigger for the chip rout was multi-pronged. Chinese AI startup Moonshot AI unveiled its Kimi K3 open-source model, claiming performance near parity with OpenAI and Anthropic at a fraction of the cost — reigniting "DeepSeek-style" competitive fears. TSMC reported a record Q2 revenue of $40.2 billion and raised full-year guidance, but shares fell for seven consecutive sessions — the longest losing streak since July 2022. Nvidia dropped 2.2% on Friday and briefly lost the title of world's most valuable company to Apple before recovering.

2. Oil Surges 16% for the Week, Hormuz Traffic Collapses

Brent crude settled at $88.10 per barrel on Friday, up 4.6% on the day — a weekly gain of 14-16%, the largest in years. WTI crude rose 4.5% to $82.49.

The surge was driven by the escalating US-Iran conflict entering its seventh consecutive night. The US struck surveillance sites, underground weapons storage, and maritime capabilities. Iran retaliated with missile and drone attacks on US bases in Kuwait, Jordan, and Bahrain, hitting power plants and water desalination facilities in Kuwait for the second time in as many days — a significant escalation targeting civilian infrastructure during extreme summer heat.

According to Kpler data, only 8 commercial vessels transited the Strait of Hormuz on July 16, down from a pre-conflict daily average of over 100. Seven of those chose the northern lane near Iran. Shipping risk firm Marisks called this a "worst-case scenario" for oil tankers, with at least 9 ships attacked in the strait since July 6.

The CME FedWatch tool now prices a 73% probability of a rate hike at the September 16 FOMC meeting, up from just 26% in mid-June. Goldman Sachs had previously forecast $100 oil if Hormuz disruptions persisted — with Brent at $88, that scenario has not been invalidated.

3. Apple Briefly Overtakes Nvidia; Netflix Plunges 7.3%; SpaceX Hits Record Low

Apple hit an all-time high of $333.74 intraday on Friday, briefly surpassing Nvidia as the world's most valuable public company — the first time since April 2025. However, the crown swap was more about Nvidia's decline than Apple's strength. Nvidia dropped more than 3% while Apple shares barely moved.

Netflix fell 7.3% after reporting Q2 revenue and Q3 guidance that both missed analyst expectations, raising concerns about streaming growth ceilings. Intuitive Surgical dropped 14.1% despite beating earnings, as markets worried about slowing procedure growth. SpaceX fell 5.4% to $123.99 — its lowest level since its Nasdaq debut just over a month ago — after aborting its Starship Flight 13 test at T-0 on Thursday.

Energy was the only sector that gained on Friday. Berkshire Hathaway rose more than 1%, signaling the rotation from overvalued tech into defensive positions.

4. Bitcoin Holds ~$63K as ETF Inflows Continue for 4th Day

Bitcoin traded near $63,600 on Friday, down 1.4% on the day, caught between geopolitical headwinds and improving inflation data. Despite price weakness, US spot Bitcoin ETFs recorded $132.3 million in net inflows on Thursday — the fourth consecutive day of inflows. BlackRock's IBIT led with $136.5 million.

Weekly Bitcoin ETF net inflows totaled $75.5 million, while Ethereum spot ETFs saw $105.5 million in net inflows for the week. Funding rates are near zero, suggesting the excessive leveraged longs that fueled June's liquidation cascades have been cleared out. Nansen data shows no meaningful rotation into stablecoins by smart money wallets, despite the geopolitical escalation.

A massive $2.5 billion Bitcoin call spread was executed on Deribit this week: traders bought 20,000 contracts of the July 31 expiry $70,000 call while selling 20,000 of the $72,000 call. The timing coincides with the Fed's July 29 rate decision, suggesting large traders expect the FOMC meeting to catalyze a breakout to $72,000.

5. E*TRADE Launches Spot Crypto Trading; Bitcoin Now Less Volatile Than Korea's KOSPI

Morgan Stanley's E*TRADE has begun rolling out spot Bitcoin, Ethereum, and Solana trading to eligible US retail clients through ZeroHash, charging a flat 0.50% fee with no spread markup. The move marks mainstream Wall Street's deepening integration of digital assets into traditional brokerage workflows.

Bitcoin's 30-day implied volatility has dropped to approximately 38%, now lower than South Korea's KOSPI at 81%. The KOSPI has crashed roughly 25% from its June peak, with a margin loan crisis affecting 1.2 million Korean retail investors — roughly one in every 30 adults. Bitcoin being less volatile than a major stock index is historically unprecedented and could reshape institutional risk frameworks.

6. Macro Outlook: Earnings Season Heats Up Next Week

Next week is pivotal for Q2 earnings: Alphabet reports on July 22, followed by Microsoft, Amazon, and Meta. Their capex guidance will be crucial for assessing AI chip demand sustainability. The Fed meets July 28-29, with markets pricing 75-80% probability of a rate hold.

June CPI came in at 3.5% — a four-year low — and PPI unexpectedly fell 0.3%, providing some relief. But Brent at $88 threatens to close that disinflation window. Cleveland Fed President Hammack said Friday that business leaders are pressing the Fed to act on inflation. The July 28-29 FOMC decision will reveal whether June's inflation data or the current oil surge carries more weight.

JPMorgan warned this week that the deleveraging process in US equities is far from over, with leveraged ETF assets already down 13%. Meanwhile, UBS Wealth Management forecasts the S&P 500 reaching 7,900 by year-end, arguing the market is shifting from macro-driven to micro fundamentals-driven.
