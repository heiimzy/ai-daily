⚡ July 20 Tech Roundup: China's MIIT Unveils AI Terminal Policy Push, SpaceX Awards Foxconn $52B AI Server Deal, Shanghai Orbits First Space Computing Constellation

1. China MIIT Press Conference: AI Terminals Become New Consumption Hotspot, Manufacturing AI Penetration Tops 30%

At a State Council Information Office press conference on July 20 at 10:00 AM Beijing time, China's Ministry of Industry and Information Technology (MIIT) released key H1 2026 economic data revealing the rapid scaling of AI adoption across Chinese industry.

AI Terminals Going Mainstream

MIIT spokesperson Xie Cun announced that AI-powered phones, computers, smart robots, AI glasses, and AI toys have become new information consumption hotspots. As of end of June, China had deployed 5.102 million 5G base stations and 32.86 million 10G PON ports with gigabit capabilities. The ministry has also approved 6GHz频段 for 6G trial frequency licenses and is accelerating Phase 2 6G technology trials.

This follows the July 17 announcement at WAIC of the first batch of AI terminal L3 certification results, with 66 products from 17 companies receiving certification across six categories: mobile terminals, computers, televisions, smart glasses, automotive cockpits, and smart speakers.

Manufacturing AI Adoption Surges

MIIT Chief Engineer Wang Weiming reported that AI technology adoption among large-scale manufacturing enterprises exceeded 30% in H1 2026, while open-source AI models have accumulated over 10 billion global downloads. Industrial robot production grew 28% year-over-year, and service robot production rose 11.9%.

Equipment manufacturing value-add grew 6.4% year-over-year, contributing 23.5% to overall industrial growth. Integrated circuit exports (in RMB terms) surged 88.7%, electronic components exports rose 62.6%, and wind turbine exports grew 35.6%.

Next-Gen Network and 6G Acceleration

MIIT outlined a three-pronged strategy for next-generation communication networks: expanding 5G-A commercial deployment, strengthening core technology research in 6G/optical communications/satellite communications, and deepening integration of brain-computer interfaces, embodied intelligence, and connected vehicles with 5G/6G/satellite networks.

2. SpaceX Awards Foxconn $52 Billion AI Server Manufacturing Contract

According to Taiwan's Economic Daily News reported on July 20, SpaceX has awarded Foxconn (Hon Hai Precision Industry) its first AI server manufacturing contract, valued at an estimated $52 billion, for over 13,000 racks of NVIDIA GB300 AI servers, each costing approximately $4 million.

Breaking the Dell-Supermicro Duopoly

The order, scheduled for delivery from Q4 2026 through Q1 2027, breaks the previous Dell-Supermicro duopoly in SpaceX's AI server supply chain. Hon Hai Chairman Liu Young-way has predicted the company will capture over 40% of the global AI server market this year, with AI rack shipments expected to double and grow quarter over quarter.

Citrini analysts noted that the ~$52 billion valuation is not included in the company's current revenue guidance, suggesting Hon Hai's AI business momentum may be stronger than previously expected.

SpaceX's AI Computing Ambitions

The massive order supports SpaceX's broader diversification into AI compute services. As Fortune reported earlier, SpaceX has been quietly building an AI compute rental business using spare capacity from its Colossus data center complex, serving clients including Google and Anthropic, while pursuing AI computing partnerships with the Pentagon. The GB300 server deployment will significantly expand this capacity.

3. Shanghai Launches "Star Pivot" Space Computing Constellation

At the WAIC 2026 Space Computing Forum in Shanghai, the "Star Pivot Plan" (星枢计划) first constellation was officially unveiled, marking China's first commercial orbital AI computing network transitioning from design to engineering implementation.

"One Main, Two Auxiliary" Satellite Cluster

The initial constellation features a three-satellite distributed architecture:

  • Central Computing Main Satellite: The "space brain" of the cluster, co-developed with the Chinese Academy of Sciences
  • Fuxi Meteorological Satellite: Equipped with Fudan University's Fuxi weather AI model
  • Instant Remote Sensing Satellite: For real-time monitoring of ports, power grids, and emergency scenarios

The three satellites are linked via inter-satellite laser communications, operating on a "compute-in-space" principle that provides on-demand orbital AI services for weather, emergency management, shipping, and urban governance.

1,000-Satellite Master Plan

The full "Star Pivot Plan" calls for 1,000 space computing satellites — 800 central computing satellites and 200 edge computing satellites. The first constellation serves as a minimum viable product to validate technology, business models, and operations. Partners include Fudan University, the Shanghai Institute of Technical Physics, and East China Normal University.

Industry analysts view this as a critical milestone for Shanghai's ambition to build a nationally leading space computing industry cluster, offering on-orbit computing services that dramatically reduce reliance on ground-based data centers.

Outlook: The Tri-Dimensional Computing Revolution

From MIIT's 5G-A policy push and SpaceX's $52B AI server deal to Shanghai's space computing constellation — three seemingly independent developments point to a single mega-trend: global computing infrastructure is undergoing a fundamental paradigm shift. Terrestrial 5G/6G networks serve mobile AI terminals, cloud data centers power massive training and inference workloads, and space-based computing satellites fill the orbital processing gap. Computing is no longer confined to ground data centers but evolving toward an integrated space-terrestrial-edge-cloud architecture.

With China's manufacturing AI adoption now exceeding 30% and open-source AI models surpassing 10 billion downloads, AI technology is rapidly moving from laboratories into every industry and household.
