⚡ July 13 Geopolitics Update: US Launches Fresh Overnight Strikes on Iran's Qeshm Island, Satellite Confirms IRGC Naval Base Severely Damaged, Iraqi PM Visits Washington for Oil Deals

The US-Iran conflict escalated further overnight Sunday to Monday as CENTCOM launched a fresh wave of precision strikes targeting Iran's strategic Qeshm Island in the Strait of Hormuz. Satellite imagery confirmed severe damage to the IRGC Navy base, while Iran reported 8 military personnel killed and a telecommunications worker dead. Meanwhile, Iraqi Prime Minister Ali al-Zaidi departs for Washington today to sign oil and gas agreements aimed at reducing dependency on the Hormuz shipping route.

Fresh US Strikes Hit Qeshm Island and Bandar Abbas with 10+ Projectiles

U.S. Central Command announced a new wave of precision strikes late Sunday targeting Iranian missile systems and air defense installations on Qeshm Island, the largest island in the Persian Gulf with a population of roughly 150,000. According to Iran's state-run IRNA news agency, approximately 10 to 11 "enemy projectiles" struck military targets on Qeshm Island, while multiple explosions were also reported in the southern port city of Bandar Abbas and surrounding areas.

CENTCOM stated the operation aimed to "degrade Iran's ability to threaten commercial shipping," with targets including missile launch sites, drone storage facilities, ammunition depots, and command-and-control infrastructure. Iran's Fars News Agency reported additional explosions in western Bandar Abbas, where air defense systems were reactivated to confront "hostile targets." Explosions were also heard in Bushehr province in southwestern Iran.

Iran's state broadcaster IRIB confirmed that 8 members of the Iranian armed forces — including personnel from both the Air Force and Navy — were killed in the strikes in southern Iran, specifically in the Bandar Abbas and Bushehr regions.

Satellite Imagery Confirms IRGC Naval Base Hangars and Piers Destroyed

Fresh Sentinel-2 satellite imagery has confirmed that the IRGC Navy base in Hormozgan province sustained significant structural damage from the overnight strikes. The imagery analysis reveals:

  • Hangar severely damaged: The large hangar at coordinates 26°33'46.47"N 57°04'58.90"E shows clear structural damage
  • Pier infrastructure hit: Two piers at 26°33'52.20"N 57°04'41.77"E and the shiplift dock at 26°33'51.38"N 57°04'55.20"E show strike damage indications
  • Roadside structure destroyed: A structure along the road at 26°33'43.67"N 57°05'06.51"E was also hit

The base, responsible for coordinating IRGC naval operations in the Strait of Hormuz, suffered what analysts describe as the most severe single attack since the conflict began. Damage to its hangar and dock facilities will significantly impair Iran's ability to project naval power in the strategic waterway.

Iran Retaliates: Hormuz Re-Closed, Claims 85 US Military Installations Destroyed

In retaliation, the IRGC once again declared the Strait of Hormuz "closed until further notice." The IRGC claimed to have destroyed 85 U.S. military installations, including the U.S. Fifth Fleet headquarters in Bahrain and Ali Al Salem Air Base in Kuwait, using missiles and drones.

Kuwait's Defense Ministry confirmed that three northern border posts were attacked, causing material damage, and that an offshore drilling platform belonging to the Kuwait Oil Company was struck by a hostile drone, injuring one worker. Kuwaiti air defense systems remain actively engaged in intercepting incoming drones and missiles. Qatar previously reported 3 wounded, including a child, and Jordan reported three Iranian missiles landing inside its territory causing minor damage.

CENTCOM pushed back on Iran's Hormuz closure claim on X, stating: "Iran does not control the strait. Traffic is flowing," adding that U.S. forces are "positioned and prepared to ensure freedom of navigation." Meanwhile, Iran's Tasnim News Agency reported that a telecommunications worker was killed and two colleagues injured in an attack on Farur Island in Bandar Lengeh, Hormozgan province.

Iraqi PM Visits Washington: Oil Deals to Reduce Hormuz Dependency

Iraqi Prime Minister Ali al-Zaidi departs for Washington today in his first foreign visit since taking office in May. Government spokesperson Haider al-Aboudi confirmed that Baghdad will sign multiple memorandums of understanding in the oil and gas sector with U.S. companies to boost production capacity and develop alternative export routes, reducing Iraq's exposure to Strait of Hormuz disruptions.

"The agreements to be signed will include several memorandums of understanding in the oil and gas sector as Iraq prepares to bring in various U.S. companies that will provide momentum to increase oil production capacity." — Haider al-Aboudi, Iraqi government spokesperson

Al-Zaidi is scheduled to meet President Donald Trump at the White House, with discussions also covering strengthening Iraq's armed forces. Iraq has been seeking to balance its ties with neighboring Iran and the U.S. amid the ongoing military escalation, as the Hormuz crisis has significantly reduced Iraqi oil export revenues.

Additionally, NATO Secretary-General Mark Rutte is set to visit Washington on July 14 to discuss Ukraine weapons aid packages with Trump, who previously previewed a "major statement" on Russia for that date.

UN Chief Calls for Immediate Ceasefire

UN Secretary-General Antonio Guterres issued a statement urging both the United States and Iran to halt the renewed fighting and return to negotiations. The statement specifically referenced U.S. strikes on Iran and Iranian attacks on Strait of Hormuz shipping and U.S. allies in the Gulf. "These attacks must stop," Guterres' spokesman Stephane Dujarric said. Pakistan's Deputy Prime Minister and Foreign Minister Ishaq Dar also called for "restraint and diplomacy" in a phone call with his Iranian counterpart.

Qeshm Island Governor Hossein Amir Teymouri told IRNA that all targets struck were military and no civilian casualties have been reported, but warned that the escalating fighting poses severe risks to the island's 150,000 residents. Since the fragile April ceasefire, the U.S. and Iran have exchanged three rounds of major strikes in the past week, hitting a cumulative total of over 300 targets. The June MoU is effectively in a state of collapse.
