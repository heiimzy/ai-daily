⚡ July 24 International Politics Deep Dive: US 13th Night Strikes Expand to Central Iran, House Votes 214-208 to Limit War Powers, Trump 'Close to Decision' on Unprecedented Massive Attack

1. US 13th Consecutive Night Strikes Expand to Central Inland Iran

The United States carried out its 13th consecutive night of airstrikes against Iran late Thursday, marking a significant escalation as the bombing range expanded from coastal provinces into central inland territory for the first time. US Central Command (CENTCOM) confirmed the strikes began at 6:45 PM ET Thursday (2:15 AM Iran time Friday), lasting approximately 2 hours and 15 minutes.

CENTCOM stated the operation targeted "Iranian military command centers, drone storage facilities, communication networks, coastal surveillance sites and maritime capabilities," aimed at further degrading the Islamic Revolutionary Guard Corps' (IRGC) ability to threaten commercial shipping. According to Iran's semi-official Tasnim News Agency, the strikes hit at least six provinces:

  • Hormozgan Province: Multiple explosions in Bandar Abbas port city, with debris hitting a building, injuring two and causing power outages from damaged utility poles. Qeshm Island — a critical energy logistics hub at the Strait of Hormuz entrance — was also struck.
  • Khuzestan Province: Ahvaz, Andimeshk, and Omidiyeh all targeted by US missiles.
  • Bushehr Province: A power substation near Iran's only civilian nuclear plant was hit, causing several hours of blackouts.
  • Markazi Province (Central): Khondab was struck — the first confirmed US strike in west-central inland Iran, signaling a major geographic expansion.
  • Lorestan Province: Khorramabad, a major western city, also hit, triggering massive explosions.

Iran's state TV IRIB confirmed that a US missile strike near the Shalamcheh border crossing with Iraq killed two people and wounded 11. Iran's Health Ministry reported that cumulative US strikes since June 27 have now killed 55 people and wounded 629, with 36 still hospitalized.

"The 13th night of strikes represents a fundamental shift in US attack strategy — from purely coastal military facilities to deep inland targets. This is not just geographical expansion; it's the substantive application of the Trump administration's 'escalation leverage.'" — Regional Security Analyst

2. US House Passes 214-208 War Powers Resolution to Limit Iran Conflict

On the same day as the 13th night of strikes, the US House of Representatives voted 214-208 to pass a war powers resolution calling on President Trump to end hostilities with Iran. This marks the second such rebuke from the House since the war began in February. Four Republicans — Tom Barrett (Michigan), Brian Fitzpatrick (Pennsylvania), Warren Davidson (Ohio), and Thomas Massie (Kentucky) — crossed party lines to join Democrats.

However, the resolution is non-binding and does not require the president's signature. Even if passed as a joint resolution requiring presidential action, Trump could veto it, and overriding a veto requires a two-thirds supermajority in both chambers — virtually impossible under current political conditions. Shortly after the House vote, Senate Democrats attempted to advance their own war powers resolution, which failed 47-49 — two previously supportive Republicans declined to vote.

Lead sponsor Rep. Pramila Jayapal (D-WA) said after the vote: "From the beginning, this war has had no clear mission, no strategy, no end goal. Congress has not been consulted. And hostilities have been driven over and over again by a president who won his election by promising to end forever wars."

The nearly five-month US-Iran war has cost an estimated $37.5 billion, with the Pentagon seeking an additional $67 billion in supplemental funding. 18 US service members have been killed and nearly 500 wounded — 100 of those in the past two weeks alone. Over 50,000 US troops are currently deployed across the Middle East.

3. Trump Tells Axios: 'Very Close to a Decision' on Massive Attack

President Trump issued his strongest escalation signal yet in an exclusive interview with Axios, saying he is "seriously considering" launching a new wave of massive military strikes against Iran — larger than the previous "Operation Epic Fury" campaign.

"I am considering a massive attack. Bigger than ever before. I am very close to making a decision. We are all set for it," Trump told Axios. Two US officials confirmed to the outlet that the White House has not yet issued new operational orders, but all options remain on the table.

Regarding allies, Trump said Israel would join "within two minutes if I asked," but stressed "we don't need anyone" to conduct new military operations against Iran. He acknowledged that Israeli participation could invite Iranian retaliation against Israel.

On diplomacy, Trump said Iran is still "desperate" for talks but "not ready" for a deal. "They need more of the same. They've got some evil intentions. We cannot let them have a nuclear weapon," he said. Secretary of State Marco Rubio echoed this stance at the ASEAN summit in Manila, saying Iran is "begging for a deal" but the US will maintain pressure until Tehran "wakes up."

In a parallel development, Trump announced on Truth Social a new policy to use frozen Iranian assets held by the United States to compensate for damages to ships, cargo, and related losses: "Effective immediately and until further notice, any damage to Ships, Cargo, or anything related thereto will be paid for by Iranian Money that the United States has in its possession, and controls." Iranian Foreign Minister Abbas Araghchi condemned the move as an "incendiary precedent," warning that "once governments normalize confiscation, no one's assets are safe."

4. Dual Blockade: Houthi Red Sea Attacks + Hormuz Closure Push Brent Past $100

The escalating Middle East crisis has evolved into a perfect storm for global energy markets. Yemen's Houthi rebels announced a full maritime blockade of Saudi Arabia this week, claiming they struck two Saudi oil tankers — the Encelia and Layla — with cruise missiles, ballistic missiles, and drones. Saudi state news agency SPA confirmed the Encelia was hit, with a fire breaking out at the bow. The UK Maritime Trade Operations (UKMTO) reported a vessel was struck approximately 70 nautical miles southwest of Al Shuqaiq, Saudi Arabia, causing a fire but no casualties.

The Houthis framed the attacks as "siege-for-siege" retaliation for what they describe as a 12-year Saudi blockade of Yemen. Houthi military spokesman Yahya Sarea claimed the group had "forced approximately 10 ships to retreat." Trump responded on Truth Social, warning that if the Houthis attack again, "major military punishment will be inflicted upon Iran and, of course, the Houthis, themselves."

In the Strait of Hormuz, the IRGC Navy claimed full control, stating that one tanker attempting to transit exploded and caught fire after entering a mined route, while two others turned back immediately. "The Strait of Hormuz is under our control... and any ship that is deceived by the US and intends to pass without coordination with the Islamic Republic of Iran will suffer the same fate," the IRGC warned. Ship-tracking data showed only one tanker transited the strait on July 24 — the lowest level since May 7.

With both the Strait of Hormuz (20% of global oil trade) and the Bab el-Mandeb Strait (7% of global oil supply) under threat, Brent crude surged more than 6% to break back above $100/barrel for the first time since late May. WTI rose above $92. Goldman Sachs warned that if the conflict escalates to cover both chokepoints, oil could hit $120/barrel in Q4. US gasoline prices have already breached $4/gallon.

5. Israeli Drone Shot Down Over Iran, Eight Nations Condemn Israeli Minister

Amid the intensifying US-Iran conflict, a new military flashpoint emerged between Israel and Iran. Iranian air defenses shot down an Israeli Orbiter reconnaissance drone that had infiltrated Iranian airspace over Hormozgan province. Iran's Southeast Air Defense Zone Command stated the drone had radar-evasion capabilities and was destroyed using a "special system." The drone's wreckage was recovered with the assistance of Hormozgan provincial maritime police. The Israel Defense Forces have not issued an official statement.

Meanwhile, the Lebanon situation is also deteriorating. Lebanon's Health Ministry reported that an Israeli strike on the town of Sirghabiya in the Nabatieh district killed 11 people, including one child and six women, with nine others wounded. Since Israel launched military operations against Lebanon on April 2, the death toll has reached 3,151 with 1,571 wounded. Israeli Chief of Staff General Zamir visited the Northern Command on Friday and approved continued operational plans against Hezbollah.

On the diplomatic front, foreign ministers from the United Arab Emirates, Jordan, Turkey, Egypt, Indonesia, Pakistan, Saudi Arabia, and Qatar issued a joint statement condemning in the "strongest terms" Israeli National Security Minister Itamar Ben-Gvir's "shocking, degrading, and unacceptable" treatment of detainees from the Gaza-bound flotilla intercepted by Israel. UN Secretary-General António Guterres warned the Security Council that the Middle East situation is "spiraling out of control," with political objectives increasingly overshadowed by the conflict itself.
