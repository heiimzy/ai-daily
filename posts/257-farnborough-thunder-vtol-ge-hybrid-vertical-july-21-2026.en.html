⚡ Farnborough Day 1 Triple Launch: Anduril's 'Thunder' Autonomous Attack Rotorcraft, GE's 30,000ft Hybrid-Electric Flight, Vertical's First Public eVTOL Transition

1. Anduril & Archer Unveil 'Thunder' Autonomous Attack Tiltrotor

The 2026 Farnborough International Airshow opened with a bang as defense tech company Anduril Industries and Archer Aviation jointly unveiled "Thunder," a Group 5 autonomous attack rotorcraft designed specifically to fly alongside AH-64 Apache and next-generation crewed attack helicopters. The announcement represents a major leap from fixed-wing autonomous combat aircraft into the rotorcraft domain.

Thunder uses a tiltrotor design combining vertical takeoff and landing (VTOL) capability with efficient wingborne cruise. Its series hybrid-electric powertrain and Optimum Speed Tiltrotor (OSTR) technology deliver range and speed comparable to the MV-75 Cheyenne II tiltrotor while reducing fuel consumption and acoustic signature. Anduril SVP Shane Arnott told reporters at Farnborough that Thunder has "the range and speed of the Cheyenne and Apache-equivalent payload capacity," covering all key performance benchmarks of current主力 attack helicopters.

The aircraft features modular internal weapon bays in its belly and nose. The main bay can carry 10 Hellfire or JAGM air-to-ground missiles, 16 launched effects (tube-launched drones), or 76 70mm rockets. The nose module can mount 12 counter-UAS interceptors. Thunder is powered by Anduril's Lattice mission autonomy software, enabling autonomous formation flying, navigation, and mission coordination while human pilots focus on tactical decisions. Anduril says a single Apache teamed with three Thunders could triple the munitions carried into combat without risking additional personnel.

Critically, Thunder is designed to be transportable inside a standard shipping container for rapid global deployment. Anduril and Archer have completed multiple test flights with full-scale surrogate aircraft, with Thunder's first flight planned for 2027 and production targeted for 2029-2030. Archer will announce its first commercial partners for the civilian variant later this week.

2. GE Aerospace Achieves World's First Hybrid-Electric Flight Above 30,000ft

On the same day, GE Aerospace announced at Farnborough that it had completed the world's first hybrid-electric flight above 30,000 feet (9,144 meters) — the same altitude as commercial passenger aircraft — in collaboration with NASA, BETA Technologies, and Boeing. The team's single longest flight in hybrid-electric operation exceeded two hours, validating the feasibility of hybrid-electric propulsion in real-world aviation conditions.

The record-breaking test campaign was enabled by GE Aerospace's fully integrated megawatt-class, multi-kilovolt hybrid-electric propulsion system developed through NASA's Electrified Powertrain Flight Demonstration (EPFD) project. BETA Technologies served as systems integrator, and BETA pilots ferried the aircraft to the UK for the airshow, operating in hybrid-electric mode during each leg of the journey. Public demonstration flights are planned as part of daily Farnborough flying displays throughout the week.

Industry analysts note that hybrid-electric propulsion is one of the critical technology pathways for aviation to achieve net-zero emissions targets. GE's 30,000ft breakthrough advances hybrid-electric from concept validation to engineering feasibility demonstration.

3. Vertical Aerospace Completes World's First Public eVTOL Transition Flight

British eVTOL pioneer Vertical Aerospace made history at Farnborough by completing the first public electric vertical take-off and landing transition flight. At 1:46 PM local time, Chief Test Pilot Simon Davies flew Vertical's full-scale prototype through a complete transition sequence — vertical takeoff, wingborne cruise, and vertical landing — in front of a global audience of aerospace leaders, regulators, and media.

The successful demonstration caps a year of significant commercial and technology milestones. Vertical recently announced a strategic partnership with Near Earth Autonomy to integrate advanced autonomous flight capability into its Valo aircraft, supporting future defense and commercial applications. The company also announced Sigma Air Mobility, a member of the Luxaviation Group, as a new customer.

Vertical plans to conduct flight demonstrations throughout the airshow week and has its full-scale Valo commercial model on display in Hall 4. Valo is a piloted, four-passenger eVTOL aircraft with zero operating emissions, and the company is also developing a hybrid-electric variant for extended range and mission flexibility.

4. Two Overarching Trends: Autonomous Warfare and Green Aviation

This year's Farnborough Airshow attracted a record 1,600 exhibitors, half of them defense companies — reflecting the profound impact of conflicts in Ukraine and the Middle East on global procurement. Major deals on Day 1 included British Airways selecting Pratt & Whitney GTF engines for 63 Airbus A320neo jets, Riyadh Air confirming six additional Airbus A350-1000s (bringing its total to 31), Philippine Airlines ordering 15 Boeing 787-10s, and SMBC Aviation Capital ordering 100 Boeing 737 MAX aircraft.

Two clear technology trends emerged from Day 1. First, the accelerated operationalization of autonomous combat systems — from Anduril's Thunder to Lockheed Martin's MORFIUS X-Rotor microwave counter-drone system (capable of destroying 50+ drones per flight), autonomous systems are rapidly moving from labs to battlefields. Second, the engineering breakthrough of green aviation technology — GE's high-altitude hybrid-electric flight and Vertical's eVTOL transition prove that electric and hybrid-electric propulsion have moved beyond conceptual stages.

UN Secretary-General António Guterres warned earlier this month: "Our institutions were built to govern machines that follow commands. They are not ready for machines that decide. And some lines, once crossed, cannot be uncrossed." This warning feels increasingly urgent in light of the autonomous systems unveiled at this year's Farnborough.
