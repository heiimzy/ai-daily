⚡ July 24 Tech Roundup: APEC Chengdu Statement Backs Open-Source AI with Security, AMD Launches Helios Rack to Rival Nvidia, Wistron Opens $700M Texas AI Chip Factory, Acrab Debuts 5nm Edge AI SoC

July 24 saw a flurry of technology developments across multiple fronts — 21 APEC economies including the US and China issued a landmark joint AI statement in Chengdu, marking the first ministerial-level consensus on open-source AI cooperation; AMD unveiled its MI400 series GPUs and Helios rack-scale AI system at Advancing AI 2026 in San Francisco, delivering 2.9 exaFLOPS of inference performance to directly challenge Nvidia's Vera Rubin; Nvidia supplier Wistron opened a $700 million AI chip factory in Fort Worth, Texas, becoming the first US-based mass production site for GB300 superchips; and AI chip startup Acrab launched its first 5nm edge AI SoC, the GELIX 1, supporting 100-billion-parameter local model inference.

1. APEC Chengdu Statement: Open-Source AI Included in Joint Ministerial Document for the First Time

The week-long APEC Digital and AI Ministerial Meeting concluded on July 24 in Chengdu, with all 21 member economies jointly releasing the "Chengdu AI Statement." The document explicitly supports open-source AI development at the APEC ministerial level for the first time, while emphasizing the importance of "strong security assurance," signaling a new phase of "open source + security" dual-track global AI governance.

China's Minister of Industry and Information Technology, Li Lecheng, stated at the press conference that the statement marks the first inclusion of open-source cooperation at the APEC ministerial level. The statement specifically supports models and projects "that employ strong security assurance through development and deployment." Li noted that all parties recognized AI risks and encouraged dialogue across government, private sector, and academia on cybersecurity, supply chain resilience, and online scams.

"Getting 21 economies, including both China and the United States, to recognize trusted open-source AI as something worth supporting is meaningful," said Wei Sun, principal analyst at Counterpoint Research. "The phrase 'strong security assurance' gives more security-conscious economies room to support open models while still demanding testing, transparency, data protection and deployment controls."

Winston Ma, adjunct professor at NYU School of Law, noted that the statement's "explicit alignment around open-source ecosystems and compute infrastructure" confirms that Asia-Pacific economies are moving from closed models to open-weight systems integrated with state-coordinated infrastructure.

The APEC meeting also signaled urgency around quantum computing preparedness. Janet De Silva, chair of the APEC Business Advisory Council Digital and Innovation Working Group, urged ministers to prepare for the quantum era, emphasizing the need for trusted encryption to secure the global financial system.

2. AMD Advancing AI 2026: MI400 Series GPUs and Helios Rack System Launch

At its Advancing AI 2026 conference in San Francisco (July 22-23), AMD CEO Lisa Su unveiled the AMD Instinct MI400 series GPUs, the Helios rack-scale AI system, and the 2nm EPYC Venice server CPU, launching AMD's most aggressive counteroffensive yet against Nvidia's AI infrastructure dominance.

Three MI400 GPU Variants

  • MI455X (Flagship): 432GB HBM4 memory, 19.6 TB/s bandwidth per GPU. 72 GPUs per rack deliver 2.9 exaFLOPS FP4 inference and 1.4 exaFLOPS FP8 training performance.
  • MI440X: Enterprise-optimized 8-GPU standard server form factor.
  • MI430X: HPC and scientific computing variant with 288 TFLOPS FP64 performance.

Helios Rack-Scale System

Helios is AMD's first complete rack-scale AI system, packing 72 MI455X GPUs alongside EPYC Venice CPUs and Pensando networking silicon. Each rack delivers 31TB of pooled HBM4 memory and 1.4 PB/s aggregate memory bandwidth. Priced at approximately $5-5.5 million per rack, AMD claims Helios outperforms Nvidia's Vera Rubin NVL72 on nearly every metric — 50% more HBM4 capacity, 50% more scale-out bandwidth, and 15-25% higher AI training performance.

Su announced that OpenAI has committed to deploying 6 gigawatts of AMD GPU capacity (including MI455X), while Meta has committed another 6 GW, totaling 12 GW. Microsoft Azure and Oracle were named as early Helios customers. OpenAI CEO Sam Altman joined Su on stage, underscoring the strategic significance of the AMD-OpenAI partnership.

EPYC Venice: World's First 2nm x86 Server CPU

Built on TSMC's 2nm process with the Zen 6 architecture, EPYC Venice scales up to 256 cores and 512 threads, delivering roughly 1.7x the performance of the prior-gen EPYC Turin. Venice begins shipping alongside Helios in the second half of 2026.

3. Wistron's $700M Texas Factory: First US-Based GB300 Mass Production

Taiwanese contract manufacturer Wistron held the grand opening of its $700 million AI chip factory in Fort Worth, Texas, on July 21 — the first US manufacturing base for an Nvidia supplier. The 324,000-square-foot (30,100 sq m) facility is already assembling Nvidia's GB300 Grace Blackwell Ultra superchips and will later produce Vera Rubin superchips.

Nvidia confirmed that Fort Worth is the first location where the GB300 has been built and mass-produced in the United States. Wistron plans to scale output to tens of thousands of computing boards per month and hire approximately 1,000 workers. Notably, before breaking ground, Wistron built a complete digital twin of the factory using Nvidia's Omniverse simulation software, testing layouts and workflows virtually before any physical construction began.

Fort Worth is rapidly becoming an AI infrastructure hub. The city and broader state have attracted a wave of AI infrastructure projects, including an $810 million data center bond nearby, as cheap land and power draw compute towards Texas. What was once exclusively an import product — Nvidia's flagship superchip — is now being assembled on American soil.

Separately, AMD and Cerebras Systems announced a technical partnership at Advancing AI 2026, combining Helios racks with the Cerebras Wafer-Scale Engine (WSE) for a "disaggregated inference" solution — Helios handles high-throughput prompt processing while Cerebras delivers ultra-low-latency token generation. The joint solution is expected in the second half of 2026 via Cerebras Cloud.

4. Acrab Launches GELIX 1: 5nm Edge AI Chip with 100B-Parameter Local Inference

AI chip startup Acrab has officially launched its first edge AI SoC, the GELIX 1, built on a 5nm process with 650 TOPS of AI compute power, enabling local inference of 100-billion-parameter models. The chip demonstrates significant advantages in hardware architecture, memory subsystems, and prefill performance.

GELIX 1 features a heterogeneous compute architecture integrating 20 Arm CPU cores, a 3 TFLOPS GPU, and a dedicated NPU. Its 650 TOPS NPU outperforms Qualcomm's Snapdragon X Elite (45 TOPS) and Intel Core Ultra (40 TOPS) by over 14x. The chip includes 8MB L1 cache and 768 GB/s shared L2 cache bandwidth, paired with 256-bit LPDDR5X memory at 8533 MT/s, effectively addressing the memory bandwidth bottleneck for LLM inference.

In prefill stage testing with a Gemma 26B model (40K KV cache, 10K token input), the chip achieved 1,416.8 tokens/s processing speed — 7.5x faster than Apple's M4 Pro Mac Mini at 188.9 tokens/s. Acrab also released a full-stack software toolchain and the Agent Box reference design terminal to lower developer adoption barriers.

The edge AI chip market is experiencing explosive growth. As large models migrate from cloud to edge, chips capable of running 100-billion-parameter models locally will become critical infrastructure for AI terminal applications.
