⚡ July 12 09:00 AI News: OpenAI Sunsets Atlas Browser, StepFun Debuts World's First AI Agent Smartphone, Korea's ReAcTree Doubles Agent Task Success Rate

⚡ OpenAI officially announces the shutdown of ChatGPT Atlas browser after less than a year, integrating its agentic browsing capabilities into the ChatGPT desktop app and a new Chrome extension; China's StepFun (Jieyue Xingchen) will unveil the world's first native AI agent smartphone from a large-model company on July 13, beating OpenAI's 2027 timeline; South Korea's ETRI presents ReAcTree hierarchical agent architecture at AAMAS 2026 — a 7B model achieves 61% task success rate, nearly double the 31% of a standard 72B approach; Stanford's Biomni AI co-scientist published in Science, now deployed in over 10,000 labs worldwide.

🔴 OpenAI Shuts Down Atlas Browser: Browser as a Feature, Not a Destination

OpenAI has announced it will sunset the ChatGPT Atlas browser on August 9, 2026 — less than a year after its October 2025 launch. Rather than abandoning browser-based AI, OpenAI is redistributing Atlas' agentic browsing features across two existing products: the ChatGPT desktop app (now with multi-tab support, account login, file downloads) and a new Chrome extension that competes directly with Google's Gemini Side Panel. TechCrunch notes that OpenAI concluded "the browser is a feature, not the destination" — AI should integrate into existing workspaces rather than inventing new ones. The Chrome extension allows users to query pages, summarize content, and start long-running tasks directly from Google Chrome.

📱 StepFun Beats OpenAI to Market: World's First AI Agent Smartphone

StepFun (Jieyue Xingchen) has announced a landmark event for July 13 at 19:00 Beijing time — the "StepFun Terminal Brand & Next-Gen Agent Strategy Launch" — where it will unveil an AI terminal brand, agent operating system, and the world's first native AI agent smartphone from a large-language-model company. This beats OpenAI's publicly stated 2027 timeline for its own AI terminal by nearly a year. The smartphone is manufactured by Huaqin Technology, a global smart hardware ODM leader whose products account for over 10% of global smartphone/notebook/tablet shipments. Huaqin strategically invested in StepFun during its Series B+ and continued through Series C, indicating a deep partnership beyond simple OEM. The launch represents a pivotal moment: large-model companies may no longer stop at API services but will deliver end-to-end hardware-software AI experiences.

🇰🇷 Korea's ETRI Presents ReAcTree: Hierarchical Agent Architecture Nearly Doubles Success Rate

The Electronics and Telecommunications Research Institute (ETRI) of South Korea presented ReAcTree at the AAMAS 2026 conference — a hierarchical AI agent system that decomposes complex tasks into tree-structured sub-agent collaborations. On the LoTA-Bench benchmark, ReAcTree with a 72B model achieves 61% task success rate, nearly double the 31% achieved by the standard ReAct method. Remarkably, when paired with a much smaller 7B model, ReAcTree still reaches 37% success rate — outperforming the conventional 72B approach. This means teams can achieve superior reliability with far less compute and lower inference costs. ETRI's Social Robotics Research Section Director Kim Do Hyung stated the next step is adding human-clarification capabilities to resolve uncertainty, pushing toward real-world deployment.

🔬 Stanford Biomni AI Co-Scientist Published in Science: 10,000+ Labs Already Using It

Stanford University's general-purpose biomedical AI agent Biomni has been published in the journal Science. Equipped with 68 specialized biomedical databases, 108 software packages, and 82 tools, Biomni autonomously executes complex research tasks including causal gene prioritization, drug repurposing, rare-disease diagnosis, and microbiome analysis. In real-world testing, Biomni completed data analysis, cleaning, and reporting in just 35-40 minutes — work that would take human scientists weeks. Over 10,000 laboratories are already using Biomni prototypes, making it the most widely deployed AI co-scientist system in the biomedical field.

📊 Industry Trend: The Race to AI Terminalization

From OpenAI folding Atlas capabilities into desktop and Chrome, to StepFun pioneering the AI agent smartphone, to the broader industry shift toward on-device AI — the second half of 2026 is clearly defined by one trend: AI is moving from cloud APIs to end-user devices. Meanwhile, Korea's ReAcTree demonstrates that "smaller model + better architecture" can beat "bigger model + traditional approach," signaling that AI Agent engineering is shifting from parameter scaling to architecture design. The industry is undergoing both a terminal revolution and an architecture revolution simultaneously.
