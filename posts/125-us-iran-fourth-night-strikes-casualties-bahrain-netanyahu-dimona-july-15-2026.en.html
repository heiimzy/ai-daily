⚡ July 15 International Politics Roundup: US 4th Night of Iran Strikes — 7 Killed, 260+ Wounded; IRGC Targets Fifth Fleet in Bahrain; Netanyahu Warns Tehran from Dimona Nuclear Reactor

1. US 4th Night of Strikes: 7-Hour Operation Hits Dozens of Targets, 260+ Wounded

US Central Command (CENTCOM) launched a fourth consecutive night of massive airstrikes on Iran on Wednesday, conducting a 7-hour precision operation targeting Iran's southern coastline and areas near the Strait of Hormuz. Fighter aircraft, drones, and naval vessels struck Iranian missile sites, drone bases, naval capabilities, and coastal defense systems, aiming to "further degrade Iran's ability to threaten commercial shipping and civilian crews."

The latest wave of strikes killed at least 7 Iranian soldiers and wounded over 260 people across the country, Iranian officials said. Government spokesperson Fatemeh Mohajerani confirmed on X that more than 30 civilians have been killed in US strikes over "recent days." Health Ministry spokesperson Hossein Kermanpour reported 260+ wounded, of whom 222 had been treated and discharged, including three minors under 18.

Cities including Abadan (a key oil hub), Bushehr (home to Iran's only civilian nuclear plant), Bandar Abbas, and Hajiabad came under repeated strikes. Bushehr governor Mohammad Mozafari told IRNA that US forces struck three locations in the city on Wednesday. A military barracks in Bampur, Sistan and Baluchestan province, was hit by 13 missiles, killing 7 soldiers and wounding many others.

2. IRGC Retaliates: Claims Destruction of Fifth Fleet HQ — Bahrain, Kuwait, Jordan Hit

Iran's Islamic Revolutionary Guard Corps (IRGC) announced Wednesday that its retaliatory operations had achieved major results. The IRGC claimed to have struck the US Fifth Fleet headquarters in Bahrain, destroying its command-and-control center, logistics facilities, fuel depots, and military equipment installations. State TV IRIB reported that the IRGC fired cruise missiles at the Mina Abdullah logistics center in Kuwait and launched drone strikes on the Al-Azraq air base in Jordan.

Bahrain's Interior Ministry sounded air raid sirens, urging citizens and residents to remain calm and head to the nearest safe place. Kuwait's military confirmed it was "engaging hostile drone attacks following the nefarious Iranian aggression." Jordan's army said it intercepted three ballistic missiles from Iran with no casualties or damage. A drone came down in Iraq's Faw port without causing damage.

The IRGC issued a stern warning: if Washington blocks regional oil and gas exports by controlling maritime routes, "other export routes serving US and allied interests could also be closed." The statement emphasized: "Oil and gas exports from the region will either be available for everyone or for no one."

3. Strait of Hormuz: "For Everyone or for No One"

The IRGC declared that the Strait of Hormuz would remain closed "until the United States ends its acts of aggression," while warning that other regional oil export routes could also become targets. "The enemy should know that now that its maritime raiders have blocked the Indian Ocean route for oil and gas exports to the world… it should also expect the closure of other oil and gas export routes that serve the interests of the United States and its allies," the statement said.

Meanwhile, Trump reversed his earlier plan to charge a 20% toll on Hormuz cargo, replacing it with trade and investment deals with Gulf states. However, the US military reimposed a full naval blockade on all Iranian ports at 20:00 GMT Tuesday. Over 20 US Navy warships and hundreds of military aircraft are now deployed across the Middle East.

Oil prices continued their upward trajectory, with Brent crude rising 1.72% to $86.19/barrel and WTI up 1.4% to $80.40/barrel, both at one-month highs.

4. Netanyahu Warns from Dimona: "Do Not Count on a Rerun"

Israeli Prime Minister Benjamin Netanyahu issued a stark warning to Iran during a speech at the Negev Conference in the southern city of Dimona on Tuesday. In a thinly veiled disclosure, Netanyahu revealed he had visited the Negev Nuclear Research Center in Dimona earlier that day, referring to it by the coded term "textile factory" — an instantly recognizable reference to Israel's undeclared nuclear facility.

"I say to the leaders of Iran: Do not count on things staying quiet if you attack us. Do not count on a rerun. Because this will not be a rerun, and the last one was powerful enough. This will be a different broadcast, far more powerful," Netanyahu warned. He emphasized that Israel has fundamentally changed its rules of engagement: "The days when someone hurts us and we do not strike them back twofold are over."

Analysts noted that Netanyahu's deliberate mention of visiting the Dimona facility before issuing the threat toward Tehran carries a multilayered message — signaling Israel's overwhelming strategic deterrence capability and its readiness to use every tool at its disposal to defend itself.

5. Trump: "Will Continue Until I Say Enough"

President Donald Trump told Fox News on Tuesday that strikes against Iran would continue "until I say enough." He further warned that if Tehran does not return to negotiations, the US would escalate next week to targeting Iran's power plants and bridges. "Next week it gets really bad for them because next week comes the power plants. Next week comes the bridges," Trump said. He added that the US is closely monitoring Iran's "Mount Pickaxe" underground nuclear facility near the heavily damaged Natanz enrichment site.

Trump revealed that Gulf states have agreed to major US investments in exchange for maintaining safe passage, eliminating the need for a shipping toll. He also confirmed that the US has struck Iran's Kharg Island — the hub for over 90% of Iran's oil exports — twice.

In a related development, the Israeli military announced Wednesday that it had sentenced an active-duty soldier to five years in prison for sending missile intercept videos to an Iranian agent in exchange for payment, underscoring Iran's extensive intelligence penetration in the region.
