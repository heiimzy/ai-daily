⚡ July 15 Financial Roundup: China H1 GDP Grows 4.7% Despite Headwinds, PBOC Record 1.4T Reverse Repo, Asia Markets Surge with KOSPI Circuit Breaker

🇨🇳 China H1 GDP Expands 4.7% as Economy Shows Resilience

The National Bureau of Statistics (NBS) released H1 2026 economic data on Wednesday, showing China's GDP reached 69.57 trillion yuan ($9.57 trillion) in the first half of 2026, up 4.7% year-on-year at constant prices. Q1 grew 5.0%, while Q2 recorded 4.4% YoY but expanded 0.9% quarter-on-quarter, matching market expectations.

NBS Deputy Head stated that the economy operated within a reasonable range, with robust production growth, stable employment, and moderate price increases. New growth drivers — particularly AI-related industries — maintained strong momentum in both investment and output. While Q2's slowdown was attributed to short-term and external factors, the NBS emphasized that H1 performance laid a solid foundation for achieving the full-year growth target of 4.5-5.0%.

Notably, China's energy supply remains sufficient, production stable, and imports under control — providing a critical buffer against global geopolitical turmoil and elevated energy prices.

🏦 PBOC Conducts Record 1.4 Trillion Yuan Reverse Repo Operation

The People's Bank of China (PBOC) executed a record-breaking 1.4 trillion yuan ($193 billion) 6-month buy-sell reverse repo operation on July 15, with a maturity date of January 15, 2027. The size sets a new all-time high for a single 6-month reverse repo operation.

With 900 billion yuan maturing on the same day, the net injection amounted to 500 billion yuan — the first net injection via 6-month reverse repo since March. Combined with a 200 billion yuan net injection from the 3-month reverse repo earlier this month, July's total net liquidity injection reaches 700 billion yuan, a sharp reversal from June's 300 billion yuan net withdrawal.

Analysts at Dongfang Jincheng cited two direct drivers: first, interbank rates (DR001 and DR007) have risen to near the policy rate of 1.4%, signaling a shift from the earlier loose liquidity environment; second, a significant ramp-up in government bond issuance in July requires monetary support. Analysts expect counter-cyclical policy adjustments to intensify in H2, with potential room for rate cuts and reserve requirement ratio (RRR) reductions.

📊 China H1 Foreign Trade Up 16.9%, AI Hardware Trade Surges 56.6%

China's total foreign trade in H1 2026 reached 25.47 trillion yuan ($3.75 trillion), up 16.9% year-on-year and surpassing 25 trillion yuan for the first time in a first-half period. Exports grew 13.4% to 14.73 trillion yuan (11 consecutive quarters of growth), while imports expanded 22.1% to 10.74 trillion yuan.

In June alone, trade totaled 4.78 trillion yuan, up 24.2% YoY — the 17th consecutive month of growth. USD-denominated June exports surged 27% (vs. 19.4% prior), imports jumped 36% (vs. 27.4% prior), with a trade surplus of $125.6 billion.

AI-driven computing hardware was the standout sector: imports and exports of electronic components and computer parts reached 5.13 trillion yuan, soaring 56.6%. AI glasses, AI translation devices, and powered exoskeletons are among the rapidly iterating smart products driving the boom.

📈 Asia-Pacific Stocks Rally Across the Board, KOSPI Surges 7% Triggering Circuit Breaker

Asia-Pacific equities surged on Wednesday, fueled by Tuesday's better-than-expected US CPI data. South Korea's KOSPI led regional gains, soaring over 7% at the open to trigger the SIDECAR circuit breaker. SK Hynix jumped over 10% in Seoul, following its ADR's 27% surge in New York. Samsung Electronics rose over 7%.

Japan's Nikkei 225 climbed 1.49%, while the Topix gained about 1%. The MSCI Asia Pacific index rose 1.2% overall.

The rally was driven by the sharp cooldown in US inflation: June CPI fell 0.4% month-on-month — the first decline in six years — pushing the annual rate down to 3.5% from 4.2%. Market pricing for a July rate hike collapsed from 41.7% to 16.6%, unleashing a wave of risk appetite across the region.

🏛️ Warsh Day 2 Senate Testimony Underway, June PPI Data Released

Federal Reserve Chair Kevin Warsh appears before the Senate Banking Committee on Wednesday for the second day of his inaugural semi-annual congressional testimony. In Tuesday's House hearing, Warsh struck a firmly hawkish tone, declaring "zero tolerance" for persistently elevated inflation and announcing five new task forces to comprehensively review the Fed's policy framework from "a blank sheet of paper."

"If we get policy right — and we will — the inflation surge of the last five years will be a thing of the past," Warsh stated in prepared remarks. He emphasized data-dependent decision-making and refused to provide forward guidance. On AI, he noted high-tech equipment spending is growing at nearly 25% annually, though the economic benefits of the AI buildout remain uncertain.

Also on Wednesday, the Bureau of Labor Statistics released June Producer Price Index (PPI) data. Analysts expect PPI to moderate slightly from May's 2.2% YoY, but rising energy costs from Middle East tensions pose upside risks. A sustained PPI uptick could feed into core PCE — the Fed's preferred inflation gauge — complicating the rate path ahead.

💎 ChangXin Memory Technology IPO Priced at 8.66 Yuan, Set to Be Largest on STAR Market

Chinese DRAM leader ChangXin Memory Technology (CXMT) set its STAR Market IPO price at 8.66 yuan per share, with subscription opening on July 16. The offering is expected to raise approximately 580 billion yuan ($80 billion) before any over-allotment option, making it the largest IPO in the STAR Market's history. If the greenshoe option is fully exercised, total proceeds could reach 666 billion yuan, implying a market capitalization of approximately 579.2 billion yuan at listing.

Strategic investors include Montage Technology, Tongfu Microelectronics, and AMEC, each receiving 18.24 million shares for 158 million yuan. CXMT's listing is widely regarded as a milestone in China's push for semiconductor self-sufficiency in the memory chip sector.

🛢️ Oil Holds Above $80, Trump Abandons Hormuz Toll Plan

Crude oil prices remained elevated above $80/barrel on Wednesday. WTI crude settled 2.16% higher at $79.83, while Brent crude gained 2.21% to $85.14. The US-Iran conflict continues to escalate, with the US reinstating its naval blockade of Iranian ports and Hormuz Strait transit volumes plummeting, affecting nearly 20 million barrels per day of global crude exports.

President Donald Trump abandoned his plan to impose a 20% "protection fee" on cargo vessels transiting the Strait of Hormuz, instead seeking trade and investment agreements with Gulf states. Oil prices pulled back slightly from intraday highs following the announcement. Citigroup noted that oil remains subject to a geopolitical risk premium, with markets closely watching the trajectory of US-Iran hostilities.

In precious metals, gold rebounded to $4,058/oz, with COMEX gold futures up 1.31%. The US Dollar Index weakened to 100.77, while EUR/USD rose to 1.1425 and GBP/USD to 1.3405. Cryptocurrencies traded with a mild positive bias, with Bitcoin testing its 50-day EMA and Ethereum breaking above the $1,800 resistance level.

Global markets are navigating a dual narrative of "cooling inflation + escalating geopolitics." The CPI surprise gives central banks breathing room, but the gunfire at the Strait of Hormuz reminds investors that energy-driven inflation risks are far from over.
