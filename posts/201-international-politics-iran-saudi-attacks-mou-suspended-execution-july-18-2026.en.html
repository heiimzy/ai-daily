⚡ July 18 International Evening: Iran Strikes Saudi Arabia for First Time in 3 Months, Formally Suspends Ceasefire MoU, Executes 2022 Protest Convict

🔴 Major Escalation: Iran Strikes Saudi Arabia for First Time in Three Months, Targets US Airbase

Iran launched missile and drone attacks on Saudi Arabia on Saturday evening, marking the first time in nearly three months that the conflict has directly touched Saudi soil. According to two sources familiar with the matter who spoke to The Straits Times, the strikes triggered early warning alarms in Al-Kharj, east of the capital Riyadh, and Yanbu, on the kingdom's Red Sea coast.

One of the attacks targeted the Prince Sultan Airbase in Al-Kharj, which hosts U.S. military personnel. This is Iran's first direct strike on Saudi territory since April, representing a significant geographic expansion of the U.S.-Iran conflict. Saudi state media did not elaborate on what triggered the early warnings, and the government media office did not respond to requests for comment. The IRGC made no mention of any attack on Saudi Arabia in its official statements.

Analysts note that by bringing Saudi Arabia into the conflict, Tehran is widening its deterrence strategy from \"Gulf allies\" to \"all U.S. allies in the Gulf.\" Saudi Arabia — the most influential Arab power in the region — had previously attempted to maintain neutrality in the U.S.-Iran confrontation.

📜 Iran Formally Suspends Islamabad MoU: "US Has Violated All Commitments"

Iran's Deputy Foreign Minister Kazem Gharibabadi announced Saturday evening that Tehran has suspended all of its commitments under the Islamabad Memorandum of Understanding (MoU). \"The US has violated and suspended all its commitments within the framework of the Islamabad MoU,\" Gharibabadi stated.

The Islamabad MoU — a temporary ceasefire agreement brokered by Pakistan in June 2026 — included provisions for reopening the Strait of Hormuz, lifting U.S. oil sanctions on Iran, and halting Iranian attacks on Gulf shipping. The agreement effectively collapsed on July 7 when Iran struck three ships in the Strait, triggering renewed U.S. airstrikes. Since then, both sides have conducted daily strikes for seven consecutive days.

Earlier, Supreme Leader military adviser Mohsen Rezaei had declared the MoU \"de facto void\" on July 17, citing five U.S. violations including failing to secure Israeli withdrawal from southern Lebanon, creating an illegal alternative shipping lane through the Strait, and launching military aggression against Iran's coast. Gharibabadi's statement marks the first official diplomatic confirmation of the suspension.

⚖️ Iran Executes 2022 Protest Convict Amid International Criticism

Iran executed a prisoner on Saturday who had been convicted of killing a security force member during the 2022 nationwide protests, according to the Iranian judiciary. The convict was found guilty of killing a Basij militia member near Tehran in November 2022. Despite repeated appeals from international human rights organizations to halt executions, Iran's judicial system proceeded with the punishment.

The 2022 protests — sparked by the death of Mahsa Amini in morality police custody — were the largest popular uprising in Iran since the 1979 Islamic Revolution. Iranian authorities subsequently prosecuted thousands of participants, with hundreds receiving long prison sentences and dozens sentenced to death. Saturday's execution adds to ongoing tensions between Iran and the international community over human rights, coming against the backdrop of an escalating war with the United States.

🚨 Bahrain Sounds Air Raid Sirens for Sixth Time; Kuwait Oil Facility Devastated

Bahrain's Interior Ministry confirmed that air raid warning sirens were activated for the sixth time on Saturday as Iran continued its aerial campaign against Gulf states hosting US forces. Iran's Revolutionary Guards claimed to have struck Sheikh Isa Air Base in Bahrain, destroying a US drone depot and the country's main artificial intelligence center. Bahrain's military said its air defense systems had \"thwarted\" multiple waves of Iranian attacks.

In Kuwait, the Kuwait Petroleum Corporation (KPC) confirmed that a vital oil facility was hit by \"repeated brutal Iranian attacks,\" resulting in multiple injuries and \"significant material losses.\" This marks the second such strike on Kuwaiti infrastructure in two days — following an earlier attack on a power generation and water desalination plant. Kuwait's Foreign Ministry condemned Iran for \"endangering the lives and safety of civilians,\" calling it a \"flagrant violation of international law\" and demanding an immediate cessation of attacks.

Jordan's military confirmed it shot down four Iranian drones entering its airspace over the past 24 hours, following the earlier interception of 10 Iranian missiles.

🛢️ Hormuz Transits Hit Three-Week Low; Brent Crude Breaks $88

Following the seventh consecutive night of U.S. airstrikes on Iran, ship transits through the Strait of Hormuz — the conduit for roughly 20% of global oil traffic — fell to their lowest level in three weeks. Shipping tracker Kpler confirmed just 8 vessels passed through the Strait on Thursday, half of which were Iranian. Iran's IRGC reiterated that no oil tankers or cargo vessels would be permitted through the Strait until U.S. military operations cease.

U.S. Central Command announced it is fully enforcing a naval blockade of Iranian ports, with more than 50,000 American service members deployed across the Middle East. Brent crude settled at $88.10/barrel on Friday, up 4.6% on the day with a weekly gain of approximately 16% — the largest in months. President Trump has threatened to expand strikes to include Iranian underground nuclear facilities at \"Mount Ghareh\" and even launch ground operations to seize Iranian islands in the Strait, options that would significantly escalate the conflict toward all-out war.

🇺🇦 Ukraine Launches Massive 379-Drone Attack Across 19 Russian Regions, 8 Killed

Ukrainian forces launched a massive drone offensive overnight on July 17-18, targeting 19 Russian regions plus occupied Crimea, the Sea of Azov, and the Black Sea. Russia's Defense Ministry claimed its air defenses intercepted 379 Ukrainian drones — one of the largest single attacks on Russian soil in nearly three years.

The attack killed at least 8 people and wounded more than 60 others. In Kotovsk, Tambov region, Ukrainian drones struck a logistics center operated by Wildberries — Russia's largest online retailer — killing 7 night-shift workers and wounding 25. A second Wildberries warehouse in Elektrostal, Moscow region, was also hit, killing 1 and wounding 37. Moscow region Governor Andrei Vorobyov said 8 of the wounded were in serious condition.

Ukrainian President Volodymyr Zelenskyy confirmed that the targeted logistics facilities were used by Russia to \"supply sanctioned components for the production of drones and navigation equipment.\" Ukrainian forces also struck an oil depot in Noginsk, sparking a massive fire that forced the evacuation of a nearby maternity hospital and residential building. Ukraine's Unmanned Systems Forces separately reported striking 13 Russian vessels in the Black and Azov Seas over the past day.

The massive drone attack comes amid political turmoil in Kyiv. Zelenskyy abruptly dismissed Defense Minister Mykhailo Fedorov on Wednesday — the 35-year-old tech visionary who had spearheaded Ukraine's drone industry expansion, including AI-driven autonomous targeting drones and ground robots. His dismissal triggered protests in Kyiv and other cities on Thursday and Friday, with thousands demanding his reinstatement.
