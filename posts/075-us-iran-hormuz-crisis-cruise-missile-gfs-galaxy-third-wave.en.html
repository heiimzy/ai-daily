⚡ July 12 World Politics: Iran Fires Cruise Missile at Cargo Ship, Strait of Hormuz Re-Closed, US-Iran Ceasefire Effectively Collapses

IRGC Fires Cruise Missile at Cyprus-Flagged Container Ship

Iran's Islamic Revolutionary Guard Corps (IRGC) Navy fired a naval cruise missile at a merchant vessel that ignored warnings early Sunday (July 12), dramatically accelerating the cycle of escalation between Tehran and Washington. The semi-official Fars News Agency reported that the missile struck a ship attempting to transit the Strait of Hormuz, causing severe structural damage and igniting a fire onboard.

The United Kingdom Maritime Trade Operations (UKMTO) confirmed an incident approximately 9 nautical miles east of Oman. U.S. Central Command (CENTCOM) identified the stricken vessel as the M/V GFS Galaxy, a Cyprus-flagged container ship. The vessel suffered "significant engineroom damage" and an onboard fire. One civilian crew member is missing, and the entire crew was forced to abandon ship into lifeboats. India's Ministry of External Affairs confirmed 11 Indian nationals were aboard — 10 rescued, one still missing — and condemned the attack.

"Iran made a poor choice. Now they pay." — U.S. Defense Secretary Pete Hegseth on social media

Strait of Hormuz Closed Indefinitely, Fresh Energy Shock Looms

The IRGC Navy declared the Strait of Hormuz closed "until further notice and until the end of U.S. interference in this region," barring all vessels from transiting the strategic waterway. The Guards said multiple ships "disregarded our warnings and instructions to correct their course and proceed along the approved route," and one "was struck by a warning shot and brought to a stop."

The closure came just hours after Iranian Foreign Minister Abbas Araghchi met with Omani Foreign Minister Sayyid Badr Albusaidi in Muscat to discuss safe passage mechanisms. CNN reported that Oman presented a draft proposal for a two-corridor system: the southern route through Omani territorial waters would revert to pre-war freedom of navigation, while the northern route through Iranian waters would require "prior approval" from Tehran — without tolls. However, Iran's delegation was reportedly unable to approve the proposal and returned to Tehran for internal consultations.

Despite the Iranian declaration, the UKMTO confirmed that the southern route remains available and has been expanded to accommodate two-way traffic. However, war risk insurance premiums have surged from roughly 2% to 5% of vessel value, and the International Maritime Organization has advised ships to avoid the area.

CENTCOM Completes Third Wave: 140 Iranian Targets Struck

In response, U.S. Central Command launched the third round of strikes this week at 7:15 p.m. ET Saturday, targeting approximately 140 Iranian military objectives including missile and drone launch sites, naval capabilities, ammunition depots, communication networks, and coastal surveillance facilities. Over three nights of operations, more than 300 targets have been struck — one of the heaviest bombardment campaigns since the war began on February 28.

Iranian state media reported explosions across multiple southern provinces: Bushehr, Hormozgan, Khuzestan, and Sistan and Baluchestan. Port cities including Bandar Abbas, Sirik, Jask, Chabahar, and Qeshm Island all reported blasts. Iran's Health Ministry said the first two rounds of strikes killed at least 17 people and wounded 115, with the latest casualty figures yet to be released.

Iran Retaliates: Missiles Rain Down on Five Gulf States

The IRGC launched a massive retaliatory barrage targeting U.S. military assets across five regional countries:

  • Qatar: Al Udeid Air Base struck by ballistic missiles; three people including a child injured by falling shrapnel. Qatar's Foreign Ministry condemned a "dangerous escalation"
  • Oman: Port of Duqm logistics and refueling platforms supporting U.S. aircraft carriers targeted. Oman government condemned the attack on its territory
  • Bahrain: Air raid sirens sounded for the third time this week at the U.S. Navy's 5th Fleet headquarters
  • Kuwait: U.S. radar site and Camp Arifjan targeted; air defense systems intercepted incoming fire
  • Jordan: Three Iranian missiles landed without casualties; IRGC claimed destruction of a U.S. command center and drone hangar

The IRGC also claimed to have struck and disabled a second vessel in the Strait of Hormuz. The UAE's National Emergency Crisis Authority activated air defense systems, later stating missile threats were "outside its borders."

June Memorandum of Understanding Effectively Dismantled

The June 17 Memorandum of Understanding (MoU) between the U.S. and Iran has effectively collapsed after a week of continuous tit-for-tat attacks. Iran's chief negotiator Mohammad Bagher Ghalibaf posted on X: "The era of one-sided deals is OVER. We told you: keep your word or pay the price. Reality is knocking." The post featured Article 5 of the MoU concerning the Strait's reopening, with the phrase "the Islamic Republic of Iran will make arrangements" highlighted.

President Trump declared the ceasefire "over" last week but also stated the U.S. "accepts Iran's request to continue talks." The White House insists negotiations remain possible, but only if Iran publicly guarantees open passage through the Strait and ceases attacks on civilian vessels. Complicating matters further, CNN satellite imagery analysis revealed Iran has been repairing and rebuilding facilities at the Parchin Military Complex's Taleghan 2 site — a facility associated with nuclear weapons-related explosives testing — while the MoU was nominally in effect. The U.S. Treasury has imposed new sanctions on Iranian businessmen linked to Supreme Leader Khamenei.

"We have taken control of the Strait of Hormuz with power, and we will preserve it with power as well." — Ebrahim Rezaei, Iranian parliament spokesman

New Supreme Leader Speaks: Vows Revenge

Iran's new Supreme Leader, Ayatollah Mojtaba Khamenei — unseen in public since the war began on February 28 — issued his first written statement on Saturday, vowing revenge for the killing of his father, former Supreme Leader Ali Khamenei. "Revenge is the will of our nation and must certainly be carried out," the statement carried on state television declared.

The statement came hours after Trump posted on Truth Social, threatening to "completely decimate and destroy all areas of Iran" in the event of an assassination attempt: "1,000 missiles are locked and loaded and aimed at the Islamic Republic of Iran, with thousands of more to immediately follow, should the Iranian government act on its threat to assassinate the sitting President of the United States."

Global Reactions and Outlook

Saudi Arabia condemned Iran's "destabilizing behavior"; Pakistan's Deputy PM Ishaq Dar held a phone call with his Iranian counterpart urging restraint; Kuwait described the attacks as a "highly dangerous escalation"; and Lebanon's PM Nawaf Salam expressed solidarity with all nations targeted by Iranian strikes.

Before the war, roughly one-fifth of the world's oil and LNG supplies passed through the Strait of Hormuz. Iran's effective control over the waterway has driven energy prices higher, creating a politically sensitive issue for Trump ahead of November's midterm elections. Analysts warn the region is at a dangerous inflection point, with the risk of escalation into a wider regional war increasing by the hour.
