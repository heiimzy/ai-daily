⚡ XPENG Unveils TuringViT Vision Foundation Model: Linear Attention Redefines Physical AI Perception Across ADAS, Cockpit, and Robotics

TuringViT: A Vision Encoder Revolution for the VLM/VLA Era

July 21 evening — XPENG Group officially released TuringViT, a high-efficiency vision encoder that systematically restructures visual architecture design, data paradigms, and training pipelines for the VLM/VLA (Vision-Language Model / Vision-Language-Action Model) era. The vision encoder serves as the "perception gateway" of physical AI — every scene understanding, state judgment, and action decision begins with high-quality visual feature extraction. The industry's prevailing approach of "repurposing open-source general ViTs" has increasingly fallen short of the combined demands for performance, latency, and customization in real-world scenarios like autonomous driving and embodied robotics.

TuringViT achieves breakthroughs across three dimensions — architecture, data, and training — building a complete technical system centered on "linear attention dominance + high-quality data curation + native dynamic resolution," delivering triple improvements in effectiveness, efficiency, and deployability. The model will serve as foundational visual infrastructure across XPENG's three core business units: advanced autonomous driving (XNGP), intelligent vehicle cockpits, and the IRON bionic humanoid robot program.

Technical Breakthrough: Linear Attention Delivers 3x Throughput

TuringViT's core innovation rests on three key technical designs:

First, Turing Linear Attention (TLA). Traditional Softmax attention in Vision Transformers incurs quadratically growing computational costs at high resolutions, creating a real-time inference bottleneck. TLA fundamentally restructures sequence modeling through linear attention mechanisms, dramatically reducing compute overhead for high-resolution image processing. At 1536×1536 resolution, TuringViT-18L achieves 3.04× the throughput of Seed1.5-ViT and 2.16× that of SigLIP2-ViT-L — meaning vehicle and robot systems can simultaneously process higher-resolution visual information without sacrificing inference speed.

Second, the VISTA-Curation data pipeline. TuringViT was trained on just 850 million image-text pairs yet achieved an average score of 83.6% across six zero-shot benchmarks, surpassing open-source baselines trained on 10 billion samples. This "10% data, superior results" outcome stems from VISTA-Curation's quality-first strategy — constructing supervision-rich image-video training data to achieve a step-change in data efficiency.

Third, native dynamic-resolution pretraining. Traditional ViTs typically pretrain at low resolution and adapt post-hoc, incurring performance degradation in high-resolution scenarios. TuringViT natively supports dynamic-resolution inputs from the pretraining stage, flexibly adapting to diverse resolutions and aspect ratios across camera and robot sensor hardware, enabling seamless transfer from training to deployment.

Three-Scenario Deployment: A Unified Vision Foundation from ADAS to Robotics

The launch of TuringViT marks a significant step forward in XPENG's full-stack self-developed physical AI capabilities. Each of the three core deployment scenarios has distinct requirements:

Smart Driving — Empowering VLA 2.0. High-resolution perception now truly lands on-vehicle. TuringViT's low-latency characteristics enable VLA 2.0 to process multi-channel HD camera data in real-time during high-speed driving scenarios, accurately recognizing road signs, pedestrians, obstacles and complex traffic scenes. XPENG's earlier second-generation VLA had already demonstrated a 72-billion-parameter foundation model capability; TuringViT provides a more efficient visual front-end for this system.

Smart Cockpit — Natural fusion of vision and language. By integrating TuringViT with language models, the cockpit system can not only "see" passenger facial expressions and gestures but also understand the intent behind these visual signals, enabling truly natural multimodal human-machine interaction.

IRON Humanoid Robot — A universal vision capability foundation. The IRON robot must navigate far more unstructured environments than autonomous vehicles. TuringViT provides a universal visual perception foundation — from indoor furniture recognition to dynamic object tracking and grasping, the same vision encoder can be reused across different robot embodiments. XPENG has indicated that the production-version IRON humanoid robot's hardware and software development is progressing smoothly and is approaching the hardware-software integration phase.

The Physical AI Strategy: Unified Intelligence from Cars to Robots

TuringViT's release represents a critical piece of XPENG's "Physical AI" strategy. XPENG's VP of Intelligent Driving, Liu Xianming, who fully unveiled the physical world foundation model technical framework at CVPR 2026, has emphasized that foundation models must understand the physical world — and understanding the physical world begins with ingesting sufficient density of physical world information, which is precisely the core value of the vision encoder.

XPENG's physical AI strategy has formed a multi-tiered technical architecture: TuringViT serves as the perception gateway at the bottom layer; the middle layer features the dual-track foundation model system of second-generation VLA and world models — the former enabling models to "act like humans" and the latter helping models understand "how the world changes after actions"; the top layer encompasses application deployment across autonomous driving, smart cockpits, IRON robots, flying cars, and other embodied carriers.

The TuringViT paper and technical report have been published on arXiv, and XPENG has also released partial model weights and evaluation code to advance the community's development of vision encoders. Analysts suggest that TuringViT may accelerate the paradigm shift in physical AI visual perception from "repurposing general ViTs" toward "scenario-customized vision encoding" — following a similar trajectory as NLP's evolution from general BERT to domain-specific models.

In XPENG's strategic blueprint, the same physical AI foundation model will power all embodied intelligence scenarios from cars to robots to flying cars. TuringViT, as the "eyes" of this unified intelligence system, directly determines the upper bound of how well higher-level models can understand the physical world.
