⚡ July 20 International Politics: Houthis Declare Maritime Blockade of Saudi Arabia, Iran Announces Full-Scale War, Pakistan-Qatar Propose 10-Day Ceasefire

1. Houthis Declare Comprehensive Maritime Blockade of Saudi Arabia

Yemen's Houthi rebels on Monday announced a comprehensive maritime blockade of Saudi Arabia, effective immediately — a dramatic escalation that threatens to close the Red Sea to Saudi shipping and tighten the noose on global energy supplies already squeezed by the Strait of Hormuz crisis.

"We declare a maritime embargo against the criminal Saudi enemy, based on the equation of 'an eye for an eye,' effective immediately upon the issuance of this statement." — Houthi military spokesman Yahya Saree

The announcement comes after the two sides traded fire last week for the first time in years. The Houthis accused Saudi Arabia of striking Sanaa International Airport on July 13 to prevent an Iranian flight carrying a Houthi delegation from landing. In retaliation, the Houthis launched ballistic missiles and suicide drones at Saudi Arabia's Abha International Airport.

Strategic implications: The Houthis control key areas around the Bab el-Mandeb Strait, the southern gateway to the Red Sea and Suez Canal — one of the world's busiest trade corridors. Saudi Arabia has been relying on its East-West pipeline to bypass the blocked Strait of Hormuz, shipping approximately 4.6 million barrels per day from its eastern oil fields to the Red Sea port of Yanbu. A Houthi blockade threatens to sever this alternative export route entirely.

2. Iran President Declares 'Full-Scale War' with the United States

Iranian President Masoud Pezeshkian on Monday declared that his country is engaged in a "full-scale war" with the United States — the first time Tehran's leadership has used this terminology since the conflict began on February 28.

"The reality is that today the Islamic Republic of Iran is engaged in a full-scale war. We must be realistic and accept the natural consequences of this resistance." — President Masoud Pezeshkian

Previously, Iranian officials had used milder terms such as "confrontation" or "standoff." The shift in language signals Tehran's recognition that the conflict has fundamentally changed in nature. Pezeshkian's statement came as his interior minister departed for Islamabad for diplomatic talks amid continued fighting.

Iran's Supreme Leader Mojtaba Khamenei, in a statement on Saturday, warned of "unforgettable lessons" if the US kept attacking the Islamic Republic and called Trump's signature on the interim ceasefire deal "worthless and invalid." Deputy Foreign Minister Gharibabadi announced that Tehran had formally suspended its commitments under the Islamabad Memorandum of Understanding.

3. Pakistan and Qatar Propose Joint 10-Day Ceasefire Plan

In a significant diplomatic push, Pakistan and Qatar jointly drafted a de-escalation proposal urging the US and Iran to return to their pre-July 9 positions "as a first step" to resume stalled peace talks, Pakistani government sources told Anadolu Agency on Monday.

Key elements of the proposal:

  • Iran to immediately reopen the Strait of Hormuz
  • The US to end its blockade of Iranian ports and lift sanctions on Iranian oil sales
  • A 10-to-14-day cessation of hostilities to halt retaliatory military strikes
  • Resumption of talks in either Islamabad or Doha, with implementation of the southern Lebanon ceasefire as a priority agenda item

Iranian Foreign Ministry spokesman Esmaeil Baqaei confirmed on Monday that Tehran had received proposals from mediators aimed at de-escalation and was reviewing them, while reaffirming Iran's commitment to diplomacy.

The diplomatic effort comes as both sides escalate militarily. The US completed its ninth consecutive night of strikes against Iran, while Iran launched waves of missiles and drones at US military facilities in Jordan, Kuwait, and Bahrain.

4. US Completes 9th Consecutive Night of Strikes, Hits Tabriz for First Time

US Central Command (CENTCOM) confirmed on Monday that it had completed its ninth consecutive night of strikes against Iran, targeting military command centers, air defense and coastal surveillance sites, maritime capabilities, missile and drone launch sites, and communications networks.

For the first time in the latest campaign, US strikes reached the northwestern city of Tabriz. Iranian state media reported that at least one person was killed and several wounded in the strike southwest of Tabriz. The city is believed to house Iran's underground "missile cities" — complex launch facilities used to fire the ballistic missiles that killed two US service members in Jordan on Friday.

Iran's retaliatory operations included:

  • Missile strikes from Lorestan province at "enemy targets" across the region
  • IRGC ballistic missile attack on US aircraft at Jordan's Aqaba Airport, claiming "heavy damage" to C-17 transport and P-8 command aircraft
  • Strikes on Kuwait's Ali Al Salem Air Base, destroying an early-warning radar system
  • Drone attacks on Bahrain's air navigation systems and US 5th Fleet facilities
  • IRGC claimed two oil tankers attempting to transit the Strait of Hormuz "exploded and were brought to a halt"

President Donald Trump told reporters after returning from the World Cup final: "We hit them very hard again tonight, and we did that in honor of the, probably three, it's probably three great patriots." He claimed Iran had "lost everything almost militarily" and that the US "controls the strait."

US Secretary of State Marco Rubio said Washington remained open to diplomacy but insisted any deal must be "real" and one Iran is "willing to live by." Brent crude rose more than 3% on Monday, climbing above $90 a barrel for the first time since June 11.

5. The Dual Blockade Crisis: From Hormuz to the Red Sea

The Middle East is facing its most severe energy supply chain threat since the 1973 oil crisis. The Strait of Hormuz — through which 20% of global oil once passed — has seen traffic fall to roughly 8 transits per day (from a pre-war average of 140). Now the Houthi blockade of Saudi Arabia threatens to close the Red Sea as well, meaning both major oil export chokepoints could be simultaneously blocked.

Analysts warn that if both the Strait of Hormuz and the Bab el-Mandeb remain inoperable for an extended period, Saudi Arabia's East-West pipeline becomes irrelevant — oil can reach the Red Sea coast but cannot be exported. Capital Economics has warned that a prolonged dual blockade could push the global economy into recession.

Israel's military chief of staff, Lieutenant General Eyal Zamir, said the IDF was maintaining "a high level of readiness" and was "prepared to immediately resume combat." The southern city of Eilat saw interceptor missiles launched as Iranian missiles flew toward Jordan's Aqaba port just across the border, bringing the risk of spillover closer to Israeli territory for the first time in weeks.

Diplomatic efforts are accelerating alongside the military escalation. Beyond the Pakistan-Qatar joint proposal, Qatari mediators have reportedly floated a separate 10-day cessation of strikes to find ways to revive the interim deal. However, with Khamenei declaring Trump's signature "worthless" and the president ordering continued strikes, the window for diplomacy is narrowing rapidly.
