⚡ July 21 Financial Morning Roundup: Asian Markets Diverge, A-Share Mega Hydro Project Explodes, China Deploys $84B Rescue, US Chip Stocks Fight Back

1. Asian Markets Open Mixed on Tuesday: Nikkei Rebounds, KOSPI Reverses, A-Shares Rally

Asian stock markets opened with mixed performance on Tuesday as investors stayed cautious ahead of a pivotal week of Big Tech earnings, while elevated geopolitical tensions and trade frictions continued to weigh on sentiment.

Japan's Nikkei 225 staged a technical rebound after Monday's Marine Day holiday, rising 0.39% to around 64,826. Kioxia surged 3.38% and SoftBank Group gained 0.74%, leading the semiconductor recovery. The rebound comes after last week's 6.4% crash — the Nikkei's worst weekly performance since April 2025.

South Korea's KOSPI opened sharply lower on panic selling but reversed to close up 0.23% at 6,531 as bargain hunters stepped in. Samsung Electronics rose 1.33% and SK Hynix gained 0.85%. The index had plunged 4.46% on Monday and is now down over 28% from its June peak, with the leverage unwind still ongoing.

India's benchmarks rallied sharply: the Sensex surged 442 points (+0.54%) to 82,200 while the Nifty 50 gained 122 points (+0.49%) to 25,090. ICICI Bank (+3%) and HDFC Bank (+2%) powered the rally after strong Q1 results, with foreign institutional investors net buying ₹375 crore.

Australia's ASX 200 was the regional laggard, falling 0.53%.

Key focus this week: Alphabet, Tesla and Intel earnings will test the AI capex narrative. Fed September rate hike probability has risen to 55%, with the 10-year Treasury yield hovering near 4.6%.

2. A-Share Morning: 1.2 Trillion Yuan Super Hydropower Project Ignites Infrastructure Rally

China's A-share markets opened higher across the board on Tuesday. The Shanghai Composite rose 0.44% to 3,559 at midday, the Shenzhen Component gained 0.29%, and the ChiNext added 0.12%. Half-day turnover reached 1.09 trillion yuan, up 70.8 billion from the previous session, with over 3,500 stocks advancing and 91 hitting the daily limit-up.

The biggest catalyst was the official launch of the lower Yarlung Tsangpo River super hydropower project — China's largest single infrastructure investment in years at 1.2 trillion yuan (~$168 billion), announced on July 19. The news sent infrastructure-related sectors into overdrive:

  • Explosives: Poly Union, Gaozheng Mining both limit-up; Beihua shares hit 3 consecutive limit-up
  • Water/Engineering: China Power Construction (PowerChina), China Energy Engineering gapped up limit; Tibet Tianlu, Guanlong Energy also surged
  • Steel/Building Materials: Liuzhou Steel hit limit-up (14th limit in 8 trading days); cement sector rallied broadly
  • Construction Machinery: Sector led gains, with major names sharply higher

Robot stocks also extended gains after Unitree officially filed for an IPO listing辅导. Shanghai New Title (上纬新材) on the STAR Market extended its winning streak to 9 consecutive limit-ups, setting the highest barometer in the current market.

In contrast, AI hardware stocks continued to correct, with some names falling over 5%. AIGC and diversified financial sectors edged lower. The market is displaying a classic seesaw dynamic between "infrastructure" and "tech" capital allocation.

3. US Overnight Close: Semiconductor Stocks Mount Rebound, AI Selloff Pauses

Wall Street closed modestly lower on Monday (July 20) but semiconductor stocks staged a powerful intraday rebound. The Dow fell 307 points (-0.59%) to 51,839, the S&P 500 lost 0.19% to 7,443, and the Nasdaq edged down 0.05% to 25,508.

The Philadelphia Semiconductor Index (SOX) surged nearly 4% at its intraday peak before closing +0.6%. The index has now fallen over 20% from its late-June peak, confirming a bear market. Key chip movers:

  • Micron +1.94%, SanDisk +2.67% — memory stocks rebound
  • AMD +1.58%, Broadcom +2%, Intel +2.1%
  • Optical networking: Lumentum +4.47%, Marvell +3.32%
  • Nvidia edged up 0.23%

Big Tech diverged: Google (+1.5%) gained on reports of developing Gemini-integrated server chips; Microsoft (+2.15%) hit an intraday record; Apple fell 2% weighing on the Dow; Tesla dropped 2.96%. Chinese ADRs outperformed with the Nasdaq Golden Dragon China Index rising 0.9%.

Brent crude eased 0.4% to $88.86/barrel, while WTI crude rose 1.1% to $82.68. Oil remains elevated as Houthi rebels declared a maritime blockade on Saudi Arabia, threatening Red Sea shipping lanes.

4. China's "National Team" Steps In: CSRC Emergency Meeting + $84B Rescue Package

After Monday's brutal session (266 stocks limit-down), Chinese authorities unleashed a coordinated market rescue campaign:

  • CSRC emergency symposium: Chairman Wu Qing hosted investor座谈 and vowed to "fully maintain stable market operations," followed by a separate symposium with brokerages and fund managers on Tuesday
  • State-backed funds deployed: China Guoxin and China Chengtong jointly injected over 60 billion yuan (~$8.4B) via PBOC swap facilities
  • ETF buying spree: In Monday's final 30 minutes alone, multiple flagship broad-based ETFs saw trading volume surge several-fold. The CSI 300 ETF (Huatai-PineBridge) recorded weekly net inflows of 21.4 billion yuan; CSI 1000, CSI 500, ChiNext and STAR 50 ETFs each saw inflows exceeding 10 billion yuan
  • Top insurers join forces: Five major insurance groups (China Life, Ping An, etc.) with combined investment assets over 20 trillion yuan announced daily net purchases exceeding 10 billion yuan, declaring "strong confidence in China's economic outlook"
  • Share buyback wave: A new wave of listed company buybacks emerged, with three brokerages announcing up to 700 million yuan in share repurchases
Analysts note this is the most aggressive rescue effort since early 2026. The CSI 300 and Shanghai 50 surged over 3% on Monday as coal, power, and banking blue chips benefited. However, small-cap tech names remain under pressure from ongoing margin liquidation, creating an acute structural divergence in the market.

5. Global Market Outlook: Triple Threat of Tech Earnings + Middle East + Trade War

Global financial markets face a complex confluence of forces this week:

Tech earnings decide AI narrative: Alphabet (Wednesday), Tesla (Thursday), and Intel report this week. The key question: can AI capex sustain its torrid pace? JPMorgan notes that while AI chip leaders have suffered double-digit declines, DRAM supply-demand tightness is expected to persist through 2028.

Geopolitics the wild card: Houthi blockade of Saudi Arabia threatens 7% of global oil supply via Bab al-Mandab. A 10-day ceasefire proposal between the US and Iran is being mediated. Brent crude is trading in a $88-91 range — a ceasefire breakthrough could trigger sharp downside, while escalation could push it above $95.

Trade tensions heating up: The Trump administration announced plans for a 50% tariff on select Canadian products (alcohol, autos, dairy), with a 30-day consultation period. Meanwhile, Commerce Secretary Howard Lutnick expressed optimism about reaching a deal with the EU.

Monetary policy outlook: Fed September rate hike probability rose to 55% from 51%. The ECB meets Thursday — rates are expected to hold but a September hike is increasingly priced in. China's PBOC kept LPR steady (1Y: 3.0%, 5Y: 3.5%). New Zealand Q2 CPI came in at 2.7% YoY, below expectations, opening the door for further RBNZ rate cuts.

Citigroup upgraded China equity allocation to "overweight," citing superior earnings growth potential in China and South Korea.
