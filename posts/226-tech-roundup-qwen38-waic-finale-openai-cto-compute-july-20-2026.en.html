Alibaba Qwen 3.8: 2.4 Trillion-Parameter Open-Weight Model Claims Second Only to Claude Fable 5

On Sunday evening, Alibaba's Qwen team announced the release of Qwen3.8-Max-Preview — a 2.4 trillion-parameter Mixture-of-Experts multimodal AI model — via its official X account, with a promise to release open weights in the future. In its announcement, Alibaba described the model as "one of the most powerful models available today, trailing only Anthropic's Claude Fable 5" among the systems it benchmarked against.

This marks Alibaba's first model crossing the 2 trillion parameter threshold, arriving just 10 months after its first trillion-parameter model Qwen3-Max-Preview (September 2025) and only 3 months after Qwen 3.6 Max (April 2026). Qwen 3.8 upgrades to a native multimodal architecture supporting text, images, video, and document inputs, using sparse MoE design to activate a subset of parameters per token for inference efficiency.

The preview is now available on Alibaba's Token Plan subscription service (international tier $6-$68/week) as well as its Qoder and QoderWork coding agent platforms. Alibaba has pledged to release open weights "soon," which would make Qwen 3.8 one of the largest open-weight models ever released. Developers using existing OpenAI and Anthropic protocol-compatible tools can point them at Qwen3.8 without rebuilding their workflows.

Notably, Alibaba has yet to publish independent third-party benchmark results. The "second only to Fable 5" claim currently rests on Alibaba's own internal evaluations — the same approach Moonshot AI used when launching Kimi K3. The AI community is watching closely for verification from Artificial Analysis and LMArena.

In terms of parameter scale, Chinese AI labs have collectively entered the "multi-trillion parameter era." Both Moonshot's Kimi K3 (2.8 trillion) and Alibaba's Qwen 3.8 (2.4 trillion) claim near-frontier performance against closed U.S. models — the performance gap between open and closed models is narrowing by the week.

WAIC 2026 Concludes: Humanoid Robot "Year of Mass Production" Officially Begins

The four-day 2026 World Artificial Intelligence Conference (WAIC) wraps up today in Shanghai. The event attracted over 1,100 exhibitors showcasing more than 3,000 products, including over 300 global debuts, with total exhibition space exceeding 100,000 square meters for the first time.

The central theme of this year's WAIC was "deployment" rather than "demonstration." Unlike previous years where robots performed flashy somersaults and dances, this year over 300 humanoid robots served across the venue as guides, information assistants, and unmanned retail operators — functioning as employees rather than exhibits. AGIBOT deployed 60 robots across the venue managed by its RaaS platform Qingtian Rent, operating with completely autonomous shift changes, charging, and fault handling.

Key industry milestones announced at WAIC include:

  • Leju Robotics' Kuafu series achieves 95%+ domestic component rate: Partnering with Huixi Intelligence to develop a domestically produced high-performance control solution, filling the final gap in full-stack domestic humanoid robot production. Their automotive factory picking and logistics solution was selected as a Ministry of Industry and Information Technology AI typical application case.
  • Overall humanoid robot core component localization rate exceeds 75%: A complete self-owned supply chain is taking shape from underlying computing chips and actuated joints to multimodal perception sensors.
  • MIIT predicts 2026 annual humanoid robot production to exceed 100,000 units, up from approximately 20,000 global shipments in 2025 — 2026 is widely recognized as the "Year of Mass Production."
  • VLA and World Model dual-track development: Tencent released three embodied foundation models (Hy-Embodied-VLM-1.0, RxBrain-1.0, VLA-0.5); Da Xiao Robot open-sourced Kairos 3.1 world model at WAIC; Ant LingBot launched Brain 2.0.

China's National Development and Reform Commission disclosed at the conference that the country's AI core industry exceeded 1 trillion yuan ($147 billion) in 2025, with 2026 growth projected above 30%. AI penetration across key industrial sectors has crossed 80%, and AI-equipped smartphone and PC sales are on track to surpass non-AI devices for the first time this year.

OpenAI Names CTO of Compute: From xAI Poach to Computing Supremacy

OpenAI has promoted former xAI infrastructure leader Uday Ruddarraju to Chief Technology Officer, Compute — placing him in charge of the company's entire compute and infrastructure expansion. Ruddarraju joined OpenAI in July 2025 after leaving xAI, where he led the Colossus supercomputing cluster project.

Announcing the promotion on LinkedIn, Ruddarraju stated OpenAI's mission is "to build the world's largest compute footprint so frontier AI can reach everyone and every workflow." He revealed the team is undertaking a systematic expansion spanning distributed systems, hardware, manufacturing, and data center engineering — covering civil, mechanical, and electrical engineering disciplines.

This appointment signals a full escalation of the AI compute arms race. In just one year, Ruddarraju has moved from xAI's Colossus to OpenAI's computing leadership, spanning the two poles of AI compute competition. OpenAI CEO Sam Altman simultaneously admitted the company "underperformed over the past year" and promised "the next 12 months will be the company's strongest yet."

In his farewell message to xAI, Ruddarraju thanked Elon Musk personally, saying the Colossus experience showed him "what focused execution really looks like." NVIDIA CEO Jensen Huang has previously remarked that Musk and his teams are "singular in what they can achieve."

NVIDIA Brings 22 Japanese Industrial Robot Giants into Cosmos Alliance

NVIDIA announced in Tokyo that 22 core Japanese industrial robotics companies will join its Cosmos open world model coalition. The list includes FANUC, Yaskawa Electric, Kawasaki Heavy Industries, Sony, Hitachi, Honda, SoftBank, and Mitsubishi — covering the two largest industrial robot manufacturers in the world by installed base.

NVIDIA's Cosmos 3 Edge, a 4-billion-parameter model based on the Nemotron family running on Jetson edge hardware, is designed to be customizable for specific robots, vehicles, or sensor rigs in approximately one day. Fujitsu is leading the most specific component, a collaborative control platform bridging digital and physical functions in industrial settings, being evaluated by FANUC, Yaskawa, and Kawasaki.

"Japan invented modern manufacturing," Huang said in Tokyo. "Now it has the opportunity to reinvent it for the era of smart industries." He emphasized that "every nation and every company should own and control its intelligence infrastructure — open models make that possible."

This strategy represents NVIDIA's play against Tesla's vertically integrated Optimus program: provide the open stack (Cosmos + Isaac + Omniverse + Newton physics engine) and let industrial partners own the development.

Anthropic Fable 5 Free Access Ends Today: Credit-Based Transition

After three deadline extensions, Anthropic's Claude Fable 5 included access for paid subscribers officially ended at 11:59:59 PM PT on July 19 (2:59 PM Beijing time July 20). Starting today, Pro, Max, and Team subscribers who want to continue using Fable 5 must purchase prepaid usage credits at $10 per million input tokens and $50 per million output tokens.

Anthropic stated all three extensions (June 22 → July 7 → July 12 → July 19) were infrastructure-driven rather than policy-driven. The company has committed to restoring Fable 5 to subscription plans once compute capacity allows, but has not provided a timeline.

Notably, Claude Max and Team Premium users retain 50% weekly access to Fable 5 within their subscription limits, while Pro and Team Standard users must switch to pay-per-use API pricing — though they receive a one-time $100 usage credit as transition compensation. Claude Code's 50% weekly usage limit boost also ended with the same deadline.
