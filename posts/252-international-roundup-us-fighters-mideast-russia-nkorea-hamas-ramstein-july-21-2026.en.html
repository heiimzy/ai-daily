⚡ July 21 International Politics Roundup: US Rushes F-16/F-35 Fighters to Middle East, Russia-North Korea Strategic Dialogue, Hamas Elects New Leader

🇺🇸 US-Iran Tensions Escalate: Pentagon Rushes Fighter Jets Back to Middle East

The United States is deploying additional F-16 and F-35 fighter jets from European bases to the Middle East amid escalating tensions with Iran, a US official told CNN on Monday. Aerial refueling tankers are also being sent. An Israeli source revealed that dozens of US tanker aircraft have already arrived in Israel in recent days, with more still incoming. According to the source, approximately 94 refueling aircraft were deployed during the March operation. That number later fell to 40 but has been rising since last week.

President Donald Trump was briefed in the White House Situation Room last week on options to escalate military action. Options under consideration include seizing Iran's key oil export terminal at Kharg Island or striking Iran's nuclear facilities at Pickaxe Mountain. However, Trump has not ruled out a diplomatic resolution. Secretary of State Marco Rubio said Sunday that the door to negotiations remains open.

Meanwhile, a US service member was killed in northern Iraq during the controlled detonation of a downed Iranian drone — the third American fatality in recent days. Two US troops were killed in a separate Iranian ballistic missile attack on a base in Jordan, with a third still missing. Iran has continued striking targets in Kuwait, Bahrain, and Jordan, with a Kuwaiti power and desalination plant hit for the second consecutive day.

🇷🇺🇰🇵 Russia-North Korea 3rd Strategic Dialogue: Jointly Condemn US Military Actions

Russian Foreign Minister Sergey Lavrov and North Korean Foreign Minister Choe Son-hui held the third round of Russia-North Korea strategic dialogue in Moscow on Monday, Russia's Foreign Ministry announced. The two ministers agreed that ongoing tensions in Northeast Asia and globally stem from "irresponsible actions" by the United States and its allies.

The joint statement noted that the US and its allies continue to intensify provocative military activities around the Korean Peninsula. In response, deepening Russia-North Korea relations serves both regional and global security interests while building a "just, multipolar" new international order based on "equality, mutual respect, and mutual benefit." The two sides also discussed implementation of the Comprehensive Strategic Partnership Treaty signed by President Putin and Chairman Kim Jong-un in June 2024.

Notably, President Putin met Choe Son-hui at the Kremlin on July 19, thanking North Korea for its assistance with Russia's special military operation. Analysts say Russia-North Korea strategic coordination is accelerating against the backdrop of the expanding US-Iran conflict and rising tensions in Northeast Asia.

🇵🇸 Hamas Elects Khalil al-Hayya as New Political Bureau Chairman

The Islamic Resistance Movement (Hamas) announced Sunday that Khalil al-Hayya has been elected as the new chairman of its Political Bureau, defeating former leader Khaled Mashal in an internal run-off vote within the 69-member Shura Council. Al-Hayya succeeds Yahya Sinwar, who was killed by Israeli forces in October 2024.

Al-Hayya, 65, was born in Gaza City and has served as Hamas's chief negotiator in ceasefire and hostage exchange talks with Israel. His election signals that Hamas's leadership center will remain in the Gaza Strip, and the organization is expected to maintain its close ties with Iran. Al-Hayya faces critical challenges including shaping negotiation strategy with Israel, deciding Hamas's future role in Gaza governance, and managing relations with Iran amid regional instability. He will lead the Political Bureau until the next scheduled elections in 2027.

🇮🇱🇱🇧 Israel Begins Pilot Zone Withdrawal in Southern Lebanon

The Israel Defense Forces began withdrawing from the first "pilot zone" in southern Lebanon on Monday, marking the first implementation of the framework agreement signed between Israel and Lebanon in Washington last month, according to Xinhua and The Times of Israel. Under the agreement, Israel will withdraw from two small areas in southern Lebanon cleared of Hezbollah infrastructure, with the Lebanese Armed Forces (LAF) taking over.

A US official confirmed the first pilot zone withdrawal would proceed "in a matter of days," with CENTCOM coordinating between both countries. Lebanon's President Joseph Aoun has linked continued participation in Rome negotiations to the start of Israeli withdrawal. Israel has emphasized that troops will remain in a 10-kilometer "security zone" as long as Hezbollah remains armed. Italy may assume a monitoring role, and the international community is expected to be called upon to help Lebanon "restore effective sovereignty."

🇺🇦🇩🇪 29th Ramstein Meeting: New Military Aid Pledges for Ukraine

The 29th meeting of the Ukraine Defense Contact Group (Ramstein format) convened online Monday, with newly appointed Ukrainian Defense Minister Denys Shmyhal participating for the first time. Shmyhal urgently called on partners to facilitate the purchase of US weapons for Ukraine, especially Patriot systems and PAC-3 interceptor missiles.

Key outcomes from the meeting include:

  • Germany: Committed to providing five Patriot air defense systems to Ukraine through a US-led initiative, plus 200,000+ rounds of Gepard ammunition and funding for Ukrainian-made long-range drones
  • Netherlands: Pledged €200 million for interceptor drones and €125 million for F-16 maintenance and logistics
  • Norway: Announced a $1.09 billion drone procurement program, of which $436 million will purchase Ukrainian-made drones
  • Canada: $14.6 million for tank maintenance under the "Danish model"
  • Sweden: Preparing a new aid package including air defense, artillery, and equipment

The United States announced a new NATO-coordinated mechanism enabling European countries to purchase American weapons for Ukraine. The meeting brought together 52 countries and organizations, including NATO Secretary General Mark Rutte, US Defense Secretary Pete Hegseth, and EU High Representative Kaja Kallas. Shmyhal noted Ukraine needs $6 billion to cover this year's defense procurement gap.
