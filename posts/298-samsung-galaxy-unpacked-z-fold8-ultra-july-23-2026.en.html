⚡ Samsung Galaxy Unpacked: Z Fold8, Fold8 Ultra, Flip8, Watch 9 and Watch Ultra 2 Debut in London

Samsung held its Galaxy Unpacked 2026 summer event in London on July 22, unveiling its most extensive foldable lineup overhaul to date. The company announced three foldable smartphones — the redesigned Galaxy Z Fold8, the first-ever Galaxy Z Fold8 Ultra, and the Galaxy Z Flip8 — alongside the Galaxy Watch 9 and Galaxy Watch Ultra 2, showcasing Samsung's vision for mobile devices in the AI era.

Galaxy Z Fold8: Redesigned with a Wider Form Factor for Mass Adoption

The highlight of the event, the Galaxy Z Fold8, introduces a completely redesigned form factor. Moving away from the tall-and-narrow design of its predecessors, the Z Fold8 is shorter and wider, folding into a compact shape reminiscent of a wallet. It features a 5.5-inch FHD+ cover display (16:10 aspect ratio) and a 7.6-inch QXGA+ inner display, offering a user experience closer to an iPad Mini.

Under the hood, the Z Fold8 is powered by the Qualcomm Snapdragon 8 Elite Gen 5 for Galaxy processor, paired with a 4,800mAh battery supporting 45W fast charging. The device measures 4.5mm thick when unfolded and 9.7mm folded, weighing just 201 grams — making it the lightest Galaxy Z Fold ever. The camera system includes a 50MP main sensor (with 2x in-sensor zoom) and a 50MP ultra-wide lens.

Samsung has applied Flex Titanium technology — a new titanium-alloy hinge structure — to the Z Fold8 for the first time, effectively reducing the display crease while improving durability. Starting at $1,899.99, the Z Fold8 is priced lower than its predecessor, signaling Samsung's push to make foldables more mainstream.

Galaxy Z Fold8 Ultra: Ultra-Level Foldable Excellence

The Galaxy Z Fold8 Ultra marks Samsung's first time bringing the "Ultra" flagship standard to its foldable lineup. It retains the familiar design language of the Z Fold 7, featuring a 6.5-inch cover display and an 8-inch main display with an upgraded resolution of 2,504 x 2,256 pixels. At just 4.1mm thick when unfolded and weighing 215g, it's the thinnest Galaxy Z Fold ever produced.

The camera system is the Ultra's standout upgrade: a 200MP main camera (f/1.7, Adaptive Pixel, 2x optical-quality zoom), a 50MP ultra-wide (upgraded from 12MP on the Fold 7), and a 10MP 3x telephoto lens, supporting 8K 30fps video recording. A 5,000mAh battery with 45W fast charging reaches 67% in just 30 minutes.

Both foldables run One UI 9 based on Android 17, featuring Galaxy AI — including multi-window productivity, real-time translation, and smart summary capabilities. The Z Fold8 Ultra starts at $2,099.99. Both models come in 256GB, 512GB, and 1TB storage options, in Graphite, Cream, and online-exclusive colors.

Galaxy Z Flip8: Compact Clamshell Refinement

The Galaxy Z Flip8 continues Samsung's clamshell foldable design with a focus on style and portability. It features a 6.9-inch main display and a 4.1-inch FlexWindow cover screen, with a slimmer and lighter build. Powered by the same Snapdragon 8 Elite Gen 5 for Galaxy processor, it offers up to 12GB RAM and 512GB storage.

The Z Flip8 introduces enhanced AI interactions on the FlexWindow cover screen, allowing users to reply to messages, make payments, and capture photos without unfolding the device. Starting at $1,199.99, it comes in Pink, Mint, and other color options.

Galaxy Watch 9 and Watch Ultra 2: AI-Powered Health Insights

The Galaxy Watch 9 and Galaxy Watch Ultra 2 bring significant health monitoring upgrades with the new BioActive Sensor 2, offering more precise heart rate, blood oxygen, and body composition analysis. Galaxy AI is deeply integrated into health management, providing personalized exercise recommendations, sleep analysis, and stress management.

The Watch Ultra 2 targets extreme sports enthusiasts with a titanium case, sapphire crystal glass, 100-meter water resistance, and precision GPS tracking in harsh environments. Both watches are available for pre-order starting today, with general availability on August 7 alongside the foldable phones.

Market Analysis and Outlook

Samsung has restructured its foldable lineup from a single Fold model into three distinct product lines spanning from $1,199 to $2,099, aiming to cover every segment from style-conscious consumers to power users. The new wider Z Fold8 design is widely seen as Samsung's strategic response to Apple's anticipated foldable iPhone.

However, Samsung's foldable market share has declined from 65% in 2024 to approximately 48% in 2026, facing strong competition from Chinese manufacturers like Huawei, Honor, and Xiaomi. Whether this trio of new devices can help Samsung solidify its foldable leadership remains to be seen.

Pre-orders begin today on Samsung.com and through major carriers and retailers. General availability starts August 7, 2026.
