⚡ July 17 Financial Midday: Netflix Plunges 8% After Hours, Nikkei Drops 3% as Chip Rout Deepens, Oil Holds Near $85

Netflix Crashes 8.8% After Hours on Weak Q3 Outlook Despite Solid Q2

Streaming giant Netflix reported Q2 2026 earnings after Thursday's market close that met expectations on most metrics — but a softer-than-expected Q3 forecast sent shares plunging 8.84% in after-hours trading, from a close of $74.35 to $67.78, marking the stock's lowest level since September 2024.

Netflix posted Q2 revenue of $12.56 billion, up 13% year-over-year but slightly below the $12.59 billion consensus. Diluted EPS came in at $0.80, up 11% from $0.72 a year ago, in line with estimates. Operating margin was 33.4%, down from 34.1% in the same quarter last year. Net income reached $3.4 billion, up 11%.

However, the company guided Q3 revenue to $12.86 billion — representing just 12% year-over-year growth — below Wall Street expectations. Full-year 2026 revenue guidance was maintained at $51.0-$51.4 billion.

Netflix shares are now down nearly 40% year-to-date and roughly 47% below their all-time high of $127.75. Analysts flagged concerns about slowing subscriber growth in mature markets, rising content costs, and increased competition from Disney+, Max, and other streaming rivals. The Wall Street Journal recently reported Netflix is exploring live channels and bundling strategies to boost engagement — moves that signal the company is "running out of levers," as one senior TV executive put it.

Nikkei 225 Plunges Over 3% Friday Morning as Chip Rout Intensifies

Japan's Nikkei 225 extended its selloff into a second day on Friday, plunging more than 3% in early morning trade to fall below the 66,000 level. The index was trading around 65,800 as of 08:16 Beijing time (09:16 JST), following Thursday's 2.8% drop. The Nikkei has now lost over 6% this week.

Chip stocks continued to lead the decline. Kioxia Holdings, which cratered 15% on Thursday, extended its losses. Tokyo Electron dropped over 4%, and Advantest fell more than 3%. SoftBank Group also declined over 2%.

The selling pressure reflects growing global concern that AI-driven semiconductor valuations have become overheated. After a historic rally that pushed the Nikkei to record highs earlier this year, the market is now reassessing the timeline for AI investment returns. South Korean markets were closed Friday for a public holiday, but Thursday's 6.4% KOSPI crash — triggered by the Bank of Korea's surprise 25bp rate hike to 2.75% and a leveraged ETF liquidation cascade — continues to hang over regional sentiment.

Brent Crude Holds Near $84.50 as Hormuz Traffic Collapses

Oil prices traded in a narrow range in Asian trading Friday, with Brent crude hovering around $84.50 per barrel and WTI at $79.50. Brent has gained approximately 11% this week as geopolitical risk premiums remain elevated amid the escalating US-Iran conflict.

The US launched its sixth consecutive night of airstrikes on Iran on Wednesday, targeting coastal defense systems and missile sites. Iran's Revolutionary Guard retaliated by striking US military facilities in Kuwait, Bahrain, and Jordan. Iran reiterated that the Strait of Hormuz is a "red line that cannot be crossed."

Shipping data from Kpler shows only seven vessels transited the Strait of Hormuz on Wednesday — the first full day after the US reimposed its naval blockade — down from 13 the previous day, with no supertankers or LNG carriers crossing. Goldman Sachs warned in a research note that if Persian Gulf crude exports remain stalled, Brent could exceed $110 per barrel in Q4 2026.

Reports of a potential US-Iran ceasefire memorandum signing on July 19 have emerged, which could trigger a sharp reversal in oil prices if de-escalation materializes.

Gold Slides Below $4,050 as Hawkish Fed Expectations Weigh

Gold traded around $4,030 per ounce on Friday, declining roughly 2% this week. Despite escalating Middle East tensions, the precious metal has failed to attract safe-haven flows as markets price in a hawkish Federal Reserve stance.

This week's US inflation data came in cooler than expected — June CPI fell to 3.5% (well below the 3.8% estimate) and PPI unexpectedly dropped 0.3% — which initially boosted equities. However, Fed Chair Kevin Warsh's congressional testimony struck a hawkish tone, emphasizing "zero tolerance" for persistently high inflation. Market-implied odds for a July rate hike stand at ~14%, while September odds are around 17%.

Multiple Fed officials spoke this week. Kansas City Fed President Jeff Schmid flagged inflation as his "biggest worry," while Dallas Fed President Lorie Logan called for higher rates. Surging oil prices have added to concerns that inflation could reaccelerate.

Silver fared even worse, declining roughly 5% on the week to around $60 per ounce. The gold/silver ratio widened from 66 to above 68, highlighting silver's relative underperformance amid industrial demand concerns.

Asian Markets Diverge: China Rises, Hong Kong Slips

Asian markets outside Japan were mixed on Friday. China's Shanghai Composite rose 0.4% to around 4,108, with semiconductor stocks leading gains. GigaDevice surged 10% (daily limit up), and Montage Technology jumped over 7%. CSRC Chairman Wu Qing stated at the Lujiazui Forum that qualified Hong Kong-listed companies will be supported for A-share listings, and AI development guidelines will be announced soon.

Hong Kong's Hang Seng Index fell 0.74% to 24,312, giving back some of Wednesday's gains driven by Apple Intelligence approval news. The market is also wary of a massive lock-up expiration wave — Goldman Sachs estimates at least HK$255 billion in shares will unlock next month, with HK$274 billion more in 2026.

US futures pointed to a mixed open Friday: Dow futures rose ~100 points, while S&P 500 futures declined 0.2% and Nasdaq 100 futures slipped 0.5%, reflecting continued chip sector weakness. Netflix's after-hours crash could further dampen tech sentiment.

The US dollar held near 101 on the DXY index, while the 10-year Treasury yield oscillated around 4.55%. Bitcoin traded near $63,000, roughly flat on the week, continuing to show decoupling from geopolitical risks.
