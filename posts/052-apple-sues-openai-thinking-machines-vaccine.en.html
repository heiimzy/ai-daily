🍎 Apple Sues OpenAI: From Partners to Adversaries

Apple filed a lawsuit Friday in the U.S. District Court for the Northern District of California against OpenAI and two key former employees — Chief Hardware Officer Tang Tan and former Apple engineer Chang Liu — alleging trade secret theft related to unannounced hardware products. The filing marks a dramatic reversal of the high-profile 2024 partnership that integrated ChatGPT into iPhone's operating system.

The complaint details how Tan allegedly used Apple's confidential project code names during recruiting, asked job candidates to bring Apple hardware components to interviews for "show and tell" sessions, and coached departing Apple employees on evading security procedures. Liu is accused of keeping an Apple-issued laptop after leaving for OpenAI and using it to download confidential technical documents including specifications, engineering presentations, and proprietary project data. Apple also alleges OpenAI used a proprietary metal finishing technique without permission, misleading a partner into believing Apple had authorized it. Apple seeks injunctions, damages, and an order forcing OpenAI to stop using its trade secrets. OpenAI responded that it has "no interest in other companies' trade secrets."

🤖 Thinking Machines Lab: 200ms Micro-Turns Redefine Human-AI Interaction

Eighteen months after leaving OpenAI, former CTO Mira Murati's Thinking Machines Lab has unveiled its first major breakthrough — the "Interaction Model." Rather than another LLM benchmark race, this is a fundamental rethinking of how humans and AI communicate, shifting from turn-based messaging to real-time, full-duplex conversation.

The core innovation is "Time-Aligned Micro-Turns" (TAMT), which divides time into 200-millisecond units. Every 200ms, the model processes incoming data and generates output simultaneously across audio, video, and text streams, weaving them into a continuous token sequence. The AI can interrupt while the user is speaking, search the web mid-conversation, or comment on video the user is watching — all natively trained into the model rather than patched together with external VAD and TTS components. Their TML-Interaction-Small model (276B MoE, 12B active parameters) scored 77.8 on the FD-bench V1.5 interaction quality benchmark — nearly double GPT-realtime-2.0's 46.8 — with just 0.40s response latency (vs GPT-realtime-2.0's 1.18s). On their custom TimeSpeak test (measuring a model's ability to proactively speak at a specified time), they achieved 64.7% accuracy versus GPT-realtime-2.0's 4.3%. Murati's vision is clear: AI should make it easier for humans to stay in the loop, not push them out.

💉 Cambridge AI-Designed Universal Vaccine: First Human Trial Succeeds

Cambridge University researchers announced that the first AI-designed universal vaccine has successfully completed initial human clinical trials, marking a pivotal step from reactive pandemic response to proactive prevention. The vaccine's active components were entirely computer-designed — a global first for a clinical-stage vaccine.

The trial was conducted at medical facilities in Southampton and Cambridge, UK, with 39 healthy volunteers aged 18-50. The vaccine targets Sarbeco coronaviruses (including SARS-CoV-2, SARS, and future related bat viruses) by using AI to learn genetic sequences of all known Sarbeco coronaviruses and design a novel antigen covering shared viral features. Results showed excellent safety with no significant side effects. The research team plans larger, more diverse follow-up studies to fully assess efficacy. This technology opens a new paradigm for integrated protection against influenza, Ebola, and other viruses with pandemic potential.

🔌 Anthropic in Talks to Use Microsoft's Maia Chips

Anthropic is in early discussions with Microsoft to rent servers powered by Microsoft's custom Maia 200 AI chips for running Claude model inference, according to sources familiar with the talks. The Maia chip is designed to accelerate inference on existing large models, outperforming comparable NVIDIA offerings, though it is not optimized for new model training.

Microsoft began deploying Maia 200 chips in its Azure data centers in January, successfully reducing Copilot operating costs. The potential deal reflects the industry's multi-chip strategy trend — Anthropic has committed at least $330 billion in compute spending across three major cloud providers. Microsoft, already a key Anthropic customer spending at least $500 million on Claude API access for Copilot, had also announced plans to invest up to $5 billion in Anthropic. A chip deal would deepen the existing cloud services relationship between the two companies.

🧠 Shanghai AI Lab Cracks RL Entropy Collapse: Major Training Breakthrough

An international team led by Shanghai Artificial Intelligence Laboratory, Tsinghua University, and the University of Illinois at Urbana-Champaign has proposed two innovative techniques — Clip-Cov and KL-Cov — that solve the critical problem of policy entropy collapse in reinforcement learning training. Policy entropy measures the balance between exploiting known strategies and exploring new ones; when it drops too low, models become over-reliant on existing policies and lose exploration capability.

The team derived an empirical formula R = a·exp(H) + b revealing the trade-off between policy performance and entropy, then developed Clip-Cov (clipping high-covariance tokens) and KL-Cov (applying KL penalties to maintain entropy levels). Experiments on Qwen2.5 models showed 2.0% improvement on 7B models and 6.4% on 32B models, with the 32B model achieving up to 15.0% gains on difficult benchmarks like AIME24 and AIME25. The results were validated across 11 open-source models (including Qwen2.5, Mistral, LLaMA, and DeepSeek), covering math and programming tasks across 8 public benchmarks. This breakthrough provides theoretical foundations for scaling reinforcement learning in language models.
