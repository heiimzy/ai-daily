⚡ July 16 International Politics: US Strikes Reach Near Tehran for First Time, Iran Declares 'Existential War', Ukraine Expands Drone Campaign to Black Sea

The US-Iran conflict escalated dramatically in the early hours of July 16 as American forces launched a fifth consecutive day of airstrikes, this time expanding their target range to northern Iran for the first time — bringing the strikes dangerously close to the capital, Tehran. Iran's Islamic Revolutionary Guard Corps (IRGC) activated air defense systems across the city as explosions were reported in its southeastern outskirts. Meanwhile, Iran's top negotiator and parliament speaker, Mohammad Baqer Qalibaf, formally declared the interim peace deal void, stating that the country is now engaged in an "essential and existential war" with America.

On another front, Ukraine's Unmanned Systems Forces (USF) expanded their "MoLoChKa" maritime strike campaign from the Sea of Azov into the Black Sea, destroying 20 Russian shadow fleet vessels in a single night and forcing Moscow to close the Azov Sea shipping channel. The simultaneous escalation of two major geopolitical conflicts is injecting fresh uncertainty into global energy and food markets.

1. US Strikes Reach Near Tehran for the First Time

The United States intensified its strikes targeting Iran early Thursday, hitting targets further north than in any previous wave, according to the Associated Press. While the previous four days of strikes focused on Iran's southern coast and Strait of Hormuz area, the new wave expanded the range significantly northward.

Iran's semi-official Tasnim News Agency reported that air defenses were activated in several areas, including eastern and western Tehran. It cited initial reports of explosions around Parchin and Pakdasht, located southeast of the capital. The governor of Pakdasht independently confirmed the activation of air defenses and reported hearing explosions.

This marks the first time in the current round of conflict that US strikes have come this close to Tehran. Previous waves over the past four days were concentrated along Iran's southern coastline near the Strait of Hormuz.

Simultaneously, new explosions were reported in the southern cities of Ahvaz, Bandar Abbas, and Chabahar. Iran's Mehr News Agency reported that US strikes hit near a children's cancer hospital in Ahvaz, forcing its evacuation. Iranian Health Ministry spokesman Hossein Kermanpour said the recent days of strikes have killed 35 people and wounded more than 300 others, with 72 still hospitalized.

2. 'Existential War': Iran Declares Peace Deal Void

Iran's parliament speaker and chief negotiator Mohammad Baqer Qalibaf issued a strongly worded statement on Wednesday, declaring that the interim peace Memorandum of Understanding (MoU) signed with the U.S. on June 17 had lost its validity.

"We are in an essential and existential war with America," Qalibaf said in the statement. "If Iran does not benefit from the MoU with the U.S., we have no reason to adhere to such an understanding."

Qalibaf added that Iran has "never welcomed war" but must always remain ready to fight while also utilizing diplomatic and negotiation tools. "Choosing either negotiation or war as the sole course of action would be a strategic error," he said. Iran's Foreign Ministry spokesman Esmaeil Baghaei was more direct, stating that Tehran currently has "no plans for negotiations" and is focused entirely on national defense.

U.S. President Donald Trump responded in a Fox News interview on Wednesday, saying Iran "better behave" while revealing that Tehran had requested a meeting. When asked about setting a deadline, Trump said: "I don't like giving deadlines, but they pretty much know, they know the story." He had previously threatened to strike Iranian power plants and bridges next week if negotiations do not resume.

3. First Blockade Enforcement: Hellfire Missile Stops Oil Tanker

U.S. Central Command announced the first enforcement action under the reinstated naval blockade of Iranian ports. A U.S. aircraft tracked the Curacao-flagged oil tanker M/T Belma as it sailed toward Iran's Kharg Island and fired Hellfire missiles into the ship's smokestack, disabling it.

"The ship is no longer transiting to Iran," CENTCOM said in a statement, releasing video footage of the strike. The operation came within 17 hours of the blockade's reinstatement. Two other commercial vessels attempting to breach the blockade were also intercepted and redirected with warning fire.

The blockade was reinstated after Iran closed the Strait of Hormuz over the weekend and attacked seven commercial ships, leaving nearly a dozen crew members killed, missing, or injured. The U.S. now has 21 naval vessels and hundreds of aircraft deployed in the Middle East. Brent crude settled at a one-month high of $84.95 a barrel on Wednesday.

4. Ukraine Opens Black Sea Front: 20 Russian Vessels Hit in One Night

The drone war in Ukraine took a major leap as the Unmanned Systems Forces (USF) expanded their maritime campaign from the Sea of Azov into the Black Sea. Commander Robert "Madyar" Brovdi announced on July 15 that Operation "MoLoChKa" had entered a new phase.

"The first round of the maritime battle is over. Now it's the Black Sea," Brovdi wrote on Telegram. "Tonight's score: 17 oil tankers, two gas carriers, and one tugboat hit."

In the nine days since July 6, Ukrainian drones had already struck 116 Russian shadow fleet vessels in the Sea of Azov. The Black Sea operation was launched to mark Ukraine's Statehood Day, involving six drone units including the 414th "Magyar's Birds" Brigade, the 412th "Nemesis" Brigade, and the 427th "Rarog" Brigade.

Russia Forced to Close Azov Sea Shipping

The relentless attacks forced Russia to close the Don-Azov Channel (connecting the Sea of Azov to the Don River) and the Kerch Strait (connecting to the Black Sea), effectively halting Azov Sea shipping. Satellite imagery shows dozens of cargo vessels stranded at both ends of the channel.

According to USDA data, Russia is the world's largest wheat exporter, accounting for about one-fifth of global wheat exports, with approximately one-quarter shipped through the Sea of Azov. Analysts warn that disruption to Azov shipping could impact global food supply chains. Russian Foreign Minister Sergei Lavrov condemned Ukraine's actions as "terrorism," while Kyiv responded that all strikes target military objectives.

5. U.S. Senate Unveils Revised Russia Sanctions Bill with 100% Tariffs

A bipartisan group of U.S. senators unveiled a revised Russia sanctions bill on Tuesday, replacing the earlier proposal of blanket 500% tariffs with a maximum of 100% on the five largest buyers of Russian crude: China, India, Slovakia, Hungary, and Azerbaijan.

The bill, named the "Lindsey Graham Russia Accountability Bill" in honor of the late senator, exempts countries that import less than 15% of their natural gas from Russia and are reducing those imports — effectively shielding most European allies. Trump has endorsed the bill and suggested it could be expanded to include sanctions on Iran and Hezbollah.

India reacted strongly to the proposal. Senior Indian officials described the approach as "double standards" — granting Europe gas exemptions while imposing 100% tariffs on India — warning that "making India a punching bag for U.S. foreign policy failures will be counterproductive."

6. Other Developments to Watch

  • Israel-Lebanon Talks: Israel and Lebanon concluded two days of U.S.-mediated talks in Rome on Wednesday, reaching framework agreement on "pilot zones" in southern Lebanon where Israeli troops would gradually withdraw and hand over control to the Lebanese Armed Forces.
  • EU Aviation Warning: The European Union Aviation Safety Agency (EASA) issued an urgent warning for all European airlines to avoid airspace over Bahrain, Kuwait, Qatar, the UAE, and parts of the Gulf of Oman.
  • Iran Nuclear Signals: Multiple Iranian lawmakers have publicly called for reviewing the country's defense doctrine, hinting at potential nuclear weaponization. Analysts warn that Iran's traditional deterrence tools — its missile forces and the Axis of Resistance — have been severely degraded in this war.
