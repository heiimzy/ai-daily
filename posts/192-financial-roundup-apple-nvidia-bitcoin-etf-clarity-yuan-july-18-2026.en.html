⚡ July 18 Financial Roundup: Apple Briefly Overtakes Nvidia as World's Most Valuable Company, Bitcoin ETFs Log 4th Day of $257M Inflows, CLARITY Act Hearing Sees Zero Democrats, Yuan Mid-Point Set at 6.4680

U.S. stocks closed lower across the board Friday, but Apple briefly surpassed Nvidia to become the world's most valuable company in a symbolic shift in AI investment sentiment. Meanwhile, Bitcoin spot ETFs recorded a fourth consecutive day of $257 million inflows as institutional participation broadens; the CLARITY Act field hearing in New York City drew zero Democrat lawmakers as the Senate window narrows to just three weeks; and China set the yuan reference rate at 6.4680 amid record H1 cross-border settlement volumes.

1. Apple Briefly Overtakes Nvidia as World's Most Valuable Company

Apple (AAPL) shares hit an all-time high of $334.99 in early trading Friday, lifting the company's market capitalization to approximately $4.91 trillion and briefly surpassing Nvidia (NVDA) at about $4.9 trillion to become the world's most valuable publicly traded company — the first time Apple has held the top spot since April 2025.

At the close, Nvidia edged back ahead: shares fell 2.21% to $202.81 for a market cap of roughly $4.92 trillion, while Apple rose 0.14% to $333.74 for a $4.89 trillion valuation. The gap between the two — about $30 billion, or less than 1% of market cap — was the narrowest in over a year.

The market cap flip was triggered by growing investor skepticism about the sustainability of AI infrastructure spending. Nvidia's stock has fallen about 12% from its all-time high of $5.7 trillion on May 14, erasing roughly $900 billion in market value. Apple, by contrast, has added more than $500 billion in market cap over the same period, climbing from approximately $4.35 trillion in mid-May.

HSBC analysts upgraded Apple to Buy on Friday, noting that Apple's capital expenditure on AI data centers represents only about 2.5% of projected 2026 sales, compared to 39% for hyperscalers like Meta, Google, Amazon, and Microsoft. In the current market environment, Apple's "asset-light" strategy has become a magnet for capital. HSBC said Apple is "well positioned to leverage its 2.5 billion installed base through the upcoming enhanced Apple Intelligence," and expects the first foldable iPhone to launch in September.

Toni Meadows, head of investment at BRI Wealth Management, said: "Apple was seen as an AI laggard because it wasn't spending to develop models, but now sentiment has changed. Apple is less exposed to capex intensity and better positioned to monetize AI via services, ecosystem lock-in, and hardware upgrades."

2. Bitcoin Spot ETFs Record 4th Straight Day of $257M Inflows

The crypto market received a strong positive signal Friday as U.S. spot Bitcoin ETFs recorded a fourth consecutive day of net inflows totaling $257.34 million. This breaks a 10-day outflow streak and has helped Bitcoin rebound from 21-month lows to approximately $61,500, with the price further climbing toward $64,000 over the weekend.

The key differentiator this time is breadth. Unlike previous sessions where inflows were concentrated in a single fund (Fidelity's FBTC accounted for roughly three-quarters of a $221.7 million inflow earlier in the week), Friday's $257 million was spread across all 11 U.S. spot Bitcoin ETFs. BlackRock's IBIT led with $94 million (after near-zero flows for three weeks), Fidelity's FBTC added $67 million, and ARKB contributed $62 million. This suggests institutional demand is arriving through multiple distribution channels rather than a single large client order.

CoinShares Head of Research James Butterfill attributed the shift to softer-than-expected U.S. inflation data. The June CPI and PPI both came in below expectations, reviving expectations of Federal Reserve rate cuts. The market is now pricing a more dovish signal from the Fed's July meeting.

Glassnode data shows the Bitcoin options put-to-call ratio has fallen to its lowest level in six months, indicating "traders are reducing downside hedges and rebuilding upside exposure, a constructive shift in sentiment." However, CoinShares cautioned that "a break above $80,000 is unlikely absent a meaningful shift in monetary policy expectations."

Separately, Strategy (formerly MicroStrategy), the world's largest corporate Bitcoin holder with approximately 840,000 BTC, unveiled a new Digital Credit Capital Framework signaling a pivot away from its buy-at-all-costs accumulation model. The company sold roughly 3,588 BTC for about $216 million to strengthen its balance sheet, boosting USD reserves from $1.4 billion to $3.0 billion. Its STRC preferred shares recovered from $75 to near $88.

3. CLARITY Act Hearing in NYC Draws Zero Democrat Lawmakers

The House Financial Services Subcommittee on Digital Assets, Financial Technology, and Artificial Intelligence held a field hearing on the Digital Asset Market Clarity Act (CLARITY Act, H.R. 3633) at Federal Hall in New York City on Friday — but no Democrat members attended.

Subcommittee Chairman Bryan Steil presided over testimony from representatives of Nova Labs, Bullish, WisdomTree, and Coin Center. "Today, here on Wall Street, we gather at the threshold of the next generation of innovation," Steil said in his opening remarks. "As the Senate prepares to consider the CLARITY Act, we can complete nearly a decade of work. Our goal is clear: replace regulation by enforcement with clear rules of the road for digital assets."

The CLARITY Act passed the House in 2025 and now sits on the Senate calendar. It requires 60 votes to overcome a filibuster. Republicans hold 52 seats following Senator Lindsey Graham's death, meaning at least eight Democratic votes are needed — but the merged Senate draft omits an ethics provision that Democrats have demanded as a precondition for their support. Senators Chris Murphy, Chris Van Hollen, and Jeff Merkley held a press conference formally opposing the bill, with Murphy stating: "There is no reason to pass a new regulatory system for crypto if this system does not stop Trump's corruption."

Senate Majority Leader John Thune has pledged a floor vote before the August 7 recess, with the week of July 20 now under active discussion. Analysts warn that failure in 2026 likely delays comprehensive federal digital asset regulation until 2030. Polymarket odds for 2026 passage have declined significantly from earlier this year.

President Trump has stepped up pressure, posting on Truth Social on July 13 that "China and other countries would like to take complete and total control of this major financial moment as well as artificial intelligence" and urging lawmakers not to "let China win on either front."

4. Yuan Reference Rate Set at 6.4680; China's H1 Cross-Border Settlements Hit Record $9.2 Trillion

China's central bank set the yuan reference rate at 6.4680 per U.S. dollar on Saturday, slightly stronger than the previous day's 6.4710 fix. The yuan has been trading in a 6.40-6.85 range this year, demonstrating resilience amid global volatility.

The State Administration of Foreign Exchange (SAFE) reported on Friday that China's bank-client cross-border receipts and payments totaled $9.2 trillion in the first half of 2026, up 21% year-on-year and a record high for any first half. The renminbi's share of cross-border settlements reached 52.9%, up 1.3 percentage points from full-year 2025. Banks recorded a net forex settlement surplus of $271.2 billion, indicating broadly balanced supply and demand in the forex market.

SAFE officials noted that corporate currency risk management continues to improve. Enterprises signed nearly $1.4 trillion in forex derivative contracts in H1 2026, up 40% year-on-year, with the hedge ratio reaching 35.3%, up 5.3 percentage points from full-year 2025. The share of renminbi-denominated trade settlements continued to trend higher, with 52.9% of cross-border flows now settled in yuan.

SAFE emphasized that China's forex market remains stable despite complex external conditions, with the yuan exchange rate maintaining basic stability at a reasonable equilibrium level. As expectations for a Fed rate-cutting cycle build, narrowing U.S.-China interest rate differentials are expected to further support yuan stability.

5. Oil Surges Past $88 on 7th Night of US-Iran Strikes, Tanker Explosions in Hormuz

In commodity markets, escalating U.S.-Iran hostilities continued to drive oil prices higher. The U.S. military launched a seventh consecutive night of strikes on Iran, while Iran's Revolutionary Guards claimed two oil tankers hit mines and exploded in the Strait of Hormuz and said it had "stopped" four ships attempting to transit the critical waterway. U.S. Central Command denied the Iranian claims.

Brent crude settled 4.59% higher at $88.10/barrel on Friday, with WTI up 4.48% at $82.49/barrel — both at six-week highs. Both benchmarks posted weekly gains of approximately 16%, the largest since April. Kpler data shows confirmed crude and condensate transit through the Strait of Hormuz has fallen 62% to 4.1 million barrels per day, with regional loadings down 47%. Saudi Arabia has diverted more than 70% of its crude exports to the Red Sea port of Yanbu.

Iran has also threatened to instruct Houthi forces to close the Red Sea shipping route if the U.S. attacks Iranian power infrastructure. Analysts warn that a "dual-risk scenario" — simultaneous disruption of both the Strait of Hormuz and the Red Sea — would put unprecedented pressure on global oil supply chains.
