⚡ July 14 Tech Roundup: Nvidia Halves Asia AI Chip Buyer List, Malaysia Tightens Export Rules, Jensen Huang Heads to Beijing

Nvidia Slashes Authorized Asia Customer List by More Than Half

Nvidia has cut its list of authorized AI chip customers in Asia by more than half, introducing a new "whitelist" system with significantly stricter compliance checks, the Financial Times reported on July 14. This is the chip giant's broadest customer compliance overhaul to date, aimed at preventing advanced AI processors from reaching China through third-country channels.

According to sources familiar with the matter, Nvidia dispatched field inspectors over the past several months to conduct on-site audits at customer facilities in Singapore, Malaysia, and Japan, and directly called customers to verify business authenticity. Customers that failed compliance reviews were removed from the authorized list — more than half of the total.

The move follows continued pressure from Washington to tighten AI chip export controls. In April, the U.S. Commerce Department imposed new restrictions on Nvidia's H20 chip, potentially costing the company $15 billion to $16 billion in lost revenue. The Trump administration partially eased restrictions in June but required Nvidia to establish more stringent end-user verification mechanisms.

"We do not compromise on compliance. Every AI chip's destination must be traceable and verifiable." — Nvidia spokesman Hector Marinez

Malaysia Mandates Strategic Trade Permits for US-Origin AI Chips

On the same day, Malaysia's Ministry of Investment, Trade and Industry (MITI) announced that effective immediately, all exports, transshipments, and transits of US-origin high-performance AI chips are subject to a Strategic Trade Permit. Malaysia serves as a critical transit hub for Nvidia's AI chips in Asia, with Southeast Asian countries including Singapore frequently used as transshipment points to China.

The new regulation falls under Section 12 of the Strategic Trade Act 2010 (Catch-All Control provision), requiring individuals or companies to notify authorities at least 30 days in advance if they intend to export, transship, or transit any item not listed on the Strategic Items List, where there is knowledge or reasonable suspicion the item may be used in restricted activities. MITI stated it "takes a firm stance against any attempt to circumvent export controls or engage in illicit trade."

Industry analysts note that Malaysia's action closely follows U.S. pressure. Reports last week indicated the Trump administration would more aggressively crack down on disguised transshipments through Malaysia and Thailand. Malaysia's statement did not directly reference China but is clearly a product of sustained U.S. pressure.

Jensen Huang to Hold Beijing Media Briefing This Week

Nvidia CEO Jensen Huang is visiting China this week and is scheduled to hold a media briefing in Beijing on July 16. The trip comes just days after he participated in the Trump-Xi summit in Alaska, and is seen as a symbolic move by Nvidia to maintain its relationship with the Chinese market amid the ongoing export control regime.

In an interview with CNN's Fareed Zakaria that aired over the weekend, Huang was asked about concerns that China could use Nvidia's chips to bolster its military capabilities. "We don't have to worry about it," he said. "They simply can't rely on it. It could be, of course, limited at any time."

Meanwhile, reports indicate Beijing is considering allowing Alibaba, ByteDance, and DeepSeek to purchase Nvidia's H200 chips, but with a cap under 200,000 units and restricted to AI training only. Inference workloads would be directed to domestic chips like Huawei's Ascend series, signaling a new phase of "conditionally limited access" in the U.S.-China AI chip dynamic.

Apple Hits All-Time High, Market Cap Approaches $5 Trillion

Amid growing skepticism about AI infrastructure investment returns, Apple (AAPL) has emerged as a "safe haven" with its relatively conservative AI capex strategy. On July 13, Apple shares hit an intraday all-time high above $321, pushing its market capitalization to approximately $4.73 trillion — just $320 billion behind Nvidia's $5.05 trillion valuation.

Analysts say market重心 is shifting from AI infrastructure builders to AI product implementers. Mark Bronzo, Chief Investment Strategist at Rye Strategic Partners, said: "Amid concerns over AI infrastructure investment returns, investors have returned to the stability of Apple." Apple's restraint in the AI spending race — notably, it has not plunged into massive data center construction — has become a competitive advantage. Citi raised its Apple price target from $315 to $365, citing margin expansion and market share gains. Apple is scheduled to report earnings on July 30 and is expected to launch its foldable iPhone in September.

The AI Chip Landscape Is Being Reshaped

From a broader perspective, the competitive landscape of the AI chip industry is undergoing profound transformation. Microsoft, Google, Amazon, and Meta collectively project approximately $725 billion in capital expenditures for 2026, up 77% year-over-year, with Nvidia capturing roughly 41.5% of that spending. However, as export controls tighten and geopolitical tensions intensify, Nvidia's operating environment in Asia is becoming increasingly complex.

Analysts note that the establishment of Nvidia's "whitelist" system marks the beginning of a "tiered access" era for AI chip trade: Tier A (U.S. and close allies) receives standard export review; Tier B (case-by-case destinations) requires additional security certifications and faces supply caps; Tier C (China and Macau) faces default denial. This tiered system may be codified into law through the advancing Chip Security Act.

Meanwhile, Malaysia's export permit requirements, China's push for domestic chip alternatives, and the global "Sovereign AI" compute buildout are all reshaping the supply-demand landscape for AI chips. The ultimate winners of this game may not be any single country or company, but those able to navigate a "multi-supply-chain" system.
