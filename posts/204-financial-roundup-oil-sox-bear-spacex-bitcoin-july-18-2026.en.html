⚡ July 18 Financial Evening: SOX Confirms Bear Market, Oil Surges 14% to $88, SpaceX Plunges for 6th Day Erasing $1T+, Bitcoin Bounces to $64K

1. Wall Street Weekly Losses: SOX Enters Bear Territory

U.S. stocks closed lower on Friday, July 17, with all three major indexes posting weekly losses. The Dow Jones Industrial Average fell 406.55 points (-0.77%) to 52,146.42, the S&P 500 dropped 1.01% to 7,457.69, and the Nasdaq Composite plunged 1.40% to 25,520.24.

For the week, the S&P 500 lost about 1.6%, the Nasdaq slid 2.9%, and the Dow was down roughly 0.9% — one of the weakest weeks for U.S. equities since the April 2025 tariff-driven selloff. Energy was the sole gaining sector, lifted by crude's sharp rally as geopolitical tensions escalated.

The most significant market event was the Philadelphia Semiconductor Index (SOX) officially entering bear market territory. The index fell 1.63% on Friday and is now down 20.2% from its all-time closing high reached on June 22, surpassing the 20% technical threshold. Global chip stocks have lost approximately $3.3 trillion in market value over three weeks. Nvidia dropped about 2% on the day and briefly lost its position as the world's most valuable company to Apple.

Jose Torres, Senior Economist at Interactive Brokers, noted: "Concerns about Chinese competition from AI startup Moonshot's Kimi K3 model, combined with worries about returns on massive AI capital expenditure, dragged the semiconductor sector into bear territory."

2. Oil Surges 14% Weekly to $88 as Hormuz Shipping Nearly Paralyzed

The escalating U.S.-Iran conflict sent global oil markets through their most violent week in months. Brent crude settled at $88.10 per barrel on Friday, up 4.6% on the day and surging approximately 14% for the week — the largest weekly gain since April. WTI crude closed at $82.49, rising 13.3% over the same period.

According to the Joint Maritime Information Center, daily crude and condensate transits through the Strait of Hormuz have collapsed 62% to just 4.1 million barrels per day, with only 8 vessels completing passage during the week. Kuwait reported that an Iranian missile strike damaged a power and water desalination facility, sparking a large fire and destroying multiple generating units, raising concerns over the vulnerability of critical infrastructure across the Gulf.

The U.S. launched its seventh consecutive night of airstrikes against Iran, expanding targets to include what it termed "military logistics infrastructure" for the first time. Iran's Revolutionary Guards retaliated by striking U.S. bases across Bahrain, Kuwait, Jordan, and Saudi Arabia. Analysts warn that Brent could breach $100 if the conflict continues to escalate.

3. SpaceX 6-Day Losing Streak: $1T+ in Market Cap Erased, Cathie Wood Buys the Dip

Space Exploration Technologies Corp. (NASDAQ: SPCX) continued its slide, plunging 5.43% on Friday to close at $123.99 — a new post-IPO low. The stock has now fallen for six consecutive sessions, trading 8.2% below its $135 IPO price and more than 40% below its all-time intraday high of $225.64. The company's market cap has declined by over $1 trillion from its peak.

The immediate catalyst was the abort of Starship Flight 13 moments before liftoff, after at least two Raptor engines on the Super Heavy booster failed to ignite during pre-flight testing. SpaceX has rescheduled the launch for no earlier than July 20. Adding to the pressure, insider lock-up periods are expiring in tranches over the coming months, creating potential overhang.

In a contrarian move, Cathie Wood's ARK Invest purchased 147,623 SpaceX shares on Friday, totaling approximately $18.3 million across four actively-managed ETFs (ARKK, ARKQ, ARKW, ARKX). This extends ARK's buying spree since SpaceX's June IPO, bringing its total position to over $475 million. Meanwhile, ARK sold about 26,002 Robinhood shares worth approximately $7.91 million on the same day.

Mark Malek, Chief Investment Officer at Siebert Financial, commented: "With lock-up expirations entering the valuation equation, many investors are likely recalibrating their initial pricing logic. Sideline capital is waiting for a more attractive entry window."

4. Bitcoin Bounces to $64K as ETF Inflows Return

The cryptocurrency market showed signs of stabilization after an AI-driven panic selloff. Bitcoin rebounded from an intraday low of $62,489 to trade in the $63,969-64,235 range, up about 1.1% in 24 hours. Ethereum recovered to around $1,843 (+0.93%). Major altcoins including BNB, XRP, Solana, and Cardano posted gains of up to 4.81%.

U.S. Bitcoin spot ETFs have recorded approximately $264.4 million in net inflows over the past two weeks, breaking an eight-week outflow streak. Fidelity's FBTC led with about $166 million in inflows, while BlackRock's IBIT recorded a single-day inflow of $138.9 million.

However, analysts remain cautious. Piyush Walke, Derivatives Research Analyst at Delta Exchange, noted: "Although Bitcoin bounced from $62,500 to $64,000, the rebound lacks strength — the price has failed to form higher highs and slipped back below the 50-day moving average, keeping the broader downtrend intact."

5. Apple Briefly Overtakes Nvidia as World's Most Valuable Company

In a historic moment during Friday's session, Apple's market cap briefly touched $4.91 trillion, surpassing Nvidia's $4.908 trillion for the first time since April 2025. At the close, Nvidia narrowly held the top spot ($4.908T vs Apple's $4.902T).

The crown swap was driven more by Nvidia's ~2% decline on the Kimi K3 selloff than by any dramatic rally in Apple shares, which remained nearly flat. HSBC analysts upgraded Apple to Buy on Friday, stating the company has "strong conditions to leverage the upcoming Apple Intelligence upgrade across its installed base of 2.5 billion devices" and predicting the first foldable iPhone for September.

6. CXMT IPO: Record 0.47% Subscription Rate on STAR Market

Chinese DRAM leader ChangXin Memory Technologies (CXMT, SH688825) launched its STAR Market IPO this week at CNY 8.66 per share, implying a valuation of CNY 579.2 billion. The offering raised approximately CNY 57.9 billion, making it the largest-ever IPO on the STAR Market and the largest in Asia this year. The final online subscription rate was 0.47%, with 9.43 million valid subscription accounts, both setting STAR Market records.

On the Hyperliquid exchange, a pre-IPO perpetual contract "CXMT" briefly surged to $8.64, implying a market cap approaching CNY 4 trillion — nearly 7x the IPO valuation. Morgan Stanley and Zephyr expect the post-listing valuation to reach between CNY 3-5 trillion.

In Q1 2026, CXMT reported net profit of CNY 24.76 billion — a 1,688% year-over-year surge. Counterpoint Research forecasts that CXMT's DRAM wafer capacity will reach 350,000 wafers per month by end-2026, approaching Micron's 385,000 level.

7. A-Share Weekend Roundup: Active ETFs Filed, No Widespread Margin Calls

Despite a panic-driven selloff in China A-shares this week with margin balances falling for 11 consecutive sessions by over CNY 170 billion, a survey by Securities China found no widespread forced liquidation wave. One major brokerage with 1,100 margin account clients reported only 1 triggered liquidation (less than 0.1%). Retail investors were net buyers during the selloff, and ETF inflows exceeded CNY 210 billion, suggesting institutional buyers are stepping in.

In a landmark regulatory development, China's securities regulator accepted the first 18 filings for active ETFs on July 17. Fund managers including E Fund, China AMC, Fullgoal, and Southern submitted products focused on large-cap value, growth, and dividend-low-volatility strategies. Analysts describe this as a major step in moving A-shares from tool homogenization toward strategy diversification.

Additionally, China's Ministry of Finance announced a differentiated battery consumption tax: sodium-ion, solid-state, and fuel cells are exempted through 2028, while traditional lithium batteries face phased-in taxation from September. Shanghai launched an "AI+Manufacturing" subsidy program offering up to CNY 40 million in computing rental subsidies.
