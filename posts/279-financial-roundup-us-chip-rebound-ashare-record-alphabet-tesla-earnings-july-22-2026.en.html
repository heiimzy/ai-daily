⚡ July 22 Financial Morning Roundup: US Chip Stocks Surge 4.9% in Powerful Rebound, China Indices Hit Yearly Highs, Alphabet & Tesla Earnings Take Center Stage

1. US Stocks Rebound Sharply: Chip Sector Soars 4.9% to Break 3-Day Losing Streak

U.S. stock markets closed sharply higher on Tuesday (July 21), with all three major indices breaking a three-day losing streak. Tech stocks led the rally, driven by a powerful resurgence in semiconductor shares as investors repositioned ahead of a pivotal week in earnings season.

The Dow Jones Industrial Average rose 374.46 points, or 0.72%, to close at 52,213.72. The S&P 500 gained 64.20 points, or 0.86%, to 7,507.48. The Nasdaq Composite surged 333.26 points, or 1.31%, to 25,841.33, while the Nasdaq 100 jumped 1.53% to 29,042. The CBOE Volatility Index (VIX) fell to 16.92, signaling a notable improvement in investor sentiment.

Semiconductor stocks led the charge. The Philadelphia Semiconductor Index (SOX) surged 4.9% to 12,319, its biggest single-day gain in nearly two weeks. Micron Technology and Sandisk both soared over 12% to lead the sector, followed by Applied Materials and Marvell, each up more than 8%. Intel gained approximately 7%. The rebound was fueled by stronger-than-expected export data from Taiwan and South Korea, underscoring resilient global chip demand.

"Last week's chip rout saw the sector drop about 10%, but 90% of S&P 500 companies that have reported so far have beaten estimates. The pullback was more about position cleaning than a trend reversal." — Ryan Detrick, Chief Market Strategist at Carson Group

General Motors (GM) surged 5% after posting a Q2 earnings beat, signaling robust consumer demand. 3M jumped 7.62%, Caterpillar gained 2.40%, and Goldman Sachs rose 1.91%, with industrial and financial stocks providing solid support to the Dow. On the downside, Charles Schwab fell 3% after missing earnings estimates.

All eyes now turn to Big Tech earnings tonight — both Alphabet (Google's parent) and Tesla will report Q2 results after the closing bell. Alphabet is expected to post $116.8 billion in revenue (+21% YoY), with Google Cloud revenue projected to grow roughly 65% to $22.5 billion. The market will be laser-focused on AI capex efficiency, Cloud margin trajectory, and concrete AI monetization metrics.

2. China A-Share Indices Hit New Yearly Highs: Coal Sector Surges 6.18%, Infrastructure Theme Explodes

China's A-share market continued its rally on Wednesday (July 22), with all three major indices closing at new yearly highs. Total turnover surged to 1.93 trillion yuan, up more than 200 billion from the previous session — the highest since March 7.

The Shanghai Composite Index closed at 3,581.86, up 0.62%, after briefly touching 3,584.72 — a new yearly high. The Shenzhen Component Index rose 0.84% to 11,099.83, breaking through the 11,000-point mark for the first time. The ChiNext Index gained 0.61% to 2,310.86. Over 2,500 stocks advanced, with 114 hitting the daily limit up.

The coal sector was the standout performer, soaring 6.18% in afternoon trade — the highest among all 31 Shenwan primary industries. Shanxi Coking Coal, Haohua Energy, Shanshui International, and Lu'an Environmental Energy all hit daily limit up. In the futures market, coking coal surged 11% during night trading, while coke futures gained 5.29%.

The infrastructure boom continued to drive markets. Building materials gained 4.49%, construction rose 3.38%, and steel climbed over 2%. The Yarlung Zangbo River super hydropower project (1.2 trillion yuan total investment) remained the key catalyst. Major capital flowed 13.9 billion yuan into infrastructure-related stocks.

Three capital flow signals confirmed fresh money entering the market:

  • Northbound capital recorded a net inflow of 12.7 billion yuan — a July single-day high — buying into NEV (+3.5B) and solar (+2.8B) sectors;
  • Margin balance rose by 21.3 billion to 1.95 trillion yuan, with margin buying as a percentage of total turnover reaching 10.29% — above the 10% threshold for the first time in four months;
  • Institutional capital rotated out of high-valuation AI and Huawei-concept names into coal, infrastructure, and other cyclical sectors.

3. Big Tech Earnings Night: Alphabet & Tesla Take the Stage

Tonight (July 22) marks one of the most important days of Q2 earnings season, with two tech titans — Alphabet and Tesla — reporting simultaneously after the US market close.

Alphabet: The $190 Billion AI Bet Under Scrutiny

Wall Street expects Alphabet to post Q2 revenue of ~$116.8 billion (+21% YoY) and GAAP EPS of ~$2.87 (+24% YoY). Key areas to watch:

  • Google Cloud growth trajectory: After 63% growth in Q1 ($20B revenue), can the segment maintain 60%+ momentum?
  • AI monetization efficiency: Gemini has reached 750 million MAUs, but concerns persist that AI Overviews raise query costs without commensurate ad revenue lift;
  • Capex efficiency: 2026 capex guidance of $180-190 billion is at record levels. Cloud margin expansion above 30% will be critical;
  • Market positioning: Options imply a ~6% swing. The stock has corrected ~13% from its May peak of $408.

Tesla: AI and Autonomy Narrative Faces Reality Check

Analysts expect Tesla to report Q2 revenue of ~$26.07 billion (+16% YoY) and adjusted EPS of ~$0.52 (+30% YoY). Q2 vehicle deliveries beat estimates, with strong demand in China and Europe. Key focal points:

  • AI investment returns: Capex is surging; cash flow trajectory will be closely monitored;
  • Cybercab progress: Robotaxi deployment timeline has disappointed, with slower-than-expected rollout;
  • FSD and Optimus: Commercialization path for AI-driven businesses;
  • Market expectations: Options imply a 7.08% move — significantly above the 3.23% average of the past four quarters. Consensus rating: Hold (10 Buy/16 Hold/3 Sell).

Intel will report on Friday (July 24). Analysts expect revenue of $14.42 billion (+12.1% YoY) and EPS of $0.22, with focus on 18A process ramp and AI chip demand recovery.

4. Oil Holds Above $90: Brent at $90.68 as Geopolitical Risk Premium Persists

International crude oil prices continued to climb on Tuesday. Brent crude settled at $90.68/barrel, up 1.64%, while WTI held above $84. The US-Iran conflict entered its 11th night, with Strait of Hormuz tensions continuing to underpin prices.

Societe Generale analysts noted that Brent has gained 12% this week, with most of the rebound driven by the resumption of US-Iran strikes and Houthi threats of a maritime blockade against Saudi Arabia. Data shows crude oil now accounts for roughly 80% of Hormuz transit volumes — about 5 percentage points higher than before the conflict. Asian crack spreads have surged 22%, reflecting heightened concern over prolonged supply disruptions.

Looking ahead, $91.30 remains the key resistance level for Brent. A sustained break above that could target $98-$100; however, any ceasefire signals could trigger a rapid pullback toward $85.

5. Fed Rate Debate Continues: July Hold Probability at ~62%

Divisions within the Federal Reserve over the rate path remain a focal point for markets. New York Fed President John Williams (permanent FOMC voter) said Tuesday that interest rates are in a "favorable position" and that there are "encouraging reasons to believe inflation has peaked and should gradually decline over the coming quarters." He projected headline inflation would fall to around 3.25% by year-end and return to the 2% target by 2028.

However, Fed Chair Kevin Warsh declined to signal whether the Fed would hike in July during his congressional testimony last week, emphasizing a "data-dependent" communication framework. Warsh said he expects a robust "family fight" at the July 28-29 FOMC meeting but offered no rate path guidance. He reiterated a "zero tolerance" stance on persistently high inflation and warned that the AI building boom could push prices higher.

According to CME FedWatch, markets currently price a 62% probability of a July hold and 38% chance of a 25bp hike. Notably, September hike odds have risen to ~52%, reflecting growing expectations of another rate increase this year. Allianz Global Investors projects two rate hikes (September and December) totaling 50bp for the remainder of 2026.

Other central banks: The Bank of Canada held rates at 2.25% (6th consecutive hold), while the Bank of Korea raised its benchmark rate by 25bp to 2.75% — its first hike in 3.5 years.
