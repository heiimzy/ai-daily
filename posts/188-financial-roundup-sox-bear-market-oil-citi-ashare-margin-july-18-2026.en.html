⚡ July 18 Financial Roundup: Philly Semiconductor Index Enters Bear Market, Oil Surges 12% for the Week, Citi Risk Gauge Hits Highest Since 2008, China A-Share Margin Calls Trigger Panic Selloff

1. Philadelphia Semiconductor Index Confirms Bear Market: 20.2% Below June Peak

The Philadelphia Semiconductor Index (SOX) closed at 11,673.89 on Friday, July 17 — 20.2% below its record closing high of 14,634.72 on June 22, officially confirming entry into a bear market. This marks the first time since 2022 that the benchmark has entered bear territory, signaling the first major test of the AI-driven semiconductor super-cycle.

The SOX fell 1.63% on Friday alone, bringing its weekly decline to 6.9% — the steepest weekly loss in over a year. Despite the brutal selloff, the index remains up nearly 65% year-to-date, far outperforming the S&P 500's roughly 9% gain over the same period, underscoring that the correction is driven by valuation compression rather than fundamental deterioration.

Nvidia (NVDA) fell another 2.21% on Friday. Applied Materials, Lam Research, Intel, KLA Corp., and Arm Holdings each dropped roughly 4%. The semiconductor complex has shed over $3.3 trillion in market capitalization over the past three weeks.

The selloff was triggered Wednesday after Chinese AI startup Moonshot AI released its Kimi K3 open-source model, claiming to narrow the gap with leading U.S. offerings. Wall Street analysts warn that enterprises may opt for cheaper Chinese open-source models rather than expensive proprietary U.S. alternatives, potentially undermining pricing power for Nvidia and other chip leaders.

Memory chip stocks showed early signs of differentiation after days of relentless selling. SK Hynix ADRs rebounded 1.13%, Seagate Technology surged 5.66%, and Western Digital gained 2.23%. However, analysts caution this is likely a technical bounce rather than a trend reversal — the VanEck Semiconductor ETF (SMH) is down over 17% month-to-date.

2. Oil Surges 12% Weekly: WTI Closes at $82.49, Hormuz Shipping Nearly Paralyzed

Crude oil prices soared as Middle East hostilities intensified for a seventh consecutive day. WTI crude for August delivery surged 4.48% to settle at $82.49 per barrel on the New York Mercantile Exchange, while Brent crude jumped 4.59% to close at $88.10. Both benchmarks posted weekly gains of 12-16%, with Brent recording a third straight weekly advance and WTI its second.

According to maritime data firm Kpler, only eight ships transited the Strait of Hormuz on Thursday, down from 13 the previous day and the lowest level in over a month. Before the conflict, roughly 20 million barrels of oil passed through the strait daily. The U.S. Navy's "steel wall" blockade, combined with Iranian threats against vessels violating it, has effectively strangled one of the world's busiest commercial waterways.

The Wall Street Journal reported Thursday that President Trump is leaning toward expanding military operations and has discussed the potential seizure of Iran's main oil export terminal on Kharg Island. Meanwhile, Iran's leadership has instructed Houthi forces in Yemen to prepare to close the Bab el-Mandeb Strait — the Red Sea chokepoint — if the U.S. strikes Iranian power infrastructure.

IEA Executive Director Fatih Birol warned at a Council on Foreign Relations event in Washington: "Oil supply security remains a critical issue. If the situation does not improve in the next few weeks, we should be worried — and I am already worried."

Goldman Sachs said Brent could rise above $110 per barrel in Q4 if Gulf export recovery remains delayed. European natural gas (TTF) surged over 15% this week to around €56/MWh, while Asian LNG spot prices (JKM) skyrocketed 18.5% in a single session to $19.93/MMBtu — a 21% weekly gain.

3. Citi Risk Gauge Hits Highest Since 2008: 11.5 of 18 Warning Signals Triggered

Citi's bear market checklist, compiled by strategists led by Beata Manthey, has triggered 11.5 of 18 warning signals for the U.S. market — the highest reading since the 2008 financial crisis. Globally, 10 of 18 indicators have been triggered.

The indicator aggregates stock valuations, market sentiment, credit spreads, yield curve dynamics, fund flows, corporate fundamentals, and financing activity. Strategists warn that once the warning count enters double digits and continues rising, it typically signals intensifying market risk and advises against blind bottom-fishing during corrections.

For context, the checklist triggered 17.5 signals before the 2000 dot-com bubble burst and 13 ahead of the 2008 financial crisis. The current reading suggests markets haven't reached "extreme euphoria" levels but risk is rapidly accumulating.

Options market analyst SpotGamma warned that several key events over the next two weeks — including Fed Chair Warsh's first policy meeting and the expiration of over $5 trillion in options contracts — could trigger significant market turbulence.

4. China A-Share Panic: 5,001 Stocks Fall, 198 Hit Limit Down, Margin Leverage Outflows Exceed 150B Yuan

The Shanghai Composite Index tumbled 3.05% to close at 3,764.15 on Friday, breaching the psychologically important 3,800 level. The ChiNext Board (growth enterprise) crashed 7.15%, while the Shenzhen Component Index fell over 5%. Across the market, 5,001 stocks declined, only 482 advanced, and 198 stocks hit their daily limit-down. Combined trading volume surged to 2.67 trillion yuan, up 252.7 billion from the previous session.

The telecom sector led the carnage with a 9.84% plunge, followed by electronics (-8%). Machinery, healthcare, computer, and defense sectors each fell over 5%. Zhongji Innolight crashed 12% to 979.46 yuan/share with a staggering 56.5 billion yuan in turnover. Eoptolink fell over 11%, while GigaDevice, Huatian Technology, and Tongfu Microelectronics all hit limit-down.

Margin liquidation was the critical accelerant. Margin debt has declined for 11 consecutive trading days — the longest deleveraging cycle in 23 months. On Thursday alone, margin balances fell by 28.586 billion yuan, with the ChiNext board seeing 9.211 billion in outflows. Friday's net margin selling reached a staggering 82.654 billion yuan, making it the single largest source of sell pressure across all market participants. From the early July peak of 3.0305 trillion yuan to Friday's close of 2.8802 trillion, over 150 billion yuan in leveraged capital has exited the market in 11 sessions. The electronics sector alone saw 70.485 billion yuan in margin reductions.

Fund managers believe the worst of the systemic selloff is behind but the market needs 1-2 weeks to bottom out. Positive signals: the ChiNext and STAR 50 indices have already corrected 12-15% from June peaks, small-cap high-flyers are down 30-50%, and selling pressure shows signs of exhaustion.

5. Asia Pacific Markets Rout: Nikkei Enters Correction, Taiwan Plunges 6.47%

The U.S. chip selloff triggered even sharper declines across Asia-Pacific markets on Friday. Japan's Nikkei 225 crashed over 5% to around 63,000, falling more than 13% from its late-June peak and officially entering correction territory. SoftBank Group (an OpenAI investor) plunged 9.01%, Tokyo Electron dropped over 10%, Screen Holdings fell 14%, and memory maker Kioxia Holdings cratered 16.10%.

Taiwan's Taiex index suffered its worst session since Trump's "Liberation Day" tariffs, plunging 6.47%. TSMC — despite reporting record Q2 earnings and raising its capex forecast — tumbled 7.29% as investors sold on the "good news" rather than buying it, reflecting deep skepticism about AI infrastructure spending returns.

South Korean markets were closed for a national holiday, but the prior day authorities had announced a temporary ban on new ETFs tied to major tech firms and raised minimum deposit requirements for retail investors in such products to curb volatility. Hong Kong's Hang Seng fell 2.5%, with the Hang Seng Tech Index plunging 5%. China's CSI 300 dropped 3.6%. Singapore's non-oil domestic exports grew 20.7% YoY in June (below the 28.7% consensus), though electronics exports surged 105.1% — a reminder that semiconductor demand, while facing valuation concerns, remains robust in absolute terms.
