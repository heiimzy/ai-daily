⚡ July 20 International Roundup: Ukraine Launches 400 Drones at Moscow, Philippines Says China Injured Sailor, Russia Strikes Grain Ship Killing 10

1. Ukraine Launches 400+ Drones at Moscow in One of Largest Attacks on Russian Capital

In the early hours of July 20, Ukraine launched one of the largest drone attacks on Moscow since the war began. Moscow Mayor Sergei Sobyanin reported that more than 400 Ukrainian drones were directed toward the Moscow region between 8:30 PM Sunday and 5:00 AM Monday. "Most were neutralized by air defense systems at long range, while 85 UAVs were destroyed on approach to Moscow," he stated.

Moscow Oblast Governor Andrei Vorobyov confirmed 10 people were wounded, with multiple residential buildings and civilian infrastructure damaged. Ukrainian drones struck multiple strategic targets:

  • Podolsk oil depot — just 40 km from the Kremlin, the fire burned for hours
  • Domodedovo — the Yuzhnye Vorota industrial park and a Wildberries warehouse were hit
  • Odintsovo district — multiple infrastructure facilities damaged
  • Noginsk — a fuel depot was struck and set ablaze

President Volodymyr Zelensky confirmed the strikes on X, stating Ukrainian forces hit "Russian logistics facilities and an oil depot in the Moscow region, as well as six Russian vessels in the Black Sea — including two tankers and four dry cargo ships." He declared: "We are responding, justly, to every Russian strike against our cities and communities. This war must be brought to an end, and that is only possible by increasing the pressure on the sole cause of this war — Russia."

This marks one of the largest Ukrainian drone attacks on Moscow in the 1,608-day war, demonstrating a major escalation in Ukraine's long-range strike capability.

2. Russia Retaliates with Massive Ballistic Missile Barrage on Kyiv

Just hours before the Ukrainian drone attack on Moscow, Russia launched one of its largest ballistic missile assaults on Kyiv on the night of July 19. Ukraine's Air Force reported 41 missiles (including 25 ballistic missiles and 10 Zircon hypersonic missiles) and 125 attack drones were fired.

The attack began at approximately 1:30 AM local time and lasted until 2:38 AM, striking five districts of Kyiv: Sviatoshynsky, Shevchenkivsky, Solomyansky, Desnyansky, and Dnipro. At least six people were killed and dozens wounded, with residential buildings, offices, and industrial sites set ablaze. Rescue teams pulled several people from burning structures.

According to the Institute for the Study of War (ISW), Russia has launched more ballistic missiles against Ukraine in July 2026 than it produces monthly. "Russia appears to be dipping into its stockpiles to continue increasing the number of ballistic missiles it launches against Ukraine, as these missiles have a higher success rate than drones and cruise missiles," the ISW noted.

Zelensky tallied Russia's weekly onslaught: approximately 1,450 attack drones, over 1,640 guided aerial bombs, and 99 missiles in the past seven days alone.

3. Russian Missiles Strike Turkish Grain Ship, Killing 10

On the evening of July 19, Russian missiles struck the Turkish-owned cargo vessel "Golden Leo" flying the flag of Guinea-Bissau, as it departed from Ukraine's Odesa port carrying a full load of corn. Ukraine's Navy reported three Kh-59/Kh-69 cruise missiles hit the vessel. At least 10 crew members — including Syrian and Indian nationals — were killed, four remain missing, and only eight were rescued.

Ukrainian Foreign Minister Andrii Sybiga declared on X: "A civilian ship. An international crew. A route serving global food security. Putin saw another target. This attack leaves no doubt — Putin's Russia does not care whom it kills."

Prime Minister Sergii Koretskyi confirmed the ship carried 17 crew members plus one Ukrainian pilot. Eight rescued sailors included two injured. The attack on a civilian grain vessel has drawn international condemnation as evidence of Russia's deliberate targeting of global food supply chains.

4. Zelensky Faces Political Crisis as Protests Erupt Over Defense Minister's Dismissal

As the military front deteriorates, Zelensky is facing his most serious domestic political crisis. On July 15, he dismissed 35-year-old Defense Minister Mykhailo Fedorov — widely credited with revolutionizing Ukraine's drone warfare capabilities — and replaced him with SBU chief Yevhen Khmara as acting minister.

Fedorov's firing has sparked mass protests across Ukraine for four consecutive days. Tens of thousands have taken to the streets in Kyiv, Kharkiv, Odesa, Lviv, Dnipro, and at least seven other cities, demanding Fedorov's reinstatement and the dismissal of Commander-in-Chief Oleksandr Syrskyi.

The protests reflect a deep generational and strategic divide within Ukraine's military leadership. Fedorov — a tech-savvy reformer who never served in the military — clashed repeatedly with Soviet-trained generals who favored conventional tactics. His dismissal is seen as a victory for the old guard over the young innovators who have driven Ukraine's asymmetric drone warfare against Russia.

In response, Ukraine's Navy, Assault, and Territorial Defense Forces commanders issued a joint statement supporting Syrskyi and condemning attempts to "divide society." Zelensky acknowledged he has held extensive talks with both Fedorov and Syrskyi but has not committed to specific personnel changes. "I hear what people are saying," he said in his evening address.

5. South China Sea Tensions: China Coast Guard Clubs Filipino Sailor

On July 20, the Philippine military accused the China Coast Guard of clubbing a Filipino sailor with a wooden baton at the Second Thomas Shoal in the disputed South China Sea. According to the Philippine statement, a Chinese rigid-hull inflatable boat carrying eight men had circled and photographed the grounded BRP Sierra Madre, which serves as a Philippine garrison.

When two Philippine Navy rubber boats were dispatched to investigate, "the (Chinese coast guard) personnel reacted violently and aggressively by striking a Philippine Navy personnel on the head with a wooden baton, causing injury and damaging the Navy rubber boat."

The Philippine Department of National Defense called the encounter part of "a clear pattern of provocative and hostile behavior" by China. The Chinese Embassy in Manila did not respond immediately. The incident comes just days after Manila filed a diplomatic protest over a Chinese state media AI-generated video that portrayed the Philippines as a monkey.

Notably, Chinese Foreign Minister Wang Yi and Philippine Foreign Secretary Enrique Manalo are scheduled to meet this week on the sidelines of the ASEAN Regional Forum in Manila, adding fresh uncertainty to bilateral relations.

  • Singapore minister resigns: Acting Minister-in-charge for Muslim Affairs Faishal Ibrahim resigned after a complaint was filed about his conduct. PM Lawrence Wong acted swiftly, referring the matter to police.
  • Japan tightens overstay enforcement: Japan's Immigration Services Agency announced an emergency staff increase to crack down on foreigners overstaying their visas.
