⚡ July 21 Financial Roundup: China's STAR 50 Soars 10.73% in 'Miracle Day', State-Owned Funds + Insurers Inject $70B+, Citi Upgrades China, Wall Street Holds Steady

China's 'Miracle Day': STAR 50 Surges 10.73%, All Major Indices Hit Yearly Highs

China's A-share market staged a historic reversal on Tuesday, July 21, in what traders are calling a "Miracle Day." After days of sharp corrections, growth stocks mounted a full-scale counterattack, pushing all three major indices to new yearly highs.

The Shanghai Composite closed at 3,864.37, up 1.79%; the Shenzhen Component surged 4.81% to 14,264.29; the ChiNext (Growth Enterprise) index jumped 7.05% to 3,685.97. But the standout performer was the STAR 50 (Science and Technology Innovation Board 50), which skyrocketed 10.73% to 1,903.16 — the largest single-day gain this year.

Combined turnover across Shanghai and Shenzhen reached 2.96 trillion yuan ($414 billion), expanding 255 billion yuan from the prior session. More than 3,100 stocks closed in positive territory, with over 100 hitting their daily upside limits.

Chip Stocks Lead the Charge: $44B in Net Capital Inflow

The semiconductor supply chain emerged as the undisputed leader, with net institutional capital inflow of 316.2 billion yuan ($44.3 billion) and 66 stocks hitting daily circuit-breaker limits.

The STAR Chip ETF (588290) surged 15.52%. Stocks including Yoke Technology, Yaxiong Integration, Dongwei Semiconductor, NAURA Technology, and Jinhai Tongda all hit limit-up. The PCB sector followed suit, with the Electronics 50 ETF (515320) gaining 9.65%.

Memory chip stocks attracted 160.3 billion yuan in net inflows with 30 stocks hitting limits; the AI Data Center sector saw 149.1 billion yuan inflow; and automotive electronics drew 146.7 billion yuan.

ETF market activity hit a fever pitch, with total ETF turnover reaching 637.7 billion yuan, up 30.7 billion from the previous day. Twenty-three core ETFs saw combined net inflows approaching 100 billion yuan, confirming aggressive bargain hunting by institutional investors.

National Team + Insurers Deploy $70B+ to Stabilize Markets

Multiple state-backed institutions deployed capital in a coordinated effort to halt the market slide, sending a powerful signal.

State capital led the charge. China Guoxin deployed over 50 billion yuan ($7 billion) via special relending facilities to increase holdings in core state-owned enterprise assets. China Chengtong added approximately 10 billion yuan focused on SOE targets and hard-tech sectors.

Five major insurers followed. On July 20, Ping An Insurance, China Pacific Insurance, PICC, China Life, and New China Life collectively pledged to increase equity allocation ratios, targeting strategic emerging industries. China Life alone executed over 10 billion yuan in single-day equity purchases.

Corporate buybacks accelerated. 55 listed companies announced buyback or share-increase plans between July 17-19, with new planned scale approaching 10 billion yuan. Month-to-date, 469 companies have announced buyback plans with total upper limits exceeding 15.7 billion yuan.

CSRC Chairman Wu Qing personally visited a broker branch on July 20 to hold an investor symposium, stating that "investors are the foundation of the capital market" and pledging to "coordinate risk prevention, strong regulation, and development promotion" to safeguard market stability.

Citi Upgrades China to 'Overweight': Global Funds Rotate In

In a significant development for global asset allocation, Citigroup upgraded Chinese equities to "overweight" in its H2 2026 Emerging Markets Outlook published July 21, while downgrading South Korean stocks. The move underscores a broader rotation by global financial institutions toward Chinese assets.

Citi highlighted that global investors are looking beyond crowded technology trades elsewhere in Asia, hunting for cheaper shares and fresh AI opportunities in China. The weakening US dollar — down 7% on a trade-weighted basis year-to-date — provides additional tailwinds for foreign capital inflows into China.

Fundamental support is building: over 1,700 A-share companies have released H1 earnings previews, with over 700 reporting pre-profits or turnaround. Fund managers estimate non-financial A-share net profit growth could reach 17.8% year-over-year in the first half.

Hong Kong Stocks Rise, Hang Seng Breaks 25,000

Hong Kong markets also strengthened, with the Hang Seng Index rising 0.68% to break above 25,000 for the first time in nearly four years. The Hang Seng China SOE Index led with a 1.06% gain. Infrastructure stocks surged on the 1.2 trillion yuan Yarlung Tsangpo super hydropower project, with building materials sector gaining 6.06% — the top performer among A-share sectors.

The Hong Kong Stock Exchange is reportedly considering extending trading hours and eliminating the midday break to enhance connectivity with mainland markets via Stock Connect.

Wall Street: Quiet Finish, Chip Stocks Attempt Recovery

US stocks ended mixed on Monday. The S&P 500 edged down 0.19% to 7,443.28, the Dow fell 0.59% to 51,839.26 (lowest close since June 24), while the Nasdaq was essentially flat at 25,508.07. Chip stocks attempted a rebound mid-session, with AMD gaining ~2% and Micron rising over 1%, though gains faded into the close.

Brent crude settled at $89.22/barrel, up 1.3%, as the US-Iran conflict continued to disrupt Strait of Hormuz shipping — only 127 vessels crossed the strait last week, down nearly 50% week-over-week. The 10-year Treasury yield climbed to 4.59%, sending the 30-year mortgage rate to its highest in nearly a year.

Iran's foreign ministry hinted at potential diplomatic channels, stating intermediaries had exchanged messages with Tehran, briefly boosting sentiment. Intel announced new layoffs in its data center group as part of a broader restructuring.
