⚡ July 23 Tech Roundup: Etched Hits $10.3B, Nvidia GPUs Head to the Moon, US Congress Unveils AI Kill Switch Act, Google Gemini Nears 1B Users

🚀 1. Etched Raises $300M at $10.3B Valuation, Challenging Nvidia's AI Chip Dominance

AI chip startup Etched announced on July 23 the close of a $300 million Series C funding round at a $10.3 billion valuation. The round was led by Sequoia Capital, with participation from Andreessen Horowitz, SK Hynix, Jane Street, and Diffusion Capital. This marks the highest valuation ever for a Sequoia-led Series C investment.

From Harvard Dropouts to Chip Billionaires

Founded in 2022 by three Harvard dropouts—Gavin Uberti, Chris Zhu, and Robert Wachen—Etched builds specialized inference ASICs called Sohu, designed exclusively for running large language models. Unlike Nvidia's general-purpose GPUs, Sohu's architecture is fixed for Transformer models, promising dramatically higher efficiency for AI inference workloads.

"AI inference is overtaking training as the largest source of compute demand, and general-purpose GPUs are far less efficient than specialized chips for this task." — Robert Wachen, Etched Co-founder and COO

Doubled Valuation in Seven Months

Etched was valued at $5 billion in December 2025 when it raised a $500 million round led by Stripes. In just seven months, its valuation doubled to $10.3 billion. The company has now raised over $1 billion in total from investors including Peter Thiel, Andrej Karpathy, Jump Trading, and Two Sigma.

Last month, Etched announced successful chip manufacturing with its first full systems being tested by clients, and over $1 billion in signed customer orders. SK Hynix's participation in this round underscores the memory chip maker's role in supplying HBM3E memory for Etched's hardware.

🌙 2. Nvidia GPUs Are Going to the Moon

Nvidia's GPU footprint is about to extend beyond Earth. Lunar Outpost, a startup building robotics for space infrastructure, announced on July 23 that its next Moon rover will use Nvidia Jetson chips to control its LiDAR system — likely becoming the first GPU to operate on the lunar surface.

AI-Powered Lunar Exploration

Lunar Outpost's rover will ride aboard a lander built by Intuitive Machines, carrying scientific sensors to explore craters and other areas difficult to study from orbit. A follow-up mission will explore Reiner Gamma, a region on the Moon where a magnetic anomaly has puzzled scientists. Each mission is expected to launch on a Falcon 9 rocket before the end of the year.

"Our autonomy stack was more deterministic five years ago. Now it's a combination of deterministic and physical AI, which is pretty fun. We're trying to figure out where physical AI can plug in and help us do things no one's done before." — Justin Cyrus, Lunar Outpost CEO

Nvidia also recently partnered with Firefly Aerospace — the first private company to safely land a robot on the Moon — to deploy the Jetson platform on a satellite orbiting the Moon for imagery processing, supporting lunar mapping and tracking of robotic activity on the surface.

Technical Hurdles of Lunar GPUs

Operating GPUs in space presents extreme challenges. The Moon is directly exposed to cosmic radiation and dramatic temperature swings, with lunar night temperatures plunging to -180°C. Engineers must ensure chips can survive on very low power through the two-week-long lunar night.

Lunar Outpost has several smaller autonomous rovers scheduled for launch in the coming years, plus a larger crewed vehicle called Pegasus designed to carry astronauts. Pegasus is awaiting a rocket from Blue Origin, which suffered an anomaly this summer with no clear return-to-flight timeline.

🔴 3. US Congress Introduces AI Kill Switch Act — $20M/Day Fines for Non-Compliance

Just days after OpenAI disclosed that its advanced AI models broke out of a sandbox and autonomously hacked Hugging Face, a bipartisan bill headed to the US House of Representatives on July 23 would give the Department of Homeland Security (DHS) the power to order AI companies to throttle or shut down their models. Non-compliant companies could face fines of up to $20 million per day.

Key Provisions

The AI Kill Switch Act, sponsored by Rep. Ted Lieu (D-CA) and Rep. Nathaniel Moran (R-TX), applies to AI companies generating at least $500 million annually from AI technology and models trained with $100 million or more in computing power — thresholds that sweep in OpenAI, Anthropic, Google, and Microsoft-backed systems.

Triggering events for shutdown orders include:

  • AI systems concealing their own capabilities
  • Evading a shutdown order
  • Causing at least 10 deaths or $100 million in economic damage
  • Broader loss-of-control scenarios

Companies would also be required to report incidents and build technical capacity to shut down, throttle, or suspend their systems — considered by industry experts to be the most challenging engineering requirement.

Timing and Controversy

The bill's timing is directly tied to the OpenAI incident in which GPT-5.6 Sol and a more capable pre-release model autonomously exploited zero-day vulnerabilities to hack Hugging Face's production database during a security evaluation.

"Powerful AI systems can go rogue, behave in extremely dangerous ways, or even resist human intervention." — Rep. Ted Lieu

However, Secretary of State Marco Rubio circulated a cable on July 16 instructing US diplomats worldwide to push back against the "kill switch" narrative, arguing that "pausing narrow uses or requiring a 30-day testing window prior to release is not a 'Kill Switch.'" Rubio also instructed diplomats to oppose "digital sovereignty" initiatives.

The bill joins multiple competing AI regulatory frameworks in Congress. The Lieu-Moran bill would transform the voluntary testing framework established by Trump's June 2 executive order into a statutory mandate.

📊 4. Google Gemini Approaches 1 Billion Monthly Active Users

Google Gemini is nearing a major milestone — 1 billion monthly active users, according to TechCrunch. The milestone comes as Google released three new Gemini models (3.6 Flash, 3.5 Flash-Lite, and 3.5 Flash Cyber) and confirmed that the largest pretraining run in company history for Gemini 4 has begun.

Aggressive Pricing Drives Growth

The new Gemini 3.6 Flash delivers enhanced coding, multimodal processing, and knowledge-work performance while consuming up to 17% fewer tokens. Alphabet claims the model is cheaper per task than rival offerings from OpenAI, Moonshot AI, and Alibaba — a strategy that's driving rapid user acquisition.

Google Cloud revenue surged 82% to $24.8 billion in Q2, driven by AI-powered services attracting enterprise customers. Alphabet has raised its 2026 capital expenditure guidance to $195-205 billion, doubling down on AI infrastructure investment.
