⚡ WAIC 2026 Opens in Shanghai: Xi Jinping Calls for Global AI Cooperation, 300+ Products Debut, AI Agent Phone and Huawei Atlas 950 SuperPod Steal the Show

The 2026 World Artificial Intelligence Conference (WAIC) and High-Level Meeting on Global AI Governance officially opened on Friday in Shanghai, marking the largest edition in the event's nine-year history. The conference spans four venues across three Shanghai districts with a record exhibition area exceeding 100,000 square meters, attracting over 1,100 companies displaying more than 3,000 exhibits — including over 300 global product debuts.

Xi Jinping's Landmark WAIC Keynote: AI as a 'Symphony of Global Cooperation'

President Xi Jinping attended the opening ceremony and delivered a keynote speech — the first time a Chinese head of state has personally addressed WAIC since its inception in 2018. Xi emphasized that "the development of artificial intelligence should not be a solo performance by any single country but rather a symphony of global cooperation," and called for opposing the "overstretching of national security concerns" in the AI field.

Xi announced that China will provide 5,000 AI training opportunities to developing countries over the next five years and extend access to a Chinese-developed AI meteorological early warning system to 30 nations. The previous day, 29 countries including Pakistan, Russia, and Kazakhstan signed an agreement with China to establish the World Artificial Intelligence Cooperation Organization (WAICO), headquartered in Shanghai — the first intergovernmental international organization focused on AI governance.

UN Secretary-General António Guterres, Kazakh President Kassym-Jomart Tokayev, and Thai Prime Minister Paetongtarn Shinawatra were among the dignitaries attending. Nine Turing Award and Nobel Prize laureates are scheduled to speak, including deep learning pioneer Yoshua Bengio and reinforcement learning founder Richard Sutton.

Huawei Atlas 950 SuperPod: A 'Supercomputer Factory' for Domestic AI

One of the most anticipated exhibits at WAIC 2026 is the public debut of Huawei's Atlas 950 SuperPod, an AI supercomputing cluster powered by 8,192 Ascend 950DT NPU chips. The system utilizes Huawei's proprietary UnifiedBus 2.0 optical interconnect technology to deliver 16.3 PB/s bandwidth, functioning as a single logical computer rather than a loose cluster of servers.

The Atlas 950 represents a major milestone in China's efforts to build sovereign AI infrastructure under the shadow of US export restrictions on advanced chips. The system has been optimized for applications across 11 major industries including finance, manufacturing, and healthcare. Alongside Huawei's showcase, Sugon also demonstrated its fully domestic 50,000-card "Dengfeng 8000" AI supercluster, underscoring the scale of China's progress in domestic AI computing infrastructure.

World's First AI Agent Phone: Nubia NaviX Ultra Makes Global Debut

ZTE subsidiary Nubia officially unveiled the NaviX Ultra at WAIC 2026, billing it as the world's first mass-produced AI agent phone. Developed in partnership with ByteDance, the device runs a deeply customized version of the Doubao large language model and features a system-level AI agent capable of observing the phone screen, understanding user intent, and autonomously executing complex multi-step tasks across applications.

Equipped with a dedicated orange AI button, NaviX Ultra can handle everything from ordering food and booking flights to responding to messages — all triggered by a single natural language command without requiring users to switch between apps. The phone won the WAIC 2026 SAIL Excellence AI Pioneer Award.

StepFun also debuted the STEPX Neo at WAIC — the first smartphone to earn China's L3 certification for terminal AI intelligence. Running the Step AOS agent-native operating system with an embedded "Amoo" agent, it represents a paradigm shift from "manual operation" to "AI-autonomous execution." Honor CEO Li Jian confirmed that the world's first Robot Phone will be showcased on July 18, featuring a foldable gimbal arm for physical stabilization and robotic interaction.

200+ Robots From Solo Acts to Teamwork: Zhiyuan A3 Ultra Leads Embodied Intelligence

The H3 Embodied Intelligence Pavilion has become the conference's most dynamic zone, assembling approximately 200 robots. Unlike previous years where robots performed solo demonstrations, 2026 emphasizes multi-robot collaboration — with multiple machines coordinating on real industrial tasks like assembly,搬运, and sorting.

Zhiyuan Robotics unveiled the Expedition A3 Ultra, described as the industry's first mass-producible, commercially deployable full-size humanoid robot. Standing 174cm tall with 360° vision and LiDAR fusion sensing, 700 TOPS of compute power, and 24/7 auto-recharging capability, the robot is already deployed in exhibition halls, hotels, retail stores, and guided tour scenarios.

Siemens launched its first engineering AI agent for industrial automation in the Chinese market, capable of independently understanding project requirements, writing control software, and iteratively optimizing system configurations — improving engineering efficiency by 2-5x. PrimeBot's Qiyuan T1, the world's first transformable personal robot that switches between wheeled humanoid and quadruped forms, also made its global debut, supporting voice-activated photography and intelligent following.

According to MIIT officials speaking at WAIC, China's annual humanoid robot production is expected to reach 100,000 units this year, as humanoid robots accelerate their journey from labs to factory floors and service industries.

261 Large Models, 108 AI Chips: The Battle for Token Economics

WAIC 2026 features a concentrated display of 261 large language models and 108 AI chip products. MiniMax officially launched its M3 multimodal large model supporting text, code, image, and video reasoning. Moore Threads partnered with Peking University to showcase the world's first 5D world model, EvoPhys-World, which has ranked first globally on Stanford's WorldScore benchmark for 37 consecutive days.

"Token cost" emerged as a dominant theme at this year's conference. Zhonghao Xinying debuted its fully self-developed TPU chip "Xu Yu," designed to reduce per-token costs through hardware-level scheduling. H3C launched its Turing pilot platform covering 5,000 chip-model适配 solutions. China Telecom showcased the industry's first token security router, enabling enterprises to granularly control and audit model invocation costs.

According to NDRC officials, 2026 is expected to be the year AI smartphones and PCs surpass non-AI devices in shipments for the first time, signaling an inflection point for ubiquitous AI terminal adoption.
