⚡ July 15 World Politics Roundup: Trump Reverses 20% Hormuz Toll, US-Iran Trade Strikes for 3rd Night, Houthis Break 4-Year Saudi Truce, UAE Tankers Hit in Hormuz (1 Dead, 8 Injured)

Trump Does U-Turn: Scraps 20% Hormuz Fee, Replaces with Gulf Investment Deals

President Donald Trump made a dramatic reversal on Tuesday (July 14), announcing he would scrap the proposed 20% fee on ships transiting the Strait of Hormuz — less than 24 hours after proposing it — and replace it with "Trade and Investment Deals" from Gulf states.

"Based on highly productive conversations with Middle East leadership, I have decided to replace the 20% United States Reimbursement Fee with Trade and Investment Deals that the various Gulf States will be making into the United States," Trump wrote on Truth Social. No further details on the investments were provided.

Speaking to reporters in the Oval Office, Trump said Saudi Arabia, UAE, Qatar, Bahrain, and Kuwait reached out to him directly. "They said, 'We'd love to do it a different way. We'd love to invest in the United States with billions and billions of dollars,' and that would be very acceptable. So there's no fee."

Just a day earlier, Trump had declared the U.S. would be known as "THE GUARDIAN OF THE HORMUZ STRAIT" and charge a 20% cargo fee. The International Maritime Organization (IMO) immediately passed a resolution opposing any fees for straits used in international navigation, stressing there is "no legal basis" for mandatory tolls.

However, Trump made clear that the naval blockade of Iranian ports would proceed as planned. CENTCOM confirmed the blockade took effect at 4:00 PM ET (20:00 GMT) on Tuesday, covering all vessel traffic regardless of flag entering or leaving Iranian ports and terminals along the entire southern coastline.

US and Iran Trade Strikes for Third Consecutive Night

U.S. forces completed a third consecutive night of large-scale strikes on Iran early Tuesday. CENTCOM said it "successfully struck" military targets across six Iranian locations — Bushehr, Chah Bahar, Jask, Konarak, Abu Musa, and Bandar Abbas — using precision munitions against Iranian coastal defense systems, missile and drone sites, and maritime capabilities to "degrade Iran's ability to attack commercial shipping."

According to an AFP tally based on Iranian media and official announcements, at least 28 people have been killed in Iran since the U.S. renewed attacks last week. Local officials in Iran's southwestern Khuzestan province confirmed that four people were wounded by U.S. projectiles early Tuesday.

Iran's Islamic Revolutionary Guard Corps (IRGC) retaliated by launching missile and drone attacks on U.S. military targets in Bahrain (home to the U.S. Navy's 5th Fleet), Jordan, and Kuwait. Jordan's army said it intercepted four missiles fired from Iran. Bahrain reported air defense systems engaging hostile targets, while Kuwait's armed forces confirmed they were confronting multiple aerial incursions.

Iranian Foreign Ministry spokesman Esmaeil Baghaei said Tehran remained in contact with mediators including Pakistan, Qatar, and Oman, but warned: "Each time the other party has failed to meet its obligations, we did not uphold ours, and we will continue to act in this manner."

Houthis Break 4-Year Saudi Truce, Strike Abha Airport

Yemen's Iran-backed Houthi rebels fired ballistic missiles and drones at Saudi Arabia's Abha International Airport on Monday (July 13), marking the first direct Houthi attack on Saudi Arabia since an informal truce took effect in March 2022. The Saudi-led coalition said its air defenses intercepted the incoming missiles.

The escalation was triggered when Yemen's internationally recognized government bombed the runway of Sanaa International Airport, which is controlled by the Houthis. The government said it targeted the airport to prevent an Iranian aircraft carrying a Houthi delegation returning from the funeral of Iran's Supreme Leader Ayatollah Ali Khamenei from landing in violation of Yemeni sovereignty.

Houthi military spokesman Brig. Gen. Yahya Saree declared in a video statement: "The de-escalation phase has ended. This aggression will not go unanswered or unpunished." He also warned international airlines to avoid Saudi airspace "until the blockade on Sanaa International Airport is lifted."

The U.N. Security Council held an emergency session on Monday, with UN Assistant Secretary-General Khaled Khiari warning: "Yemen and the wider region cannot afford another cycle of escalation." Analysts caution that renewed Houthi attacks on Saudi Arabia risk further destabilizing the region and could threaten Red Sea shipping lanes.

Two UAE Oil Tankers Hit by Iranian Missiles: 1 Dead, 8 Injured

The UAE Defense Ministry confirmed that two of its oil tankers, the Mombasa and Al Bahiyah, were struck by Iranian cruise missiles while transiting the Strait of Hormuz in Omani waters. One Indian national crew member was killed, and eight others — including two Ukrainians and six Indian nationals — were wounded, four seriously.

The IRGC claimed responsibility, saying it had struck "two offending supertankers" that ignored repeated warnings and turned off their navigation systems. Brig. Gen. Hossein Mohebi, the IRGC spokesman, said: "The two offending supertankers were lured by the U.S. into using that route and to turn off their navigation systems. They ignored repeated warnings from the Strait of Hormuz Security Center and chose to pass through the mined route, so they were struck and disabled."

India summoned Iran's ambassador in New Delhi to officially protest the killing of its national. The UAE condemned the attack as "a shameless attack" that "constitutes a serious violation and a breach of international law."

A Norwegian tanker was also hit by an explosion caused by an unidentified device off the Omani coast early Tuesday, according to crisis response company MTI Network.

Trump to Address Nation on July 16, Threatens Iran's Underground Nuclear Site

Trump formally notified Congress on Monday that U.S. forces resumed combat operations against Iran on July 7, in a letter that gives the Pentagon 60 days of military action without further congressional approval. "The military operations I have ordered are based on my responsibility to protect the American people and national security and foreign policy interests," Trump wrote.

The president announced he will deliver a national address on Thursday, July 16 at 9:00 PM ET to update the American people on the Iran situation — his second prime-time address since declaring war on April 1.

In a radio interview with conservative host Hugh Hewitt, Trump threatened to destroy Iran's deeply buried nuclear facility at "Pickaxe Mountain" (Kuh-e Kolang Gaz La) near Natanz, where Western intelligence suspects Iran is building an undeclared enrichment facility. "Tell the Iranians to be ready. Let them know we're coming — and there's not a damn thing they can do about it," Trump said.

Israeli Prime Minister Benjamin Netanyahu issued a stark warning from Dimona — widely believed to house Israel's undeclared nuclear arsenal — telling Iranian leaders: "The days are over when someone strikes us and we don't hit back with a decisive blow."
