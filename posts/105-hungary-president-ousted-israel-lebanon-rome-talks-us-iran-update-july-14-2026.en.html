⚡ July 14 World Politics Roundup: Hungary Ousts Orban-Era President in 139-6 Landslide, Israel-Lebanon Rome Talks Enter 6th Round, US-Iran Fighting Intensifies as Oil Breaks $87

🇭🇺 Hungary Parliament Ousts President, Ending Orban Era

Hungary's parliament passed a historic constitutional amendment on Monday (July 13) with 139 votes in favor and only 6 against, immediately terminating the term of President Tamas Sulyok. The move represents Prime Minister Peter Magyar's most decisive step yet in dismantling the power structures built during Viktor Orban's 16-year rule.

Magyar's center-right Tisza Party won a landslide two-thirds supermajority in April's parliamentary elections, ending Orban's Fidesz party's 16-year grip on power. Since then, Magyar has launched "Operation Cleansing Fire," a sweeping reform program targeting Orban's influence across the judiciary, media, and state institutions. Removing Sulyok — an Orban appointee who became president in February 2024 — was the critical next step.

Magyar had repeatedly called on Sulyok to resign, calling him "unworthy to embody the unity of the Hungarian nation" and a "puppet" of Orban. When Sulyok refused, Tisza used its supermajority to pass a tailor-made constitutional amendment ending his term. The amendment also introduces far-reaching institutional reforms:

  • Establishes a National Asset Recovery and Protection Office to investigate alleged financial abuses under the previous government
  • Imposes a 12-year (three-term) limit on parliamentary mandates
  • Reduces Constitutional Court judge terms from 12 to 9 years and reinstates a 70-year age limit, removing four sitting judges
  • Restores certain powers of the Constitutional Court

Sulyok now has five days to sign the amendment into law. If he refuses, parliament has vowed to launch impeachment proceedings. Fidesz lawmakers boycotted the vote entirely. Fidesz caucus leader Gergely Gulyas resigned in protest, calling the amendment "the beginning of tyranny." Orban, who left the country ahead of the vote to attend the FIFA World Cup semi-finals in the United States, posted a photo of Magyar captioned: "Democratic Hungary: 1990–2026."

Magyar has promised to draft a new constitution after nationwide consultations and is exploring direct presidential elections to give the largely ceremonial office greater democratic legitimacy.

🇮🇱🇱🇧 Israel-Lebanon Begin 6th Round of US-Mediated Talks in Rome

The sixth round of direct Israel-Lebanon negotiations opened Tuesday (July 14) at the U.S. Embassy in Rome, with both delegations led by their ambassadors to Washington. Unlike previous rounds, no military representatives are attending, signaling a shift from operational details toward political framework implementation.

The talks focus on implementing the June 26 framework agreement, particularly the establishment of "pilot zones" — areas in southern Lebanon where Israeli forces would withdraw and the Lebanese Armed Forces would assume security control. The agreement also calls for the eventual dismantling of Hezbollah's military infrastructure in southern Lebanon.

Israeli Foreign Minister Gideon Saar said Tuesday: "We are ready to move forward implementing these two pilot zones. I hope and tend to believe that this round of discussions in Rome will promote it." He emphasized that "Israel has no territorial ambitions in Lebanon."

Lebanese President Joseph Aoun, who is scheduled to meet President Trump in Washington on July 21, has demanded immediate Israeli withdrawal from the pilot zones before any further discussions. The Lebanese presidency said the delegation in Rome has been instructed "to demand the immediate start of Israeli forces' withdrawal from the two pilot zones."

The core deadlock remains sequencing: Hezbollah refuses to disarm while Israeli forces remain on Lebanese soil, while Israel insists it will only complete withdrawal after Hezbollah no longer poses a "military threat."

The Rome round is expected to establish three joint committees covering political affairs, security, and "good neighborly relations." A U.S. official said the first pilot zone transfer could begin "within days."

🌍 US-Iran Conflict Intensifies: Third Night of Strikes, Hormuz Blockade, Tanker Attacks

Third Consecutive Night of U.S. Strikes

U.S. Central Command (CENTCOM) completed its third consecutive night of strikes on Iranian military targets Tuesday, a five-hour mission hitting six cities: Bushehr, Chabahar, Jask, Konarak, Abu Musa, and Bandar Abbas. Targets included coastal defense systems, missile and drone sites, and maritime capabilities. CENTCOM released video footage showing explosions near piers at the Bandar Abbas Naval Base.

Iran's state media reported more than 20 people killed and 100 wounded since hostilities restarted. Four people were injured in Omidiyeh in Khuzestan province. Explosions were also reported on Kish Island and Qeshm Island.

Trump Reinstates Blockade, Proposes 20% Tolls

President Trump declared Monday: "The Hormuz Strait is OPEN, and will remain OPEN, with or without Iran. We are reinstating THE IRANIAN BLOCKADE." He further announced the U.S. would become "THE GUARDIAN OF THE HORMUZ STRAIT" and charge a 20% toll on all cargo shipped through the strategic waterway.

The U.S. Navy-led Joint Maritime Information Center said the blockade takes effect Tuesday at 20:00 GMT, applying to all vessel traffic regardless of flag. The measure covers Iran's entire coastline but exempts neutral transit passage to or from non-Iranian destinations. Humanitarian shipments will be permitted subject to inspection.

The toll proposal drew sharp international criticism:

  • International Maritime Organization (IMO) passed a resolution opposing any fees for straits used in international navigation, stating there is "no legal basis for mandatory tolls"
  • Brazilian President Lula da Silva called the plan "piracy," saying it would turn the U.S. into a "pirate state"
  • Iranian Foreign Minister Abbas Araqchi mocked Trump's plan: "20% is of course too much. We will be fair."
  • German shipping giant Hapag-Lloyd said the fee would be "fundamentally wrong"

Iranian Cruise Missiles Hit Two UAE Tankers: 1 Dead, 8 Wounded

The UAE Defense Ministry confirmed Tuesday that two Emirati oil tankers — the Mombasa and Al Bahiyah — were struck by Iranian cruise missiles while transiting the southern shipping lane of the Strait of Hormuz within Omani territorial waters. One Indian crew member aboard the Mombasa was killed, and eight others were injured (six Indian, two Ukrainian nationals), four of them seriously. Fires broke out on both vessels but have since been brought under control.

Iran's Islamic Revolutionary Guard Corps (IRGC) said the two "offending supertankers" were targeted after ignoring repeated warnings, turning off their navigation systems, and attempting to pass through "a mined route." Iran also claimed to have shot down a U.S. MQ-1 drone over Hormuz and struck a "hostile" U.S. vessel with cruise missiles.

Escalating International Response

  • India summoned Iran's Deputy Chief of Mission in New Delhi to protest the attack that killed an Indian national. India's MEA called it an "unacceptable act of violence."
  • European Union Aviation Safety Agency (EASA) warned all airlines to avoid the airspace of Kuwait, Bahrain, Qatar, UAE, and the Gulf of Oman until July 29.
  • Norwegian chemical tanker Stolt Magnesium suffered an explosion from an unidentified external device off the coast of Oman, with a fire breaking out in the engine room. All crew are safe.
  • Bahrain sounded missile alert sirens and said its air defense systems intercepted multiple Iranian attacks.
  • Jordan intercepted four missiles entering its airspace from Iranian territory.
  • China expressed "deep concern" and urged both sides to restore safe passage through Hormuz.
  • Qatar announced four days of mourning following the death of former Emir Sheikh Hamad bin Khalifa Al Thani at age 74.

Brent Crude Surges 5% to $87.49

Oil prices extended their rally Tuesday, with Brent crude futures gaining 5% to $87.49 per barrel — the highest since June 12. Prices had already surged more than 9% on Monday. MarineTraffic data showed vessel activity through the Strait of Hormuz declined by approximately 52% between July 10-12 compared to the previous week. Analysts warn that a prolonged blockade could send prices back toward the record highs seen in March.
