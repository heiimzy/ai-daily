⚡ WAIC 2026 Day 2: Zhiyuan Launches 5 New Products with A3 Ultra Humanoid Robot, Joyson Electronics Debuts Dexterous Hand & Semi-Solid Battery, Trump Admin Plans "AI FINRA"

1. Zhiyuan Robotics Unveils 5 Products at WAIC 2026, A3 Ultra Named "Treasure of the Hall"

On the morning of July 18, the second day of the 2026 World Artificial Intelligence Conference (WAIC 2026) in Shanghai, Zhiyuan Robotics (also known as Agibot) released five major new products spanning humanoid robots, service robots, educational robots, dexterous hands, and the world's first cycling robot, demonstrating its comprehensive embodied AI portfolio.

The flagship product, the Zhengzheng A3 Ultra, stole the spotlight, being named WAIC 2026's "Treasure of the Hall." The A3 Ultra features a massive upgrade from its predecessor, equipped with a 700TOPS embodied processor, LIDAR, multi-position fisheye cameras, and a high-degree-of-freedom dexterous hand. It is positioned as a mass-producible, commercially deployable full-size humanoid robot targeting industrial manufacturing, logistics sorting, and commercial service scenarios.

Other new products include the Elf G2 Max service robot (greeting, guiding, and selling), the Lingxi X2 EDU educational version (supporting multiple end-effector types and secondary development), the Critical Point OmniHand 3 Ultra-M dexterous hand, and the world's first cycling robot "Kutuo." At the interactive deployment zone, Zhiyuan recreated the Chengdu Kuanzhai Alley tourist center, with Lingxi X2 explaining local culture and Elf G2 handling shopping services, showcasing embodied AI's application potential in cultural tourism. At the industrial zone, Elf G2 demonstrated semiconductor wafer tray loading/unloading, autonomously identifying, grasping, and transporting chip trays.

On the technology front, Zhiyuan showcased the GO-2 self-developed embodied foundation model — the "decision brain" for robot operations, featuring an innovative action chain-of-thought and asynchronous dual-system architecture that bridges planning and execution. The World Model GE-2 has evolved from "world simulation" to "world learning," while the Genie Evolver 1.0 closed-loop reinforcement learning system enables continuous robot evolution through real-world interaction and human expert guidance.

2. Joyson Electronics Debuts Dexterous Hand & Semi-Solid Battery, Robot Components Enter Mass Production

At WAIC 2026, automotive parts giant Joyson Electronics made a high-profile debut as a robot components supplier, unveiling dexterous hands, semi-solid (hybrid liquid-solid) batteries, 3rd-generation AI head assemblies, electronic skin, and embodied AI brains, announcing that its controllers have already entered mass production for leading robotics companies.

Dexterous Hand: 20-DOF "Palm-Integrated Hybrid Drive"

Joyson's first self-developed "Lingxi" dexterous hand (TeleHand) series features a professional-grade version with an industry-first "palm-integrated hybrid drive" design, offering 20 degrees of freedom with direct-drive, tendon, and linkage mechanisms. The micro-actuator is 50% smaller and 30% lighter, with 2-3x higher torque density than competitors. The self-developed electronic skin uses the world's first physically decoupled 3D force sensing technology with human-level resolution. A more cost-effective basic version is also available for industrial and service applications.

Semi-Solid Battery: 60% Range Boost for Robots

To address the critical robot endurance bottleneck, Joyson's Juen Energy subsidiary launched a hybrid liquid-solid (semi-solid) battery pack with 380Wh/kg energy density, delivering approximately 60% improved range, 2000+ charge cycles, -20~60°C wide-temperature operation, and 30-minute fast charging to 80%. CTO Xu Wei noted that semi-solid batteries are currently the only rapidly scalable route — all-solid-state technology is not yet mature, while liquid batteries have hit performance limits. The batteries have been shipping since April 2026.

Embodied AI Brain: 40TOPS to 2070TFLOPS Computing Range

For robot intelligence, Joyson unveiled an automotive-grade edge physical AI platform central controller already in mass production for leading robotics customers. The new EAOS+EAPC solution offers "one brain for multiple forms, software-hardware integration, and full-scenario adaptation," covering a compute range of 40TOPS to 2070TFLOPS. It has been deployed in real industrial and automated charging scenarios. The EAOS operating system integrates a world model, Agentic OS, and Memory system, enabling robots to complete complex long-horizon tasks and achieve autonomous evolution.

Joyson Electronics stated that with robots on the eve of mass production, the company is accelerating the deployment of reliable, scalable, and low-cost robot core components through its "self-development + investment" dual-track strategy.

3. Trump Administration Considers "AI FINRA" as Silicon Valley Pushes Back on Export Controls

According to sources familiar with the matter, the Trump administration is considering establishing an independent regulator to review AI model safety — reporting to the SEC, modeled after the Financial Industry Regulatory Authority (FINRA). Treasury Secretary Bessent helped draft the proposal, which is currently under review by White House Chief of Staff Susie Wiles.

The proposal comes amid growing Silicon Valley frustration with what industry leaders describe as "ad hoc" government intervention in frontier AI releases. Last month, Anthropic was forced to disable its Fable 5 and Mythos 5 models due to export control impacts, while OpenAI made major adjustments to its Sol model release at government request. Both companies objected to the scale of intervention.

The proposed framework would provide greater certainty for top AI labs through an industry-funded independent technical expert committee that would conduct pre-release safety reviews, covering areas such as cybersecurity and biological threats. The plan aligns closely with a proposal by Google DeepMind CEO Demis Hassabis, who this week advocated for a FINRA-style AI oversight body. The proposal has won public endorsements from Microsoft CEO Satya Nadella, OpenAI CEO Sam Altman, and SpaceX CEO Elon Musk.

Notably, the rapid progress of Chinese AI models like Kimi K3 — and the resulting global chip stock selloff — has accelerated the White House's internal push for an AI regulatory framework. A White House official said the administration is considering multiple proposals to "consolidate America's leading position in AI and strengthen cybersecurity." The framework remains in discussion stages and has not yet been reviewed by President Trump.

4. WAIC 2026: Embodied AI Explodes, Shanghai AI Industry Enters Value Delivery Phase

WAIC 2026 reveals a clear trend: embodied AI has officially moved from concept to reality. Exhibitor count surged from 800+ last year to 1,100+, with exhibition space growing nearly 50%, most of it driven by the embodied AI track. A wave of new embodied AI startups — including Xingdong Jiyuan, Lingchu Intelligent, and Songyan Power — made their WAIC debuts, with the entire supply chain from harmonic reducers to full robot systems on display.

Beyond embodied AI, WAIC showcased the progress of domestic AI chip alternatives. Huawei's Ascend Atlas 950 SuperPod made its public debut with 8,192-chip supernode architecture; Zhongke Shuguang released China's first domestically produced 100,000-card AI supercluster (Shuguang 8000); and domestic chip makers including Enflame, Moore Threads, and MXube showcased their full-stack computing capabilities.

On the economic front, Shanghai's AI industry has entered a concentrated value delivery phase. The number of Shanghai-based companies with market caps exceeding 100 billion yuan ($14B) grew from 16 to 26, with IT companies surging from 3 to 11 and their market cap share jumping from 21.8% to 51.7%. SMIC leads with a 1.23 trillion yuan market cap. China's NDRC announced at WAIC that the country's AI-related industry exceeded 1 trillion yuan ($147B) in 2025, with 2026 growth projected above 30% and AI penetration across key industrial sectors exceeding 80%.

WAIC 2026 runs through July 20, with more product launches and the SAIL Award results still to come.
