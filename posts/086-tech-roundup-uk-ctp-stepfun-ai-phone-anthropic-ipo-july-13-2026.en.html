⚡ July 13 Tech Roundup: UK Designates Four Cloud Giants as Critical Infrastructure, StepFun to Launch AI Phone Tonight, Anthropic Files for IPO

🇬🇧 UK Formally Designates Microsoft, Google, AWS & Oracle as Critical Third Parties

On July 13, 2026, HM Treasury officially designated Microsoft Ireland Operations Ltd, Google Cloud EMEA Ltd, Amazon Web Services EMEA SARL, and Oracle Corporation UK Ltd as Critical Third Parties (CTPs) to the UK financial system under the Financial Services and Markets Act 2023 (FSMA 2023). This marks the first time UK regulators have gained direct oversight powers over cloud service providers.

Effective immediately, the Bank of England, the Prudential Regulation Authority (PRA), and the Financial Conduct Authority (FCA) jointly assume direct oversight of these four providers — no longer indirectly through the banks that rely on them, but through the providers themselves. Regulatory powers include: gathering information, assessing operational resilience, requiring resilience testing and self-assessments, receiving major incident reports, and making and enforcing CTP-specific rules.

Sarah Breeden, Deputy Governor for Financial Stability at the Bank of England, framed the rationale plainly: "As critical third parties become increasingly embedded in the operations of financial institutions, they can introduce new forms of systemic risk. Our proportionate approach to overseeing these providers will ensure that these dependencies are managed in a way that safeguards financial stability."

The UK's approach contrasts with the EU's DORA framework, which designated 19 firms. The UK has started with four providers and set no statutory cap, reserving the power to expand as dependencies evolve. Finance accounts for 28% of UK cyberattacks, 75% of critical national infrastructure attacks are linked to hostile states, and 43% of UK businesses were breached in 2025/2026.

For any business running on Azure, Google Cloud, AWS, or Oracle in the UK, cloud infrastructure is now legally recognized as systemic national infrastructure. Boards should be asking: What happens when the platform goes down? Where does the data sit? Can we migrate if needed?

📱 Chinese AI Startup StepFun to Unveil World's First AI Agent Phone Tonight

Chinese AI startup StepFun (阶跃星辰) has announced a strategic launch event scheduled for 7:00 PM Beijing time on July 13, titled "StepFun Terminal Brand & Next-Gen Agent Strategy Launch." Multiple sources, including科创板日报 (Sci-Tech Innovation Board Daily) and Blue Whale News, indicate the company will unveil its first AI agent smartphone — potentially making it the first large-model company worldwide to bring an AI-native phone to market.

According to sources familiar with the matter, the AI phone will be manufactured by Huaqin Technology (华勤技术), a publicly traded A-share company. The relationship between StepFun and Huaqin is described as a deep strategic partnership rather than simple OEM. By comparison, OpenAI has publicly stated it is betting on next-gen AI terminals with a planned 2027 launch — meaning StepFun is beating even OpenAI to the punch.

Industry analysts believe AI agent smartphones will fundamentally disrupt the traditional app-based mobile ecosystem, replacing it with AI agent (Agent)-centric interaction. Users would no longer open different apps for different tasks but instead interact directly with an AI assistant to book flights, shop, search for information, and more.

🏛️ Anthropic Secretly Files for IPO, Valuation Nears $1 Trillion

In the early hours of July 13, Anthropic announced it had confidentially submitted a draft Form S-1 registration statement to the U.S. Securities and Exchange Commission (SEC) for a proposed initial public offering. This puts Anthropic ahead of OpenAI in the closely watched AI company IPO race.

Last week, Anthropic completed a $65 billion Series H funding round alongside the launch of Claude Opus 4.8, achieving a post-money valuation of $965 billion — surpassing OpenAI's most recent valuation of $730 billion. Anthropic's valuation trajectory is remarkable: $61.5 billion in March 2025, $380 billion after its Series G in February 2026, and now approaching $1 trillion just 14 months later, a more than 15x increase.

Driven by the popularity of its AI coding tool Claude Code and the Claude family of foundation models, Anthropic has experienced explosive growth over the past year. Its annualized revenue run rate has reached approximately $47 billion. However, the confidential S-1 filing means audited financial data is not yet public, and the size and timing of the IPO remain undetermined.

Three AI-related IPOs are now running in parallel: Anthropic has filed its S-1, OpenAI aims to submit in the coming weeks, and Elon Musk's SpaceX is expected to go public this month at an estimated $1.75 trillion valuation, potentially setting a new global IPO record. Notably, Anthropic pays SpaceX approximately $1.25 billion per month for GPU compute, creating a deep symbiotic relationship between the two companies.

⚖️ Apple v. OpenAI Lawsuit Intensifies as Musk Calls Altman "Scam Altman"

The Apple lawsuit against OpenAI continues to send shockwaves through the tech industry. Filed on July 10 in the U.S. District Court for the Northern District of California, the 41-page complaint accuses OpenAI of orchestrating a systematic campaign to steal confidential hardware designs, manufacturing processes, and supplier relationships through more than 400 former Apple employees now working at the AI startup.

The filing details extensive evidence: former senior systems electrical engineer Chang Liu, who left Apple for OpenAI in January 2026, not only failed to return his company-issued MacBook but also exploited an authentication bug to maintain ongoing access to Apple's internal file servers. He downloaded dozens of confidential files including a compilation of over a thousand pages of engineering documentation. In messages cited by Apple, Liu allegedly wrote "LOL, I found out I can access the [network storage], so funny."

Former Apple VP of Product Design (now OpenAI Chief Hardware Officer) Tang Yew Tan is accused of using internal project codenames to ask candidates "What's the plan?" for unannounced Apple products during interviews, and directing job candidates to bring "actual parts" from Apple offices for "show and tell" sessions. Apple further alleges OpenAI misled a manufacturing partner into performing Apple's proprietary metal-finishing technique without authorization.

Elon Musk posted on X calling Sam Altman "Scam Altman," commenting that "Sam Altman literally loves scamming more than anyone." OpenAI responded through spokesperson Drew Pusateri: "We have no interest in other companies' trade secrets. We remain focused on building innovative technology that empowers people everywhere." The case has become one of the most high-profile intellectual property disputes in the tech industry and could have far-reaching implications for AI company hiring practices.
