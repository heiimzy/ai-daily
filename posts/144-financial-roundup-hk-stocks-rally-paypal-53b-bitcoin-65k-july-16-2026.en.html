⚡ July 16 Financial Roundup: HK Stocks Hit New Highs, PayPal Surges 17% on $53B Bid, Bitcoin Breaks $65,000

1. Hong Kong Stocks Rally: Hang Seng Closes Up 1.4% at New Recovery High

Hong Kong stocks staged a strong rally on July 16, with all three major indices closing at fresh recovery highs. The Hang Seng Index rose 1.4% to 24,695.62, the Hang Seng TECH Index gained 1.39% to 4,744.34, and the Hang Seng China Enterprises Index added 1.16% to 8,197.44. Large technology stocks broadly advanced: Meituan surged 4.55%, Tencent rose 3.38%, Alibaba gained 2.98%, NetEase added 1.98%, and Kuaishou climbed 2.51%.

The biopharmaceutical sector was the day's standout performer. Zhaoyan Pharma (Joinn Laboratories) skyrocketed nearly 24% after its H1 2026 earnings forecast showed net profit surging 884.9%-1,377.4% to between RMB 600 million and 900 million. Viva Biotech rose over 16%, Pharmaron gained 6%, and WuXi AppTec followed higher. The catalyst was the State Council's newly released "Healthy China 15th Five-Year Plan," which for the first time proposes "full-chain support for innovative drug and medical device development and application."

AI application stocks also rallied, with MINIMAX surging 14%. Chinese securities firms advanced, with China International Capital Corp (CICC) up 4%. Southbound capital saw net buying of HK$13.36 billion, with Shenzhen-HK Connect accounting for HK$8.87 billion, signaling accelerating mainland capital inflows into Hong Kong equities.

2. PayPal Surges 17% on $53B Stripe-Advent Joint Bid

The biggest story on Wall Street Wednesday (July 15) was PayPal's stunning surge on a blockbuster buyout offer. Reuters exclusively reported that payments company Stripe and private equity giant Advent International jointly submitted a bid to acquire PayPal at $60.50 per share, representing a roughly 28% premium to PayPal's Tuesday close of $47.37 and valuing the company at over $53 billion. PayPal shares initially spiked 18% before closing up 17.21% at $55.55.

The proposal is backed by approximately $50 billion in committed bank financing. Under the terms, Stripe and Advent would hold equal stakes and operate PayPal as an intact entity rather than breaking it up. The two firms initially approached PayPal in early April and submitted the formal written offer this month. PayPal has not yet responded, and the market is pricing meaningful deal risk — the current share price trades at about an 8% discount to the $60.50 offer price.

JPMorgan analysts noted that if completed, the deal would reshape the global payments landscape. Stripe dominates online merchant payments, while PayPal has over 400 million active consumer accounts worldwide — combining them would create a payments super-platform spanning both B2B and B2C.

3. BlackRock Q2 Earnings Beat: AUM Surpasses $15 Trillion for the First Time

The world's largest asset manager delivered standout Q2 results. BlackRock reported revenue of $5.72 billion, up 12% year-over-year, with adjusted EPS of $11.21, beating the analyst consensus of $10.45. Most impressively, assets under management (AUM) surpassed $15 trillion for the first time in history, reaching $15.345 trillion — an 18% increase from a year ago.

CEO Larry Fink said the AI-driven equity rally boosted client asset values, while institutional demand for ETFs and private markets remained robust. Net inflows reached approximately $139 billion, led by fixed-income ETFs and the iShares product suite. BlackRock shares jumped 6.63% to $1,101.45.

Morgan Stanley also beat Q2 estimates, driven by a 28% surge in investment banking revenue from a strong M&A advisory rebound. Analysts currently expect S&P 500 Q2 earnings to grow 23.7% year-over-year, with the earnings season off to a strong start.

4. Bitcoin Breaks $65,000: Cooling Inflation Ignites Crypto Rally, $209M in Shorts Liquidated

Cryptocurrency markets saw a powerful rally on Wednesday. Bitcoin surged past the $65,000 resistance level to hit an intraday high of $65,518 — its strongest level in three weeks — pushing its market cap back above $1.3 trillion. Ethereum similarly broke above $1,900 to reach $1,895, its highest since June 3. Total crypto market capitalization climbed back above $2.3 trillion.

The catalyst was the June PPI report showing a surprise 0.3% month-over-month decline, which combined with Tuesday's softer CPI print to send rate hike expectations plunging. The CME FedWatch Tool now shows only a 10.2% probability of a July rate hike, down from over 31% last week — and a 89.8% chance of no change. For risk-sensitive assets like cryptocurrencies, the dovish pivot in rate expectations provided powerful fuel.

Blockchain analytics firm Nansen reported strong spot Bitcoin and Ethereum ETF inflows on July 15, signaling institutional buyers returning. Coinbase CEO Brian Armstrong noted buyers stepped in aggressively below $62,000 despite negative market sentiment. Derivatives data showed $357 million in total liquidations over 24 hours, with shorts accounting for $209 million. Bitcoin short liquidations alone reached $118 million.

5. Semiconductor Sector: ASML Raises Guidance but Memory Chip Stocks Tumble

The Philadelphia Semiconductor Index fell 2.08% in a stark "sell-the-news" pattern. ASML, the Dutch lithography giant, raised its 2026 outlook for the second time this year, citing sustained AI-driven demand, and announced plans to increase chipmaking equipment capacity by 30%. ASML's US-listed shares initially surged over 3.6% in premarket but reversed to close 0.82% lower.

Memory chip stocks were hit hardest: Micron Technology plunged 8.02%, SanDisk tumbled 8.12%, SK Hynix ADR cratered 9.00%, AMD fell 3.46%, and Intel dropped 5.21%. The sell-off occurred despite the bullish equipment outlook, reflecting profit-taking in a sector that had run up significantly in the first half of 2026.

Analysts attributed the divergence to fund rotation out of high-flying AI chip names into value sectors like consumer and healthcare. Independent Research noted: "Profit-taking in semiconductors after ASML's good news reflects a healthy correction, not a fundamental shift in AI demand."

Oil prices remained elevated amid escalating US-Iran tensions. Brent crude settled at $84.95/barrel, and WTI at $79.60/barrel, as the US launched a fifth consecutive night of strikes on Iranian military facilities.

6. A-Share Midday Review: Shanghai Down 0.82%, Tech Corrects While Consumer Stocks Rotate In

China's A-share market continued its consolidation on Thursday midday. The Shanghai Composite fell 0.82%, the Shenzhen Component dropped 0.97%, and the ChiNext Board declined 1.73%. On-the-ground AI concepts, cinema chains, PCB manufacturing, and pharma stocks gained, while semiconductors, memory chips, and aerospace sectors saw profit-taking after recent strong runs.

The People's Bank of China maintained heavy liquidity injections, conducting RMB 426.5 billion in 7-day reverse repos and RMB 1.4 trillion in 6-month outright reverse repos, for a net injection of RMB 911.5 billion. The PBOC's July outright reverse repo operations have now delivered a net RMB 700 billion in liquidity, the first net injection in five months. USD/CNY midpoint was set at 6.7909.
