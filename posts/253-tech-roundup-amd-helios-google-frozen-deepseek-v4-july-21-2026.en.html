⚡ July 21 Tech Roundup: AMD Helios Takes On Nvidia's Crown, Google's Frozen v2 Chip Promises 6-10x Efficiency, DeepSeek V4 Open-Sources 1M Context

🔴 AMD Officially Unveils Helios — Its First Rack-Scale AI System, Microsoft Joins as Latest Customer

Chip giant AMD on Monday formally launched Helios, its first AI-focused rack-scale system, named after the Greek god who drives the sun chariot across the sky. The system represents AMD's first direct challenge to Nvidia's wildly popular Grace Blackwell and Vera Rubin systems, marking a genuine competitive shift in the AI chip market for the first time in years.

Microsoft signed on as Helios' flagship customer. "We are expanding the Azure infrastructure portfolio with AMD Helios to deliver the performance, scale, and choice our customers need to build and run next-generation AI applications," said Microsoft CEO Satya Nadella. Helios will power inference workloads for Azure AI services. AMD shares rose over 4% on the news, while Microsoft gained over 1%.

Forrest Norrod, head of AMD's data center business, said Helios integrates four self-developed components — GPU, CPU, network, and software — all designed to deliver the "lowest total cost per token." At an event in May, AMD CEO Lisa Su claimed Helios offers "significant advantages over Nvidia's rack-scale systems in terms of inference, memory bandwidth, and memory capacity."

Helios Technical Specifications

  • Weighs 7,000 lbs (~3.2 tons) — wider and heavier than Nvidia's Vera Rubin
  • 18 compute trays, each with 4 Instinct GPUs driven by a single EPYC CPU
  • Up to 12 network chips per tray (built on Pensando technology acquired in 2022)
  • Estimated cost: $5–5.5 million per unit (vs. $3.5–4M for Vera Rubin per Futurum Group)

Meta committed to up to 6 GW of AMD GPU deployment starting with 1 GW on Helios later this year. OpenAI and Oracle have also made major deployment commitments, and India's Tata Consultancy Services has signed on. Eight of the world's top 10 AI companies already run workloads on AMD Instinct GPUs. Nvidia currently controls over 95% of the data center GPU market, with AMD holding just 4.5%, but analysts at Futurum Group predict AMD could capture 20–25% market share — representing hundreds of billions in revenue. AMD plans to generate tens of billions in data center AI revenue from 2027 onward, with the majority coming from the Helios family. The data center segment grew 57% year-over-year in Q1 2026.

🔵 Google Reportedly Building 'Frozen v2' Server Chip for 6-10x Gemini Efficiency Boost

Google is developing a new server chip codenamed "Frozen v2" designed to dramatically improve the efficiency of running its Gemini models, according to reports on July 21. The chip partially embeds Gemini's architecture permanently into silicon, reducing the compute and data transfer required for each user query.

Google engineers estimate that Frozen delivers 6 to 10 times the token processing capability per watt compared to the company's latest TPU (Tensor Processing Unit). This efficiency leap would allow Google to process significantly more inference requests without increasing power costs. Google shares rose nearly 3.7% on the news in early trading.

Google plans to deploy Frozen by 2028. The chip will not replace general-purpose TPUs but will serve as a more specialized branch of Google's custom silicon lineup. The design has a trade-off: reduced flexibility — it will only be compatible with future Gemini models that maintain the same underlying architecture. Google currently views Frozen v2 as an experimental project with no mass production plans.

The project aims to address Google's increasingly severe compute shortage, which has intensified internal resource competition and reportedly forced Google Cloud to turn away external clients. Last month, Google agreed to pay SpaceX nearly $1 billion per month to bridge its compute gap.

Google stated that its teams "continuously research and experiment with innovative technologies to deliver the best performance and highest efficiency," noting that while not all projects reach production, "rigorous exploration is a core component of our full-stack strategy." However, Google's AI division faces near-term challenges — the next-generation Gemini Pro has been delayed, and several senior AI researchers have departed for competitors.

🟢 DeepSeek-V4 Preview Released: Million-Character Context Open-Sourced, Agent Capabilities Rival Top Closed Models

In the early hours of July 21, DeepSeek released the preview version of its new DeepSeek-V4 series model and open-sourced it. DeepSeek-V4 features a million-character (1M) ultra-long context, leading both domestic and open-source fields in Agent capabilities, world knowledge, and reasoning performance. The model comes in two variants — Pro and Flash — and is available immediately via the official website and app.

DeepSeek-V4-Pro: Performance on Par with Top Closed-Source Models

  • Agent capabilities significantly improved: Achieves best-in-class performance among open-source models on Agentic Coding benchmarks, with internal feedback ranking it above Sonnet 4.5 and close to Opus 4.6 non-thinking mode
  • Rich world knowledge: Significantly ahead of other open-source models, trailing only Gemini-Pro-3.1 among closed-source models
  • World-class reasoning: Outperforms all publicly evaluated open-source models in math, STEM, and competitive coding — matching the world's top closed-source models

Architectural Innovation: Novel Attention Mechanism

DeepSeek-V4 introduces a new attention mechanism that compresses at the token dimension, combined with DSA Sparse Attention (DeepSeek Sparse Attention), achieving world-leading long-context capability while significantly reducing compute and memory requirements. From now on, 1M context will be standard across all official DeepSeek services. The model has been optimized for mainstream Agent products including Claude Code, OpenClaw, OpenCode, and CodeBuddy. The DeepSeek API now supports V4-Pro and V4-Flash with both OpenAI ChatCompletions and Anthropic interfaces, supporting non-thinking and thinking modes with configurable reasoning_effort parameter (high/max). Model weights and the technical report have been released to the open-source community.

Industry analysts view DeepSeek-V4 as bringing million-token context to the mainstream. With significantly enhanced Agent capabilities, China's open-source LLM ecosystem continues to gain ground in the global AI race.
