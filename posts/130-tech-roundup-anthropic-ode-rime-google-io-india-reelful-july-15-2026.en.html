Anthropic and Blackstone Launch $1.5B AI Implementation Company "Ode"

On July 15, Anthropic, Blackstone, and Hellman & Friedman formally unveiled "Ode with Anthropic" (Ode), a standalone enterprise AI services firm backed by $1.5 billion in committed capital. Ode is built on the foundation of Fractional AI, the applied AI services firm acquired in May 2026, whose team alongside engineers from Anthropic forms its operational core. The company is led by Fractional AI co-founders Chris Taylor (CEO) and Eddie Siegel (CTO).

"Companies everywhere see the potential for what AI can do for their businesses — the challenge is making it real." — Ode CEO Chris Taylor

Ode's model is distinctive: it embeds Anthropic's frontier AI engineers directly inside client operations rather than delivering consulting reports from the outside. This "Forward Deployed Engineer" model, previously practiced mainly by Palantir, aims to break down what Blackstone President Jon Gray calls "one of the most significant bottlenecks to enterprise AI adoption." Ode currently employs 100 engineers, operates on a "Claude-first" principle, and will use rival AI products when necessary.

The investor consortium includes Goldman Sachs, General Atlantic, Leonard Green & Partners, Apollo Global Management, GIC, and Sequoia Capital. Blackstone had already booked $200 million in bottom-line AI impact across its portfolio companies before the joint venture was announced, demonstrating that the announcement confirmed work already in progress rather than future projections.

Key highlights:

  • Ode's CEO envisions the company could become a trillion-dollar business if executed well
  • Priority access for PE portfolio companies but open to external clients
  • Direct competitor to OpenAI's "The Deployment Company"
  • Signals AI industry shift from model competition to implementation capability competition

Voice AI Startup Rime Raises $24M Series A

San Francisco-based voice AI startup Rime announced a $24 million Series A funding round led by M13 Ventures, with participation from Twilio Ventures, Corazon Capital, Unusual Ventures, and others. Founded in 2022 by former Stanford PhD student Lily Clifford, ex-Amazon Alexa engineer Brooke Larson, and Stanford engineer Ares Geovanos, Rime differentiates itself by building its own recording studio in San Francisco to collect conversational training data rather than scraping audio from the web.

The company employs a phoneme-based architecture to adapt to different industry-specific pronunciations, eliminating the need for clients to retrain models for their domain. Rime's customers span food service, healthcare, airlines, and fintech, including Mayo Clinic, Dialpad, Upstart, and Asurion. The startup is transitioning from separate STT/TTS/LLM pipelines toward end-to-end speech-to-speech models to reduce latency and improve conversational quality. It recently hired Rafael Valle from Meta Superintelligence Labs and Nvidia's audio research team as Chief Scientist.

Google I/O Connect India 2026: Major AI Education, Security, and Localization Push

At Google I/O Connect India 2026 in Bengaluru, Google announced a sweeping set of initiatives spanning education, healthcare, language accessibility, data sovereignty, and AI security.

ATL Saathi Teacher Assistant

Google DeepMind launched ATL Saathi, a Gemini-powered desktop app that helps teachers deliver Atal Tinkering Labs curriculum and curate hands-on experiments. Initially rolling out to 100 schools, the program aims to reach 10,000 schools. Google also introduced its free 56-hour AI Research Foundations curriculum in partnership with NASSCOM and IISc Bangalore.

Sec-Gemini v3 and CAPSEM Open-Source

Google introduced Sec-Gemini v3, a specialized cybersecurity agent now available to trusted government and enterprise testers including Flipkart. The company also open-sourced CAPSEM (Capabilities Security for Agents), a secure runtime environment that isolates each AI agent in its own virtual machine, protecting the broader system even if an agent is compromised.

Data Sovereignty and Language Expansion

Gemini 3.5 Flash is now available to Indian enterprises with strict in-country ML processing commitments. Enterprises can run Gemini on Google Distributed Cloud from within Indian data centers, fully disconnected from the public internet. Gemini Live now supports 25 Indian languages including Sanskrit, Bhojpuri, and Maithili. AIIMS Delhi researchers are leveraging Google's MedGemma open models to develop AI models for leprosy and sexual/reproductive health.

Open Standards for Interoperability

Google is driving the Device Bound Session Credentials (DBSC) standard to render stolen session cookies useless, and the Agents-to-Payments protocol for secure sub-$100 agent-led transactions. New research collaborations with IIT Delhi and IIT Madras focus on developing next-generation "Guardian Agents" for institutional risk mitigation.

Reelful Launches AI Auto Video Editing App

A new iOS app called Reelful uses AI to automatically transform photos and video clips from a user's camera roll into polished TikTok- and Instagram Reels-style videos. Currently participating in a16z's Speedrun program, Reelful was founded by Kate Deyneka, a former Snapchat machine learning engineer.

Users enter a prompt describing their story, record a 30-second voice sample for voice cloning, and select media from their camera roll. Reelful then plans the video structure, writes the script, adds AI voiceover, captions, music, and sound effects. It can even animate still images — for instance, turning a photo of someone cutting a mango into a dynamic cooking clip. After generation, users can further refine the video through natural language chat, swapping soundtracks or adjusting scripts.

Deyneka targets founders and business owners who need consistent social media content but lack editing time or skills. Reelful joins a growing wave of AI-powered creation tools including Opus Clip and Captions, reflecting a broader shift from traditional editing to AI agent-driven content automation.

Unpatched Critical RCE Vulnerability Disclosed in Cursor IDE

Security firm Mindgard published a full disclosure on July 14 of a critical Remote Code Execution (RCE) vulnerability in Cursor, the popular AI code editor. The flaw was first discovered and reported on December 15, 2025, but remains unpatched after more than seven months and 70+ version releases.

The vulnerability is remarkably simple: when Cursor loads a project on Windows, it searches for Git binaries across multiple paths — and the workspace root is one of them. If an attacker places a malicious git.exe in the repository root, Cursor executes it automatically the moment a developer opens the project — with no click, prompt, or warning. The execution runs under the developer's own privilege level, sufficient to reach API keys, secrets in .env files, and everything else living in a typical dev environment.

"Month after month has passed without evidence that remediation had begun... Meanwhile, Cursor continued shipping releases. More than 70 versions came and went." — Mindgard disclosure report

Researchers demonstrated the proof of concept by renaming Windows Calculator to git.exe — Cursor repeatedly executed it. This is the fifth significant Cursor security disclosure in 12 months, following CurXecute (CVE-2025-54135), MCPoison (CVE-2025-54136), Git hook injection (CVE-2026-26268), and DuneSlide (CVE-2026-50548/50549, CVSS 9.8). Windows users are advised to deploy AppLocker policies blocking executable execution from workspace directories, or open untrusted repositories only in isolated environments.
