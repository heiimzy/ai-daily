⚡ July 23 Tech: Gaode Unveils Full-Stack ABot Embodied AI Upgrade — 5 Models Achieve 17 SOTA Records, Enabling 'Eyes-Guided Movement, Coordinated Hands-Feet, Never-Forgetting Memory'

On the morning of July 23, Alibaba subsidiary Gaode (AMAP) announced a comprehensive full-stack upgrade of its ABot embodied AI system, releasing five models — ABot-N1, ABot-M0.5, ABot-ER, ABot-AgentOS, and ABot-C0 — spanning navigation, manipulation, brain-level decision-making, agent middleware, and motion control. The system achieved SOTA (state-of-the-art) results on 17 authoritative benchmarks, making Gaode the first company worldwide to simultaneously reach SOTA in both embodied navigation and manipulation — marking a paradigm shift from incremental technical improvements to systematic evolution in embodied AI.

World's First Full-Stack Embodied AI System

Gaode's ABot is the world's first unified embodied technology stack that couples world models, foundation models, and embodied Agent architecture into a single, highly synergistic system. Unlike most industry solutions that focus on optimizing individual model capabilities, ABot's core differentiator is its self-evolving closed-loop flywheel: simulation training → physical interaction → memory scheduling — where data accumulated from every navigation, manipulation, and interaction feeds back to drive continuous system improvement.

The five models correspond to a robot's "hands, feet, brain, central nervous system, and motor nerves":

  • ABot-N1 (Feet — Navigation Foundation Model): Features a fast-slow dual-system architecture — the slow system handles long-range logical reasoning while the fast system manages real-time control, achieving 92.9% success rate in outdoor navigation tasks.
  • ABot-M0.5 (Hands — Manipulation Foundation Model): Decomposes movement and manipulation into two parallel action streams, outperforming the previous SOTA by 20.4% on complex tasks in RoboCasa-365.
  • ABot-ER (Brain — Embodied Reasoning Model): Unifies end-to-end judgment from perception to action, enabling robots to "understand" relationships between objects, spaces, and tasks beyond simple visual discrimination.
  • ABot-AgentOS (Central Nervous System — Agent Operating System): Adapts to humanoid, quadruped, and wheeled robots with cross-task lifelong memory that optimizes storage structure based on failure trajectories.
  • ABot-C0 (Motor Nerves — Motion Control System): Builds a unified behavioral foundation for quadruped robots, translating decisions into precise robot actions.

Technical Breakthroughs Behind 17 SOTA Records

Navigation: Five Tasks Unified in One Model

ABot-N1 is the world's first model to integrate five core navigation tasks — Point-Goal, Object-Goal, Instruction-Following, POI-Goal, and Person-Following — into a single architecture. It set new world records across seven authoritative benchmarks including CityWalker, SocNav, and R2R-CE, with a 40.5% surge in SocNav success rate.

Manipulation: Dream Self-Healing and Data Reflux

ABot-M0.5's unique "Dream Self-Healing" training method generates actions based on its own predicted "noisy visual frames" to prevent task failure from minor deviations. Self-generated data flows back through the ABot system to create a continuous evolution flywheel, making it perform better on harder tasks.

Physics World Model: From Pixels to Physics

At the system's core lies ABot-PhysWorld, a 14-billion-parameter Diffusion Transformer model that embeds physical laws into the generative process — the first-ever embodied world model that serves as a differentiable, evolving physics engine rather than a video generator. It ranked first on the WorldArena global leaderboard, outperforming Google and NVIDIA.

From Digital Navigation to Physical World OS

Gaode's evolution from "navigating for humans" to "building a physical world operating system for robots" is powered by two decades of spatiotemporal data accumulation and large-scale mapping engineering. The company has built the industry's largest embodied navigation data engine, comprising approximately 8,000 high-fidelity 3D scenes and nearly 17 million expert demonstrations.

In real-world deployment, Gaode's quadruped robot "Tutu" has completed guide-dog level urban road tests in Beijing's Yizhuang area — autonomously guiding visually impaired individuals through dynamic and static obstacle avoidance, narrow passage traversal, and unexpected interference scenarios. This application demonstrates Gaode's unique cross-domain extension from map navigation to physical world intelligence.

Market and Industry Impact

Gaode's ABot full-stack upgrade arrives at a critical juncture in the global embodied AI race. Industry data shows that in the first half of 2026, global investment in the "world model" track exceeded 40 billion yuan, with 33 companies rushing into the space. Among China's 46 AI financing rounds, embodied AI accounts for a rapidly growing share.

Analysts highlight two strategic implications of Gaode's ABot: First, it validates the viability of migrating from digital maps to physical world intelligence. Second, Gaode has announced it will open-source parts of ABot's capabilities — covering world models, navigation, manipulation, and the Claw interface — positioning spatial intelligence as foundational infrastructure for the embodied era.

From a competitive perspective, while Google and NVIDIA remain focused on cloud AI capabilities, Gaode has chosen a differentiated path from "spatial data → spatial intelligence → physical world OS" — a move that could represent a pivotal lane-change overtaking strategy for Chinese AI in the embodied intelligence race.
