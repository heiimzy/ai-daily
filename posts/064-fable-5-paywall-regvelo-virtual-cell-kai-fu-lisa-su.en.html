Fable 5 Free Era Ends Tonight: Usage Credits Begin July 13

Anthropic's Claude Fable 5 promotional free access period concludes tonight at 11:59 PM Pacific Time. This is the final extension after Anthropic moved the original July 7 cutoff to July 12 following widespread user backlash. Starting July 13, all Fable 5 usage will require Usage Credits at $10 per million input tokens and $50 per million output tokens (with 90% discount on prompt caching). Subscribers can still use up to 50% of their weekly plan limit on Fable 5 at no extra cost, but any usage beyond that threshold requires credits or switching to Opus 4.8 or Sonnet 5. Anthropic describes this as a temporary capacity measure and plans to restore Fable 5 to subscription plans when capacity allows. This marks a striking contrast with OpenAI's GPT-5.6 Sol pricing at $5/$30 per million tokens, which remains fully included in Plus and above subscription tiers. The pricing bifurcation among frontier models is now clearly established — Fable 5's exit from free inclusion means paid subscribers face narrower top-tier model options within their existing budgets.

RegVelo Virtual Cell Model Published in Cell: AI Predicts Cell Fate with Unprecedented Accuracy

A team led by Prof. Fabian J. Theis of Helmholtz Munich and Prof. Tatjana Sauka-Spengler of Oxford University has published RegVelo (Regulatory Velocity) in Cell — the first end-to-end deep learning model that fuses neural networks with cellular dynamic processes in a single framework. By combining differential equations with neural networks to model gene regulatory dynamics, the system can simulate how altering gene regulation (e.g., knocking out transcription factors or modulating regulatory circuits) changes cell fate. In zebrafish neural crest development experiments, RegVelo achieved a Spearman correlation of 0.52 between predictions and actual experimental data for 11 transcription factor knockouts — far exceeding the sub-0.25 scores of all other methods. Co-first author Weixu Wang of Helmholtz Munich highlighted organoid research as the most exciting application: "We can computationally predict 'if we activate or inhibit a transcription factor at a certain developmental window, how will the final cell composition change?'" — compressing differentiation protocol optimization from pure experimental trial-and-error to computation-guided targeted validation. The team is collaborating with Google and NVIDIA on AI scientist and virtual cell projects, signaling that AI-driven drug target screening is moving from vision to operational platform tools.

Kai-Fu Lee & AMD's Lisa Su: "Performance AI Must Stop" — Enterprise AI Must Impact P&L

At AMD's Shanghai AI Developer Day, 01.AI CEO Kai-Fu Lee and AMD CEO Lisa Su delivered a frank assessment of AI's enterprise adoption crisis. Lee's blunt diagnosis: "If your AI deployment doesn't ultimately change any number that appears on a quarterly earnings call, then you haven't done a real AI transformation — you've just wasted money building an AI lab." He traced the evolution — 2024 asked "Can AI complete a task?"; 2025 shifted to "Can AI complete an entire workflow?"; 2026's core question has now advanced to "Can AI replace an entire corporate function?" Yet almost every enterprise is deploying AI in low-risk, low-value scenarios: meeting summaries, HR chatbots, internal search — "surface-level work." Lee predicted that CIO-led bottom-up AI transformation will likely fail; enterprise AI must be a CEO-driven top-down initiative focusing on core P&L-impacting areas: revenue, profit, fraud prevention, dynamic pricing, supply chain, and speed-to-market. at the event, 01.AI and AMD jointly launched Cube01, a privatized computing node designed to solve the cost and privacy challenges of deploying frontier models in the enterprise.

BAAI Orca World Model: 4B Parameters Matches Specialized Robotics Systems

The Beijing Academy of Artificial Intelligence (BAAI) has released Orca, a world foundation model that takes a fundamentally different approach — instead of predicting the next token or video frame, it predicts the next state of the world in an abstract internal representation. Orca-4B matches the specialized π0.5 system across five robotic manipulation tasks (shelving books, stacking bowls, scooping sugar) despite its base model never having seen a single action label during pre-training. On the PRICE-V0.1 image prediction benchmark, Orca-4B scores 59.8%, outperforming FLUX.2 small (56.1%) and OmniGen2 (39.6%). Notably, Orca demonstrates error-recovery capability — retrying after failed grasps while π0.5 gets stuck in repeated failures. Trained on 125,000 hours of video, Orca offers a promising path to solving robotics' chronic shortage of labeled action data by learning world dynamics from passive observation alone.
