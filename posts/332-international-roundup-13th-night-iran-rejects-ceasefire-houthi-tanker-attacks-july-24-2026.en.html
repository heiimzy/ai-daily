⚡ July 24 International Politics Deep Dive: US 13th Night Strikes Hit Across Iran, Tehran Rejects Ceasefire, Houthi Missiles Strike Two Saudi Tankers Driving Brent Past $100

🇺🇸 US Launches 13th Consecutive Night of Strikes on Iran

US Central Command (CENTCOM) confirmed that American forces initiated the 13th round of consecutive nightly strikes on Iran at 6:45 PM ET Thursday (06:45 Beijing time Friday). The operation targeted Iranian military command centers, missile and drone storage facilities, coastal surveillance systems, and air defense assets, according to CENTCOM, which said the strikes aimed to "further degrade the Islamic Revolutionary Guard Corps' ability to threaten commercial shipping."

Iranian state media reported that US missiles struck the southwestern city of Ahvaz, with explosions also heard in nearby Omidiyeh. The strategic port city of Bandar Abbas and Qeshm Island—both located on the Strait of Hormuz—were hit by multiple projectiles. Iran's IRNA news agency reported that more than 10 missiles struck targets on Qeshm Island.

A power substation near the Bushehr nuclear power plant was hit for the third consecutive day, causing several hours of blackout in nearby villages. The deputy governor of Iran's Khuzestan province confirmed that a US strike on a passenger terminal near the Shalamcheh border crossing killed two people and injured 11 others.

Iran's navy confirmed that two naval search-and-rescue vessels were severely damaged in US strikes on Hormozgan province. Meanwhile, the US Navy tightened its naval blockade of Iranian ports—CENTCOM announced that since resuming the blockade nine days ago, it has redirected 12 commercial vessels attempting to access Iranian ports and disabled one.

🚫 Iran Rejects Latest US Ceasefire Proposal: No 'Temporary Deal'

According to The New York Times, citing Iranian and Iraqi officials, Iraqi Prime Minister Ali al-Zaidi traveled to Tehran on Friday with a fresh US ceasefire proposal crafted after his earlier meeting with President Trump at the White House. Iran flatly rejected the offer.

Iranian officials told the newspaper that Tehran is not interested in a "temporary deal" that fails to address the final status of the Strait of Hormuz—the core sticking point in negotiations. The US proposal was seen in Tehran as a superficial ceasefire that avoids resolving the fundamental question of who controls the strategic waterway through which one-fifth of the world's oil once flowed.

Al-Zaidi's office said the visit aimed to "call for peace and dialogue" and promised that Iraqi territory would not be used for attacks on Iran. Iran's Foreign Minister Abbas Araghchi had previously stated that "as long as the United States fails to understand that the only way forward is to respect the Iranian people and Iran's national interests, there is no prospect of progress."

The rejection effectively scuttled the White House's hopes of announcing an agreement on July 24, as US officials had been briefing just hours earlier.

🔥 Houthi Red Sea Escalation: Two Saudi Tankers Hit, Brent Crude Breaches $100

Yemen's Iran-backed Houthi movement announced Thursday that its forces carried out a major operation against two Saudi oil tankers—the Encelia and the Layla—in the Red Sea, using cruise missiles, ballistic missiles, and drones. Saudi Arabia's official SPA news agency confirmed that the Encelia was hit, with a fire breaking out at the bow of the vessel.

The UK Maritime Trade Operations (UKMTO) center reported that a tanker was struck by an "unknown projectile" approximately 70 nautical miles southwest of Al Shuqaiq on Saudi Arabia's Red Sea coast, causing a fire that the crew was battling. No casualties were reported. The Houthis claimed to have caused "massive balls of fire and devastation," framing the attack as a "siege-for-siege" response to what they described as Saudi Arabia's 12-year blockade of Yemen.

The attacks mark a significant escalation from the Houthis' earlier announced blockade to actual combat operations. Data tracking shows that five oil tankers diverted away from the Bab el-Mandeb Strait on July 22 alone, following three fully laden Saudi crude supertankers bound for China and India that had turned around the previous day. Analysts warn that Saudi Arabia—now sending approximately 75% of its oil exports through the Red Sea via Yanbu after the Strait of Hormuz closure—faces a potential dual chokehold.

Brent crude futures for September delivery surged past $100 per barrel for the first time since May 26, with intraday gains exceeding 4%, as markets priced in the risk of a simultaneous closure of two of the world's most critical energy chokepoints.

💣 Trump's Multi-Pronged Strategy: 'Massive Attack' Warning, Frozen Asset Penalty, Nuclear Deal Condition

President Donald Trump told Axios in an interview Thursday that he is "seriously considering" restarting major combat operations in Iran. "I am considering a massive attack. Bigger than ever before. I am close to making a decision. We are all set for it," Trump said, adding that Iran "needs more of the same" and is "not ready yet" for a deal.

In a series of Truth Social posts, Trump announced several new policies:

  • Frozen Asset Compensation Mechanism — "From this point forth, any and all damages done to Ships, Cargo, or anything related thereto, will be paid for by Iranian Money that the United States has in its possession, and controls." Trump acknowledged the damages "may be very substantial" but called it "the fair and equitable thing to do."
  • Holding Iran Accountable for Houthis — Warning that if the Houthis attack again, the US will hold Iran responsible and inflict "major military punishment" on both Iran and the Houthis.
  • Saudi Nuclear Deal Condition — Clarifying that the US-Saudi civilian nuclear agreement is "totally subject to Saudi Arabia joining the very respected and successful Abraham Accords," and that there will be "no enrichment of material" by Riyadh.

Iranian Foreign Minister Abbas Araghchi responded: "Our defense doctrine is clear: eye for an eye. Any aggression against Iran, including our infrastructure, will compel a powerful and decisive response." Iran's joint military command warned that if Trump follows through on his threat to bomb bridges and power plants, Iranian forces would target regional oil, gas, electricity, and economic infrastructure, preventing the export of "even a single drop of oil."

🛡️ Mossad Foils Iranian Infiltration Assassination Plot

Israel's Mossad intelligence agency revealed Thursday that it had thwarted an Iranian plot to infiltrate operatives into Israel to assassinate senior officials. According to the agency, Iran recruited an Israeli resident codenamed "Philippe" through proxy intermediaries, tasking him with assembling a cell to enter Israel and carry out attacks.

Mossad said it identified and monitored the cell members before they could enter Israel, and suspects have been arrested. This marks one of the most significant Iranian infiltration attempts disclosed since the outbreak of the US-Israel war with Iran in February.

Separately, Israeli Defense Minister Israel Katz convened an emergency security assessment with IDF Chief of Staff Lt. Gen. Eyal Zamir and senior military officials. Katz issued a stark warning to Iran, declaring that any attack on Israel would be met with a "crushing blow." Multiple Israeli cities have opened public bomb shelters amid elevated risk assessments of Iranian missile strikes over the weekend.

🌐 Iran Strikes Back Across Multiple Fronts: US Assets in Kuwait, Jordan Hit

Iran's Islamic Revolutionary Guards Corps (IRGC) announced the continuation of Operation "Nasr-2," claiming attacks on US military facilities across the region on July 23-24:

  • Kuwait — The IRGC claimed to have destroyed a large military equipment warehouse, a Patriot missile defense system, and an MQ-9 drone hangar at Ali Al Salem Air Base.
  • Jordan — The IRGC claimed to have destroyed a THAAD missile defense system radar with "lightning strikes," along with a Patriot system and a C-RAM radar. Fuel tanks at the base were reportedly set ablaze, and a large helicopter equipment warehouse and repair shed were destroyed.
  • Kuwait-Iraq Border — Kuwait's Defense Ministry confirmed a drone attack on the Abdali border crossing with Iraq, causing property damage but no casualties.

Iranian army spokesman Brig. Gen. Mohammad Akraminia warned: "The armed forces' retaliatory attacks will continue as long as the US attacks on the country's infrastructure and coastal areas continue."

According to Iran's Health Ministry, US strikes since June 27 have killed 55 people and wounded 629. US Defense Secretary Pete Hegseth confirmed in Senate testimony that 18 US service members have been killed and over 450 wounded, with the total cost of the war reaching $37.5 billion.
