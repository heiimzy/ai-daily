Anthropic Upgrades Claude Voice Mode: Multi-Model Support with Office App Connectivity

Early on July 24, Anthropic announced a major upgrade to Claude's voice mode, now supporting model selection between Opus, Sonnet, and Haiku. The system automatically defaults to the fastest version of whichever model the user last used in their text chat.

Previously limited to the Haiku model, Claude's voice mode was fast but struggled with complex tasks. The upgrade enables longer, more sophisticated conversations — providing feedback on communication style, rehearsing client pitches, and brainstorming product-market research.

The standout feature is the new ability to connect with external apps — Gmail, Google Calendar, Slack, Canva, and Notion are all supported. Users can now ask Claude to schedule a meeting, draft an email, or create a Notion document — all by voice. This marks a clear differentiation from OpenAI's voice mode, which despite its conversational improvements still lacks the ability to take action through connected tools.

This is Claude voice mode's core differentiator: an AI voice assistant that is no longer just a "talking tool" but a smart agent that can connect to enterprise productivity ecosystems and execute real work through natural language.

Anthropic has also expanded multilingual support. Voice conversations are now available in English, French, German, Hindi, Indonesian, Italian, Japanese, Korean, Portuguese (Brazilian), and Spanish (Latin America/Spain), though users must manually specify their language.

The new voice mode is available to all users in beta across platforms. Free users can access the Haiku model with one connected app, while paid subscribers unlock Sonnet or Opus with more app connections. Anthropic did not upgrade the underlying voice model in this release, so interruption handling and conversational fluidity still trail OpenAI's latest voice solution.

AegisAI Raises $36M Series A: AI Agents Defending Against AI-Powered Spear Phishing

AegisAI, founded by former Google security executives Cy Khormaee and Ryan Luo, announced a $36 million Series A round led by Battery Ventures, with participation from Accel and Foundation Capital. The startup, less than a year old, has now raised $49 million in total.

Khormaee and Luo previously developed Google's Safe Browsing technology and reCAPTCHA. They identified that traditional rule-based email security systems relying on "if-then" logic can no longer keep pace with AI-powered cyberattacks.

"AI-powered attacks bypass existing controls more than half the time now, which means they're almost twice as effective as they used to be," Khormaee told TechCrunch. "They've researched you, they understand everything about you, and they're targeting attacks that are perfectly bespoke to you."

AegisAI's solution trains specialized AI agents that analyze each email like a human security analyst, catching subtle anomalies that even the most exhaustive checklists would miss. The technology can already identify malicious PDF attachments with built-in passwords and CAPTCHAs — techniques increasingly used to bypass standard spam filters.

In less than a year, AegisAI has onboarded dozens of enterprise customers, including crypto payments company Mash, AI startup LangChain, and Google-owned privacy compliance platform Lokker. "The bad guys are using email to attack us using AI at a much faster pace than we can keep up with," said Dharmesh Thakker, general partner at Battery Ventures. "Defending against that is going to be a number one priority for a lot of companies."

AegisAI plans to start with email security and expand into data security and other defense verticals. "The core idea of building customized, highly advanced agents that can do investigations is going to determine who becomes the next dominant security company," Khormaee said.

OpenAI Rolls Out ChatGPT Health to All US Users: 300 Million Health Queries Per Week

OpenAI announced the full rollout of ChatGPT Health to all US users aged 18 and older across Free, Go, Plus, and Pro tiers. The feature allows users to connect health data from Apple Health and MyFitnessPal, as well as electronic medical records from Epic and Oracle Health systems, for personalized health information and guidance.

Since launching as a dedicated hub in January, ChatGPT Health has seen explosive demand. Weekly health-related queries have grown from 230 million to 300 million. Notably, approximately 70% of health queries occurred outside the dedicated hub — users habitually ask health questions in general conversations. Now they can draw on connected health information from any chat, such as checking food allergy information during meal discussions.

OpenAI stated that even the smallest model in its latest lineup, GPT 5.6-Luna, outperforms the previous-generation GPT 5.5 on the internal HealthBench evaluation. The company emphasized it does not use any user data for model training and continues to work with over 260 physicians to improve health-related response quality.

The launch comes amid controversy — the day prior, a former Florida pastor sued OpenAI, claiming ChatGPT's medical advice led to a pulmonary embolism that nearly killed him. OpenAI repeatedly states in its terms of service that its products are "not intended for use in the diagnosis or treatment of any health condition" and urges users to consult professionals before making medical decisions.

300 million weekly health queries speaks volumes about AI demand in healthcare — from understanding lab results to preparing for doctor visits, from dietary advice to insurance comparison. AI is becoming the first stop for hundreds of millions seeking health information. But the line between "assistance" and "replacement" remains the industry's defining challenge.

ChatGPT Health is now available to US users 18+ on Web and iOS. Android support is expected in a future update.
