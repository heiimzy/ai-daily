⚡ July 14 Financial Roundup: CPI Posts Biggest Drop Since April 2020, Goldman Beats by 44%, Warsh Testimony Looms

Tuesday July 14, 2026, has shaped up to be a "Super Tuesday" for financial markets — with the June CPI report, Big 5 bank earnings, and Fed Chair Kevin Warsh's first congressional testimony all converging on the same day.

The June CPI, released at 8:30 AM ET, showed headline CPI falling 0.4% month-over-month — the largest single-month decline since April 2020 — pulling the annual rate down to 3.5% from 4.2% in May. Core CPI (excluding food and energy) was unchanged month-over-month at 2.6% year-over-year. Meanwhile, Goldman Sachs crushed Q2 expectations with GAAP EPS of $20.98 versus the $14.47 consensus, fueled by a record-breaking investment banking quarter that included the SpaceX IPO.

Here are the five key stories driving markets today.

1. US June CPI: -0.4% MoM — Biggest Drop Since Pandemic, But Core Still Sticky

The Bureau of Labor Statistics reported on Tuesday that the Consumer Price Index for All Urban Consumers (CPI-U) decreased 0.4% on a seasonally adjusted basis in June — the largest 1-month decline since April 2020, when it fell 0.8%. Over the last 12 months, the all items index increased 3.5%.

The energy index was the primary driver of the decline, falling 5.7% in June after surging 3.9% in May. Gasoline prices plunged 9.7% month-over-month. Despite the monthly drop, energy prices are still up 15.7% year-over-year.

The closely watched core CPI (all items less food and energy) was unchanged in June (+0.0% MoM), with the annual rate easing to 2.6% from 2.9% in May. Shelter costs rose just 0.1% in June, the smallest monthly increase in over two years, providing hope that housing inflation is finally cooling.

However, Fed Governor Christopher Waller warned on Monday that "if this week's core inflation comes in hot again, the FOMC will have to consider tightening monetary policy in the near term." The market is now pricing nearly a 50% probability of a July rate hike — up from below 40% on Monday morning.

"The June CPI decline is welcome news, but 2.6% core inflation is still far from the Fed's 2% target. Markets shouldn't overreact to a single month of data — the oil price rebound effect could reappear in July." — Wall Street analyst note

2. Goldman Sachs Q2 Earnings: EPS $20.98 Crushes Estimates, SpaceX IPO Boosts Investment Banking

Goldman Sachs reported Q2 2026 earnings before the bell on Tuesday, delivering a massive beat: GAAP EPS of $20.98 versus the $14.47 consensus (44.5% above expectations), and revenue of $20.34 billion versus the $16.49 billion consensus.

Investment banking was the standout performer. Goldman served as a lead underwriter for SpaceX's record-breaking $75 billion IPO — the largest in history — and helped Alphabet raise over $80 billion in equity financing for AI infrastructure. Through mid-June, Goldman's bankers had participated in over $1 trillion in M&A transactions this year, the fastest pace ever to reach that milestone.

Equities trading revenue is also expected to exceed $5 billion for Q2, just shy of the record set in Q1. The broader Wall Street "trading盛宴" continues — analysts project the Big 5 banks (JPMorgan, Bank of America, Citigroup, Goldman Sachs, Wells Fargo) will report combined Q2 trading revenue of nearly $39 billion.

JPMorgan Chase also reported Tuesday with consensus expecting revenue of ~$51.8 billion and EPS of ~$5.84. Key focus areas include net interest income guidance, consumer credit provisions, and the ongoing CEO succession question following Jamie Dimon.

3. Fed Chair Warsh's First Congressional Testimony: Markets on Edge

Federal Reserve Chair Kevin Warsh will deliver the central bank's semi-annual monetary policy report to Congress on Tuesday at 10:00 AM ET (10:00 PM Beijing time). This marks Warsh's first congressional testimony since taking the helm of the Fed in June 2025.

Warsh's testimony comes just hours after the June CPI release, giving him the opportunity to incorporate the latest inflation data into his remarks. Nick Timiraos, the "Fed Whisperer" at the Wall Street Journal, noted that several of Warsh's colleagues have grown increasingly concerned about inflation and may push for a rate hike discussion at the July 28-29 FOMC meeting.

The 2-year Treasury yield has already surged to 4.28%, its highest level since February 2025, reflecting growing rate hike expectations. BMO Capital Markets' Ian Lyngen said "the market is now treating the July 29 FOMC decision as a potential rate hike event."

4. US Stocks Fall Monday: Chip Rout Hits Nasdaq, Apple Defies the Carnage

Ahead of the CPI data and earnings, US stocks sold off sharply on Monday. The three major indexes all closed in the red:

  • Dow Jones: -138.37 points (-0.26%) to 52,498.64
  • S&P 500: -60.05 points (-0.79%) to 7,515.34
  • Nasdaq: -408.43 points (-1.55%) to 25,873.18

The Philadelphia Semiconductor Index cratered 4.78%, leading the sell-off. Memory and chip stocks were hit hardest: SanDisk plunged 12.63%, SK Hynix ADR tumbled 9.32% (profit-taking after Friday's 12% Nasdaq debut surge), Intel fell 6.12%, Micron dropped 4.32%, and Nvidia declined 3.52%.

In stark contrast, Apple rose 0.71% to $316.91, hitting a new all-time high during the session. Analysts attribute the divergence to a "capital rotation" trade — money flowing out of overheated AI/semiconductor names into defensive quality stocks. The Energy sector was the S&P 500's only notable gainer, surging 3.16% on the back of crude's near-10% spike.

5. Commodities & FX: Brent Surges 9.6% Past $83, Gold Breaks Below $4,000

Oil prices surged on Monday as US-Iran tensions over the Strait of Hormuz escalated dramatically:

  • Brent crude settled at $83.30/barrel, up 9.6% (+$7.29) — the biggest single-day dollar gain since April 2020
  • WTI crude settled at $78.14/barrel, up 9.4% (+$6.73)
  • President Trump reinstated the naval blockade on Iran and proposed a 20% "guardian fee" on all cargo transiting the Strait of Hormuz

The oil spike reignited inflation fears, pushing gold below the psychologically critical $4,000 level:

  • Spot gold fell 2.87% to $3,986.64 — breaking below $4,000 for the first time since early June
  • Spot silver dropped 3.71% to $57.61
  • The US Dollar Index rose 0.28% to 101.24; the 10-year Treasury yield climbed to 4.62%

Market Outlook

Wednesday brings June PPI and retail sales data. Bank earnings season continues with Morgan Stanley reporting on Wednesday, followed by tech giants next week.

The market is currently pricing in at least one 25-basis-point rate hike this year. Warsh's congressional testimony will be the key variable for market direction this week. A hawkish tilt could trigger another round of tech selling, while a steady-as-she-goes message could ease anxiety.

As Nick Timiraos put it: "Warsh now holds the June CPI print — one of the last major data points before the next meeting. Every word he says will be parsed by markets."
