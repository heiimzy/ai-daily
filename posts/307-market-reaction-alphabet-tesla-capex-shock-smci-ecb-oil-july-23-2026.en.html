⚡ July 23 Financial Morning Briefing: Alphabet Plunges 5% After Hours on $205B Capex Shock, Tesla FCF Turns Negative, ECB Decision Takes Center Stage

Alphabet Q2: Revenue Beats But $205B Capex Guidance Sparks After-Hours Selloff

Alphabet reported impressive Q2 2026 earnings after Wednesday's close, but the market reaction told a very different story. Revenue came in at $119.8 billion (+24% YoY), beating estimates, while GAAP net income surged 298% to $112.1 billion — though that figure was inflated by $99 billion in unrealized gains from stakes in Anthropic and SpaceX.

The real shock came from capital expenditures. Q2 capex doubled year-over-year to $44.9 billion, slightly above the $44.2 billion consensus. More critically, management raised full-year 2026 capex guidance to $195-205 billion, up from the $180-190 billion range provided just last quarter. CFO Anat Ashkenazi further warned that 2027 spending would "significantly increase" again.

The massive spending pushed free cash flow to negative $5.86 billion — Alphabet's first-ever negative quarterly FCF. Just last year, the company was generating over $24 billion in quarterly free cash flow. The deterioration stunned Wall Street.

Google Cloud was the standout performer, with revenue surging 82% to $24.8 billion. The cloud backlog surpassed $514 billion, signaling explosive AI demand. Alphabet's "other income" line — including gains from Anthropic and SpaceX stakes — added $99 billion. Yet investors focused entirely on the spending trajectory. Alphabet shares, already down 1.46% in regular trading, plunged nearly 5% in after-hours trading.

Tesla: Record Revenue Can't Mask EPS Miss and Negative FCF

Tesla also reported after the bell, posting record quarterly revenue of approximately $27.6 billion (+15% YoY). However, adjusted EPS of $0.36 missed the $0.50-0.55 consensus, and net income fell 5% to roughly $1.1 billion.

The core issue was the explosion in capital spending. Q2 capex surged to $6.7 billion (+168% QoQ) as Tesla ramped up AI infrastructure and robotics investments. Free cash flow swung from +$1.44 billion in Q1 to -$3.25 billion in Q2. The company raised its full-year 2026 capex guidance from $20 billion to $25 billion.

Elon Musk told analysts on the earnings call that being "a little less capital efficient is acceptable" and warned capex would remain elevated for the next 2-3 years. Automotive gross margin excluding regulatory credits slipped from 19.2% in Q1 to approximately 18%, as price wars and investment costs pressured profitability. Tesla shares slipped about 3% in after-hours trading.

SMCI Soars 20% After Hours on $60B+ Order Backlog

Super Micro Computer was the big winner of the evening. The server maker released preliminary Q4 results showing more than $60 billion in new orders — far exceeding expectations. Management also guided for improved gross margins, citing sustained demand for AI infrastructure. SMCI stock surged nearly 20% after hours, lifting peers Dell Technologies and Hewlett Packard Enterprise.

"We are seeing unprecedented market demand, especially in AI infrastructure," CEO Charles Liang said. "Customers are deploying AI compute capacity faster than ever before."

IBM Cuts Full-Year Outlook as Clients Reroute IT Budgets

IBM released a pre-earnings warning indicating Q2 preliminary revenue of $17.2 billion, below the $17.86 billion consensus. The company cut its full-year forecast, blaming a rapid shift in customer IT spending toward AI servers and storage hardware — away from IBM's traditional software and services. Infrastructure revenue fell 7% YoY, consulting was flat, and management admitted it "did not adapt fast enough." IBM shares came under pressure after hours.

ECB Rate Decision Today: Hold at 2.25% Expected, September Hike in Focus

The European Central Bank announces its July rate decision later today, with markets pricing less than a 7% chance of a move. The deposit rate is expected to remain at 2.25% after June's 25bp hike. All 74 economists polled by Reuters anticipate a hold.

The real action will come from President Christine Lagarde's press conference. A Reuters survey found 70% of economists expect at least one more rate hike this year, most likely in September. ING analysts said Brent crude trajectory is the key variable — a sustained move above $100/bbl would force the ECB to hike in September. Eurozone inflation stands at 2.8%, well above the ECB's 2% target, with energy costs rising 8.7% YoY.

Commodities and Asia Markets: Brent Near $95, Gold Steady, Asia Mixed

Brent crude futures continued their rally Thursday, approaching $95/barrel — a six-week high. The US-Iran conflict entered its 12th day, with Houthi forces threatening Red Sea shipping and Hormuz Strait traffic collapsing. WTI crude rose to approximately $87. Analysts warn Brent could break $100 if the conflict escalates further.

Spot gold held above $4,120/oz, supported by sustained safe-haven demand. India's MCX gold futures hit 144,570 rupees, a weekly high.

Asia-Pacific markets opened mixed on Thursday. Japan's Nikkei 225 fell ~1.7% below 53,000, and South Korea's KOSPI dropped ~3.7% on continued chip stock weakness (SK Hynix -4%+). In China, the Shanghai Composite hovered near flat at 3,860 (+0.07%), but the ChiNext index plunged 3.23% as tech growth stocks saw profit-taking while capital rotated to defensive sectors like power and precious metals.两市成交缩量至2.65万亿元。
