⚡ WAIC 2026 Day 3: Tencent Unveils Three Embodied AI Foundation Models and Xiao Liu Robot, Ant LingBot Launches Brain 2.0 World Model, AGIBOT Deploys 30+ Robots Across Venue

July 19, 2026 — The third day of the World Artificial Intelligence Conference (WAIC 2026) in Shanghai saw no slowdown in momentum. Across over 100,000 square meters of exhibition space, more than 300 AI products made their global debut. The spotlight shifted from large language models to the industrial deployment of embodied intelligence: Tencent released three embodied AI foundation models alongside its next-generation "Xiao Liu" robot; Ant LingBot Technology unveiled its Brain 2.0 platform, with the LingBot-World 2.0 world model sparking discussions across the global developer community; and AGIBOT hosted the Embodied AI Forum while deploying over 30 robots across the venue.

Tencent Fires Three Salvos: Embodied AI Foundation Models Break the "Perception-Body-Action" Barrier

On the afternoon of Day 3, Tencent's Robotics X Laboratory and Futian Laboratory, in collaboration with Hunyuan, released a new suite of embodied AI products that systematically bridged the "perception–body–action" loop for the first time. Three foundation models were unveiled, each handling a distinct layer of the embodied intelligence stack.

Hy-Embodied-VLM-1.0: Lightweight Visual Perception Model

This next-generation vision-language model tackles three progressively complex challenges: "see and understand" — recognizing object depth, orientation, functional parts, and grasp points; "see and anticipate" — predicting the outcome of potential actions; and "see and remember" — tracking progress across long-horizon tasks, analyzing failures, and replanning. Across 37 benchmark tasks, the model significantly outperformed comparable-sized alternatives. Remarkably, at just A3B (≈3 billion) parameters, it delivers performance close to the previous A32B flagship model at a fraction of the computational cost, making it ideal for on-robot deployment.

Hy-Embodied-RxBrain-1.0: Native World Cognition Model

This model unifies "reasoning" and "imagination." Given a task, it simultaneously produces step-by-step verbal instructions and generates visual representations of what the environment should look like after each step — enabling downstream action models to not only "hear what to do" but also "see what done looks like." Tencent established the RxBrain-Bench evaluation benchmark in collaboration with industry partners, where RxBrain significantly outperformed all comparable systems and matched dedicated embodied world models on short-term state prediction.

Hy-Embodied-VLA-0.5: Vision-Language-Action Unified Model

The VLA model's core advantage lies not in raw parameter count but in its end-to-end learning stack spanning data collection, training, and deployment. Tencent accumulated over 10,000 hours of human demonstration data through a sub-millimeter precision UMI acquisition system. On the third-party RoboDojo benchmark, it achieved the highest composite simulation score, ranking first in both long-horizon tasks and memory tasks — the most demanding dimensions of continuous robotic execution.

All three models have been open-sourced. Tencent confirmed they are already deployed in real industrial environments, including retail guidance, elderly care, and consumer goods factories — where HyVLA-0.5 achieves over 95% success rates with cycle times under 6 seconds per unit, and new SKU model adaptation takes under 3 days.

Apexio Agent and TairosAgent Framework: From "Disembodied" to "Embodied Native"

Tencent also introduced the Apexio persistent embodied agent, featuring a three-layer architecture operating at different frequencies: the top-layer cognitive system activates on demand for deep reasoning; the mid-layer perception-action system continuously processes multimodal information at approximately 15Hz; and the bottom-layer execution system operates at the highest frequency, reflexively handling collisions and balance disruptions. The system is driven by both "goal-driven" (task completion) and "survival-driven" (safety, energy management, operational status) imperatives — it continues perceiving and self-preserving even without external commands.

The TairosAgent framework, purpose-built for robotics from day one, offers a three-tier architecture (perception-reaction, cognitive decision-making, exploration-consolidation) with persistent environmental, ontological, and task memory. Through a unified hardware adaptation layer and standardized Skill interface, it supports diverse models, skills, and robot morphologies. Response times for navigation, explanation, and recovery-after-interruption scenarios dropped from tens of seconds with general-purpose frameworks to just 2–3 seconds.

Based on these new capabilities, Tencent's open-source Tairos platform has been comprehensively upgraded, offering models, agents, and development tools to the broader robotics community.

Meet "Xiao Liu": The Next-Generation Demonstration Robot

Tencent also showcased its latest demonstration robot, "Xiao Liu" (Little Six), the successor to the 2024 "Xiao Wu" platform. Xiao Liu features tendon-cable transmission and a unique "Meridian Soothing Massage" technique. Tencent developed a custom data acquisition system that fuses force, tactile, and visual sensing to capture high-quality manipulation data, training the robot through reinforcement learning to precisely replicate massage trajectories and pressure.

Ant LingBot Brain 2.0: LingBot-World 2.0 World Model Stuns Global Developer Community

Another Chinese embodied AI company, Ant LingBot Technology, upgraded its Brain 2.0 platform on Day 3 of WAIC, releasing a suite of embodied AI models covering vision, action, manipulation, and physics prediction. The headliner was LingBot-World 2.0, a native world model that generates physically consistent long-duration video supporting multi-person, multi-robot interactive virtual environments.

The model is positioned as critical "infrastructure" for embodied AI: traditional robot training is constrained by the cost of real-world data collection, but LingBot-World 2.0 constructs an infinitely expansible digital world governed by physical laws. Robots can undergo massive trial-and-error and skill acquisition at minimal cost — whether it's navigating extreme weather or complex terrain, they can rehearse scenarios "inside their brain" before deploying in the physical world.

The model received widespread acclaim from the overseas developer community. Prominent AI blogger AK noted in a tweet: "Hour-long generation with zero quality drift — this is the only open-source model that achieves this." A former Meta and NVIDIA embodied AI practitioner commented: "Spatial models are delivering incredible value for spatial understanding." This means LingBot-World 2.0 can serve not only as a tool for game or film pre-visualization but as an "infinite training ground" for embodied intelligence.

Ant LingBot's release signals a broader shift in Chinese AI from "follower" to "trendsetter" — as Chinese teams begin to define the "embodied native" technical paradigm, the global influence of China's AI industry is transitioning from quantitative accumulation to qualitative transformation.

AGIBOT Embodied AI Forum: 30+ Robots Serving Across WAIC Venue

On Day 3, AGIBOT (Zhiyuan Robotics) hosted the 2026 Embodied AI Forum at booth H3-C101, bringing together researchers, technology leaders, and robotics entrepreneurs from China and abroad to discuss the development of Physical AI, advances in embodied intelligence, and the path from research systems to scalable real-world applications.

Across the exhibition halls, more than 30 AGIBOT robots were deployed throughout WAIC venues, providing visitor guidance, information services, and interactive demonstrations. This marked a significant milestone: AGIBOT had just announced its 15,000th robot rolling off the production line in June. Co-founder, President and CTO Peng Zhihui stated: "The industry should no longer judge embodied AI by whether a robot can complete an impressive demonstration once, but by whether it can be manufactured, delivered, and integrated reliably into real operating environments."

Day 3 Trend: Embodied AI Enters the Era of Industrial Competition

After three days of WAIC 2026, a clear pattern has emerged: embodied AI has moved beyond "proving the ceiling" to "defining the floor." The industry is no longer focused on demonstrating how high robots can jump, but on defining what each system does best. With 108 chips, 261 large models, 208 embodied AI terminals, and over 300 physical robots on display, the scale of this year's conference would have been unimaginable just two years ago.

Industry insiders broadly agree that 2026 is the "first year of mass production" for embodied AI, with a scale inflection point expected in 2027 or 2028. From Tencent's three open-source foundation models to Ant LingBot's LingBot-World 2.0, from AGIBOT's venue-wide robot deployment to the broader ecosystem of embodied AI startups, China's embodied intelligence industry is charting a complete trajectory from "open-source models, shared data, open platforms" to "deployed scenarios, industrial closed loops."
