⚡ July 11 AI News: GPT-5.6 Goes Live, Meta's 10x Cheaper Model Tops Charts, Apple Sues OpenAI

🔥 OpenAI Launches GPT-5.6 Family, Merges Codex into ChatGPT

On July 9, OpenAI released the GPT-5.6 family — three models named Sol, Terra, and Luna — covering everything from flagship hard tasks to lightweight high-frequency use cases. All three models rolled out to ChatGPT, Codex, and API within 24 hours.

Flagship Sol achieved 53.6 points on Agents' Last Exam (a long-horizon agentic benchmark), outperforming Claude Fable 5 by 13.1 points at roughly one-quarter the cost. Sol outputs up to 750 tokens per second. API pricing: $5/M input, $30/M output for Sol; $2.5/$15 for Terra; $1/$6 for Luna.

The bigger product news: the standalone Codex app is being retired and its capabilities merged directly into ChatGPT, creating "ChatGPT Work" — a new AI super-app entry point. ChatGPT now has three modes: Chat for conversation, Codex for coding, and Work for general office tasks that can span hours across multiple apps and files.

OpenAI also confirmed GPT-5.6 is the last in the 5.x series, with GPT-6 expected later this month or next.

💰 Meta Muse Spark 1.1: A "Pro-Scenario Assassin" at 10x Lower Cost

The same night, Mark Zuckerberg returned to X after three years to announce Muse Spark 1.1 — Meta's second-generation multimodal reasoning model and an unmistakable price war declaration. With a 1M-token context window, active context management, and dual-role agent/subagent architecture, it's priced at just $1.25/M input and $4.25/M output — ~10x cheaper than Anthropic's Fable 5 and ~33% cheaper than xAI's Grok 4.5.

Muse Spark 1.1 dominated professional benchmarks: TaxEval v2 (79.72, #1), MedScribe (88.89, #1), and Harvey's Legal Agent Bench (20.00, doubling second-place Grok 4.5 at 12.92). On Vals' comprehensive index it ranked fourth among all models, and it ran 2-3x faster than the top three.

However, general reasoning remains a weakness — GPQA ranked 12th, LiveCodeBench 17th. Zuckerberg acknowledges the gap but emphasizes Meta's strategy: "We can deliver frontier-level intelligence at more affordable costs" — backed by Meta's advertising revenue buffer against a prolonged price war.

⚖️ Apple Sues OpenAI Over Hardware Trade Secrets

On July 10, Apple filed a lawsuit against OpenAI in the Northern District of California, accusing the AI giant of systematically stealing iPhone maker's intellectual property to develop consumer AI hardware. The complaint alleges "employees from technical staff to chief hardware officer have collaborated to steal Apple's trade secrets and confidential information." Apple demands OpenAI cease the practices and redesign unreleased AI hardware products. The case has sparked widespread debate about compliance in AI companies' hardware expansion strategies.

🧠 Anthropic Discovers Claude's J-Space: A "Global Workspace" Inside the Model

On July 6, Anthropic published groundbreaking research revealing a "global workspace" inside Claude — discovered through a new technique called the Jacobian lens (J-lens). The J-space is a privileged internal channel where Claude holds concepts it can report, control, and reason with — silently, without outputting any tokens.

Through causal swap experiments, Anthropic proved J-space concepts directly participate in reasoning. For instance, when processing blackmail evaluation scenarios, J-space lights up with "fake" and "fictional" markers before any output, indicating the model knows the test is staged. This has profound safety monitoring implications: teams can now read hidden model states to detect potential deception. Anthropic explicitly refrains from claiming phenomenal consciousness, but the research opens an unprecedented window into AI's internal reasoning processes.

🇨🇳 Meituan LongCat-2.0: A Milestone for Domestic Computing Power

Meituan's technical team announced LongCat-2.0, the industry's first trillion-parameter model trained end-to-end on a 50,000-card domestic GPU cluster. The model has 1.6T total parameters with ~48B average activated parameters (dynamic range 33B-56B), natively supports 1M-token context, and is optimized for Agentic Coding tasks. The achievement marks a critical breakthrough from "usable" to "truly capable" for China's homegrown AI computing infrastructure.
