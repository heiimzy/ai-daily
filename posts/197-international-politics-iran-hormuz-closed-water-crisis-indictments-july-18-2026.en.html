⚡ July 18 International Evening: Iran Declares Hormuz "Completely Closed", Two Tankers Explode, 10,000 Without Water, Iran Indicts Trump and Netanyahu

🚢 Iran's IRGC Declares Strait of Hormuz "Completely Closed" as Two Tankers Hit Mines

Iran's Islamic Revolutionary Guard Corps (IRGC) announced on Saturday that the Strait of Hormuz has become "extremely unsafe" and is now "completely closed." In a statement published by state news agency IRNA, the IRGC claimed that two oil tankers exploded and caught fire after hitting mines while attempting to cross a mined area south of the strait. The statement said the vessels were "deceived by American intelligence agencies" into entering the minefield. The US Central Command (CENTCOM) swiftly denied the claims, stating: "Like most IRGC claims, this is false."

The IRGC Navy also announced it had "stopped" four ships attempting to transit the critical waterway in a combined missile and drone operation. The Corps warned all vessel owners to heed IRGC warnings, declaring that until US military operations cease, "not a single drop of oil, cubic meter of gas, or ton of chemical fertilizer will be allowed to leave the region."

According to shipping tracking data from MarineTraffic and Kepler, only 6 to 8 vessels transited the strait in the past 24 hours — the lowest level in three weeks. The waterway, which handles approximately one-fifth of the world's oil supply, has effectively ground to a halt. Brent crude settled at $88.10/barrel on Friday, surging over 12% for the week. The International Energy Agency (IEA) warned that global energy security could face a "red alert" if Hormuz traffic does not resume within weeks.

💧 US 7th Night of Strikes: Desalination Plant Destroyed, 10,000 Lose Water Access

The US military completed its seventh consecutive night of strikes on Iran on Friday. CENTCOM said the latest wave targeted "surveillance sites, military logistics infrastructure, underground weapons storage, and maritime capabilities." Defense Secretary Pete Hegseth shared footage of a destroyed surveillance tower at Chabahar port, calling it part of an IRGC maritime surveillance network.

Iran's southern Hormozgan province bore the brunt of the strikes. Government officials reported that US attacks destroyed seawater desalination pumps in the coastal city of Jask, cutting off drinking water for approximately 10,000 people across 20 villages. Local lawmaker Ahmad Moradi told Tasnim that "about seven to eight civilians" were killed in the past two nights across Hormozgan province, including six people killed when their family car was hit on a bridge in Bandar Khamir, and a woman killed in the Tappeh Allaho Akbar neighborhood of Bandar Abbas.

Iranian state media IRNA reported explosions in Sirik, Ahvaz, Yazd, Jask, and Khorramabad. Iran's Foreign Ministry called for an emergency UN Security Council meeting to halt what it described as "US acts of aggression."

⚠️ Iran Issues Ultimatum: Full-Scale Offensive If US Strikes Continue 2-3 More Days

Major General Mohsen Rezaei, a senior military adviser to Iran's Supreme Leader and former IRGC commander, issued a stark warning on Saturday: if US airstrikes continue for another two to three days, Iran will transition from "deterrence and retaliation" to a phase of "offense and complete destruction."

"Iran will no longer limit itself to retaliatory, like-for-like responses... and no political border will be safe," Rezaei told state broadcaster IRIB. He warned Washington against launching any ground operation, stating that Iran's "offensive forces" would pursue US bases and soldiers across all borders. IRGC Aerospace Force Commander Majid Mousavi also said "targeted strikes from across Iran against the enemy will continue" until US operations against Iranian coastal facilities around Hormuz cease.

The IRGC issued a direct warning to all countries hosting US military forces in the region, urging them to "activate civil defense units, protect civilians, and move them away from potential military targets."

⚖️ Iran's Judiciary Indicts Trump and Netanyahu

Iran's judiciary spokesperson Asghar Jahangir announced on Saturday that Tehran prosecutors have formally issued indictments against US President Donald Trump and Israeli Prime Minister Benjamin Netanyahu for "international crimes" committed during the recent conflict. While Iran's legal jurisdiction is not widely recognized internationally, the move is seen as a significant political and propaganda effort to pressure the two leaders.

Earlier, Iran's UN Ambassador Amir-Saeid Iravani sent a letter to UN Secretary-General Antonio Guterres condemning US attacks on Iranian civilian infrastructure, which he said caused "extensive damage to ports, transportation networks, communications facilities, logistics hubs, radar installations, and coastal defense systems." Guterres' spokesperson expressed "deep concern" over the escalation, particularly "attacks on civilian infrastructure in Iran and across the region," calling them "unacceptable."

🔥 Kuwait Plant Hit Again, Bahrain Sounds Multiple Air Raid Sirens

In retaliation for US strikes, Iran's IRGC escalated attacks on US regional allies. Kuwait's military confirmed that Iranian missiles and drones struck multiple targets: a power generation and water desalination plant caught fire, with large numbers of generation units shut down. Kuwait's fire service reported that several firefighters and a worker were injured battling two separate fires. Kuwait Airways rescheduled most flights after Kuwait International Airport temporarily suspended operations.

Bahrain activated air raid sirens at least five times on Saturday. The country's army said air defenses "intercepted and destroyed a number of treacherous Iranian aerial assaults." The IRGC claimed to have destroyed a US drone depot and Bahrain's "main artificial intelligence center."

Jordan's military said it intercepted 10 Iranian missiles overnight with no casualties or damage reported. The IRGC claimed it struck Jordan's Muwaffaq Salti Air Base, destroying at least two US fighter jets and three other aircraft — a claim denied by the US. Saudi Arabia's civil defense issued early warnings for the first time in months.

The US State Department advised all Americans to reconsider travel to the Middle East, specifically warning against travel to northern Israel, Gaza, and the Egyptian border.

🇺🇦 Ukraine Drones Strike Moscow Region Oil Depot, Killing 7 at Logistics Hub

In a major coordinated operation early Saturday, July 18, Ukrainian drone strikes targeted multiple logistics and energy sites across Russia and occupied Crimea. The Noginsk oil depot in the Moscow region caught fire after a drone strike. The facility operates 24 storage tanks with a total capacity of 11,500 cubic meters, storing gasoline, diesel, and kerosene.

A more severe strike hit two Wildberries logistics complexes — in Kotovsk (Tambov region) and Elektrostal (Moscow region). Local authorities confirmed that seven Wildberries employees were killed in the Kotovsk strike. The Elektrostal facility is the company's second-largest logistics hub by order processing volume.

In occupied Crimea, the Krymsky Veter monitoring channel reported over 20 explosions near the Gvardeyskoye military airfield, as well as blasts in Sevastopol, Feodosia, and Kerch. The Balaklava Thermal Power Plant was reportedly also targeted.

The strikes are part of Ukraine's ongoing campaign against Russian fuel infrastructure. Earlier in the week, Ukraine struck the Gazpromneftekhim Salavat complex in Bashkortostan and the Afipsky Oil Refinery in Krasnodar Krai. Ukraine's General Staff confirmed the strikes targeted facilities producing fuel and lubricants that support the Russian military.

🌐 Other International Developments

Lebanese President Visits US: President Joseph Aoun departed Beirut for Washington on Saturday for a meeting with President Trump, focusing on the Lebanon-Israel ceasefire, Israeli withdrawal from southern Lebanon, and regional stability following the conclusion of Lebanon-Israel talks in Rome.

US Partially Restores Hong Kong Status: The US Treasury Department allowed a 2020 executive order targeting Hong Kong's national security law to expire, partially restoring Hong Kong's special legal status. China welcomed the "positive direction," though Hong Kong Chief Executive John Lee remains on sanctions lists.

India Launches First Private Orbital Rocket: Skyroot Aerospace successfully launched its Vikram-1 rocket from Sriharikota on July 18, carrying six payloads into a 450 km low Earth orbit. India becomes the third country after the US and China with private orbital launch capability. Prime Minister Modi personally called to congratulate the team.
