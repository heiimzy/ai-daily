⚡ July 16 Financial Roundup: PBoC Injects 444.6B Yuan, MiniMax Valued at $4B+ Eyes IPO, Bitcoin ETF Inflows Rebound

1. PBoC Conducts Massive 444.6B Yuan Net Injection, Shibor Rates Fall

The People's Bank of China conducted 520.1 billion yuan in 7-day reverse repo operations on July 16, maintaining the policy rate at 1.40%. With 75.5 billion yuan in reverse repos maturing, the open market operations resulted in a net injection of 444.6 billion yuan — one of the largest single-day net injections in recent months.

Following the massive liquidity injection, Shanghai Interbank Offered Rates (Shibor) turned lower across the board. Overnight Shibor fell 6.90 basis points to 1.4660%, 7-day Shibor declined 4.20 bps to 1.5040%, and 14-day Shibor dropped 2.00 bps to 1.5590%. In the pledged repo market, the weighted average DR001 rate fell 6.1 bps to 1.4689%, while DR007 declined 4.1 bps to 1.5291%. Total trading volume in the overnight repo market increased by 1,803 billion yuan.

The injection comes as China enters a peak tax period and faces accelerated government bond issuance, which had been tightening interbank liquidity. Traders reported that the money market showed balanced conditions after the PBoC's operation, with overnight repo rates stabilizing around 1.50% after the injection, though conditions tightened again in the afternoon session before easing toward the close.

"This large-scale net injection reflects the PBoC's proactive approach to managing liquidity during the tax season and government bond issuance peak, demonstrating its commitment to keeping banking system liquidity reasonably ample." — China Securities Journal

In the negotiable CD market, 127 certificates of deposit were issued on July 16 with a total volume of 301.2 billion yuan. One-year major bank CDs traded around 1.62%, with short-tenor rates declining 1-2.5 basis points from the previous session.

2. AI Unicorn MiniMax Nears $300M Funding at $4B+ Valuation, Eyes A-Share IPO

Chinese AI large-model startup MiniMax (Shanghai Intellio Technology) is nearing completion of a ~$300 million funding round that would value the company at over $4 billion, sources familiar with the matter told Financial Associated Press (财联社). The same sources revealed that MiniMax is actively seeking an A-share stock market listing.

MiniMax is among China's "Big Six" AI model startups, known for its consumer-facing Hailuo AI product and enterprise-grade API services. The funding and IPO plans signal that China's AI startups are accelerating their capital market strategies amid intensifying competition in the large language model space.

Separately, Nvidia CEO Jensen Huang stated at the China International Supply Chain Expo that China's open-source AI is "a catalyst for global progress," enabling countries and industries worldwide to participate in the AI revolution. Huang emphasized that the next wave of AI will be robotics systems with reasoning, execution, and physical world understanding capabilities.

3. Bitcoin ETFs Record $181M Inflow, Ether ETFs Add $58M in Sharp Reversal

U.S. spot Bitcoin exchange-traded funds staged a strong rebound on Tuesday, recording $181 million in net inflows after a massive $425 million outflow the previous session. Ether ETFs simultaneously added $58 million in fresh capital.

  • Bitcoin ETFs: BlackRock's IBIT dominated with ~$139 million in inflows, followed by Fidelity's FBTC at $21 million. No Bitcoin ETF reported net outflows. Total AUM climbed back to approximately $78 billion.
  • Ether ETFs: BlackRock's ETHA accounted for the entire $58 million inflow. Total ether ETF assets surpassed $10 billion for the first time.
  • Price Action: Bitcoin consolidated around $64,500, while ether advanced to ~$1,875, both posting 3-5% daily gains.

ETF flows have alternated sharply this month — July 13 saw the largest single-day Bitcoin ETF outflow of July at $425 million, followed immediately by Tuesday's $181 million recovery. This pattern reflects the market's tug-of-war between improving inflation data (June CPI and PPI both came in cooler than expected) and escalating Middle East geopolitical risks that keep energy prices elevated. Fed Chair Kevin Warsh's congressional testimony also influenced sentiment.

The Crypto Fear & Greed Index remains in "Extreme Fear" territory at 22, indicating fragile sentiment despite the rebound, though it has recovered from recent lows.

4. China A-Shares Edge Lower; Pharma and Robotics Buck the Trend

China's A-share market continued its consolidation pattern on July 16, with all three major indices closing slightly lower. Total turnover on the Shanghai and Shenzhen exchanges reached 1.44 trillion yuan, down ~170 billion yuan from the previous session.

  • Shanghai Composite: -0.03% to 3,503.78, finding support at the 10-day moving average for a second consecutive session
  • Shenzhen Component: -0.22% to 10,720.81
  • ChiNext Index: -0.22% to 2,230.19
  • Market breadth: Over 3,200 stocks advanced despite index declines

Pharma Stocks Surge on Policy Catalyst

China's 11th round of national centralized drug procurement was recently initiated, reaffirming the principle that innovative drugs will be excluded from volume-based procurement. This policy clarity sent pharma stocks sharply higher, with Lianhuan Pharmaceutical, Hsan三联, and others hitting daily limit-up. GeneScience and Woowa Biotech each surged ~16%. Guoyuan Securities noted that commercial insurance is expected to play a larger role in China's multi-tiered medical security system, providing payment support for high-priced innovative drugs.

Humanoid Robotics Theme Continues to Heat Up

Shangwei New Materials extended its winning streak to six consecutive limit-up sessions, becoming the market's strongest recent theme. Unitree Robotics' founder disclosed that shipments of the G1 humanoid robot, launched last year, have grown significantly. Nvidia CEO Jensen Huang's remarks on robotics as "AI's next wave" further fueled interest. Financial stocks broadly declined, while steel, non-ferrous metals, and coal sectors underperformed.
