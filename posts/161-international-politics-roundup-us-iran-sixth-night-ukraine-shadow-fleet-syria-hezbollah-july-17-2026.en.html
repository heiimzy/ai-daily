⚡ July 17 International Politics Roundup: US Launches Sixth Night of Strikes on Iran, Ukraine Drones Destroy 147 Russian Shadow Fleet Vessels, Syria Seizes Hezbollah Weapons

1. US Launches Sixth Consecutive Night of Strikes on Iran, Hits New Targets

The military conflict between the United States and Iran entered its sixth consecutive night of strikes on July 16-17. US Central Command (CENTCOM) initiated the latest round of attacks at 18:00 GMT Thursday (2:00 AM Beijing time Friday), with strikes covering multiple cities across southern Iran.

According to Al Jazeera reports published in the early hours of July 17, Iranian media reported that US strikes hit previously untouched targets:

  • Iranshahr Airport: Iran's Tasnim news agency reported that US fighter jets launched a missile attack on Iranshahr airport in Sistan and Baluchestan province, approximately 100 km from the Iran-Pakistan border.
  • Bandar Abbas Communications Tower: US forces struck a communications tower on a hill in the city, causing a widespread power outage in the area. Tasnim reported seven people were injured. Additionally, a railway substation in Bandar Abbas was hit, injuring two people.
  • Bandar-e Khamir Bridge: Iran's Fars news agency reported a suspected US attack on a bridge connecting Bandar Abbas and the city of Lar, a vital transportation link. The attack occurred at 11 PM local time (19:30 GMT).

In addition to these new targets, strikes also hit Qeshm Island, Chabahar, Ahvaz, and other previously struck locations. Iranian air defenses were activated over Tehran, and the Islamic Revolutionary Guard Corps (IRGC) said it shot down a US MQ-9 drone over Andimeshk.

Iran's Health Ministry said US strikes have killed at least 35 people and wounded over 300 since the renewed fighting began last week. Kuwait's military reported detecting 32 "hostile drones" in its airspace since dawn Thursday.

White House Says 'Always Open to Diplomacy'

Compared to Trump's earlier threats to hit Iranian power plants and bridges, the White House struck a more diplomatic tone on Thursday. Press Secretary Karoline Leavitt said Trump would hold Iran "accountable" but "is always open to diplomacy at the very same time."

"They [Iranian officials] have expressed they still want to make a deal to the president. We're talking to them, but again, the president is not going to allow them to fire on ships in the strait without paying a consequence for that." — White House Press Secretary Karoline Leavitt

Meanwhile, Iran's Foreign Ministry said Wednesday that Tehran has "no plans" to engage in talks with the US and is focused solely on defending the country. Iran's chief negotiator Mohammad Bagher Ghalibaf warned that "a memorandum of understanding only has meaning when its clauses are valid and being implemented."

Brent crude oil traded above $85 per barrel on Thursday, up more than 15% since the escalation. Maritime tracking firm Kpler reported just 11 fuel tankers and cargo vessels crossed the Strait of Hormuz on Wednesday, far below the pre-conflict average of 138 per day.

2. Ukraine Launches Mass Drone Campaign Against Russian Shadow Fleet

Ukraine's Security Service (SBU) announced on July 16 that it had struck two Russian shadow fleet tankers — the Louise 1 and Banda — in the Black Sea using Mamai naval drones, in coordination with the Ukrainian Navy.

According to the SBU, Louise 1 was used to transport Russian crude oil in circumvention of G7 and EU embargoes, carrying nearly 3 million metric tons of Russian Urals crude in 2026 alone. The vessel sailed from Baltic and Black Sea ports with its automatic identification system switched off. Banda transported Russian crude from the ports of Ust-Luga, Kerch, Novorossiysk, and Nakhodka.

Video released by the SBU shows multiple Mamai drones ramming the vessels' hulls, with Russian aircraft attempting to intercept using machine-gun fire and aerial bombs, without success.

Ukraine's Unmanned Systems Forces Commander Robert "Madyar" Brovdi announced that under Operation "MoLoCHKa" between July 6-16, Ukrainian forces hit a total of 147 vessels linked to Russia's shadow fleet — 117 in the Sea of Azov and 30 in the Black Sea. On July 16 alone, forces struck 11 commercial vessels, including five oil tankers, one gas carrier, three dry bulk carriers, and two tugboats.

"Destroying shadow fleet ships is a systematic way of depriving the Kremlin of money for war. These tankers, despite international sanctions, transport Russian oil and fill the aggressor state's budget with billions of dollars. Every strike on the shadow fleet is a direct blow to Russia's ability to continue its aggression." — SBU Statement

Ukraine's General Staff confirmed that overnight July 15-16, strikes also hit the Shakhtarsk oil depot and bridges in Prymorsk and Chongar.

3. Ukrainian Drone Strikes Engels Air Base, 600 km from Front Line

Ukraine reportedly struck Russia's Engels-2 air base in Saratov Oblast with drones overnight on July 16, according to Ukrainian media. Photos and videos posted to social media by local residents show a fire burning in the area of the base.

The Engels air base, approximately 600 km from the Ukraine front line, hosts the 184th Heavy Bomber Aviation Regiment and 121st Guards Heavy Bomber Aviation Regiment, operating Tu-95, Tu-22, and Tu-160 strategic bombers regularly used to launch missiles at Ukrainian cities. The base has been targeted multiple times since December 2022.

ASTRA investigation group confirmed that a drone struck a multi-story apartment building in the Ladya residential complex, approximately 2 km from the base runway. The Saratov governor said civilian infrastructure was damaged with no casualties.

4. Zelenskyy Appoints Acting Defense Minister Amid Fallout from Fedorov's Dismissal

Ukrainian President Volodymyr Zelenskyy appointed Yevhenii Khmara, acting head of the Security Service of Ukraine (SBU), as acting defense minister on July 16, replacing the dismissed Mykhailo Fedorov. Zelenskyy said he would ask parliament to approve Khmara's appointment, citing the security official's experience coordinating long-range operations against Russia.

The political fallout from Fedorov's dismissal continues. Fedorov launched a scathing public attack on Armed Forces Chief Oleksandr Syrskyi, accusing the general of blocking ministry initiatives. Deputy Air Force Commander Pavlo Yelizarov, a key drone warfare leader, resigned, calling Fedorov's dismissal "a great evil" for Ukraine's defense.

Over 1,000 protesters gathered outside Zelenskyy's office in Kyiv chanting "Shame!" and carrying placards reading "For what?" and "The Russians are celebrating." The new government under Prime Minister Sergii Koretskyi was approved by parliament Thursday, but formal appointments for defense and foreign ministers were postponed until August 18.

5. Syria Intercepts Large Weapons Shipment Bound for Hezbollah

Syrian authorities foiled an attempt to smuggle a large shipment of advanced weapons across the Syrian-Iraqi border, destined for Lebanon's Hezbollah militia, the state news agency SANA reported on July 16. This is the largest weapons seizure since the fall of the Assad regime in December 2024.

The Interior Ministry said specialized security units found a suspicious oil tanker truck near the border. A search uncovered a cache including long-range missiles, anti-tank guided missiles, and drones. Preliminary investigations indicated the shipment was intended to transit Syrian territory before being delivered to Hezbollah.

Israel's Alma Research and Education Center analyzed that the seized weapons included at least 100 fiber-optic guided FPV drones, Iranian-made Almas anti-tank missiles, and components for Paveh family cruise missiles. Iraqi Prime Minister Ali al-Zaidi ordered the formation of a high-level investigative committee to coordinate with Syrian authorities.

The Interior Ministry said protecting Syria's borders and national sovereignty remained a top priority, and it would not allow Syrian territory to be used as a transit route for arms smuggling.
