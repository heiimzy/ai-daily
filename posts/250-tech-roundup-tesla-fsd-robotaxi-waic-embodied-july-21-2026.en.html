⚡ July 21 Tech Roundup: Musk Announces Tesla FSD to Integrate Robotaxi Tech, WAIC 2026 Closes With Embodied AI Moving From Exhibits to Employment, FSD Enters Personalized Learning Era

1. Elon Musk Officially Announces Tesla FSD Will Fully Integrate Robotaxi Technology — Three Core Modules to Migrate

On the morning of July 21, Tesla CEO Elon Musk announced on X (formerly Twitter) that technology upgrades from the Austin Robotaxi project will be fully integrated into the general FSD (Full Self-Driving) version. Musk stated that with the public release of the Austin Robotaxi upgrade, full self-driving technology will experience a "major leap in improvement."

Three core technology modules to migrate:

  • Complex intersection navigation logic: Intelligent traffic logic designed for complex urban intersections, proven through extensive real-world testing in Austin's robotaxi fleet, enabling FSD to handle multi-directional traffic flow scenarios more effectively.
  • Extreme weather response strategies: Algorithm modules ensuring driving safety under adverse conditions such as heavy rain and fog, validated through Austin's variable weather patterns.
  • High-precision map real-time verification technology: Enhanced positioning accuracy and route planning reliability through real-time verification of map data against sensor inputs.

Musk emphasized that the engineering team has implemented validation mechanisms to ensure the Austin improvements do not negatively impact FSD performance in other regions. "We need to verify that Austin-specific improvements don't cause regressions elsewhere," he clarified in his X post.

Notably, the upgraded FSD technology still requires regulatory approval in major markets including Europe and China before rollout. According to IT之家, Tesla HW4 users can fully benefit from the upgrade with 4K visual processing and multimodal fusion inference, while HW3 users may face up to 40% feature reduction due to computational limitations.

"Tesla is running the Robotaxi fleet with a completely different FSD branch than what's released to the public. The Robotaxi FSD has approximately 4x the parameter count of the public customer version, and after rigorous geofenced validation, it will be merged into the general release." — Industry Analysis

Market sources also indicate that privately owned vehicles will soon join the Robotaxi network — Tesla has established a dedicated team, and related software updates are already in preparation. This means future Tesla owners could add their vehicles to the Robotaxi sharing fleet, earning approximately $0.65 per mile. Tesla is accelerating the expansion of its driverless ride-hailing service, bringing L4 capabilities to mass-produced FSD systems.

According to QQ News, this announcement comes just ahead of Tesla's Q2 earnings report on July 23, where the Robotaxi platform expansion plan will face close scrutiny from Wall Street.

2. Tesla FSD's Major Strategic Shift: Saying Goodbye to One-Size-Fits-All, Entering the Era of Personalized Learning

Just one day before the Robotaxi integration announcement (July 20), Musk confirmed another major FSD evolution — the system will learn and remember each owner's individual driving preferences.

Responding to a user on X, Musk explicitly stated: "The vehicle will gradually remember your operations and adjust according to each owner's personal preferences." This marks a fundamental shift in Tesla's FSD philosophy — from a fleet-wide "one-size-fits-all" driving system to genuine personalization.

Key scenarios for personalization:

  • Parking preferences: Different people have completely different parking habits — some prefer reversing into spots, others pull in forward. FSD will learn the owner's parking preferences, ensuring the car always parks where the user wants at home, office, or school.
  • Lane change strategy: A long-standing pain point — FSD users have complained about the system unnecessarily leaving fast lanes. FSD will now learn from user intervention patterns to adopt strategies more aligned with individual driving intuition.
  • Intervention feedback loop: Tesla's recently introduced takeover feedback menu appears whenever a driver takes control from FSD, asking for the reason. This data is helping Tesla prioritize which issues to fix first.

Musk previously revealed that the next major consumer FSD version — FSD v15 — is expected to launch in late 2026 or early 2027 and will feature approximately 10x the model parameter count. This dual iteration of parameter scale and learning logic means the system will truly understand "who the driver is," not just "where the car is."

As of July 17, Tesla's supervised FSD has surpassed 12 billion miles of cumulative driving globally, providing the massive data foundation needed for personalized learning. Industry observers note that personalization will not only improve the L2 assisted driving experience but also pave the way for customized Robotaxi passenger experiences in the future.

3. WAIC 2026 Concludes: Embodied AI Moves From Exhibits to Employment, "Making AI Actually Work" Becomes Urgent Industry Priority

The four-day 2026 World Artificial Intelligence Conference (WAIC 2026) officially concluded in Shanghai on July 20. With the theme "Intelligent Partners, Creating the Future Together," the conference featured over 1,100 exhibitors across a record-breaking 100,000 square meters of exhibition space. More than 300 products made their global debut, 57 core application scenarios were demonstrated, and 16.2 billion yuan in意向 cooperation was achieved.

Key industry shifts:

  • Embodied AI takes center stage: The embodied AI track gathered over 200 companies, up from just 80 last year. Exhibition robots shifted from dance performances to real work — material handling, assembly, inspection, and other actual operational tasks replaced choreographed shows.
  • AI evolves from "can talk" to "can work": Among 130 AI model and generative AI exhibitors, 83 (63.8%) chose AI Agent/smart applications as their primary label, while only 18 remained focused on foundational AI models. The spotlight has completely shifted from parameter-count competition to scenario-ready agents.
  • Domestic AI compute enters "ecosystem-first" era: Huawei's Atlas 950 supernode made its true-machine debut alongside over a dozen domestic GPU manufacturers. The market has moved beyond single-chip performance battles — mature developer ecosystems supporting upstream hardware and downstream applications are now the decisive factor.
  • President Xi Jinping attended the opening ceremony: Chinese President Xi Jinping delivered a keynote speech emphasizing the need to jointly build a fair and reasonable global AI governance system.

Major conference announcements:

  • World AI Cooperation Organization founded: An agreement to establish a World Artificial Intelligence Cooperation Organization was signed on July 16, with headquarters in Shanghai, as an independent intergovernmental organization.
  • China's AI industry surpasses trillion yuan: The National Development and Reform Commission revealed that China's AI-related industry scale exceeded 1 trillion yuan in 2025, with over 30% growth expected in 2026. AI penetration in key industries has surpassed 80%.
  • Kimi K3 launched: Moonshot AI released the 2.8 trillion-parameter Kimi K3, the world's first open-source model at the 3 trillion scale, described by CITIC Securities as "another DeepSeek moment."
  • 60 robots serving the conference: Zhiyuan Robotics provided 60 embodied intelligent robots for the conference, handling wayfinding and information services — the first-ever "deployed state" public service by embodied AI at an industry scale.

Ministry of Industry and Information Technology data shows that China produced approximately 20,000 humanoid robots in 2025, with first-half 2026 production exceeding 40,000 units. Full-year 2026 production is projected to surpass 100,000 units. Chinese-made quadruped robots account for nearly 70% of global sales, and China boasts over 400 humanoid robot products — more than half the global total.

4. WAIC 2026 Quick Takes: Domestic AI Compute Ecosystem, AI Earbuds, Luckin Coffee AI Blueprint, and the "Meta Hospital"

Several other notable industry developments emerged from this year's WAIC:

Domestic compute full-stack ecosystem debuts: The Chip+Compute theme hall covered 10,000 square meters, gathering over 100 companies including Huawei, Kunlun Core, Moore Threads, and TsingMicro. For the first time, a complete closed loop from chips to cluster systems to software stacks to industry applications was systematically presented.

United Imaging launches "Meta Hospital" strategy: United Imaging Intelligence unveiled its "Meta Hospital" strategy at WAIC, featuring "Meta Doctor" as a central agent that understands clinical intent and dispatches AI agents to work collaboratively. The company also globally debuted a multimodal AI application for early Alzheimer's screening via smartphone.

Luckin Coffee publishes AI blueprint: Luckin Coffee systematically unveiled its AI-driven industrial blueprint for the first time, covering the full "people, products, places" chain. The company announced a "Smart Brew Ecosystem" in partnership with Alibaba Cloud, Baidu AI Cloud, Huawei Cloud, and Tencent Cloud.

NetEase Youdao AI earbuds debut: NetEase Youdao publicly showed the Apple MFi-certified AI earbuds OpenPods, whose charging case doubles as a standalone translator — no phone or internet required, covering the full "record-transcribe-summarize-translate" pipeline.

Smart Future releases vivago R1: Smart Future (Zhixiang Weilai) released vivago R1, the world's first unlimited-duration content creation agent, capable of generating and editing unlimited-length videos, while forming a "Belt and Road Token Export Alliance" with partners.

As the conference theme suggests, AI is moving decisively from "technology exhibition" to "service delivery" and "productivity realization." When a cup of coffee's entire journey from bean to cup is redefined by AI, and when robots move from exhibition booths into factories and hospitals, "Intelligent Partners" is no longer a concept — it's a tangible productivity force reshaping industries across the board.
