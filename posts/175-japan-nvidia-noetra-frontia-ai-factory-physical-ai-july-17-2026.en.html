⚡ Japan & NVIDIA Launch World's First National Physical AI Infrastructure: 27,500 Rubin GPUs, Trillion-Parameter Models, $6.2B Sovereign Bet

TOKYO — Japan's Ministry of Economy, Trade and Industry (METI) and NVIDIA jointly announced on July 16 the construction of the world's first national AI infrastructure dedicated to physical AI. The Noetra AI factory — operated by a consortium of 44 Japanese companies including Sony Group, SoftBank, NEC, and Honda Motor — will deploy 27,500 NVIDIA Rubin GPUs and 13,750 Vera CPUs across 140 megawatts of data center capacity, backed by up to ¥1 trillion ($6.2 billion) in government funding over five years.

The Largest National Physical AI Compute Cluster

The AI factory will be built on the NVIDIA Vera Rubin NVL72 platform, comprising approximately 382 NVL72 racks. Each rack houses 72 Rubin GPUs and 36 Vera CPUs connected by sixth-generation NVLink 6, delivering 3.6 TB/s bidirectional bandwidth per GPU and 260 TB/s of all-to-all fabric bandwidth across the full rack — exactly double the per-GPU bandwidth of the Blackwell-generation NVLink 5.

"Japan invented modern manufacturing," said Jensen Huang, founder and CEO of NVIDIA, at the Tokyo announcement. "Now, it is building the AI factories that will power the next industrial revolution."

Noetra Corp. was established in January 2026 by SoftBank, Sony, NEC, and Honda as core shareholders, with CEO Hironobu Tamba — previously lead developer of SoftBank's large language model — at the helm. The company began full-scale operations on July 16.

FRONTia Project: Japan's 'Third Path' to Sovereign AI

The infrastructure serves as the computing foundation for METI's FRONTia Project — formally titled "Development of Multimodal Foundation Models with a View to AI Robotics and Physical AI." Noetra and the National Institute of Advanced Industrial Science and Technology (AIST) jointly won the NEDO public tender to run the project.

"Japan has launched the FRONTia Project, which will serve as the core of the country's physical AI ecosystem," said Ryosei Akazawa, Japan's Minister of Economy, Trade and Industry. "By leveraging Japan's strengths — its onsite expertise and manufacturing technology infrastructure — we will build highly reliable multimodal foundation models."

Noetra's roadmap targets a reasoning foundation model by fiscal 2026 (ending March 2027), an omni-modal model handling text, images, video, and audio by fiscal 2028, and "real-world native AI" with spatial awareness by fiscal 2030. All pretrained model weights will be broadly shared with domestic developers and enterprises — a deliberate "third path" distinct from both US closed-source models and fully open-source ecosystems.

"Bringing physical AI into the real world requires enormous computing, data and foundational technologies — challenges no single company can solve alone." — Hironobu Tamba, CEO of Noetra

Physical AI: The Next Frontier Beyond LLMs

Physical AI refers to systems that perceive their environment, make autonomous decisions, and execute physical actions through robots and other machinery — distinct from generative AI that processes text or images on servers. Training these systems requires enormously more compute than language models, as the AI must learn from millions of simulated and real-world physical scenarios before safely operating in unpredictable factory environments.

"Together we're bringing physical AI to mechatronics, and the next industrial revolution will be made also in Japan," Huang said at a media briefing in Tokyo.

NVIDIA separately announced expanded partnerships with Toyota Motor Corp. for AI-powered traffic control systems at Woven City, and is helping Fujitsu forge partnerships with Fanuc, Yaskawa Electric, and Kawasaki Heavy Industries to develop AI-powered production robots for manufacturing, logistics, and healthcare.

Japan's AI Robotics Strategy: 30% of Global Market by 2040

Japan's AI Robotics Strategy, released in March 2026, targets capturing more than 30% of the global AI robotics market by 2040 — an estimated $133 billion opportunity. The plan calls for deploying approximately 10 million AI-equipped robots across 18 industry sectors, including food service, manufacturing, healthcare, and logistics.

Prime Minister Sanae Takaichi's government has designated AI as one of 17 sectors critical to economic growth and national security. The industry ministry aims to introduce 10 million AI-powered robots nationwide by 2040, addressing severe labor shortages and a declining skilled workforce.

The Noetra AI factory joins a broader Japanese AI infrastructure push that includes SoftBank's Blackwell-based DGX supercomputer (announced 2024) and the $740 million FugakuNEXT zetta-scale supercomputer project with RIKEN, Fujitsu, and NVIDIA targeted for around 2030.

Industry Impact: The Sovereign AI Race Intensifies

Japan's move marks a new phase in the global sovereign AI race. Unlike China's DeepSeek open-source breakthrough or Moonshot's Kimi K3 frontier-performance approach, Japan has chosen a national infrastructure + industrial consortium model: government-funded compute platforms openly accessible to domestic enterprises, rather than relying on single-company closed-source products or fully open-source communities.

CEO Tamba explicitly stated that Noetra aims to create a "third option" — distinct from both US closed-source AI dominance and reliance on foreign open-source models. This model could serve as a blueprint for Europe, Southeast Asia, and other regions seeking sovereign AI capabilities without homegrown tech giants.

Industry analysts note the deal is a commercial and geopolitical win for NVIDIA: the Vera Rubin NVL72 is deployed at scale for the first time as a G7 nation's sovereign AI infrastructure reference architecture, setting a benchmark for the global sovereign AI market.
