⚡ July 12 11:00 Tech Giants Roundup: Microsoft Drops 7 MAI Models & 'Dream Machine', Tencent Cloud Slashes DeepSeek-V4 by 97.5%, Alphabet Plans $80B AI Raise

Microsoft Build 2026: 7 MAI Models + Surface RTX Spark 'Dream Machine' Dev Box

At today's Build 2026 developer conference, Microsoft unveiled its most ambitious AI hardware and software lineup to date, signaling a new phase in the company's AI infrastructure strategy.

The centerpiece was the release of seven MAI (Microsoft AI) models spanning reasoning, code, image, speech, and transcription domains. This is Microsoft's first complete public portfolio of self-developed models, all deeply optimized for Azure cloud and the Copilot ecosystem. Benchmarks suggest the MAI family performs competitively with GPT-5.5 and Claude-class models across multiple tasks.

On the hardware front, Microsoft introduced the Surface RTX Spark Dev Box — a developer workstation powered by NVIDIA's RTX Spark superchip. Satya Nadella called it a "dream machine" during the keynote, designed for local AI development, inference, and fine-tuning. NVIDIA CEO Jensen Huang joined via video, declaring that "the PC is evolving from a personal computer into a personal AI."

Microsoft also demonstrated upgraded Copilot capabilities across Windows, Office 365, and Azure, including persistent Agent workspaces, advanced multimodal interactions, and enterprise-grade AI governance tools.

Tencent Cloud Cuts DeepSeek-V4 Prices by Up to 97.5%

Tencent Cloud announced dramatic price cuts across its DeepSeek-V4 series today, with the highest reduction reaching 97.5%.

DeepSeek-V4-Pro sees both input and output inference prices slashed by 75%, from ¥0.012 to ¥0.003 per thousand tokens and ¥0.024 to ¥0.006 respectively. The cache hit price saw the steepest cut — from ¥0.001 to just ¥0.000025 per thousand tokens, a 97.5% reduction. DeepSeek-V4-Flash's cache hit price also dropped 90%, from ¥0.0002 to ¥0.00002.

At these prices, AI inference in cached scenarios is approaching zero marginal cost. Analysts expect this move to trigger a new price war in China's AI cloud services market, accelerating enterprise adoption.

For an enterprise system processing 100 million tokens daily, inference costs would drop from ¥120,000 to roughly ¥3,000 per day — a 97%+ reduction.

Alphabet Plans $80B Equity Raise for AI Infrastructure

Bloomberg reports that Google-parent Alphabet plans an $80 billion equity raise — the largest single fundraising effort in the company's history — entirely earmarked for AI infrastructure, compute buildout, and capital expenditures.

Meanwhile, DeepMind unveiled Gemini Omni, its new multimodal flagship model. Omni demonstrates unprecedented fluency across text, image, video, and audio modalities — capable of real-time animation generation, astrophysical concept recognition, and protein folding visualization. It is now deeply integrated into the Gemini App as the default AI engine.

Separately, Arm CEO Rene Haas expressed confidence that the company's $15 billion self-designed chip revenue target may be achieved ahead of schedule, driven by AI-fueled demand across PC and server markets.

OpenAI Expands Codex to 6 Plugin Verticals, GPT-5.5 Lands on AWS Bedrock

At OpenAI's "Intelligence at Work" livestream, the company announced that Codex's core capabilities will be embedded directly into ChatGPT, eliminating the need to switch interfaces. OpenAI also launched role-specific plugins extending Codex's use from software development to investment banking, data analytics, and marketing.

On the downside, OpenAI reset Codex's free and Go-tier account quota refresh cycles from 7 days to 30 days — a significant reduction that has sparked community discussion.

In a major partnership milestone, AWS announced that GPT-5.5, GPT-5.4, and Codex are now generally available on Amazon Bedrock. Enterprise customers can invoke OpenAI frontier models within their existing AWS security, compliance, and governance workflows.

Tesla Cybercab Begins Steering-Wheel-Free Road Testing, SpaceX Starship IFT-13 Set for July 16

In cutting-edge transportation, Tesla has started testing steering-wheel and pedal-free Cybercab robotaxis on public roads in Austin, Texas. Tesla AI head Ashok Elluswamy confirmed the milestone on X. The fully autonomous two-seater is expected to reach the market under $30,000 before 2027.

In aerospace, SpaceX's Starship IFT-13 is scheduled for no earlier than July 16 from Starbase, Texas. The mission's highlight: Starship will deploy 20 next-generation Starlink V3 satellites — the first commercial payload deployment ever for the Starship program. V3 satellites deliver 1 Tbps per satellite (10× V2 Mini capacity), with solar arrays spanning ~60 meters. At full capacity, Starship can carry up to 60 V3 satellites.

SpaceX says IFT-13 will also gather critical data for the IFT-14 goal of "chopstick arm booster recovery at sea," pushing reusable rocket technology to new frontiers.

ByteDance, Leica, Alibaba: Organizational Moves

Other notable news: ByteDance's Seed Robotics team now reports to multimodal head Zhou Chang rather than Li Hang, who transitions to an academic advisory role; HSG (formerly Sequoia Capital China) is leading the bid to acquire Blackstone's 45% stake in Leica Camera AG, valued at approximately €1 billion; and Alibaba CTO Wu Zeming has been elevated to the Alibaba Partnership Committee, joining the company's highest decision-making body.
