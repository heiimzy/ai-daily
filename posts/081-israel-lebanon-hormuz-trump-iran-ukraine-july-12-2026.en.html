⚡ July 12 World Politics Roundup: Israel Shells South Lebanon Breaking Ceasefire, Trump Says Hormuz Open, Iran Calls Strait 'More Important Than Nukes', Zelenskyy Replaces PM

1. Israel Shells Southern Lebanese Village, Straining Ceasefire Framework

Israeli artillery struck the southern Lebanese village of Zawtar al-Sharqiyah near Mayfadoun in the Nabatiyeh area on Sunday (13:50 GMT), marking one of the most significant military actions since the US-brokered ceasefire framework was signed last month.

Lebanon's National News Agency (NNA) reported that Israeli artillery targeted the key hilltop village near the "yellow line." Last month, Lebanon and Israel signed a US-facilitated framework agreement aimed at ending hostilities and ending Israel's occupation of southern Lebanon.

More than 4,200 people have been killed in Israeli attacks since early March, with hundreds of thousands displaced from their homes.

According to security sources cited by Asharq Al-Awsat, Israeli forces have made two separate attempts since Sunday to advance toward Zawtar al-Sharqiyeh by crossing the Litani River to its northern bank. This marks the first offensive operation by Israel in the area since the ceasefire took effect three weeks ago. Israeli warplanes simultaneously carried out heavy airstrikes and sustained artillery bombardment across the Nabatiyeh district, targeting hilltop communities including Zawtar al-Gharbiyeh, Arnoun, Yohmor, Mayfadoun, and Choukine.

Hezbollah claimed it fired rocket barrages at Israeli troop concentrations and vehicles near the Deir Siryan area and set up an ambush for advancing Israeli forces on the outskirts of Zawtar. The Israeli military, in turn, reported that Hezbollah has launched 70 explosive drones at its forces since the ceasefire, 11 of which caused casualties.

2. Israeli Drone Strike Kills 4 in Gaza City

An Israeli drone strike targeted Gaza City on Sunday (13:40 GMT), hitting a metal workshop on al-Sinaa Street in southwestern Gaza City, killing at least four Palestinians and wounding several others. Since October 7, 2023, Israeli attacks on Gaza have killed at least 73,221 people and wounded 173,643. Despite a ceasefire agreement between Israel and Hamas on October 10, 2025, at least 1,098 people have been killed since the "ceasefire" took effect.

3. Trump: Strait of Hormuz Open to Commercial Traffic

President Donald Trump told NBC's Meet the Press on Sunday that the Strait of Hormuz remains open to commercial shipping despite Iran's declaration of a "closure." US Central Command (CENTCOM) reinforced this message on X, stating: "Iran does not control the strait. Traffic is flowing."

US Ambassador to NATO Matthew Whitaker said the US-Iran interim deal was "always performance-based," with one condition being that Tehran "cannot terrorise vessels" in the Strait of Hormuz. "President Trump wants a deal with Iran," Whitaker said, but defended recent US strikes: "Right now, as President Trump has said… this country is controlled by a bunch of crazy people, and ultimately they're going to have to understand the United States is very serious about this."

Earlier Sunday, Iran's Islamic Revolutionary Guard Corps (IRGC) Navy announced the "indefinite closure" of the Strait of Hormuz, claiming multiple vessels ignored warnings and used unauthorized routes — one was struck by a warning shot and brought to a stop. CENTCOM responded by launching a third round of strikes this week against Iran, hitting approximately 140 military targets.

Iran: Hormuz 'More Important Than Dozens of Nuclear Bombs'

Mohsen Rezaee, an advisor to Iran's Supreme Leader Mojtaba Khamenei, declared that the Strait of Hormuz is "more important than dozens of atomic bombs," vowing Iran will protect this strategic waterway. Iran's Persian Gulf Strait Authority stated that transit through the strait is currently not possible due to "illegal movements of US military forces," adding that permits will be reviewed once "stability and calm are restored."

In a notable diplomatic development, Oman summoned Iran's ambassador on Sunday — a rare public protest — handing a formal complaint over Iranian drone strikes on Omani territory in the Musandam and al-Batinah governorates. This comes just hours after Oman hosted Iran's Foreign Minister for talks on the Strait of Hormuz, signaling a significant rift between the mediator and Tehran.

4. Zelenskyy Announces Cabinet Reshuffle, Replaces PM Svyrydenko

Ukrainian President Volodymyr Zelenskyy announced a government reshuffle on Sunday (July 12), proposing the replacement of Prime Minister Yulia Svyrydenko and the heads of several law enforcement agencies. In a lengthy social media post, Zelenskyy said: "Ukraine is changing its political strategy. Each priority foreign policy direction will be overseen by a specific individual with substantial experience who is capable of delivering on the agreements reached at the leaders' level."

Svyrydenko, 39, became prime minister a year ago, succeeding Denys Shmyhal. She previously served as first deputy prime minister and economy minister, and was widely credited with negotiating a critical minerals agreement between Washington and Kyiv that helped thaw relations between Trump and Zelenskyy. "I am proud to have had the honour of leading the Government during one of the most difficult periods in Ukraine's modern history," she wrote on X.

Zelenskyy said the new strategy would focus on licensing Patriot air defense system production, advancing Ukraine's EU membership bid, and deepening ties with the Gulf region. He thanked Svyrydenko for offering to lead a "new significant direction in relations with a key partner," indicating she will continue serving Ukraine in a new capacity.
