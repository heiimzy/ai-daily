1. Thinking Machines Lab Launches Inkling: A 975-Billion Parameter Open Weights Model

On Wednesday, July 15, Thinking Machines Lab — the AI startup founded in early 2025 by former OpenAI CTO Mira Murati — released its first independently developed open-source AI model, code-named Inkling. The launch marks the first time an American company has produced an open weights model capable of going head-to-head with leading Chinese open-source models such as DeepSeek V4, GLM 5.2, and Kimi K2.6.

975B Parameters: America's Largest Open Weights Model

Inkling uses a Mixture of Experts (MoE) architecture with 975 billion total parameters, making it the largest American open weights model to date. During inference, it activates only approximately 41 billion parameters (6 out of 256 routed experts), striking a careful balance between massive scale and operational efficiency. Running the model at native 16-bit precision requires more than 2 terabytes of GPU memory — roughly eight Nvidia B300 accelerators or sixteen H200s. Thinking Machines also released an NVFP4 quantized version that halves the hardware requirements.

The model was pre-trained on 45 trillion tokens of text, images, audio, and video using Nvidia GB300 NVL72 systems. While Thinking Machines acknowledges the architectural inspiration from DeepSeek-V3, the company emphasizes that Inkling was trained from scratch. The model supports a 1-million-token context window, enabling it to handle large codebases and complex retrieval tasks.

Performance and Openness

Inkling is released under the permissive Apache 2.0 license, giving developers full freedom to fine-tune it for their specific use cases. On coding benchmarks, Inkling matches Nvidia's Nemotron 3 Ultra (previously America's largest open model at 550B parameters) using roughly one-third the tokens. A smaller variant, Inkling-Small (276B total parameters, 12B active), is also in preview for latency-sensitive workloads.

"Our goal is not to build a general-purpose chatbot," said Thinking Machines CEO Mira Murati. "We want to provide enterprises with a highly customizable AI foundation — so every organization can have its own frontier model."

The model is available immediately via Thinking Machines' Tinker platform, with third-party API support coming to TogetherAI, Fireworks, Modal, Databricks, and Baseten. Weights are available on Hugging Face and other major model repositories. The company is also previewing Inkling-Small, a 276-billion-parameter MoE model with 12 billion active parameters optimized for lower latency.

2. Hyundai Motor Group Takes Full Control of Boston Dynamics, Accelerating Physical AI

Boston Dynamics will become a 100% subsidiary of Hyundai Motor Group. According to a July 16 announcement, SoftBank exercised its put option on the Boston Dynamics equity it held under a 2020 contract, and Hyundai has begun the process of acquiring the remaining shares. Once completed, Hyundai will hold full ownership of the legendary robotics company.

SoftBank's Put Option Triggers Full Buyout

SoftBank's stake in Boston Dynamics is known to be less than 10%. The remaining equity is held by Hyundai Motor Group entities and Chairman Chung Eui-sun — HMG Global (a Hyundai Motor, Kia, and Hyundai Mobis joint venture) holds approximately 56%, Chairman Chung holds 23%, and Hyundai Glovis holds 11%.

A Hyundai Motor Group official stated: "As part of a long-term Robotics strategy, we have been reviewing ways to expand investment cooperation in Boston Dynamics. Going forward, Hyundai Motor Group and Boston Dynamics will continue to strengthen competitiveness in the Robotics business while pursuing synergy creation."

Atlas Humanoid Robot Heads to Factory Floors

Full ownership is expected to significantly accelerate Hyundai's Physical AI strategy and the commercialization of the Atlas humanoid robot:

  • Starting 2028 — Atlas will be deployed at Hyundai Motor Group Metaplant America (HMGMA) for validation by process unit, beginning with sequencing work for parts classification
  • From 2030 — The scope will expand to parts assembly

Atlas recently demonstrated its advanced mobility at public events, including a halftime appearance during a World Cup round-of-16 match at MetLife Stadium in New Jersey. Hyundai's robotics vision, "AI Robotics, from the lab to life," aims to develop robots into partners that advance humanity through manufacturing innovation and a global robotics ecosystem.

3. OpenAI's First Branded Hardware: The $230 Codex Micro Keypad

OpenAI officially released its first branded hardware product on July 15-16 — the Codex Micro, a compact mechanical macropad co-designed with boutique keyboard manufacturer Work Louder. Priced at $230, it is purpose-built for developers managing multiple Codex AI coding agents.

Agent Keys with Live RGB Status

The Codex Micro's signature feature is six frosted "Agent Keys" with embedded RGB LEDs that display real-time status of Codex agents through color-coded lighting:

  • White — Idle
  • Blue — Thinking
  • Green — Task Complete
  • Amber flashing — Needs human feedback
  • Red — Error

Users can monitor up to six parallel agents without switching windows. A single tap on a lit key brings the corresponding thread to the foreground.

Additional Controls

The keypad features an 8-way mini-joystick for launching common Codex workflows — reviewing PRs, debugging errors, refactoring code — and a dial for adjusting reasoning level on the fly. Six customizable Command Keys can be mapped to accept/reject code suggestions, start new chats, push-to-talk, and more. The device supports both Bluetooth and USB-C connectivity and comes in clicky and silent switch variants.

Codex Micro is described as a limited-run collaboration sold through OpenAI Supply Co. and Work Louder "while supplies last." OpenAI is separately developing a Jony Ive-collaborated AI smart speaker expected in 2027.

4. GPT-Red: OpenAI Builds an AI Super-Hacker That Beats Human Red Teamers

OpenAI this week detailed GPT-Red, an internal automated red-teaming model trained through self-play reinforcement learning to find prompt injection vulnerabilities in AI systems. Its attack success rate already far exceeds human red team performance.

How It Works: AI vs. AI

GPT-Red operates like a human red teamer: it sends prompts, watches how a GPT model responds, and iterates toward a goal such as successful data exfiltration. Training uses self-play reinforcement learning across many scenarios — the attacker earns rewards for eliciting failures while defender models earn rewards for resisting attacks. As defenders harden, GPT-Red discovers stronger and more varied attack techniques. OpenAI runs this training at the compute scale of its largest post-training jobs and keeps GPT-Red isolated from any shipped product.

Results: 84% vs. Single-Digit Human Success

GPT-Red demonstrated extraordinary performance across multiple tests:

  • Indirect prompt injection benchmark — Against GPT-5.1, GPT-Red achieved an 84% success rate compared to single-digit percentages for human red teamers
  • Real-world vending machine attack — GPT-Red hacked an AI-run vending machine in OpenAI's office, dropping prices to $0.50 and canceling another customer's order
  • Codex CLI data exfiltration — Across 10 held-out tasks, GPT-Red extracted sensitive data more successfully than a prompted GPT-5.5 baseline

Impact on GPT-5.6 Sol

OpenAI feeds GPT-Red's discovered attacks directly back into production model training — a process used since GPT-5.3. GPT-5.6 Sol is the culmination so far, with 6× fewer failures on OpenAI's hardest direct prompt injection benchmark compared to the best model from four months ago. An early GPT-Red version discovered "Fake Chain-of-Thought," which fooled GPT-5.1 over 95% of the time. On GPT-5.6 Sol, that rate is now below 10%. Against GPT-Red's own direct prompt injections, GPT-5.6 Sol fails on only 0.05% of attempts.

"We will continue to scale compute and data while making algorithmic improvements to train future versions of GPT-Red that are stronger than today's model," OpenAI said. "And in turn, these models will help make future GPT releases safer. We will be releasing a pre-print with more details later this week."
