⚡ July 17 International Politics: Burnham Becomes UK Labour Leader, Ukraine Crisis Deepens with New PM as Protests Rage, Qatar Hit by Iranian Missiles for First Time, Vance Calls Out Israeli Interference

🇬🇧 UK: Andy Burnham Elected Labour Leader, Set to Become PM on July 20

British politics saw a major transition on Friday as former Greater Manchester Mayor Andy Burnham was formally elected Labour Party leader at a special conference in London. With Labour commanding a comfortable majority in the 650-seat House of Commons, Burnham will become the UK's seventh prime minister in a decade on July 20, succeeding Sir Keir Starmer.

Burnham, 56, secured the backing of 349 Labour MPs and major trade unions, running unopposed for the leadership. He returned to Parliament just weeks ago after winning the Makerfield by-election on June 18 by a landslide, making him the first prime minister in 50 years to represent a constituency in northwest England.

In his first speech as Labour leader, Burnham vowed to be "unashamedly Labour" and pledged to undo much of Margaret Thatcher's economic legacy, arguing that "Britain took a series of wrong turns in the 1980s" when "political power was centralized and economic power privatized." He plans a nationwide "listening tour" during the summer parliamentary recess in August.

Outgoing Prime Minister Starmer chose Kyiv for his final foreign visit, personally assuring President Zelenskyy that UK support for Ukraine would remain unchanged under Burnham's leadership. "I have received assurances from Andy Burnham. Ukraine will win this war," Starmer said.

🇺🇦 Ukraine Political Crisis Deepens: New PM Approved, Nationwide Protests, Russian Missile Strikes

President Volodymyr Zelenskyy's government reshuffle has plunged Ukraine into a rare political crisis. On Thursday, parliament approved Serhii Koretskyi, the former CEO of state energy company Naftogaz, as the new prime minister with 289 votes. However, the appointment of a new defense and foreign minister was postponed, with no vote expected until parliament reconvenes on August 18.

The crisis was triggered by Zelenskyy's dismissal of reformist Defense Minister Mykhailo Fedorov, 35, who had served only six months in office. Fedorov, widely credited with revolutionizing Ukraine's drone warfare capabilities and modernizing military procurement, has become a symbol of effective wartime leadership for many Ukrainians.

Fedorov vs. Syrskyi: A Public Break

In a fiery press conference, Fedorov publicly accused Armed Forces Commander-in-Chief General Oleksandr Syrskyi of systematically blocking ministry initiatives. "Every initiative we proposed was blocked," Fedorov stated, revealing he had urged Zelenskyy to replace both Syrskyi and chief of staff Andrii Hnatov, demands that were refused. Zelenskyy acknowledged "systemic friction" between the defense ministry and military command, saying both sides bear responsibility.

Deputy Air Force Commander Colonel Pavlo Yelizarov resigned in protest, calling Fedorov's dismissal "a great evil" for Ukraine's defense. Renowned military tech adviser Serhii "Flash" Beskrestnov also stepped down. Protests erupted across at least 15 cities, with over 1,000 demonstrators gathering outside Zelenskyy's office in Kyiv chanting "Shame!" and "Bring back Fedorov."

Russia Strikes Kyiv with 8 Ballistic Missiles

As protests unfolded Thursday night, Russia launched at least eight ballistic missiles (Iskander-M and S-400) at Kyiv, killing two and wounding six — including a 16-year-old boy. This marks the seventh ballistic missile attack on Kyiv in July alone, a pace approaching one strike every other day. Mayor Vitali Klitschko confirmed the strikes hit warehouses and commercial infrastructure: "This is the seventh attack this month. The math is not complicated."

Fedorov, before his dismissal, had warned of declining interception rates: "For every missile we bring down, two more get through on the next attack." Ukrainian forces continued their own offensive operations, striking 11 Russian shadow fleet vessels in the Black and Azov Seas — including five oil tankers, one gas tanker, three cargo ships, and two tugboats.

🇮🇷🇺🇸 US-Iran Conflict: Qatar Hit for First Time, Vance Accuses Israel

The US-Iran confrontation entered its sixth consecutive night of airstrikes early Friday, with US forces expanding their campaign to target infrastructure — hitting five bridges in Iran's southern Hormozgan province. Iranian state news agency IRNA reported at least seven killed and nine wounded in strikes on bridges in Bandar Khamir. US Central Command said fighter jets, drones, and warships struck "dozens of Iranian military targets," including coastal surveillance sites, air defense positions, and logistics infrastructure.

Qatar Suffers First Direct Iranian Missile Attack

In a significant escalation, Qatar — a key mediator in the US-Iran conflict — came under direct Iranian missile attack for the first time on Friday morning. Multiple missiles and drones targeted Doha, with Qatari air defenses intercepting incoming fire. The Interior Ministry confirmed a child was injured by shrapnel, and the government issued emergency shelter warnings to all residents via mobile phone alerts. Bahrain and Kuwait also faced renewed Iranian strikes, with Kuwait reporting 32 Iranian drones intercepted by its air defenses.

Meanwhile, Reuters reported that Iran has instructed Yemen's Houthi rebels to prepare to close the Bab el-Mandeb Strait — the southern gateway to the Red Sea — if the US strikes Iranian power infrastructure as Trump has threatened. A source close to the Houthis confirmed they have deployed missiles and drones near the strait and are awaiting orders.

Vance: Israeli Government Elements Trying to Manipulate US Opinion

Vice President JD Vance made explosive claims in a recent Joe Rogan podcast, asserting that "people within the Israeli government" have been actively trying to manipulate American public opinion to prolong the Iran war. "I know beyond a shadow of a doubt that there have been people within the Israeli government who are trying to shift us away from diplomacy because they want to continue the military campaign," Vance said.

Vance alleged that Israeli-linked actors have been funding media campaigns, providing leaks to journalists, and posting on social media to undermine the US-Iran memorandum of understanding brokered last month. He went further, stating that the US "would not have engaged in the Iran war were it not for Israeli influence." The Wall Street Journal characterized Vance's remarks as evidence of a widening rift between Washington and Jerusalem.

The White House reiterated that Trump remains open to diplomacy while insisting that Iran must stop attacking commercial shipping in the Strait of Hormuz.
