🛑 UK NCSC Warns: GPT-5.6 Has Same Cyber Vulnerabilities as Banned Fable

On July 10, the UK's National Cyber Security Centre (NCSC) released an assessment concluding that OpenAI's GPT-5.6 model family "likely has similar cyber vulnerabilities" to Anthropic's Fable — the very same model that triggered U.S. export controls due to its dangerous cybersecurity capabilities. The assessment found that GPT-5.6 Sol, when jailbroken, demonstrated dangerous cyberattack capabilities including automated vulnerability exploitation and malicious code generation.

The finding raises the specter of OpenAI facing similar export restrictions that forced Anthropic to withdraw Fable 5 from global markets. While OpenAI's system card argues the model is "better at finding and fixing vulnerabilities than exploiting them," the NCSC's independent evaluation reached the opposite conclusion. This cybersecurity tug-of-war could directly impact GPT-5.6's global deployment, particularly for government and critical infrastructure sales channels. The report comes just days after GPT-5.6 received Trump administration approval for public rollout following a two-week restricted preview period.

🏛️ Fed Minutes: AI Infrastructure Investment Emerges as Major Inflation Driver

The Federal Reserve's June FOMC minutes, released July 8, mention "AI" 21 times — up from just 8 mentions in April — marking a pivotal moment where artificial intelligence shifted from a technology story to a core macroeconomic concern. The minutes explicitly link AI infrastructure demand to sustained upward pressure on tech product and electricity prices, contributing to persistent inflation that is keeping the Fed from cutting rates.

"Many participants noted that ongoing strong demand for AI infrastructure would likely sustain upward pressure on prices for technology products and electricity," the minutes state. "Most participants remarked that growth in economic activity that exceeded that of potential output, owing in part to strong AI business investment, could contribute to more persistent inflationary pressures." Fed Chair Kevin Warsh reinforced this at the ECB forum, declaring: "We're going to deliver price stability in the U.S." The CME FedWatch Tool now prices in at least one 25 basis point rate hike this year. The AI narrative has officially shifted from "productivity gains" to "investment-driven inflation."

🧠 BAAI Orca: China's 125K-Hour Video-Trained World Model

On July 9, the Beijing Academy of Artificial Intelligence (BAAI) released its Orca world model, trained on 125,000 hours of internet video data. Orca takes a fundamentally different approach from traditional vision-language-action models: it first learns a unified world state representation, then "reads out" understanding, prediction, and action capabilities from it.

Orca's core innovation is "Next-State Prediction" — the model learns how the world's state evolves under natural dynamics and external interventions. The team pioneered a dual learning framework: "Unconscious Learning" extracts dense state changes from continuous video, while "Conscious Learning" introduces language and event-level semantic constraints. The training dataset comprises 125K hours of video, 160M event annotations, and 11.5M visual QA pairs. Built on the FlagScale framework with FSDP2 optimizations, Orca achieves 2.91 Samples/Sec/GPU throughput on H100 clusters — 4.4× faster than baseline. In downstream tasks, freezing the backbone and training only lightweight readout modules still delivers significant gains in out-of-distribution object and scene generalization. The global AI research community has called it "closer to a true universal world model" than existing approaches.

📱 T-4 Days: 8M+ AI Characters Deleted as China Enforces AI Companion Ban

With just four days until China's "Interim Measures for the Management of AI Anthropomorphic Interactive Services" takes effect on July 15, ByteDance's Doubao, Alibaba's Tongyi Qianwen, and Tencent's Yuanbao have all shut down their AI companion/avatar features. According to 36Kr, over 8 million user-created AI characters on Doubao alone are affected, with 26 million monthly active users. ByteDance is migrating companion features to standalone app "Catbox," while Alibaba has removed the creation portal entirely.

The regulation, jointly issued by five government agencies including the Cyberspace Administration, prohibits content that induces extreme emotions in minors, fosters unhealthy emotional dependency, or trains models on sensitive user conversation data. Crucially, the ban targets companion-style chatbots — not productivity agents like office assistants or coding tools. Major tech firms are now splitting their AI products into two distinct tracks: task-oriented "agent tools" and relationship-oriented "companion products" with separate compliance frameworks. This marks one of the most sweeping AI product reshuffles in China to date.
