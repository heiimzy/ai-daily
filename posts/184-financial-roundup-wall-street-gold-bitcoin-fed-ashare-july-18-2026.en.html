⚡ July 18 Financial Roundup: Wall Street Ends Week Lower, Gold Returns Above $4,000, Bitcoin Breaks Below $63K, Fed Officials Turn Hawkish

1. Wall Street Slides: All Three Major Indices Close Lower for the Week

U.S. stocks closed firmly in the red on Friday, July 17, capping a losing week as AI-driven selling spread from semiconductors across the broader technology sector, compounded by escalating geopolitical tensions in the Middle East.

The Dow Jones Industrial Average fell 406.55 points, or 0.77%, to close at 52,146.42. The S&P 500 lost 76.08 points, or 1.01%, ending at 7,457.69. The tech-heavy Nasdaq Composite dropped 361.70 points, or 1.40%, to 25,520.24. For the week, the Dow slipped 0.93%, the Nasdaq tumbled 2.9%, and the S&P 500 declined 1.55%.

The Philadelphia Semiconductor Index (SOX) fell another 1.63% on Friday to 11,673.89, bringing its weekly loss to 6.9% and its drawdown from June highs to approximately 19% — dangerously close to bear market territory. Nvidia (NVDA) shed 2.21%, while Apple (AAPL) managed a marginal 0.14% gain to reclaim the title of the world's largest company by market cap. Tesla (TSLA) fell 2.61%, Alphabet (GOOGL) dropped 2.17%, Amazon (AMZN) lost 1.06%, and Microsoft (MSFT) slipped 1.82%.

In a sign that some bottom-fishing capital is entering the market, memory chip stocks showed tentative signs of stabilization. Seagate Technology (STX) surged 5.66%, Western Digital (WDC) gained 2.23%, and SK Hynix (SKHY) rose 1.13%. Analysts cautioned that this is more likely a technical bounce than a trend reversal, as AI valuation normalization still has room to run.

SpaceX (SPCX) continued its post-IPO slide, falling 5.43% to $123.99, well below its issue price. The company aborted a Starship V3 launch attempt on Thursday after engine ignition failure; Elon Musk said two Raptor engines will be replaced and another launch attempt is expected early next week.

2. Gold Returns Above $4,000 but Posts Weekly Loss of Over 3%

Gold prices climbed 0.97% on Friday to reach $4,014.93 per ounce, bouncing back above the psychologically significant $4,000 level. However, the precious metal still posted a weekly decline of more than 3% — its steepest since early June.

The gold market is caught between two opposing forces. On the one hand, softer-than-expected U.S. inflation data — headline CPI fell 0.4% in June, pulling the annual rate down to 3.5%, while core CPI cooled to 2.6% — supported the case for eventual Fed easing. On the other hand, escalating U.S.-Iran tensions drove crude oil up 12% for the week, reigniting inflation fears and bolstering expectations that the Fed will keep rates elevated.

Cleveland Fed President Beth Hammack said Friday that "current inflation levels are too high," citing widespread concerns from businesses about energy costs, supply chain disruptions, and rising insurance premiums. Dallas Fed President Lorie Logan went further, explicitly calling for another rate hike. These hawkish signals weighed on zero-yielding gold.

Silver settled at $56.09/oz, up 1.07% on the day but down over 14% for the week. Platinum fell 1.75% to $1,613.80. Copper lost 1.14% to $6.22/lb. Industrial metals broadly declined amid mounting growth concerns.

3. Bitcoin Breaks Below $63,000: AI Contagion Spreads to Crypto

The cryptocurrency market experienced a broad selloff on Friday, driven by the same risk-off sentiment that pummeled equity markets. Bitcoin (BTC) recovered from an intraday low of $62,500 to trade around $63,055, down approximately 1.2% on the day. Ethereum (ETH) fell 1.74% to roughly $1,832. Total crypto market capitalization shrank 1.86% to $2.16 trillion.

CoinDesk attributed the decline to a global de-risking event rather than crypto-specific catalysts. Nasdaq 100 futures fell 1.91%, S&P 500 futures slipped 0.96%, and Japan's Nikkei 225 plunged 4%. The U.S. Dollar Index (DXY) rose to 100.75 while gold climbed above $4,000 — the classic rotation out of risk assets into havens.

Technically, Bitcoin is testing the 78.6% Fibonacci retracement level at $63,167. A confirmed breakdown below this level would open the path toward the June range floor at $57,779. Recovery attempts have repeatedly stalled in the $65,000-$66,000 zone. On a more constructive note, the average relative strength index (RSI) across crypto pairs has dipped to 42.23, approaching oversold territory that triggered July's relief bounce.

Privacy coins were among the few bright spots: Zcash (ZEC) rose 1.56% to $531, Dash (DASH) gained 0.78%. AI-themed tokens FET and TAO posted modest gains of about 0.20%. Fundstrat's Tom Lee published a bullish call on Ethereum on X, calling it "the key downstream story in AI." Lee's firm Bitmine holds 5.77 million ETH — 4.8% of circulating supply.

4. Fed Officials Sound Hawkish Alarm: September Rate Hike Odds Rise to 50%

A chorus of hawkish commentary from Federal Reserve officials this week has dramatically shifted interest rate expectations. Dallas Fed President Lorie Logan explicitly called for another rate hike on Friday, while Vice Chair Philip Jefferson said he would support tighter policy if inflation doesn't show further improvement in the near term.

Cleveland Fed President Hammack noted that despite June's CPI pullback, consumer spending remains robust and the unemployment rate stays low. She flagged energy costs and supply chain disruptions as persistent upward pressures on prices. The market is now pricing approximately a 50% probability of a September rate hike, according to CME FedWatch.

Adding to the hawkish narrative, June import prices rose 0.3% month-over-month and surged 7.1% year-over-year — the largest annual increase since August 2022 and sharply above economists' expectations of a 0.8% decline. This suggests that the disinflation narrative may be driven almost entirely by temporary energy base effects.

5. Consumer Sentiment Hits 5-Month High, but Inflation Expectations Stay Sticky

The University of Michigan's preliminary consumer sentiment index for July jumped to 54.4 from 49.5 in June, well above the market expectation of 51.0 and marking a five-month high. The improvement was largely attributed to falling gasoline prices in late June — the energy index dropped 5.7% for the month, with gasoline prices plunging 9.7%.

However, one-year inflation expectations remained elevated at 4.2%, indicating that consumers are not yet convinced price pressures are abating. Analysts warn that the oil price rally since early July (Brent crude surging from below $70 to above $85) will likely reverse this improvement in August's survey data.

TradingKey analysts noted that June's disinflation was almost entirely energy-driven and is likely a one-off. The July CPI report, due August 12, is expected to reflect the oil price surge. The market's prevailing "disinflation → rate cut" thesis is increasingly vulnerable to geopolitical risks.

6. China A-Shares Suffer Deep Correction: Shanghai Composite Loses 3,800

China's A-share market experienced a severe correction on Friday, July 17. The Shanghai Composite Index plunged 3.05% to close at 3,764.15, breaching the 3,800-point level. The ChiNext Index (growth enterprises) crashed 7.15%, while the Shenzhen Component Index fell over 5%. Across the market, 5,001 stocks declined — including 198 hitting their daily down limit — while only 482 advanced. Total trading volume surged to 2.67 trillion yuan.

The telecommunications sector led the decline with a 9.84% rout, followed by electronics (-8%), machinery, healthcare, and computer sectors all falling over 5%. Zhongji Innolight plunged 12% to 979.46 yuan with a staggering 56.5 billion yuan in trading volume. Multiple chip stocks including GigaDevice and Tongfu Microelectronics hit their daily down limits.

Fund managers attributed the selloff to valuation compression in AI and semiconductor names after their massive first-half rally, combined with liquidity drainage from the CXMT IPO, northbound capital outflows amid Fed uncertainty, and weak Q2 GDP data. The consensus is that the market may need 1-2 weeks to digest the selling pressure before finding a bottom.
