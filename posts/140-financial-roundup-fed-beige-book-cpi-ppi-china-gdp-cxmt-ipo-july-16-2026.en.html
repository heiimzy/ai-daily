⚡ July 16 Financial Roundup: Fed Beige Book Confirms Cooling Inflation, US CPI & PPI Both Miss Estimates, China H1 GDP Grows 4.7%

📋 Fed Beige Book: 11 Districts Report Modest Growth, Inflation Pressures Ease

The Federal Reserve's latest Beige Book, released Wednesday, showed that U.S. economic activity expanded at a slight to modest pace in 11 of 12 Federal Reserve districts from late May through June, with the remaining district reporting no change. The overall pace of growth was roughly in line with the prior period.

On prices, the report noted a generally modest pace of increase. Nine districts reported modest price increases, two reported stronger increases, and one reported smaller gains — the overall pace was steady or slower compared to the previous reporting period. While inflation pressures have eased, they continue to deliver "unpleasant shocks." Contacts across districts generally expect continued economic expansion in the coming months, though several noted high uncertainty regarding fuel cost prospects.

The labor market continued to improve across multiple sectors but has not yet pushed up wage costs — affirming the Fed's long-standing view that labor is not currently a primary driver of inflation. However, non-labor costs continued to rise across services, construction, and manufacturing, driven by increases in energy, transportation, and raw material costs.

A notable highlight was the 2026 World Cup, mentioned roughly 12 times in the report for its positive impact on host city economies — from Boston bar beer sales to Miami tourism inflows, the tournament's economic effect was significant.

📉 US June CPI & PPI Both Miss, July Rate Hike Probability Plunges to 17%

Both the June Consumer Price Index (CPI) and Producer Price Index (PPI) came in well below expectations, marking a significant阶段性 easing of inflation pressures.

CPI: June CPI fell 0.4% month-over-month, the largest single-month decline since April 2020. Year-over-year, CPI rose 3.5%, down sharply from May's 4.2% and below the 3.8% consensus. Core CPI (excluding food and energy) was flat month-over-month, with the annual rate slowing to 2.6% from 2.9%, below the 2.8% expected.

PPI: The Producer Price Index unexpectedly declined 0.3% in June (consensus expected flat). Year-over-year PPI slowed to 5.5% from 6.5% in May. Core PPI rose 4.7% year-over-year, with monthly growth of just 0.2% — both below expectations.

The sharp drop in energy prices was the main driver: the energy index fell 5.7% month-over-month, with gasoline prices declining 9.7%. Housing inflation also continued to cool, with the shelter index rising just 0.1% — the smallest increase since January 2021.

Following the data releases, market expectations for a July rate hike collapsed from 42% to roughly 17%, while September hike expectations fell from over 90% to about 70%. The 2-year Treasury yield dropped 14 basis points to 4.14% at one point.

However, Fed Chair Kevin Warsh maintained his hawkish stance in Congressional testimony, stressing that one month of data does not constitute victory over inflation. New York Fed President Williams reiterated the need to bring inflation back to 2%. Fed Governor Cook warned that inflation risks now outweigh labor market concerns and stands ready to act if inflation doesn't cool further.

🏭 China H1 GDP Grows 4.7%, Q2 Slows to 4.3%

China's National Bureau of Statistics reported on July 15 that H1 2026 GDP reached 69.6 trillion yuan ($9.6 trillion), up 4.7% year-over-year — in line with the annual growth target. In absolute terms, H1 GDP increased by 3.6 trillion yuan from a year ago, the largest H1 increment in five years.

By quarter, Q1 grew 5.0% and Q2 grew 4.3% (below the 4.5% consensus), with sequential quarterly growth of 0.9%. Despite the Q2 slowdown, June industrial production expanded 5.3%, beating the 4.6% forecast, while retail sales rose 1.0% — significantly ahead of the -0.1% expected.

On trade, H1 goods imports and exports exceeded 25 trillion yuan for the first time historically. Exports grew 13.4%, with electromechanical product exports up 20.1%. Foreign exchange reserves remained stable above $3.4 trillion, and the yuan appreciated roughly 3% against the dollar since the start of the year.

The IMF recently upgraded its full-year growth forecast for China by 0.2 percentage points, making China one of the few major economies to receive an upward revision amid a global downgrade cycle. PBoC Vice Governor Zou Lan stated the central bank will strengthen counter-cyclical adjustments and is studying increasing the frequency of overnight reverse repurchase operations.

💾 CXMT Launches $8B IPO on Shanghai STAR Market

China's leading DRAM manufacturer ChangXin Memory Technologies (CXMT, ticker 688825) officially launched its IPO subscriptions on July 16, pricing shares at 8.66 yuan each for a market cap of approximately 579.2 billion yuan ($80 billion). The IPO is expected to raise roughly 57.9 billion yuan ($8 billion), with the greenshoe option bringing the total to 66.6 billion yuan — making it the largest IPO on the STAR Market this year.

In an investor Q&A session on July 15, CXMT Chairman Zhu Yiming stated that the company's DRAM products primarily serve mobile devices, servers, and PCs. Benefiting from surging data center demand, CXMT launched DDR5 and next-generation products in late 2024, with server DRAM revenue achieving a compound annual growth rate of 536.24%.

Zhu cautioned that the DRAM industry is highly cyclical with significant price volatility. By shipment volume and sales, CXMT ranks first in China and fourth globally among DRAM manufacturers.

📊 Global Markets: Big Tech Lifts Wall Street, Kospi Surges 7%, A-Shares Mixed

US Markets: All three major US indices closed higher Wednesday. The Dow Jones Industrial Average rose 150.37 points (+0.29%) to 52,658.64; the Nasdaq Composite gained 162.22 points (+0.62%) to 26,269.23; and the S&P 500 added 28.81 points (+0.38%) to 7,572.40. Apple surged over 4%, while Amazon, Microsoft, and Alphabet each gained approximately 3%.

Bank stocks shone as five of the largest US banks reported a combined $49 billion in Q2 profits. Goldman Sachs jumped over 9%, JPMorgan rose 2.5%, and BlackRock gained over 7%. However, IBM cratered 25% in its worst single-day drop since 1987 after reporting weaker-than-expected quarterly revenue, highlighting the massive capital rotation toward AI infrastructure spending.

Asia Markets: South Korea's Kospi surged 7%, led by semiconductor heavyweights SK Hynix and Samsung Electronics. Japan's Nikkei rose 1%, and the MSCI Asia-Pacific ex-Japan index gained 2.4%. Hong Kong's Hang Seng Index rose 1.4%, with AI concept stocks MiniMax and Zhipu surging 13% and 16% respectively. China's A-shares edged lower — the Shanghai Composite fell 0.29% — though pharmaceutical and robotics sectors bucked the trend.

Commodities & FX: WTI crude settled at $79.60/barrel, Brent at $84.95/barrel. Spot gold traded at $4,059.95/oz. The dollar index fell 0.41% to 100.51, while the 2-year Treasury yield settled at 4.143%.
