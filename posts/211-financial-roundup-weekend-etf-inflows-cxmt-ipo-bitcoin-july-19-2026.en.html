⚡ July 19 Financial Briefing: A-Share ETF Inflows Surge Past ¥320B, 18 Active ETFs Filed, CXMT Records Largest STAR Market IPO, Bitcoin Holds Steady at $64K

📈 A-Share ETFs See Record ¥320B+ Net Inflows in July

As of July 17, stock-themed ETFs in China's A-share market have attracted over ¥320 billion ($44.6B) in net inflows since the start of July, with ¥200 billion+ coming in the week of July 13-17 alone — setting a single-week record for 2026. On July 17 alone, ETF net inflows exceeded ¥75 billion amid market volatility, with multiple broad-based ETFs seeing unusually high trading volumes.

According to Shanghai Securities News, the surge in capital inflows reflects growing investor recognition of current A-share valuation lows and strengthening policy signals. Huatai-PineBridge Fund noted that sustained large net subscriptions into broad-based ETFs indicate institutional and long-term allocation capital is accelerating its entry into the market. The CSI 300 and STAR 50 were the primary beneficiaries of these inflows.

"Sustained ETF volume expansion has historically been a key signal of market bottoms. Every major market trough in recent years has been accompanied by significant ETF asset growth." — Head Strategist at a major Chinese brokerage

🏦 Milestone: 18 Active Management ETFs Filed in China

On July 17, the first batch of actively managed ETFs was officially filed with China's securities regulator. Eighteen fund management companies submitted registration materials simultaneously — nine each for the Shanghai and Shenzhen exchanges. Participants include industry giants such as China Asset Management, E Fund, Guotai AMC, Huatai-PineBridge,南方 (Southern),华宝 (Fortune), Fullgoal, Hua An, Harvest, Tianhong, Penghua, China Merchants, ICBC Credit Suisse, Ping An, Dacheng, Yongyin, J.P. Morgan Asset Management, and CCB Principal.

The products cover diverse investment styles including value, large-cap, balanced, dividend, and growth. This initiative follows CSRC Chairman Wu Qing's support for active ETFs at the 2026 Lujiazui Forum in June, which materialized in just one month. Industry insiders say this marks a historic shift from a purely passive index-tracking ETF market toward a "passive + active" model.

Unlike traditional passive ETFs, active ETFs allow fund managers to independently select investment strategies without tracking a specific index. Fortune Fund's quantitative investment division noted that active ETFs will diversify on-exchange products and give managers greater flexibility in sector allocation and stock selection. Fullgoal Fund highlighted four key benefits: price discovery, style drift prevention, breaking homogeneous competition, and meeting long-term institutional capital needs.

💎 CXMT: Largest IPO in STAR Market History at ¥57.9 Billion

ChangXin Memory Technologies (CXMT) will announce its IPO lottery results on July 20. Priced at ¥8.66 per share with a valuation of approximately ¥580 billion ($80.8B), CXMT is expected to raise about ¥57.9 billion — surpassing SMIC's 2020 IPO to become the largest in STAR Market history.

China's leading DRAM manufacturer posted first-quarter revenue of ¥50.8 billion and adjusted net profit of ¥26.3 billion, representing year-over-year growth of 719% and 1,993%, respectively. While its global market share of 7.67% still lags behind industry leaders (Micron at 22%), its growth trajectory is remarkable.

Proceeds will fund three key areas: 12-inch DRAM wafer fab upgrades, HBM and automotive-grade memory technology development, and next-generation memory R&D — covering consumer electronics, AI servers, and automotive applications. Strategic investors include upstream equipment leaders AMEC and NSIG, as well as downstream giants ZTE, Alibaba Cloud, NIO, and Transsion.

CXMT's IPO comes amid a cyclical peak in the global memory chip market. While SK Hynix and Samsung Electronics have posted surging profits, their stock volatility reminds investors of the semiconductor industry's perennial "boom-bust" cycle risk.

₿ Bitcoin Holds $64,810 as CLARITY Act Vote Looms

Bitcoin traded steadily near $64,810 over the weekend, up 1.44% over 24 hours. Ethereum followed with a modest gain to $1,845, lifting the total crypto market cap back to $2.19 trillion. US Bitcoin spot ETFs recorded approximately $200 million in net inflows this week, with $132.3 million on July 17 alone — marking four consecutive days of positive flows and signaling renewed institutional demand.

Market attention is focused on the upcoming Senate vote on the CLARITY Act, expected between July 20 and July 24. The bill aims to classify Ethereum and other established tokens as digital commodities, creating clearer regulatory frameworks. Representative Bryan Steil expressed optimism about the bill's prospects on July 17. Passage would be a major catalyst for the crypto industry.

On the macro front, June US CPI cooled to 3.5% (below the 4.2% forecast), pushing market expectations for a July Fed rate hold to 94%. However, Fed Chair Kevin Warsh reiterated his commitment to price stability, and rising oil prices from US-Iran tensions keep September rate hike odds near 50%. On-chain, a wallet dormant for 8 years moved 5,908 BTC (~$383M), prompting analyst Ali Martinez to issue a high-volatility alert.

📱 Apple Raises Japan iPhone Prices 10% on Chip Costs and Yen Weakness

Apple raised iPhone prices across its entire Japanese lineup on July 17. The iPhone 17 series (256GB base model launched September 2025) jumped from ¥129,800 to ¥142,800 — a 10% increase. The Pro and Air series saw similar 10% hikes across the board. The move reflects dual pressure from rising memory chip costs and the yen's slide to 162 against the dollar — its weakest level since 1986.

Japan's 10-year government bond yield has surged toward 2.9%, a 30-year high, as the Bank of Japan continues its policy normalization. The BOJ raised rates by 25 bps to 1% on June 16, with markets expecting another hike to 1.25% by year-end. BNP Paribas forecasts the BOJ will maintain a gradual tightening pace of one hike every 4-5 months. BlackRock strategists issued a warning about "increasingly crowded yen short positions," noting that any short-squeeze trigger could cause sharp reversals.
