⚡ US-Iran War Night 9: Brent Surges Past $90, Iran Warns of 'Punitive Operation,' IRGC Strikes US Targets in Syria for First Time

As of 14:00 Beijing time on July 20, 2026, the United States has completed its ninth consecutive night of airstrikes against Iran, with the conflict entering a dramatically escalated new phase. Brent crude breached the $90 per barrel mark for the first time since June 11, traffic through the Strait of Hormuz has dropped to zero, and Iran's Revolutionary Guard (IRGC) has expanded the battlefield to Syria for the first time. Here is the latest comprehensive update.

Brent Breaches $90 as Strait of Hormuz Completely Shuts Down

Asia-Pacific markets opened on July 20 with crude oil prices surging sharply. Brent crude rose more than 3% to $90.79 per barrel—the highest level since June 11—while West Texas Intermediate (WTI) climbed to $84.68. The immediate trigger was the IRGC Navy's announcement on Sunday that traffic through the Strait of Hormuz has dropped to zero, with any vessel attempting unauthorized passage subject to military action.

The UK Maritime Trade Operations (UKMTO) reported a vessel on fire off the coast of Oman near the Strait of Hormuz on Monday, with the cause and identity of the ship yet to be confirmed. Iran claimed that two oil tankers attempting to transit the strait "exploded and were forced to cease movement" after entering an Iranian minefield—claims that could not be independently verified.

Market analysts noted that global commercial crude inventories have been steadily depleted over recent months, with strategic emergency reserves approaching exhaustion. If the strait remains closed for an extended period, supply gaps will widen rapidly. Multiple institutions forecast that geopolitical risk premiums will remain elevated in the near term, with Q3 Brent crude price expectations being revised sharply upward.

Iran Warns US Military of 'Punitive Operation'

Brigadier General Ebrahim Zolfaghari, spokesperson for Iran's Khatam al-Anbiya Central Headquarters—the joint operational command coordinating operations between the IRGC and Iran's regular armed forces—issued a stark warning on Monday: as long as what he called "American aggression" continues, the Strait of Hormuz will remain unsafe for oil and gas shipments, and the US military must prepare for a "punitive operation." Zolfaghari did not specify the form such an operation might take, but analysts see it as a potential precursor to a wider Iranian military campaign.

Meanwhile, Iran's Supreme Leader Mojtaba Khamenei issued a written statement declaring President Trump's signature "utterly worthless and devoid of credibility," accusing Washington of repeatedly violating the Islamabad memorandum of understanding. He warned that Iran has "unforgettable lessons" for the United States if military escalation continues.

IRGC Strikes US al-Tanf Base in Syria—Conflict Spills Into Third Country

In a major escalation, the IRGC announced Monday that it had carried out a missile strike on the US al-Tanf garrison in southeastern Syria—a key US outpost housing a Special Operations command center. This marks the first publicly acknowledged Iranian attack on US-linked military targets on Syrian territory since the conflict began in late February.

The IRGC said the strike was retaliation for US airstrikes that killed Iranian conscript soldiers near Iranshahr. CENTCOM had previously denied an earlier Iranian claim that al-Tanf had been attacked but did not immediately comment on Monday's operation.

Iranian state media additionally reported fresh missile launches from Lorestan province targeting what were described as "enemy positions." The IRGC also claimed to have struck US military aircraft at Jordan's Aqaba Airport, saying some planes suffered "severe damage," and alleged attacks on a US ammunition depot and radar installations in Kuwait.

Rubio Urges Global Cooperation on Hormuz, Signals Diplomacy Remains Possible

Amid the escalating military confrontation, US Secretary of State Marco Rubio called on other nations to play a greater role in protecting shipping through the Strait of Hormuz. Speaking to reporters before departing for the Philippines, Rubio said Iran is using the strategic waterway as leverage and that while the United States will continue working to keep maritime routes secure, "every country should bear their share of the responsibility."

Rubio reiterated that Washington remains open to a diplomatic resolution, maintaining that military pressure and negotiations can proceed in parallel. This echoes Vice President JD Vance's position earlier this week. Iran's chief negotiator and parliament speaker, Mohammad-Bagher Ghalibaf, similarly described war and negotiations as complementary tools for defending national interests.

Analysts suggest the parallel diplomatic messaging from both capitals indicates the two governments may be seeking to strengthen their bargaining positions ahead of another round of talks, rather than abandoning the diplomatic track entirely—even as the battlefield widens.

Gulf Crisis Deepens: Kuwait Desalination Plant Hit Again, Bahrain and Jordan Sound Air Raid Sirens

Kuwait's military confirmed Monday that air defense systems were intercepting "hostile drone attacks" attributed to Iran. The Ministry of Electricity, Water, and Renewable Energy reported that a power generation and water desalination facility was struck for the second consecutive day, causing fires and taking multiple generation units offline. Kuwait depends on desalination for 90% of its drinking water, and repeated attacks on this vital infrastructure raise severe humanitarian concerns.

Bahrain's Interior Ministry issued a statement late Sunday through Monday activating nationwide air raid sirens. The US Embassy in Manama warned it had received information suggesting Iran may target unspecified locations in central Manama, urging American citizens to take precautionary measures.

Jordan's Foreign Ministry summoned Iran's chargé d'affaires in Amman, delivering a strongly worded protest over "continuous aggressive and unjustified Iranian attacks" on the kingdom. Jordan's military reported intercepting three Iranian missiles on Sunday afternoon, with a fourth landing in a remote southern area, causing no casualties. Jordan has now faced four Iranian missile and drone attacks in five days.

Israel on Full Combat Alert as Regional Spillover Risk Rises

Israel Defense Forces Chief of Staff Lieutenant General Eyal Zamir declared Monday that the military is "closely monitoring developments in Iran and maintaining a high level of readiness" and is "prepared to immediately resume fighting." The alert came as Iranian missiles were detected targeting the Aqaba area in Jordan, which borders the southern Israeli city of Eilat.

According to The Times of Israel, multiple interceptor missiles were observed in the skies over Eilat, with Israel's air defense systems operating at full readiness. Analysts note that with multiple military forces now on combat alert, the conflict has expanded well beyond the US-Iran dyad, with Jordan and Israel now drawn into the confrontation chain. Any expansion of Iran's missile campaigns or an Israeli decision to initiate military action could trigger an even broader regional conflagration.

At press time, Brent crude stood at $90.79, WTI at $84.68, and gold fell to $3,997. Neither Washington nor Tehran has signaled a willingness to cease hostilities, and the prospect of the Strait of Hormuz reopening remains distant.
