Alibaba Cloud Supernode Successfully Runs 2-Trillion Parameter Model, Marking a Milestone for Domestic AI Infrastructure

On July 24, Alibaba Cloud announced that its Zhenwu M890 × Panjiu AI128 supernode has successfully adapted and deployed Qwen3.8, making it the first domestic infrastructure in China to run a large language model exceeding 2 trillion parameters. The model is now available for inference services on Alibaba Cloud's Bailian platform.

The "supernode" architecture is a novel infrastructure design for building大规模 AI computing clusters. Its core concept uses advanced interconnection technology to integrate multiple independent servers into a single unified computing unit, significantly reducing data transfer latency between nodes — a bottleneck known in the industry as the "communication wall" or "memory wall." Compared to traditional large-model inference architectures, the supernode approach can reduce cross-server communication latency by several times, dramatically improving inference efficiency.

According to Alibaba Cloud, the Zhenwu M890 is a next-generation high-performance AI server featuring the latest GPU interconnect and memory pooling technologies. The Panjiu AI128 is a high-performance AI infrastructure platform delivering ultra-high bandwidth and extremely low-latency inter-node communication. Together, they enable Qwen3.8 — a 2+ trillion parameter MoE (Mixture of Experts) model — to achieve efficient data and model parallelism across multiple nodes.

Analysts have responded positively. China Securities Co. noted that the inflection point for domestic semiconductor equipment and domestic computing power has further clarified, with AI-driven demand keeping PCB, storage, and AI power supply sectors in a high prosperity cycle. Guohai Securities added that conditions for scaled mass production of domestic supernodes are gradually maturing, and downstream verification and adoption may accelerate faster than expected.

The significance of the domestic supernode extends beyond the technical breakthrough — it demonstrates that China's AI computing infrastructure has achieved autonomous, end-to-end capabilities for supporting trillion-parameter-scale models, a development with far-reaching strategic implications for the domestic AI industry.

ASUS Launches Zenni Claw Agentic AI Beta: Bringing AI Workflows to Everyday Users

On July 24, ASUS officially announced the beta release of Zenni Claw, an agentic AI software designed to help users turn everyday prompts into guided AI workflows. First unveiled at Computex 2026, Zenni Claw makes agentic AI more approachable for mainstream users across work, travel, and daily planning scenarios.

Zenni Claw is built around four core principles: Easy to start — simple setup that lets users enjoy the benefits of agentic AI without complexity; Ready-to-use skills — built-in assistant skills tailored for everyday scenarios, eliminating the need to start from a blank prompt; Flexible AI experiences — supports both local and cloud-based AI processing, adapting to device performance and workflow needs; Built-in protection — guardrails and safety mechanisms that allow users to explore agentic AI confidently.

Unlike traditional AI chatbots that primarily respond to questions, Zenni Claw is designed to help users move through tasks. By combining guided workflows with ready-to-use skills, it provides a simpler way for everyday computer users to understand and benefit from what agentic AI can do in real-world scenarios. ASUS describes this as a key step in its broader strategy to "make AI PC experiences more practical, personal, and accessible."

The beta version is now available for supported ASUS devices. While ASUS has not disclosed specific hardware requirements, the software is expected to be compatible primarily with ASUS laptops and all-in-ones featuring next-generation AI processors.

Alibaba Qwen-Image-3.0: 4,500-Token Input and 10-Pixel Text Rendering

Alibaba's Qwen team has released Qwen-Image-3.0, the third generation of its image generation model. Unlike previous versions focused on producing "beautiful" images, Qwen-Image-3.0 is purpose-built for productivity scenarios — generating complex newspaper layouts, infographics, exam papers, and storyboards in a single pass.

The model's key specifications are impressive: Extended prompt capacity — supports up to 4,500 tokens of input, 4.5 times more than the previous generation, enough to describe complex multi-panel layouts, diagrams, formulas, and fine-print text in a single generation; Pixel-level text rendering — can precisely generate legible text as small as 10 pixels, with support for LaTeX mathematical equations, subscripts, superscripts, fractions, and summation symbols; Multilingual support — native accurate rendering in 12 languages, including mixed Chinese-English scenarios; Single-pass layout generation — produces complete newspaper pages, exam papers, or infographics without stitching multiple images together.

In Alibaba's demo examples, Qwen-Image-3.0 generated a complex infographic containing nine independent information panels — each with its own diagrams, formulas, captions, and fine-print details — all produced in a single generation. Another demo showed a simulated algebraic geometry paper page with multi-line LaTeX equations and handwritten annotations.

Currently, Qwen-Image-3.0 is available through invite-only API access, with plans to integrate into first-party apps like Qwen Chat soon. Unlike Qwen-Image 1.0, which was released as open source, the open-source plans for 3.0 remain unclear.
