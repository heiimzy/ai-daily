⚡ July 17 Tech Roundup: PrimeBot Debuts Shape-Shifting Robot at WAIC, Nubia AI Pet iMoochi Goes on Sale, Thinking Machines Inkling Prices Rise 50%, Nvidia Launches Cosmos 3 Edge

1. PrimeBot Unveils World's First Transformable Personal Robot

The 2026 World Artificial Intelligence Conference (WAIC 2026) opened today in Shanghai, and PrimeBot (Qiyuan Robotics) marked the occasion by launching the Transformer 1 (T1), described as the world's first transformable personal robot capable of switching autonomously between a wheeled humanoid and a quadruped form. This represents a significant breakthrough in consumer-grade robot form factor design.

Transformer 1 is built by PrimeBot, a brand incubated by Shanghai-based advanced materials developer Swancor, which was acquired by Agibot (Zhiyuan Robotics) through a controlling stake purchase between July and November 2025 for approximately 2.1 billion yuan. The robot features an advanced morphing mechanism — in wheeled humanoid mode, it can walk steadily, carry objects, and interact naturally with humans, while quadruped mode allows it to navigate stairs, rubble, and uneven terrain. Equipped with a multi-modal perception system supporting voice, gesture, and facial recognition, the T1 is positioned as a home service and personal assistant device.

2. Nubia AI Pet Robot iMoochi Goes on Sale Today

Nubia, the smartphone brand under ZTE, announced that its AI companion robot iMoochi will begin official sales today (July 17) at 10:00 AM CST via ZTE Mall and major e-commerce platforms. First unveiled at Mobile World Congress 2026 (MWC26), iMoochi targets the emotional companionship needs of urban singles with its cute appearance and lifelike interactive design.

Powered by a Unisoc T8300 chip and equipped with five touch sensors, iMoochi can sense petting, patting, and hugging, responding with appropriate emotional feedback. To meet diverse needs, Nubia launched five different personality variants including lively Hopami, quiet Mimiu, and mischievous Cynomi. Nubia says iMoochi's core mission is to provide "a warm AI companionship experience" for young urban-dwellers, addressing the loneliness prevalent in modern city life. The launch marks ZTE's strategic shift from traditional smart devices to lifelike interactive experiences.

3. Thinking Machines Inkling API Prices Rise 50% Effective Today

Thinking Machines Lab, founded by former OpenAI CTO Mira Murati, released its first open-weight model Inkling on July 15 — a 975-billion-parameter Mixture-of-Experts model with 41 billion active parameters per query. The model, released under Apache 2.0 license and available on Hugging Face, is the largest American open-weights model to date.

However, Tinker platform pricing documentation confirms that Inkling's API pricing will increase approximately 50% effective today (July 17). At the 64K context window tier, per-million input token prices will rise from $1.87 to approximately $2.80, and per-million output tokens from $4.68 to approximately $7.02. The 256K context tier pricing doubles accordingly. The company describes this as a "normal pricing adjustment following the introductory discount period." Inkling achieves 74.1% on MCP Atlas for agentic tool use — nearly 30 points above Nvidia's Nemotron 3 Ultra — and 77.6% on SWE-Bench Verified, though it trails Chinese rival GLM 5.2 (82.7%) on Terminal Bench 2.1 coding benchmarks.

4. Nvidia Launches Cosmos 3 Edge, Partners with Japan on Physical AI

Nvidia released Cosmos 3 Edge on July 16, a new edge AI model designed to help robots and vision-based AI agents interpret and respond to physical environments in real time. The model is built for deployment beyond data centers — in factories, hospitals, laboratories, and warehouses.

Alongside the release, Nvidia announced deep collaboration with Japanese industry leaders. "The next frontier of AI is in the physical world, and this is a once-in-a-generation opportunity for Japan," said Nvidia CEO Jensen Huang. "Japan invented modern manufacturing. Now, it has the opportunity to reinvent it for the age of intelligent industries."

Japan's Noetra Corp consortium will deploy 27,500 Nvidia Rubin AI GPUs and 13,750 Vera CPUs to build the world's first national physical AI infrastructure, delivering 140 megawatts of data center capacity. SoftBank, Preferred Networks, NEC, Fujitsu, and dozens of other companies are participating. Japan's Ministry of Economy, Trade and Industry (METI) is providing approximately 387.3 billion yen ($2.4 billion) in government funding, targeting 30%+ of the global robotics market by 2040. Nvidia is also partnering with Kawasaki Heavy Industries, Fanuc, Yaskawa Electric, and other Japanese industrial giants to integrate Cosmos world models into their robotics and automation lines.
