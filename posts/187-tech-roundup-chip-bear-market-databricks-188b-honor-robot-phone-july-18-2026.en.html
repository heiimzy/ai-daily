⚡ July 18 Tech Roundup: Chip Index Enters Bear Market, Databricks Hits $188B, Honor Robot Phone Debuts at WAIC

📉 Philadelphia Semiconductor Index Crashes 20%, Confirming Bear Market — $3.3 Trillion Wiped Off Chip Stocks

The global semiconductor sector suffered its worst week since 2022. The Philadelphia Semiconductor Index (SOX) closed Friday more than 20% below its all-time high from late June, officially entering technical bear market territory. According to PRISM MarketView, global chip stocks have lost approximately $3.3 trillion in market value since June 22.

The catalyst was Chinese AI startup Moonshot AI's Thursday release of Kimi K3 — a 2.8-trillion-parameter open-weight model whose benchmark performance rivals Anthropic's Fable 5 and OpenAI's GPT-5.6 Sol at a fraction of the cost. Investors fear that Chinese developers are rapidly closing the AI capability gap while offering services at substantially lower prices, potentially dampening demand for expensive AI hardware.

"This selloff is not a uniform collapse — Nvidia has remained nearly flat over the same stretch and is still the best-performing large-cap chip stock. The pain is concentrated in memory chips and second- and third-tier names." — PRISM MarketView

Weekly worst performers include: Astera Labs down 23%, Marvell Technology down 20%, Arm Holdings down 19%, and Micron Technology down 13%. Nvidia closed Friday at $202.81, down 2.2%, shedding roughly $242 billion in market cap in a single day. Apple surpassed Nvidia during Friday's session to reclaim the title of the world's most valuable company.

Notably, the equal-weighted S&P 500 rose during the chip rout, with transports, regional banks, and homebuilders attracting inflows — indicating sector rotation rather than a full-scale flight from risk. Brent crude climbed above $86 amid escalating U.S.-Iran conflict, adding to the risk-off tone.

💰 Databricks Hits $188B Valuation in Coatue-Led Round

Databricks announced Thursday that it has signed a term sheet for a strategic funding round led by existing investor Coatue Management at a $188 billion valuation — roughly $3 billion in new capital according to the Wall Street Journal. The valuation marks a 40% jump from the $134 billion price tag attached to Databricks' February financing.

The round, which is expected to close later this summer, will also include additional new and existing investors. Proceeds will accelerate Databricks' AI strategy across three core product lines:

  • Unity AI Gateway — multi-AI governance for managing model access and cost control
  • Genie — AI coworker that turns business data into trusted answers and actions
  • Lakebase — serverless Postgres database purpose-built for AI agents

CEO Ali Ghodsi framed the strategy as moving from "tokenmaxxing to valuemaxxing" — choosing the model that produces the best outcome per dollar rather than routing every task to the most expensive frontier option. Databricks has become a prominent example of enterprises adopting affordable Chinese open-weight models, with Ghodsi recently publishing internal benchmarks showing Z.ai's GLM 5.2 matching frontier proprietary models on coding tasks at a fraction of the cost.

The company's valuation trajectory has been extraordinary: from $62B in December 2024 to $100B in September 2025, $134B in February 2026, and now $188B — nearly tripling in 18 months.

🤖 WAIC 2026 Day 2: Honor Robot Phone Makes Global Debut — World's First Robotic-Gimbal Smartphone

The 2026 World Artificial Intelligence Conference (WAIC) entered its second day in Shanghai, with Honor making its debut as an exhibitor. The company hosted a forum titled "From Digital Screens to Embodied Intelligence — A New Paradigm for the Physical World," showcasing the much-anticipated Honor Robot Phone to the public for the first time.

The Honor Robot Phone is the world's first smartphone with a built-in robotic gimbal camera system. Hidden in the back panel is a foldable, four-degree-of-freedom mechanical arm that emerges to position a camera gimbal above the device. Specifications include a 200MP main camera (f/1.6 aperture, 23mm, 4D gimbal stabilization), a 200MP periscope telephoto, and a 50MP ultra-wide camera — co-engineered with cinema brand ARRI. The device is powered by a Snapdragon 8 Elite Gen 5 chipset with a 6,000mAh+ battery supporting 120W fast charging.

On the AI front, the Robot Phone runs Honor's new Agentic OS — described as the industry's first "companion-type agent operating system" — featuring four core traits: intent-driven interaction, natural conversation, proactive intelligence, and seamless cross-device connectivity. The YOYO AI agent can autonomously execute multi-step tasks including adjusting settings, booking taxis, and managing schedules.

Futurist Kevin Kelly — dubbed the "godfather of Silicon Valley spirit" — attended as a special guest speaker, joining Honor's technical team in discussing the evolution path of AI terminals from "tools" to "companions."

"A phone shouldn't just be a boring black rectangle with a touchscreen. We decided to give it a brain, and also give it hands and feet." — James Li, CEO of Honor

The Honor Robot Phone will be available in Silver White and Graphite Black, with retail sales in China expected to begin in August at a price point above the Magic flagship series. A global launch may follow at Berlin's IFA trade show in September.

WAIC 2026 runs through July 20 at three venues across Shanghai, featuring 1,100+ exhibitors, 3,000+ products, and over 300 global debuts including Nubia's AI agent phone NaviX Ultra, StepFun's STEPX Neo, and Huawei's Atlas 950 SuperPod.
