⚡ July 21 Financial Roundup: Fed's Five Working Groups Ignite Policy Debate, Nasdaq Breaks 21,000 Intraday, Gold Surges Past $3,410, KOSPI Rally Triggers Circuit Breaker

1. Fed's "Five Working Groups" Spark Policy Debate: Goldman Sachs Pours Cold Water

Federal Reserve Chair Kevin Warsh recently announced the formation of "five monetary policy advancement working groups," seeking to reshape the Fed across five dimensions: communication mechanisms, the balance sheet, data collection, AI and productivity, and the inflation framework. However, Goldman Sachs delivered a sobering reality check in a July 20 research report: investors betting that Warsh can leverage the "AI has structurally deflationary effects" narrative to push for rate cuts will be disappointed.

Goldman noted that Warsh's aggressive proposals — including significant balance sheet reduction and dovish policy based on AI expectations — face substantial friction with the Federal Open Market Committee's (FOMC) institutional inertia. Most FOMC members will remain skeptical of arguments supporting current accommodative policy based on future AI productivity gains. The likely outcome is a series of compromise changes that "appear significant to Warsh but have limited substantive impact on other officials," such as discontinuing publication of the dot plot median or fine-tuning asset purchase composition.

September Rate Hike Probability Rises to 52%

CME FedWatch data shows that while July rate maintenance is already a consensus, the probability of a 25-basis-point rate hike in September has climbed to 52%. The 2-year Treasury yield is hovering around 4.18%. Goldman maintains its federal funds rate forecast at 3.50%-3.75% for full-year 2026, gradually declining to 3.00%-3.25% by 2027.

Treasury Secretary Scott Bessent has provided political backing for the Fed to hold steady, while simultaneously asserting that the Fed's inflation judgment is "fundamentally wrong" — core CPI rose only 0.2%, and the energy shock is merely transitory. "If they want to wait for more clarity, I understand," Bessent said. "But once the data is clear, rates should be cut significantly."

2. Nasdaq and S&P 500 Hit New Highs: 85% of Companies Beat Earnings

U.S. stocks closed mixed on Monday (July 21), but the Nasdaq and S&P 500 both reached new all-time highs. The Dow Jones Industrial Average edged down 19.12 points to 44,323.07 (-0.04%), the S&P 500 rose 0.14% to 6,305.6, and the Nasdaq Composite gained 0.38% to 20,974.17. All three major indices hit intraday highs, with the Nasdaq breaking above 21,000 for the first time.

Earnings season is peaking, with better-than-expected results driving the rally. According to FactSet, more than 85% of the 62 S&P 500 companies that have reported so far exceeded expectations. The "Magnificent Seven" tech giants are expected to post 14% year-over-year earnings growth in Q2, compared to just 3.4% for the other 493 S&P companies.

Goldman Sachs projects the S&P 500 will rise 10% to 6,900 over the next 12 months, supported by strong earnings performance and optimism around 2026 growth. HSBC's Chief Multi-Asset Strategist Max Kettner also believes "stocks, especially U.S. stocks, still have room to run higher."

Major tech stocks mostly rose, with Google up over 2%, and Meta, Broadcom, and Amazon each gaining more than 1%. Precious metals and mining sectors led gains, with Century Aluminum surging over 9%. Block shares jumped over 7% ahead of its inclusion in the S&P 500 on July 23.

3. Gold Breaks Above $3,400: COMEX Rises 1.55% to $3,410

Gold prices surged on Monday, with COMEX gold futures closing up 1.55% at $3,410.3 per ounce. COMEX silver futures rose 2.02% to $39.24 per ounce. The precious metal's strong performance reflects its renewed status as a preferred safe-haven asset.

Analysts attribute the rally to multiple factors: escalating U.S.-Iran conflict keeping Middle East geopolitical risk elevated; cooling June U.S. inflation data creating uncertainty about the Fed's tightening path; and weak consumer spending weighing on the economic outlook. The Conference Board's Leading Economic Index (LEI) fell 0.2% to 99.1 in June, with weak consumer expectations and declining building permits as primary drags.

4. Oil Markets Swing Wildly: Brent Spikes to $91 Then Plunges to $86.67

Oil prices experienced extreme volatility on Monday, reversing sharply from intraday highs after Iran signaled the possibility of renewed negotiations.

Early in the session, crude hit one-month highs — Brent surged 3.8% to $91.42 per barrel and WTI climbed to $86.40. However, Iranian Foreign Ministry spokesman Esmaeil Baghaei stated that mediators continued exchanging information with Iran and that negotiations could proceed based on national interests. Reports of a proposed 10-day ceasefire triggered a dramatic selloff.

Brent crude settled 1.6% lower at $86.67 per barrel, while WTI fell 2.2% to around $80. "Traders have trimmed some of the geopolitical risk premium accumulated recently," said Daniela Hathorn, Senior Market Analyst at Capital.com. "While the conflict is far from resolved, the prospect of renewed talks has alleviated immediate concerns about further supply disruption and Strait of Hormuz shipping."

Still, Brent remains more than $16 above the lows hit in June when the U.S.-Iran memorandum of understanding was signed. Stephen Innes, Managing Partner at SPI Asset Management, noted that the modest pullback "doesn't mean markets are letting their guard down," but rather that investors view the conflict as an event they can "cognitively ringfence."

5. Korea's KOSPI Surges 4.7%, Triggering Circuit Breaker

South Korea's KOSPI index extended its rebound on Tuesday, surging 4.7% to 6,827 points and triggering the Sidecar mechanism that halts program buying. SK Hynix jumped over 6.7% and Samsung Electronics gained over 7%. Morgan Stanley maintained its overweight rating on Korea with a 12-month KOSPI target of 12,500.

Korea's trade data for the first 20 days of July showed exports surging 52.3% year-over-year, with a trade surplus of $12.219 billion. The strong export performance was the primary driver of the market rally.

6. China A-Shares V-Shaped Rebound: Shanghai Composite Up 1.79%, STAR 50 Surges 10.73%

China's A-share markets staged a dramatic V-shaped reversal on July 21. After opening higher and initially plunging, major indices rallied sharply. The Shanghai Composite closed up 1.79% at 3,864.37, the Shenzhen Component rose 4.81% to 14,264.29, the ChiNext surged 7.05% to 3,685.97, and the STAR 50 index skyrocketed 10.73% to 1,903.16. Total两市 turnover reached 2.96 trillion yuan, with over 3,100 stocks rising and more than 100 hitting the daily limit up.

The semiconductor supply chain led the rally, with multiple semiconductor equipment ETFs hitting the daily limit. State capital and insurance funds jointly stabilized the market — five major insurers collectively pledged to increase equity holdings, with 50 billion yuan from state capital and 10 billion yuan from insurers. Citigroup upgraded Chinese equities to "overweight," citing global capital rotation toward China.
