⚡ July 18 Geopolitical Update: US Strikes Iran for 7th Consecutive Night, Iran Retaliates Against Kuwait Jordan Bahrain, Trump Delays Massive Offensive

1. US Completes 7th Consecutive Night of Strikes on Iran

US Central Command (CENTCOM) announced it completed its seventh consecutive night of strikes against Iran on Friday, July 17, at 9:30pm ET (01:30 GMT July 18). The command stated that "US forces employed fighter aircraft, aerial drones, and warships, in addition to other assets, to hit surveillance sites, military logistics infrastructure, underground weapons storage, and maritime capabilities."

CENTCOM emphasized that it continues to "hold Iran accountable at the Commander in Chief's direction while fully enforcing a naval blockade against Iranian ports," adding that "more than 50,000 American service members are operating across the Middle East and remain vigilant, lethal, and ready."

Iranian state news agency IRNA reported explosions in the cities of Sirik, Ahvaz, and Yazd. The provincial government of Hormozgan confirmed that a tunnel had been closed due to enemy attacks and that two bridges on key transport routes were also struck. Local authorities reported three people killed and eight wounded in the attacks. A missile also hit the maritime navigation control tower on Larak Island.

According to Iranian media, US strikes also hit Iranshahr Airport (in Sistan-Baluchestan province bordering Pakistan), Bandar Abbas railway station, and a key bridge connecting Bandar Abbas to Shiraz. Video verified by Reuters showed rubble, broken railings, and a damaged vehicle on a smashed bridge in Bandar Khamir.

2. Iran Launches Broad Retaliation: Strikes US Bases in Kuwait, Jordan, Destroys Bahrain's AI Center

In response to US strikes, Iran's army and the Islamic Revolutionary Guard Corps (IRGC) launched multi-directional retaliatory attacks on Friday:

  • Kuwait: Iranian forces struck multiple US military targets including an ammunition depot at Al-Adiri Camp, headquarters buildings and ammunition depots at Ali Al-Salem Base, and several communication bridges. Kuwait's Defense Ministry reported that several Kuwait Land Force personnel were wounded in Iranian drone strikes on army facilities. A power generation and water desalination plant was also hit, causing a fire and disrupting multiple electricity-generating units.
  • Jordan: Iranian forces targeted fuel tanks at the Al-Azraq Base. Separately, Axios reported, citing a US official, that Iran fired a ballistic missile at a US base in Saudi Arabia — the first direct Iranian attack on the kingdom in four months.
  • Bahrain: The IRGC said it destroyed a US unmanned surface vessel depot with multiple ballistic missiles and drones, with many vessels burned. Bahrain's "main artificial intelligence center" was also completely destroyed — Iran accused the US of using the center "to assist the enemy in target selection for committing war crimes."

The IRGC warned that if the US continues targeting bridges and transportation infrastructure in Iran, it would strike "the most important industrial, information technology and artificial intelligence assets of companies with American shareholders" across countries hosting US military bases in the region.

3. Strait of Hormuz Crisis Deepens: Tanker Explosion Dispute, Four Ships Intercepted, MQ-9 Shot Down

The situation around the Strait of Hormuz deteriorated sharply on Friday. Key developments:

Oil Tanker Explosion Rival Claims

The IRGC claimed that two oil tankers exploded and caught fire after attempting to pass through a minefield south of the Strait of Hormuz, accusing "deceptive American intelligence agencies" of luring the vessels into the danger zone. The IRGC warned that the strait remains "completely closed" and that oil, gas, and chemical fertilizer exports from the region would remain blocked until US military operations cease.

However, CENTCOM categorically denied the claim on X (formerly Twitter): "Like most IRGC claims, this is false." The post featured a comparison graphic showing the IRGC's claim on the left and "FACT: This is false" on the right.

Four Violating Ships Intercepted

The IRGC Navy announced it had "stopped" four ships attempting to transit the Strait of Hormuz during a combined missile and drone operation. The vessels were alleged to be attempting passage with US military support. The IRGC did not identify the ships but warned owners not to rely on "baseless support" from the US military.

Iran Shoots Down US MQ-9 Reaper Drone

The IRGC said its newly deployed advanced air defense system shot down a US MQ-9 Reaper drone over Bushehr province. The drone was reportedly engaged moments after entering Bushehr airspace. Separately, Iran's navy fired a shore-to-sea cruise missile at what it called a "hostile" US vessel in the northern Indian Ocean, forcing the ship to move out of range.

Saudi Arabia's civil defense issued early warnings for Al-Kharj city and Yanbu for "potential danger" but later declared the all-clear. UAE Foreign Minister Sheikh Abdullah bin Zayed Al Nahyan confirmed that Iran did not strike UAE territory, following rumors that Tehran was expanding its target list.

4. Iran Warns: 2-3 More Days of US Attacks Will Trigger "Offensive and Destructive" Phase

Mohsen Rezaei, military adviser to Iran's Supreme Leader Mojtaba Khamenei, issued a stark warning in an interview with state-run IRIB TV. Rezaei declared that the policy of "both war and negotiation" has reached a dead end, and if US attacks continue for another two to three days, Iran's armed forces will transition beyond "deterrence and retaliation" into a phase of "offense and complete destruction."

"At that point, US bases and soldiers beyond political borders will be directly targeted, and no political border will be safe," Rezaei said. "The United States must wait for expanded waves of missile and drone attacks."

Rezaei accused the United States of violating the terms of the Iran-US memorandum of understanding, citing Israel's failure to withdraw from southern Lebanon, US creation of illegal shipping lanes in the Strait of Hormuz, disrespect for Iranian sovereignty, and failure to unfreeze Iranian assets. He called on the people of Kuwait, Jordan, the UAE, and Qatar to help prevent further escalation.

IRGC Aerospace Force Commander Majid Mousavi also stated that "targeted strikes from across Iran against the enemy will continue" until the US halts operations against Iranian coastal facilities around the Strait of Hormuz.

UN Secretary-General António Guterres expressed concern over the escalation, particularly regarding "attacks on civilian infrastructure in Iran and across the region." The International Maritime Organization (IMO) called on all parties to exercise "maximum restraint and de-escalation."

5. Trump Delays Massive Offensive at Request of Qatar, Saudi, UAE Leaders

According to Axios and multiple media reports, the Trump administration informed Israel it was sending "dozens more" refueling aircraft ahead of a possible expansion of military operations. Axios reported, citing US officials, that President Trump is considering a "massive offensive" in Iran including strikes on power plants and nuclear sites, with escalation possible "in the coming days."

However, the situation took a dramatic turn on Saturday. Trump himself stated that the US had planned to launch a "very significant" military strike on Iran on Sunday (July 19), but decided to temporarily delay operations for "two to three days" at the request of the leaders of Qatar, Saudi Arabia, and the UAE. The decision was communicated to Israel and other Middle Eastern allies. Trump said he "does not want to take action" but "may have no choice."

Diplomatic channels remain active. Iran's Foreign Ministry spokesperson Baghaei confirmed that Iran has transmitted its latest negotiation text to the US through Pakistani mediation. Iranian President Pezeshkian stated that negotiations "do not mean surrender" and that Iran will negotiate on the basis of preserving dignity, strength, and national rights. However, a senior US official said the White House believes Iran's latest proposal contains no substantive improvements — only "cosmetic adjustments."

In a significant institutional development, Iran announced on Saturday the establishment of a "Persian Gulf Strait Authority" to manage Strait of Hormuz affairs. The IRGC also claimed that, under the 1982 UN Convention on the Law of the Sea, it can impose licensing, regulation, and fees on submarine fiber-optic cables passing through the strait. Analysts view this as Iran building an institutional framework for long-term control of the waterway.

6. Market Impact: Brent Nears $88, Global Shipping Paralyzed

The escalating US-Iran conflict continues to reverberate across global markets. Brent crude oil closed at $88.10/barrel on Friday, gaining 12% for the week. Shipping tracking data showed only eight vessels transited the Strait of Hormuz on Thursday, down from 13 the previous day — effectively paralyzing the world's most vital energy chokepoint. Global equity markets came under pressure as risk aversion grew, with the S&P 500 falling on Friday and Asian and European markets also feeling the impact.

Trump has previously threatened "broad-based air strikes" on Iranian infrastructure and has not ruled out a ground assault on Iran's coast or islands. Analysts warn that if the situation continues to deteriorate and the Strait of Hormuz remains closed for an extended period, global oil supplies could face the most severe crisis in decades, with Brent potentially breaking $110 in the fourth quarter.
