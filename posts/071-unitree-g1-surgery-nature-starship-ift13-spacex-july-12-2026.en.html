🏥 World First: Unitree G1 Humanoid Robot Performs Remote Surgery on Live Pigs, Published in Nature

A team from the University of California San Diego (UCSD) has achieved a world first — using a Unitree G1 general-purpose humanoid robot to successfully perform remote-controlled laparoscopic gallbladder removal surgery on live pigs. The breakthrough was published in the prestigious journal Nature.

The paper's first and corresponding author is Zekai Liang, a Chinese PhD student born in the 2000s, who graduated from Huazhong University of Science and Technology in 2023 and is now pursuing his doctorate at UCSD specializing in surgical robotics.

From Dancing Robot to Surgeon: Meet "Surgie"

The Unitree G1 humanoid robot — originally known for its agility in dancing and boxing — stands approximately 1.5 meters tall and weighs about 27 kg. The research team fitted it with custom adapters allowing it to grip standard laparoscopic surgical instruments, nicknaming it "Surgie."

The experiment comprised two live pig gallbladder surgeries:

  • First surgery: A single G1 robot operated under remote human control with an assistant, completing the procedure in 56 minutes with no major complications
  • Second surgery: Two G1 robots collaborated without any human intervention — one holding the endoscope as "eyes" and the other manipulating surgical instruments — finishing in just 32 minutes
"General-purpose humanoid robots can perform surgery at a fraction of the cost of specialized systems." — The research team in their Nature paper

The Potential for Medical Robotics Revolution

The study emphasizes this is not autonomous AI surgery but rather a human-guided remote operation — the robot serves as a "remote physical extension" of the surgeon. The breakthrough's significance lies in:

  • Dramatic cost reduction: Compared to da Vinci surgical systems costing millions, general-purpose humanoids offer a fundamentally cheaper alternative
  • Rapid deployment: Can adapt to existing hospital instruments and operating rooms without specialized renovations
  • Breaking geographic barriers: Could enable specialist surgeons in major cities to remotely support rural clinics, battlefield hospitals, or even space missions

Michael Yip, senior author and professor at UCSD's Department of Electrical and Computer Engineering, noted: "Teleoperated and future autonomous humanoid robots have the potential to expand healthcare coverage, especially in areas with doctor shortages or limited medical resources."

However, the team acknowledged this remains a proof-of-concept. The robot's movements were noticeably slower than human surgeons, and minor bile leakage and liver bed bleeding were observed in the second surgery. Significant regulatory, safety, and ethical hurdles remain before clinical application in humans.

🚀 SpaceX Announces Starship IFT-13: July 16 Launch with First 20 Starlink V3 Satellites

SpaceX officially announced that Starship's 13th Integrated Flight Test (IFT-13) is targeting liftoff as early as Thursday, July 16, from Starbase in Texas. The 90-minute launch window opens at 5:45 PM CDT (6:45 AM Beijing time on July 17).

First Commercial Payload Deployment: Starlink V3's "Generational Leap"

The biggest highlight of IFT-13 is that Starship will deploy 20 next-generation Starlink V3 satellites — the first time a Starship mission carries real commercial payloads. Six of the satellites are specially modified with camera systems to capture footage of Starship's heat shield and transmit images in real-time.

Compared to the current V2 Mini satellites, V3 represents a generational upgrade:

  • Per-satellite capacity: 1 Tbps, over 10x that of V2
  • Uplink speed: 160 Gbps, 24x that of V2
  • Latency: Expected to drop from 40-50ms to under 20ms (with laser inter-satellite links)
  • Physical size: Each satellite exceeds 2 tons, over 3x the mass of V2 Mini; solar array wingspan ~60 meters
  • Maximum capacity: This test carries 20 satellites; Starship can carry up to 60 at full load

Starship V3 and Super Heavy V3 Fly Together for the First Time

IFT-13 will use the next-generation Starship V3 paired with Super Heavy V3. On July 10, SpaceX completed a static fire test of Booster 20 with all 33 Raptor 3 engines firing for approximately 25 seconds. SpaceX has addressed engine ignition timing issues and secondary ignition anomalies observed during the 12th test flight.

Elon Musk noted that this test will gather critical data for the 14th flight's planned "chopsticks" mechanical arm at-sea booster recovery.

🔬 Nvidia Unveils RoboLab: A Standardized Benchmarking Platform for Robot Policy Evaluation

Nvidia Research has announced RoboLab, a simulation-based benchmarking platform designed to address fundamental challenges in evaluating general-purpose robot policies and foundation models (RFMs).

As RFMs gain rapid traction in 2026, traditional evaluation methods have lagged behind, suffering from benchmark saturation and limited diagnostic insight. RoboLab tackles these issues with:

  • Task diversity: 120 curated tasks spanning visual recognition, procedural reasoning, and relational logic
  • Deep diagnostics: Tracks partial task completion, motion smoothness (via SPARC Spectral Arc-Length), and granular failure events
  • Robot-agnostic design: Evaluates policies across different robot embodiments and architectures
  • Stress testing: Assesses performance under increasing language instruction complexity, scene clutter, and multi-step task horizons

Initial RoboLab features will integrate with Nvidia's open-source Isaac Lab-Arena platform in August 2026. The launch comes as competitors like Generalist AI and Mind Robotics have each raised $400M this year for robotic intelligence — making a standardized evaluation framework increasingly essential for the industry.
