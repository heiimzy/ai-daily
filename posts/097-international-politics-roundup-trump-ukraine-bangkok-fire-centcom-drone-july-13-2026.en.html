⚡ July 13 World Politics Roundup: Ukraine Destroys 90 Russian Vessels in a Week, Trump Shifts Stance on Ukraine After Intelligence, Bangkok Bar Fire Kills 27, US Deploys Suicide Drones Against Iran

Ukraine's Drone Forces Destroy 90 Russian Ships in One Week

Ukraine's Unmanned Systems Forces (USF) struck 90 Russian vessels in the Sea of Azov between July 6 and July 12, according to commander Robert "Madyar" Brovdi. The tally includes tankers, tugboats, ferries, and dry cargo ships — an average of one strike every 112 minutes throughout the week.

The General Staff of the Armed Forces of Ukraine reported that overnight on July 11-12, Ukrainian forces hit 10 Russian oil tankers and four ferries in the Sea of Azov. The tankers are used to transport Russian oil and petroleum products to evade international sanctions, while the ferries support Russian military logistics.

Russian officials confirmed the incidents. Rostov Region Governor Yuri Slyusar reported a drone attack on a tanker in the Don-Azov Canal, saying the vessel was empty, the fire was contained, and there were no casualties. He added that Russian air defenses intercepted 20 Ukrainian drones in the Rostov region.

The maritime campaign coincided with strikes on Russian energy infrastructure. Ukraine's General Staff reported that defense forces struck the Syzran oil refinery in Russia's Samara region on July 11 and early July 12. The facility has an annual capacity of 8.5 million tons of oil, producing gasoline, diesel, and aviation fuel for the Russian Armed Forces.

Trump's Rhetoric on Ukraine Shifts After Intel Briefing

US President Donald Trump has altered his stance on Ukraine after reviewing intelligence reports detailing Kyiv's expanding long-range strike capabilities, according to The Wall Street Journal. Trump has been impressed by the effectiveness of Ukraine's domestically-produced long-range drones striking deep inside Russia — a stark contrast to his February 2025 Oval Office comment that Ukraine had "no cards" to play.

European officials at the recent NATO summit in Ankara noted a markedly different tone from Trump, describing his conduct as "decent and serious." The most tangible outcome: Trump announced on the summit sidelines that the US would grant Ukraine a license to domestically manufacture Patriot interceptor missiles.

"Ukraine has completely destroyed the very idea that Russia has a strategic rear. Yesterday, Ukrainian drones broke through Russia's defenses and hit a Russian oil refinery in Siberia. This is a new reality — there is not a single large oil refinery left in Russia that Ukraine would not hit." — President Zelensky at the NATO Ankara Summit

Orysia Lutsevych, deputy director of Chatham House's Russia and Eurasia Programme, noted that the Patriot production license could eventually neutralize Russia's ballistic missile attacks. Some European officials indicated Trump is considering expanding military aid to help Ukrainian forces break through entrenched Russian defenses.

Bangkok Bar Fire: 27 Dead, 63 Injured, Emergency Exits Blocked

A devastating fire at the Rong Beer Na Lat Phrao bar and restaurant in Bangkok's Chatuchak district has killed at least 27 people and injured 63 others, with 22 in critical condition. The blaze broke out at 11:57 PM local time on Sunday (July 12). Victims included 9 men and 18 women, with many bodies found near the rear restroom area.

According to a band member at the venue, smoke suddenly filled the room after the lights briefly went out, followed by a massive explosion and fire. The flames spread rapidly through the ceiling toward the front entrance, forcing customers to rush to the back. Bangkok Governor Chadchart Sittipunt said both emergency exits were obstructed — one by beer crates near the kitchen, the other by a table.

"The fire was not that aggressive, but the smoke had engulfed 100% of the venue," said firefighter Chakrit Khongkom, 45. "Most of the survivors were choking on smoke." A passing driver who spotted the fire at 11:30 PM broke a window and helped rescue two people before firefighters arrived.

Thai Prime Minister Anutin Charnvirakul visited the scene and confirmed 27 bodies had been recovered. Bangkok authorities have opened a coordination center for victims' families and ordered urgent inspections of business licenses and fire-escape systems citywide. So far, 10 victims have been identified — nine Thai nationals and one from Laos.

US Deploys One-Way Attack Sea Drones Against Iran for First Time

US Central Command (CENTCOM) released detailed operational footage and reports of its latest wave of strikes against Iran on Sunday. The strikes began at 5:00 PM ET on July 12, targeting Iranian air defense systems, coastal radar installations, missile and drone launch sites, and small attack boats.

In a historical first, the US military deployed one-way attack sea drones (suicide drone boats) in combat operations. CENTCOM stated that fighter jets, naval vessels, one-way attack aerial drones, and the newly deployed sea drones struck dozens of targets across multiple locations. Over the past three nights, US forces have hit more than 300 Iranian military targets.

Iran's Islamic Revolutionary Guard Corps retaliated by claiming attacks on two US military bases in Kuwait — the Ali Al-Salem Air Base (fuel storage and Patriot systems reportedly "completely destroyed") and the Ahmed Al-Jaber Air Base (FPS radar system damaged). Iran called the operation the third phase of its "Eye for an Eye" campaign and warned the US against further interference in the Strait of Hormuz.

CENTCOM reiterated that Iran does not control the Strait of Hormuz and that the US remains positioned to ensure freedom of navigation. Brent crude futures rose over 3% to $78.35/barrel during Asian trading on Monday.
