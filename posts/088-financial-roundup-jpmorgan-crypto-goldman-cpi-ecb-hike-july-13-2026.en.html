⚡ July 13 Financial Roundup: JPMorgan Turns Bearish on Crypto, Goldman Flags CPI as Key Risk, ECB's Nagel Hints at Rate Hike, MicroStrategy Stock Craters 70%

1. JPMorgan Turns Cautious on Digital Assets, Advises MicroStrategy to Rebuild Dollar Reserves

JPMorgan has officially shifted its stance on digital assets from "overweight" to "cautious" in its "Alternative Investments Outlook and Strategy" report published July 13, marking a significant Wall Street pivot on cryptocurrencies.

The analysis team led by managing director Nikolaos Panigirtzoglou noted that MicroStrategy's recent sale of 32 Bitcoin — even if "symbolic and voluntary" — has "spooked the market." The company's current dollar reserves cover only approximately 6.3 months of dividend payments, and analysts believe rebuilding those reserves is critical to restoring investor confidence.

MicroStrategy currently holds 843,706 Bitcoin at an average cost of $75,699 per coin, representing a paper loss of approximately $11.5 billion at current prices around $63,000. JPMorgan also expects MicroStrategy to purchase roughly $32 billion in Bitcoin this year, up from approximately $22 billion in both 2025 and 2024.

"In our opinion, a rebuilding of the company's dollar reserves might be needed to restore confidence and reduce investor concerns that the company would sell more Bitcoins to cover dividend payments." — JPMorgan Alternative Investments Outlook and Strategy

Key bearish factors cited by JPMorgan include:

  • Bitcoin below production cost: BTC has traded below its estimated production cost of ~$87,000 for most of the year, rendering the historical "soft floor" ineffective
  • Capital flows halved: Total digital asset inflows of ~$22 billion YTD imply an annualized pace of ~$52 billion, roughly half of 2025 levels
  • Regulatory uncertainty: Passage odds for the Clarity Act have fallen below 50% as U.S. midterm elections approach
  • Institutional preference for private chains: Traditional finance favors permissioned blockchains over public networks, potentially causing structural value loss

2. Goldman Warns: June CPI Is the Biggest Near-Term Risk for U.S. Equities

Goldman Sachs has flagged the June 2026 CPI release (Tuesday, July 14) as "the single most consequential near-term variable" for U.S. equity markets, warning that even a modest upside surprise could trigger a significant repricing.

Goldman's core CPI estimate stands at approximately 0.17% month-over-month, below consensus. If shelter or core services re-accelerate above that level, rate expectations could shift and growth stock valuations could face immediate pressure.

Options Markets Price Volatility

Options market pricing implies an S&P 500 move of approximately 0.8% on CPI day itself, widening to 1.1% by week's end — both above the 12-month average for CPI-day volatility. The 50 basis points of cumulative tightening currently priced into markets through mid-2027 is Goldman's identified central risk variable:

  • Hot CPI: Tightening expectations rise beyond 50 bps → valuations compress, rate-sensitive names sell off
  • Soft CPI: Priced-in tightening partially unwinds → relief rally, growth stocks recover

Fed Chair Kevin Warsh will also deliver his first semiannual congressional testimony on July 14-15, adding another layer of monetary policy uncertainty to an already packed week.

3. MicroStrategy Stock Plunges 70%, Standard Chartered Demands Strategy Clarity

Following MicroStrategy's $216 million Bitcoin sale, its common stock has cratered to $94.64 — a 70% collapse from its 52-week high of $457.22. Standard Chartered published a client note on July 13 urging founder Michael Saylor to clarify the company's shift away from its "never sell" Bitcoin accumulation model.

Geoff Kendrick, Global Head of Digital Assets Research at Standard Chartered, noted that the capital reallocation transitions Bitcoin reserves from a pure holding vehicle into active collateral to fund a newly increased 12% preferred stock dividend and replenish $2.55 billion in dollar reserves. However, the abrupt departure from the long-standing "HODL" mantra has confused both retail and institutional backers.

"Saylor's cryptic social media signaling is actively muddying the waters and pressuring Bitcoin in the near term." — Geoff Kendrick, Standard Chartered Global Head of Digital Assets Research

Despite the turmoil, Standard Chartered maintains its $100,000 year-end Bitcoin target, viewing current friction as "a messaging failure rather than a structural collapse." MicroStrategy's year-to-date Bitcoin purchase pace implies ~$32 billion in annual acquisitions.

4. ECB's Nagel Warns: Rate Hike Possible if Iran Conflict Intensifies Inflation

European Central Bank Governing Council member and Bundesbank President Joachim Nagel issued a hawkish warning: if price pressures intensify further due to the Iran conflict, the ECB will need to consider raising interest rates next month.

The warning comes amid ongoing Strait of Hormuz tensions. Iran's Revolutionary Guards have declared the waterway closed indefinitely, and while Brent crude has eased to ~$76/barrel, it remains about 6% above pre-escalation levels. Several ECB officials have recently emphasized that the inflation fight is "far from over," with Stournaras even stating the bank is "back to square one" on inflation.

Markets currently price in over 30 basis points of additional ECB tightening this year. Nagel's comments reinforce that expectation, raising the probability of a September rate move if Middle East tensions persist.

5. Key Financial Calendar This Week

  • Monday (Jul 13): OPEC meeting on production quotas; Fed Governors Bowman & Waller speak; U.S. Federal Budget data
  • Tuesday (Jul 14): U.S. June CPI (core CPI est. 0.17% MoM); China June trade data; Warsh House testimony
  • Wednesday (Jul 15): China Q2 GDP + June activity data; Warsh Senate testimony
  • Thursday (Jul 16): TSMC Q2 earnings (guided $39.0-40.2B revenue); U.S. June PPI
  • Friday (Jul 17): University of Michigan sentiment & inflation expectations

This is one of the most consequential economic data weeks of summer 2026. With the S&P 500's forward P/E at 20.1x — above both 5-year and 10-year averages — valuations have little buffer against any hawkish surprise. CPI and the Warsh testimony will set the market's tone for the next month.
