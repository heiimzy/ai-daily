⚡ July 21 International Politics Deep Dive: US Strikes Iran for 10th Straight Night, Tanker Attacked in Hormuz, Iran Declares Full-Scale War, Houthis Blockade Saudi Arabia, Ukraine Requests $6B at Ramstein

1. US-Iran Conflict Night 10: Another Tanker Attacked in Strait of Hormuz

Iran attacked a tanker in the Strait of Hormuz early Tuesday, forcing its crew to abandon the ship, even as the United States conducted its 10th consecutive night of airstrikes targeting the Islamic Republic, the Associated Press reported. The 10 consecutive nights of U.S. airstrikes have not compelled Tehran to loosen its grip on the strait, through which about a fifth of all crude oil and natural gas once passed in peacetime.

U.S. Central Command announced a new wave of strikes early Monday (July 20), targeting "Iranian military command centers, air defense facilities, coastal surveillance sites, naval forces, missile and drone launch positions, and communications networks." Iranian state media reported explosions heard in southern and northwestern Iran.

The British military reported that a vessel caught fire near the Omani coast in the Strait of Hormuz early Monday. Iran's paramilitary Revolutionary Guard subsequently claimed it was attacking tankers in the strait. Brent crude rose above $90 per barrel on Monday, amplifying the global energy crisis caused by the conflict.

2. Trump Vows Iran Will 'Pay Many Times Over' While Rubio Keeps Diplomatic Door Open

President Donald Trump posted on Truth Social: "Every time Iran kills an American soldier, they will pay many times over for it!" He added that the directive had been conveyed to Defense Secretary Pete Hegseth, Joint Chiefs Chairman General C.Q. Brown, and "every leader" in the military.

U.S. Central Command confirmed on July 18 that two U.S. service members were killed in Jordan by Iranian ballistic missiles and drone attacks, with one missing. The next day, another American service member died in northern Iraq while disposing of a downed Iranian drone. Nearly 100 U.S. personnel have been wounded by Iranian attacks this month, bringing total U.S. military fatalities since the war began on February 28 to 17.

Secretary of State Marco Rubio told reporters the U.S. remains open to negotiations with Iran but "must be realistic." "We will continue to respond. If the diplomatic door opens — if those who want to do something constructive for Iran prevail and take control of the system, or take control of the negotiations — that would be a very positive development. Unfortunately, tonight we are not there yet," Rubio said.

Iranian Foreign Ministry spokesman Esmail Baghaei said Iran makes war and diplomacy decisions based on its national interests. Iranian authorities reported at least 50 killed and 517 wounded in recent rounds of U.S. strikes.

3. Iran's President Pezeshkian Declares 'Full-Scale War'

Iranian President Masoud Pezeshkian has officially declared that the country is in a state of "full-scale war" with the United States, according to Global Times reporting on July 21. This marks the strongest rhetoric yet from Iranian leadership and represents a significant redefinition of the conflict's nature by Tehran.

Meanwhile, Tehran continues retaliating by striking U.S. allies across the Middle East. Bahrain sounded missile alarms Monday morning, while Kuwait reported its air defense systems intercepting incoming Iranian missile salvos. The U.S. reimposed a maritime blockade on Iranian ports last week, with the military reporting it had intercepted six vessels and disabled one since implementation.

Trump has threatened to strike Iran's power plants and bridges to force Tehran to loosen its grip on the Strait of Hormuz. Recent strikes suggest the U.S. military is executing this plan. Iranian provincial officials reported that bridges, railways, airports, communication towers, and water desalination plants have been hit by U.S. airstrikes, including six bridges destroyed in Hormozgan province and 116 communication towers damaged.

4. Houthis Announce Comprehensive Maritime Blockade of Saudi Arabia

Yemen's Houthi movement announced a comprehensive maritime blockade of Saudi Arabia on Monday (July 20), threatening the alternative export route via the Red Sea. Saudi Arabia pumps millions of barrels of oil daily through a pipeline to a Red Sea export terminal, which has served as a crucial relief valve for global crude markets during the U.S.-Iran war.

The Houthi action comes at Iran's request. Tehran had instructed the Houthis to blockade the Bab al-Mandab Strait — connecting the Red Sea to the Gulf of Aden — in response to continued U.S. strikes on Iranian energy infrastructure. Combined with Iran's effective blockade of the Strait of Hormuz, up to 25% of global oil and gas transportation is now at risk.

A senior Houthi official previously told Iranian state television that the group could close the strait if Saudi Arabia continued its attacks. Iran's Revolutionary Guard previously warned: "Energy exports from this region will either be shared by all, or denied to all."

5. Ramstein 29th Meeting: Ukraine's New Defense Chief Requests $6 Billion

The Ukraine Defense Contact Group (Ramstein format) held its 29th meeting on July 21 — the first for new Ukrainian Defense Minister Denys Shmyhal. He called on partners for continued support, stating Ukraine needs $6 billion to cover this year's procurement deficit.

Shmyhal emphasized that Patriot air defense systems and interceptor missiles are "critically important for protecting Ukrainian cities." The funding would enable Ukraine to build more FPV drones, interceptor drones to counter Shahed drones, and additional long-range weapons.

The meeting was co-chaired by the UK and Germany, with participants including U.S. Defense Secretary Pete Hegseth, NATO Secretary General Mark Rutte, and NATO's new commander in Europe, Alexus Grynkievich. Previously, President Trump announced strict secondary tariffs on Russia and its allies if a peace agreement is not reached within 50 days. However, recent large-scale Russian attacks indicate Moscow is using this time to inflict more casualties.

Ukraine continues striking deep into Russian territory. President Zelensky confirmed Ukrainian forces hit two logistics facilities in Moscow and Tambov regions, plus six Russian vessels in the Black Sea. Unmanned Systems Forces Commander Robert Brovdi reported striking three tankers and four dry cargo ships as part of Operation "MoLoCHKa."

6. Lithuania Warns Putin Seeks New Targets in Baltics as Ukraine War Stalls

Lithuanian Defense Minister Robertas Kaunas warned that the Kremlin, struggling in Ukraine, is seeking fresh escalation and sees the Baltic countries and Poland as the "closest targets," in an interview with LRT public broadcaster published July 20.

"The Kremlin, Putin needs a new escalation, some kind of new victory, and in this case the Baltic region and Poland are the closest targets," Kaunas said. Recent weeks saw leaders from Poland and Lithuania warn about potential Russian provocations against their countries.

NATO officials have not ruled out the possibility of a conventional Russian assault in coming years. German army chief Christian Freuding said it is "NATO-agreed intelligence" that Moscow will be ready to invade a member state as early as 2029. NATO Military Committee Chair Admiral Giuseppe Cavo Dragone told the Kyiv Independent that NATO is ready to respond: "Russia would lose a lot, much more than what they could gain just by touching Poland or the Baltic states."

These warnings come amid growing concerns about NATO unity as President Trump adopts a tougher stance toward European allies and his administration signals a review of the U.S. military presence in Europe.
