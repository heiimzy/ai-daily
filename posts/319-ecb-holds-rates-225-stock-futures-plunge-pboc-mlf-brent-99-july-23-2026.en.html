⚡ July 23 Financial Evening: ECB Holds at 2.25% but 73% Chance of September Hike; US Futures Plunge Over 1% as AI Capex Fears and Oil Surge Batter Sentiment; PBOC Front-Loads 500B Yuan MLF; Brent Crude Breaks $99

1. ECB Holds Rates at 2.25% Unanimously, Lagarde Leaves Door Open for September

The European Central Bank announced its July monetary policy decision at 14:45 CET (20:45 CST) on July 23, keeping all three key interest rates unchanged — the deposit facility rate at 2.25%, the main refinancing rate at 2.40%, and the marginal lending facility at 2.65%. The decision was unanimous and widely expected.

The ECB's statement noted: "The outlook for energy prices, while highly volatile, currently stands close to the baseline of the June Eurosystem staff projections and well above the levels recorded prior to the conflict in the Middle East. Uncertainty remains high and the full inflationary impact of the energy shock has yet to play out."

ECB President Christine Lagarde emphasized during the press conference that the Council will follow a "data-dependent, meeting-by-meeting approach" with no pre-committed rate path. She pushed back against characterizing June's 25bp hike as an "insurance move," insisting it was a necessary response to genuine inflation pressures.

Market pricing now implies a 73% probability of a rate hike at the ECB's September meeting, with a total of approximately 47 basis points of tightening expected by year-end. Lagarde had previously signaled at the Sintra forum that inflation is projected to return to the 2% target only in late 2027, and only if monetary policy tightens further.

The pause comes after eurozone inflation eased to 2.8% in June (from 3.2% in May), with core inflation slowing to 2.4%. However, the renewed surge in oil prices — Brent crude back above $90 — introduces fresh upside risk to the inflation outlook for H2 2026.

2. US Stock Futures Slide Sharply: Nasdaq -1.33% as AI Capex Anxiety Grips Markets

As of 09:00 AM ET (21:00 CST) on July 23, US equity futures had deepened their losses significantly:

  • Dow Jones Futures: 51,910, down 539 points (-1.03%)
  • S&P 500 Futures: 7,468, down 72.25 points (-0.96%)
  • Nasdaq 100 Futures: 28,792.5, down 388.75 points (-1.33%)

Three factors driving the sell-off

① Big Tech AI capex spooks investors

Alphabet (GOOGL) reported strong Q2 results — revenue of $119.8B (+24% YoY), net income of $112.1B (+298% YoY) — but raised its full-year 2026 capex guidance to $195B–$205B, 8% above the prior midpoint. Investors grew concerned about returns on massive AI infrastructure spending, sending the stock down over 4% in after-hours trading.

Tesla (TSLA) missed Q2 profit expectations by 35%, with free cash flow turning negative for the first time in two years. The company continues to invest heavily in AI, Optimus robots, and Robotaxis. TSLA fell over 5% after-hours.

IBM reported just 1% revenue growth and cut full-year guidance, with Z-series mainframe sales plunging 42%.

② Oil surge reignites inflation fears

Brent crude broke above $99/barrel, WTI topped $91 — six-week highs. Rising energy costs feed directly into inflation expectations, squeezing corporate margins and threatening equity valuations.

③ US Treasury yields at 2-year high

The 10-year Treasury yield reached 4.67%, its highest since January 2025. CME FedWatch data shows the probability of a Fed rate hike at the July meeting jumping from 10% a week ago to 24%, with September odds at 69-78%.

3. PBOC Front-Loads 500B Yuan MLF, Net Injection of 100B Yuan to Stabilize A-Share Market

On July 23 after the A-share market close, the People's Bank of China announced it would conduct a 500 billion yuan ($69.6B) 1-year Medium-term Lending Facility (MLF) operation on July 24, using a fixed-quantity, rate-bidding, multiple-price auction format. With 400 billion yuan in MLF maturing on July 27, this represents a net injection of 100 billion yuan.

The early announcement and pre-settlement (the original maturity was July 27) signals clear liquidity support intent. This is the 8,000th billion yuan the PBOC has injected this month through MLF and buy-out reverse repo operations combined:

  • July 3: Buy-out reverse repo net injection of 200B yuan
  • July 15: 6-month buy-out reverse repo net injection of 500B yuan
  • July 16-20: 7-day reverse repo operations scaled up significantly
  • July 23: MLF front-loaded net injection of 100B yuan

The move comes at a critical juncture for A-shares. The STAR 50 (科创50) index fell 3.78% today, with semiconductor stocks under heavy pressure (Hua Hong Semiconductor -12%). Total two-market turnover shrank to 2.2 trillion yuan, down 458 billion from the prior session. The PBOC's sustained liquidity injections provide a buffer against market volatility.

The PBOC's Q2 2026 Monetary Policy Committee meeting emphasized "continued implementation of moderately accommodative monetary policy with enhanced counter-cyclical adjustment." Analysts view the MLF front-loading as a targeted measure to stabilize market expectations amid the tech sector correction.

5. Commodities: Brent Approaches $100, Gold Pulls Back to $4,050

Oil prices extended their five-day rally as geopolitical risk premiums continued to expand:

  • Brent Crude: $99.60/barrel, up 5.88% — approaching the psychologically critical $100 level
  • WTI Crude: $91.24/barrel, up 5.08%
  • Gold Futures: $4,048.54/oz, down 1.99% — rate hike expectations dampen demand for non-yielding assets
  • Silver Futures: $57.35/oz, down 3.98%

The deteriorating Middle East situation remains the key driver of the oil rally. Houthi forces claimed attacks on two Saudi oil tankers in the Red Sea. Iran's Revolutionary Guards declared the Strait of Hormuz effectively closed, while the US completed its 12th consecutive night of strikes on Iran. President Trump threatened to destroy "one bridge or power plant" for every ship Iran attacks. Iran responded by threatening to cut off all oil flows from the Gulf region.

Goldman Sachs warned that the dual blockade of the Red Sea and Strait of Hormuz could disrupt up to 9 million barrels per day of oil shipments, of which roughly 4 million bpd would be difficult to reroute — representing nearly 9% of global oil supply.
