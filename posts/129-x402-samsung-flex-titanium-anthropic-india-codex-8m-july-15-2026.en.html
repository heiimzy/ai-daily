⚡ July 15 Tech Roundup: x402 Foundation Launches with 40 Members Including Visa, Mastercard, Google, AWS for AI Agent Payments; Samsung Unveils Flex Titanium Display; Anthropic Rolls Out India Pricing; OpenAI Codex Surges Past 8M Users

⚡ Linux Foundation Launches x402 Foundation: 40 Companies Unite for AI Agent Payment Standard

The Linux Foundation announced on July 15 the operational launch of the x402 Foundation, an open-governance consortium of 40 founding members dedicated to standardizing internet-native payments between software agents. The protocol activates the long-dormant HTTP 402 "Payment Required" status code — reserved since 1991 but never implemented at scale — allowing AI agents to complete microtransactions autonomously without human intervention.

The premier member roster reads like a who's who of the global payments and tech industry: Visa, Mastercard, American Express, Stripe, Adyen, Fiserv, Google, Amazon Web Services, Cloudflare, Shopify, Ripple, Circle, Coinbase, Solana Foundation, Stellar Development Foundation, and MoonPay. The x402 protocol was contributed by Coinbase to the Linux Foundation, ensuring vendor-neutral governance.

75 Million Transactions Validate the Market

According to x402 Foundation data, the protocol processed approximately 75 million transactions over the past 30 days, totaling $24 million in volume with an average transaction value of just $0.32. While this represents a fraction of Visa's $40 billion daily volume, the growth trajectory signals strong product-market fit in the machine-to-machine (M2M) micropayment niche — precisely the use case driving the emerging AI agent economy.

AI agents cannot open bank accounts, pass credit checks, or sign SaaS contracts, but they can sign blockchain transactions. Under x402, an agent requesting a paid resource receives an HTTP 402 response with machine-readable payment instructions — price, accepted stablecoin, blockchain network, and destination wallet. The agent signs a USDC transfer and retries the request with proof of payment attached. Total round-trip time: 2-4 seconds. Transaction cost: approximately $0.0001.

"AI agents are moving from concept to commercial reality, and payment standardization is the critical infrastructure determining the speed of this transformation." — Jim Zemlin, CEO, Linux Foundation

Google has already wired x402 into its Agent Payment Protocol (AP2), and Cloudflare ships native x402 support in its Agent Toolkit. SoftBank founder Masayoshi Son predicted that by 2040, roughly 100 trillion AI agents will be created — if each executes just one $0.32 transaction per day, daily volume would reach $32 trillion.

⚡ Samsung Unveils Flex Titanium: Foldable Display with 20x Stronger Alloy Film

Samsung Electronics on July 15 unveiled Flex Titanium, a breakthrough foldable display technology built on seven generations of foldable innovation. The technology will debut in the eighth-generation Galaxy Z Fold 8 and Galaxy Z Fold 8 Ultra, set to be officially unveiled at the Galaxy Unpacked event in London on July 22.

Titanium Material Revolution

Flex Titanium integrates two titanium-based components: a titanium-alloy film below the OLED panel measuring roughly one-third the thickness of a human hair, delivering 20 times greater mechanical rigidity than conventional polymer film; and a flexible titanium plate with micro-patterned holes in the folding section that provide robust support while maintaining flexibility. This eliminates air gaps between the display module and the support plate, enabling a slimmer, more durable foldable design with significantly reduced crease visibility.

"By introducing sophisticated micro-patterned holes to the folding section of the titanium plate, we have successfully secured flexibility with robust durability," said Kyung-Jin Yoo, EVP and Head of Mobile Display Product Development at Samsung Display. Combined with high-resolution architecture and next-generation organic materials, the new display delivers ultra-vivid resolution with reduced power consumption.

Notably, Samsung Display is a long-time Apple supplier, raising speculation that Flex Titanium technology could appear in the rumored foldable iPhone expected later this year.

⚡ Anthropic Launches India Rupee Pricing for Claude, Extends Fable 5 Free Access

Anthropic on July 15 officially rolled out localized rupee pricing for Claude AI subscriptions in India — its second-largest market globally, accounting for 5.8% of worldwide Claude usage. The move eliminates the friction of dollar-denominated transactions for Indian users.

Pricing Details

Claude Pro is priced at ₹2,000/month (annual billing, ~$21), slightly above the $17 US price due to local taxes. Claude Max starts at ₹11,999/month (~$125), and Team plans at ₹2,399/seat/month (~$25). However, UPI payment support is not yet available — users must pay by credit/debit card or through Apple/Google in-app billing.

Simultaneously, Anthropic extended the Claude Fable 5 free promotional access through July 19 and increased Claude Code weekly usage limits by 50% — widely interpreted as a competitive response to OpenAI's Codex milestone.

⚡ OpenAI Codex Surges Past 8M Weekly Active Users Driven by GPT-5.6 Sol

OpenAI's AI coding tool Codex is experiencing explosive growth. Since the launch of GPT-5.6 Sol on July 9, combined weekly active users for Codex and ChatGPT Work have surpassed 8 million, with weekly usage growing 2.5x in a single week and overall user base expanding 7x in five months.

OpenAI CEO Sam Altman confirmed the milestone on X on July 14. Codex engineering lead Tibo Sottiaux detailed the trajectory: fewer than 1 million weekly active users in February, 5 million by early June, then hypergrowth — 6 million by July 12, 7 million within 24 hours, and 8 million by Sunday.

Infrastructure Under Strain

The surge pushed OpenAI's infrastructure to its limits, with traffic reaching approximately 2x the previous peak within 48 hours of launch. OpenAI responded with multiple optimizations: reasoning improvements delivering ~10% more capacity per subscriber, context window reduction from 372K to 272K tokens to address billing issues, rollback of experimental "juice value" reasoning settings, and temporary removal of the 5-hour usage cap for Plus, Business, and Pro subscribers.

Community controversy erupted when users perceived GPT-5.6 Sol Max tier as "getting dumber," with investigations revealing the internal "juice value" reasoning budget had been cut from 960 to 128 during diagnostic experiments. Sottiaux confirmed the settings have been reverted and emphasized the changes were temporary engineering experiments, not permanent capability reductions.

The Codex growth surge is reshaping the AI coding competition. Within hours of OpenAI announcing the 7 million user milestone, Anthropic extended Claude Fable 5 promotional pricing and boosted Claude Code weekly limits by 50%. The battle for AI coding tool dominance is accelerating rapidly.
