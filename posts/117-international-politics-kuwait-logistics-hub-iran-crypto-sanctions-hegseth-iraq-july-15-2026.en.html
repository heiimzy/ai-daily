⚡ July 15 International Politics Roundup: IRGC Claims Kuwait Logistics Hub Destroyed, US Freezes $130M in Iran-Linked Crypto, Hegseth Pressures Iraq on Iran-Aligned Militias

IRGC Claims 4th Wave of Nasr 2 Destroyed US Logistics Hub in Kuwait

At 02:03 GMT on Wednesday, Iran's Islamic Revolutionary Guard Corps (IRGC) announced that the fourth wave of its "Nasr 2" operation had successfully struck and destroyed what it described as "the US military's main logistics and support centre in West Asia" in Mina Abdullah, Kuwait. In a statement carried by state broadcaster IRIB, the IRGC said the facility was set "ablaze and destroyed."

Kuwait's defense ministry confirmed it was responding to Iranian drone attacks, stating that loud sounds heard across the country were from air defense interceptions. The ministry reported that since late Tuesday, Kuwaiti forces had intercepted one ballistic missile, five cruise missiles, and 33 hostile drones. Four Kuwaiti navy personnel were wounded in Iranian attacks and are in stable condition after receiving medical treatment.

US Central Command (CENTCOM) Commander Admiral Brad Cooper condemned Iranian attacks on seven commercial ships over the past week, stating that Iran had "intentionally targeted civilians across the region," resulting in "nearly a dozen civilian crew members killed, missing, or injured." Cooper declared that "US forces are holding Iran accountable for unwarranted aggression that continues to endanger innocent lives."

Iran's 8th Saeqeh Wave Strikes Jordan's Azraq Base F-18 Positions

At 01:55 GMT, Iran's army announced the eighth phase of its "Saeqeh" operation, launching a new wave of drone attacks against "US military bases" in the region. State broadcaster IRIB reported that explosive drones targeted areas housing F-18 fighter jets and large equipment hangars at Jordan's al-Azraq Air Base for the second time.

Jordan had previously confirmed that its air defenses intercepted four Iranian missiles entering its airspace. Jordanian Foreign Minister and Deputy Prime Minister Ayman Safadi met with US Secretary of State Marco Rubio in Washington to discuss efforts to end the regional escalation.

Brent Crude Surges Past $86 as US Freezes $130M in Iran-Linked Crypto

Oil prices continued rising early Wednesday as President Donald Trump reimposed a naval blockade on all Iranian ports and Iran launched retaliatory strikes. Brent crude rose 1.72% to $86.19 a barrel as of 00:29 GMT, while US West Texas Intermediate (WTI) climbed 1.4% to $80.40 a barrel.

At 01:40 GMT, the US Treasury Department sanctioned "multiple wallets tied to the Central Bank of Iran, resulting in the freeze of over $130 million," Treasury Secretary Scott Bessent announced. Washington is "committed to disrupting and degrading Iran's illicit financial activities, including its abuse of digital assets," Bessent said. "We will continue to aggressively follow the money and deny the Iranian regime access to the proceeds of its illicit revenue schemes."

Hegseth Pressures Iraq at Pentagon: Disarm Iran-Aligned Militias

At 01:39 GMT, US Defense Secretary Pete Hegseth hosted Iraqi Prime Minister Ali al-Zaidi at the Pentagon and laid out Washington's conditions for a deeper partnership. In a post on X, Hegseth said Iraq "must assert its sovereignty and disarm the Iran-aligned militias" that he blames for more than 600 attacks on US personnel during the spring.

Following the meeting, Iraqi Prime Minister al-Zaidi announced that US forces would leave Iraq by September 30, ending a 23-year military presence. "US forces will be out of Iraq by September 30, while US companies will be inside Iraq," al-Zaidi said through an interpreter. President Trump, standing alongside him, commented: "We don't think we need the military there any more." Approximately 2,500 US troops are currently stationed in Iraq.

Schumer Refuses to Back Unauthorized War, Ilhan Omar Votes to Cut Israel Aid

At 01:20 GMT, US Senate Minority Leader Chuck Schumer publicly denounced Trump for dragging the nation deeper into conflict with Iran. "Donald Trump is dragging America deeper into a war in Iran with no authorization, no plan, and no exit strategy. Democrats will not go along," Schumer wrote on X.

Shortly after, Congresswoman Ilhan Omar announced she would vote yes on an amendment to block $3.3 billion in military aid to Israel. "It is unconscionable we continue to fund this genocidal apartheid regime. Voting YES," Omar wrote on X. House Minority Leader Hakeem Jeffries allowed Democrats to vote their conscience on the measure.

The Senate had earlier voted 50-46 to block debate on the $1.15 trillion National Defense Authorization Act (NDAA) in protest of Trump's war on Iran. Schumer argued that Trump "expects Congress to look the other way and ignore his illegal war, just days after he tore up his own ceasefire deal."

US Strikes Iranian Barracks at Bampur Base; Trump Threatens to Expand Targets

At 00:18 GMT, US forces struck a military base in Bampur, in Iran's southeastern Sistan and Baluchestan province. According to Iran's Student News Network (SNN), projectiles appeared to hit accommodation quarters where soldiers were resting. There is no confirmed information yet on casualties or the extent of damage.

In a Fox News interview, Trump warned that strikes would continue "until I say it's enough" and threatened to expand targeting to Iranian power plants and bridges starting next week. "We're going to knock out all their power plants. We're going to knock out all their bridges unless they get to the table and negotiate," Trump said. He claimed that "Iran has no choice but to make a deal" and revealed that US representatives had been in contact with Iranian officials as recently as an hour before his interview.

Meanwhile, Iran's permanent representative to the UN, Amir-Saeid Iravani, wrote to Secretary-General Antonio Guterres, documenting 42 "clear and fundamental violations" of the Islamabad MoU by the United States and describing Washington's "deliberate attacks on civilians and civilian infrastructure" as constituting "a war crime and a crime against humanity."

Iraq Sets September 30 Deadline for Full US Troop Withdrawal

During his Washington visit, Iraqi Prime Minister al-Zaidi formally announced the timeline for the US military withdrawal. Under the agreement reaffirming a 2024 pact to end the mission against ISIL, approximately 2,500 US troops currently in Iraq will complete their withdrawal by September 30, 2026.

Analysts noted the tension between Iraq's demand for US withdrawal and Hegseth's pressure to disarm Iran-aligned militias before departure. The US administration is pushing Baghdad to weaken Iran's influence network in Iraq, where Iranian-backed militia groups have launched over 600 attacks on US forces.

Iran Says MoU Dismantled; UN Warns of Humanitarian Consequences

Iran's Deputy Foreign Minister Kazem Gharibabadi confirmed that Tehran considers the Islamabad Memorandum of Understanding effectively "dismantled" after the US renewed its naval blockade, declaring Iran no longer has any commitments under the MoU. Ebrahim Rezaei, a member of Iran's parliament national security committee, stated bluntly: "The enemy has officially entered a state of war; the MoU has been invalidated."

UN High Commissioner for Human Rights Volker Türk condemned the renewed hostilities in the Middle East, warning that the re-blockade of the Strait of Hormuz would have major human rights impacts beyond the region. "Interruption of shipments of food, medicine, and other essential goods will have severe socio-economic and humanitarian consequences," Türk said, calling on Washington and Tehran to "immediately" cease hostilities and restore the ceasefire.

Brent crude has surged approximately 14% this week, though it remains well below its wartime peak of $126/barrel. According to Kpler ship-tracking data, only 22 vessels crossed the Strait of Hormuz on July 9 — an 85% collapse from the pre-war daily average of 147 crossings in February.
