⚡ July 19 International Politics: 2 US Troops Killed in Jordan, Khamenei Says Trump's Signature 'Worthless', Ukraine Strikes Moscow Region with 379 Drones

1. Breaking: 2 US Service Members Killed in Iranian Attack on Jordan Base

The US Central Command confirmed on Saturday that two US service members were killed in action and one remains missing following Iranian ballistic missile and drone attacks on a US military base in Jordan on Friday. Four additional American service members were medically evacuated to Jordanian hospitals and have since been discharged. This marks the first time US troops have been killed directly by Iranian fire since the US-Iran war erupted in February 2026, representing a dramatic escalation in the conflict.

US Defense Secretary Pete Hegseth wrote on social media platform X: "Godspeed, heroes." According to US media reports, a total of 16 American service members have now died in military operations related to the US-Iran war.

The US State Department issued a worldwide travel advisory urging Americans to "exercise increased caution," noting that "US diplomatic facilities, including outside the Middle East, have been targeted" and that "groups supportive of Iran may target other US interests overseas."

2. Iran's Supreme Leader Issues Written Statement: Deal 'Utterly Worthless'

In his first written statement since the war began, Iran's Supreme Leader Mojtaba Khamenei declared that President Donald Trump's signature on the US-Iran interim peace deal is "worthless and invalid", and warned that Iran and its Axis of Resistance allies would deliver "unforgettable lessons" if the United States continues its attacks. The statement was read out on state television.

Hours earlier, Iranian Deputy Foreign Minister Kazem Gharibabadi told state TV that Tehran has suspended all commitments under the Islamabad Memorandum of Understanding (MoU), accusing the US of violating every term of the deal signed approximately one month ago. No new mediation efforts have been reported.

Key points from Khamenei's statement: ① Trump's signature is "worthless"; ② Iran reserves the right to launch a "full-scale offensive"; ③ warns that "no political border is safe"; ④ Iran's military is shifting from "proportional retaliation" to "strategic offense"

3. US Strikes Iran for 7th Consecutive Night, Targets Infrastructure

US Central Command confirmed the seventh straight night of strikes against Iran, stating that targets included "surveillance sites, military logistics infrastructure, underground weapons storage, and maritime capabilities." This is the first time US military statements have explicitly mentioned "infrastructure" among targeted categories since operations resumed.

Iranian media reported that enemy attacks struck Iran's southern Hormozgan province along the Strait of Hormuz coast, causing casualties and damaging two bridges and a road tunnel. Multiple missiles hit power generation and desalination facilities in the southern city of Jask, cutting off drinking water to several villages. The previous day, Iranian media reported at least five bridges had been damaged in southern Iran.

Iran's Health Ministry released updated figures showing that 50 Iranians have been killed and more than 500 wounded in US strikes over the past three weeks.

4. Iran Expands Retaliation, Strikes US Base in Syria for First Time

The IRGC launched an unprecedented 16-wave retaliatory campaign ("Operation Nasr 2") on Saturday, striking US military assets across the Middle East:

  • Al-Tanf, Syria: IRGC struck the US special operations command center in Syrian territory for the first time, destroying radar systems and several special operations helicopters, inflicting heavy US casualties
  • Kuwait: Wave 12 destroyed a missile defense detection radar, multiple major weapons depots, and two HIMARS launchers at US bases. Kuwait's military reported intercepting 32 drone attacks
  • Bahrain: IRGC claimed destruction of US combat aircraft at Sheikh Isa Air Base plus an intelligence data center, destroying at least 2 fighter jets
  • Jordan: Wave 14 targeted US fighter jets and aerial refueling aircraft, destroying several tankers and fighters
  • Qatar: Wave 16 struck Al Udeid Air Base, destroying long-range radar systems and strategic refueling aircraft
  • Oman: IRGC struck maritime surveillance radar on Salameh Rocks and US air control radar in Ghanem

Arab League Secretary-General Nabil Elaraby condemned Iran's strikes on Gulf nations as "an aggressive approach that cannot be tolerated or accepted."

5. Ukraine Launches 379 Drones Across 19 Russian Regions, Hits Moscow Logistics Hub

On the Ukraine front, Kyiv launched one of its largest drone offensives since the war began. Russia's Defense Ministry claimed to have intercepted and destroyed 379 Ukrainian drones across 19 regions overnight, spanning land, sea, and air domains.

The heaviest losses occurred in Kotovsk, Tambov region: Ukrainian drones struck a major logistics center operated by Russia's largest e-commerce company Wildberries, killing 7 night-shift workers and wounding 25. Wildberries founder and CEO Tatyana Kim called it a "terrible night." A second Wildberries warehouse was hit in Elektrostal, Moscow region, injuring 24. An oil depot in Noginsk, Moscow region, was set ablaze by drone debris.

President Zelensky publicly claimed credit for the strikes, calling them "symmetrical retaliation" for Russia's long campaign of bombing Ukrainian civilian infrastructure. He revealed that the Wildberries warehouses were actually sanctions-evading transit hubs for Russian drone components and navigation equipment — hidden military logistics nodes. Ukraine's drone forces commander said Ukrainian strikes had hit 172 Russian vessels in the Black and Azov Seas over the past week.

Meanwhile, protests over the dismissal of Defense Minister Mykhailo Fedorov entered their third day in Kyiv and other Ukrainian cities. Demonstrators demand Fedorov's reinstatement and the removal of Commander-in-Chief Oleksandr Syrskyi.

6. Outlook: Full-Scale War Risk Continues to Rise

Analysts view the US troop deaths as a critical inflection point. While the Trump administration has conducted seven consecutive nights of strikes, it had previously avoided direct engagement of US ground forces. The two deaths in Jordan may force the White House to make a clearer choice between escalating military operations and pursuing a diplomatic off-ramp.

The Wall Street Journal reported that Trump, after briefings, is leaning toward expanding military operations, with options including deploying ground forces to seize Iranian islands in the Strait of Hormuz and bombing Iran's underground nuclear facility "Mountain of Pickaxes." However, Vice President JD Vance has publicly dissented, denying plans for regime-change ground operations and insisting that "you can't win with force alone — you must return to the negotiating table." Iran's Supreme Leader advisor Mohsen Rezaei has warned that if US strikes continue past the weekend, Iran will "enter the full-scale offensive phase in the coming days."

Brent crude has surged past $86, and global shipping risks continue to escalate. Strait of Hormuz traffic has fallen to a three-week low. Iran has also ordered Houthi forces to prepare to blockade the Bab el-Mandeb Strait in the Red Sea. If both chokepoints are severed simultaneously, the global energy supply would face an unprecedented crisis.
